A systematic review of patient decision aids for hypertension.

Revue : BMC Medical Informatics and Decision Making | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-11

Resultats principaux : This systematic review screened 1,874 records to identify and evaluate patient decision aids (PDAs) for arterial hypertension using the International Patient Decision Aid Standards (IPDAS) criteria, ultimately including five PDAs developed between 2019 and 2024 from the United States, United Kingdom, and Germany. All five PDAs addressed medication and lifestyle as the two primary option areas, but differed substantially in the number of individual treatment options presented, ranging from 2 to 12 options, and in the depth of information provided. All five tools were freely accessible online and shared common design features such as option grids, visual aids, and value clarification exercises to support shared decision-making between patients and clinicians. Quality assessments using the IPDAS Minimal Criteria revealed considerable variability, with overall scores ranging widely from 49/116 to 87/116, reflecting important gaps in development rigor across the identified tools. Notable limitations include the small total number of qualifying PDAs despite the high global prevalence of hypertension, the restriction to English and German language publications, and the absence of randomized evidence on PDA effectiveness in hypertensive populations.

Implications Medical Affairs : The scarcity of high-quality hypertension PDAs represents an unmet need in patient-centered care that pharmaceutical companies could address through co-development partnerships with healthcare systems and academic centers, thereby enhancing their medical affairs profile. Structured shared decision-making tools that present evidence-based medication options may improve patient adherence and strengthen prescribing patterns for established antihypertensive classes, making PDA co-development a strategic lever for Medical Affairs. KOL engagement initiatives targeting health technology assessment experts and patient advocacy groups could be built around the standardization and validation of next-generation hypertension PDAs.

Night shift work as a potential risk factor for hypertensive disorders of pregnancy: the Japan environment and children's study.

Revue : International Archives of Occupational and Environmental Health | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-11

Resultats principaux : This nationwide prospective cohort study analyzed data from 90,660 mother-child dyads drawn from the Japan Environment and Children's Study, examining whether night shift work during pregnancy independently increases the risk of hypertensive disorders of pregnancy (HDP). The overall incidence rate of HDP was 2.5%, with early-onset HDP at 0.5% and late-onset HDP at 2.0% of the cohort. After adjusting for potential confounders, night shift work was significantly associated with overall HDP risk (adjusted OR 1.20, 95% CI 1.01-1.44, p=0.04), representing a 20% increase in risk. The association was specifically driven by late-onset HDP (aOR 1.23, 95% CI 1.01-1.49, p=0.04), while no significant association was observed between night shift work and early-onset HDP (aOR 1.09, 95% CI 0.72-1.66, p=0.68). Key limitations include the single-point assessment of night shift work via questionnaire during the second/third trimester, and the inability to disentangle specific mechanisms such as circadian disruption, sleep deprivation, or psychosocial stress.

Implications Medical Affairs : The specific association between night shift work and late-onset HDP introduces occupational exposures as an underappreciated risk factor in gestational hypertension, warranting integration into pre-conception and antenatal risk stratification tools that Medical Affairs can support through educational initiatives. From a product positioning perspective, this study reinforces the need for antihypertensive options that are both safe in pregnancy and suited for women with irregular schedules and circadian disruption, highlighting an unaddressed therapeutic niche. Collaboration with KOLs in maternal-fetal medicine and occupational health to co-author guidelines or white papers on occupational risk in gestational hypertension would strengthen scientific leadership in this emerging area.

Bidirectional associations of hypertension and chronic kidney disease risks under metal exposure: A mixture exposure and exploratory decomposition analysis.

Revue : Medicine (Baltimore) | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-10

Resultats principaux : This cross-sectional study enrolled 4,776 adults from the NHANES 2011-2018 dataset to evaluate the joint effects of co-exposure to multiple metals on the risks of hypertension and chronic kidney disease (CKD) using five complementary statistical methods. The blood metals mixture was positively associated with both CKD and hypertension risks, with lead (Pb) and cadmium (Cd) identified as the primary contributing metals driving these associations. Conversely, the urinary metals mixture exhibited an inverse association with disease risk, with barium (Ba) and thallium (Tl) identified as main contributors in this inverse direction. Restricted cubic spline analyses revealed heterogeneous dose-response relationships, including linear, U-shaped, L-shaped, and inverse U-shaped patterns across different metals. Exploratory decomposition analysis demonstrated a bidirectional mediation structure: renal function partially mediated the metal-hypertension association, while blood pressure partially mediated the metal-CKD relationship, indicating a shared pathophysiological axis. Critical limitations include the cross-sectional design precluding causal inference, reliance on single-point metal biomarker measurements, and the inability to assess long-term cumulative exposure.

Implications Medical Affairs : The identification of blood Pb and Cd as principal drivers in the metal-hypertension-CKD nexus extends the environmental risk factor evidence base for hypertension, which Medical Affairs teams should incorporate into disease burden communications targeting public health stakeholders and regulatory bodies. The bidirectional mediation findings suggest that patients with CKD and comorbid metal exposure represent a high-risk subgroup for whom early antihypertensive intervention may interrupt a pathological cycle between renal function decline and blood pressure elevation. Future research should prioritize interventional designs examining whether aggressive antihypertensive management attenuates metal-induced CKD progression, a topic well-suited for KOL-driven investigator-initiated studies.

Analysis of the influence of apparent temperature on hypertension outpatient visits in Haikou city using a distributed lag nonlinear model.

Revue : Medicine (Baltimore) | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-10

Resultats principaux : Using daily data from September 2016 to April 2018 in Haikou, a tropical Chinese city, this study applied correlation analysis and a distributed lag nonlinear model (DLNM) to quantify the relationship between apparent temperature (AT) and daily hypertension outpatient visits. Cold temperature emerged as the dominant risk driver: when AT fell below 24.9 degrees Celsius, outpatient risk increased with decreasing temperature, peaking at 9.1 degrees Celsius with a same-day relative risk of 2.37 (95% CI: 1.771-3.173). In contrast, high temperatures above 24.9 degrees Celsius were associated with a decreased outpatient risk, reaching a minimum at 29.9 degrees Celsius (RR 0.856, 95% CI: 0.741-0.989). Subgroup analyses revealed that cold effects were stronger in males than females, while heat effects were observed only in the working-age group, with the cold effect on juveniles limited to the day of exposure but extending to lag days 2 and 6 for working-age and elderly groups, respectively. A meteorological risk grade index for hypertension was established with critical AT cut-points of 11.07 degrees Celsius, 12.34 degrees Celsius, and 14.88 degrees Celsius for high, relatively high, and medium risk levels. Limitations include the retrospective single-city design, absence of individual-level confounders, and a study period that may not reflect current climate conditions.

Implications Medical Affairs : Temperature-dependent seasonal fluctuations in hypertension risk provide a basis for developing climate-integrated patient monitoring programs, and Medical Affairs teams could leverage this evidence to support healthcare system partnerships for seasonally optimized antihypertensive therapy protocols. The significant lag effects observed in elderly patients suggest that temperature-responsive treatment intensification strategies could reduce hospitalizations in this high-risk group, providing a compelling health economic argument for proactive pharmacological management. Collaboration with tropical medicine and climate health KOLs to establish evidence-based meteorological early warning systems for hypertension would position a pharmaceutical company as a leader in environmental cardiovascular medicine.

Serum Mucin-1 (CA15-3) in Relation to Pulse Pressure Amplification in Untreated Chinese Patients.

Revue : Journal of Clinical Hypertension | Tier : Tier 3 | IF estime : 3–10

Date de publication : 2026-07

Resultats principaux : This prospective single-center study enrolled 1,761 outpatients suspected of hypertension but without antihypertensive medication for at least two weeks, measuring serum mucin-1 (CA15-3) concentrations via ELISA and assessing pulse pressure amplification (PPA) as a marker of arterial stiffness. The population included 916 women (52.0%), 578 participants with confirmed clinic hypertension (32.8%), and a mean age of 51.3 plus or minus 10.6 years. After multivariable adjustment, higher CA15-3 was significantly associated with higher peripheral and central systolic and diastolic blood pressure (all p less than or equal to 0.009) and with lower PPA (p=0.037), indicating greater arterial stiffness. Among 428 participants with baseline PPA greater than or equal to 130%, 185 progressed to lower PPA over a median follow-up of 4.65 years; hazard ratio for PPA progression was 1.22 per 1 SD increment in log-transformed CA15-3 (p=0.027). A significant sex-specific interaction was identified (p for interaction 0.015), with women showing a significantly higher risk of PPA progression with increasing CA15-3, while no such association was observed in men. Limitations include the single-center outpatient design, the use of ELISA which may have measurement variability, and the need for multi-ethnic validation.

Implications Medical Affairs : Serum mucin-1 (CA15-3) as a novel biomarker of arterial stiffness progression in untreated hypertensive patients opens new avenues for risk stratification-driven treatment initiation, a concept Medical Affairs can champion in dialogues with guideline committees and clinical decision-making bodies. The sex-specific effect, with women being significantly more susceptible to mucin-1-associated arterial stiffening, aligns with growing evidence of sex differences in hypertension pathophysiology and supports tailored treatment messaging and product differentiation strategies. Medical Affairs could initiate collaborations with KOLs at leading hypertension research centers to validate CA15-3 as a companion biomarker in antihypertensive drug development programs specifically targeting vascular stiffness as a therapeutic endpoint.

Association of Echocardiographic Ventricular-Arterial Coupling With Elevated 10-Year ASCVD Risk in Hypertensive Patients.

Revue : Echocardiography | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07

Resultats principaux : This cross-sectional study enrolled 190 hypertensive patients and 203 normotensive controls, evaluating ventricular-arterial coupling (VAC) using the AVI/LVGLS ratio (aortic velocity pulse index to left ventricular global longitudinal strain) and estimating 10-year ASCVD risk via the China-PAR model, with high risk defined as greater than or equal to 10%. AVI/LVGLS was significantly higher in hypertensive patients compared to controls (p less than 0.05), reflecting impaired VAC in the setting of chronic pressure overload. LASSO regression identified AVI/LVGLS as the most important predictor of high ASCVD risk among all echocardiographic parameters assessed. Receiver operating characteristic analysis demonstrated that AVI/LVGLS outperformed individual metrics including VVI, AVI alone, and LVGLS alone in discriminating high ASCVD risk. In multivariable logistic regression, AVI/LVGLS below the 5th percentile was independently associated with elevated ASCVD risk after adjustment for conventional cardiovascular risk factors (OR=6.106, 95% CI: 1.352-27.565, p=0.019). Limitations include the relatively small sample size limiting statistical power for subgroup analyses, the cross-sectional design preventing temporal inference, and the China-PAR model's limited external applicability.

Implications Medical Affairs : The VAC metric AVI/LVGLS represents an integrated non-invasive echocardiographic tool for cardiovascular risk stratification in hypertensive patients that could supplement or refine traditional risk algorithms, a development that Medical Affairs teams should track for its potential to reshape patient selection in clinical trials. As a composite endpoint capturing both myocardial and vascular alterations, AVI/LVGLS could serve as a sensitive secondary endpoint in cardiovascular outcomes trials for antihypertensive agents with claimed benefits on cardiac mechanics and arterial stiffness. Engaging echocardiography and preventive cardiology KOLs to incorporate VAC metrics into hypertension management algorithms could create a scientifically defensible product differentiation platform for compounds with demonstrated effects on cardiac remodeling.

Association analysis of weight-adjusted waist index with hypertension and its subtypes.

Revue : Frontiers in Public Health | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-26

Resultats principaux : Using stratified multistage random sampling, this cross-sectional study enrolled 6,974 permanent residents aged 18 years and older from six cities in Jilin Province, northeastern China (2021-2022), to examine associations between the weight-adjusted waist index (WWI) and hypertension subtypes including systolic-diastolic hypertension (SDH), isolated systolic hypertension (ISH), and isolated diastolic hypertension (IDH). The highest WWI quartile (Q4) demonstrated a significant association with overall hypertension risk compared to the lowest quartile (adjusted OR=1.974, 95% CI: 1.611-2.420). Subtype analysis revealed the strongest effect size for SDH (OR=2.448, 95% CI: 1.827-3.281), while no significant association was observed for IDH. Mediation analysis indicated that HDL-C and LDL-C partially mediated the WWI-hypertension relationship, with mediated proportions of 2.35% and 3.92%, respectively, while HDL-C fully mediated the IDH association (18.2% mediation). ROC analysis showed that WWI had comparable predictive performance to BMI and waist circumference for ISH (p greater than 0.05). The cross-sectional design limits causal inference and prospective cohort confirmation is needed.

Implications Medical Affairs : The WWI as a refined obesity metric that outperforms conventional anthropometric measures for specific hypertension subtypes could support more precise patient stratification in clinical practice guidelines, an area where Medical Affairs engagement with guideline authors can add strategic value. The partial mediation through HDL-C and LDL-C pathways identifies metabolic-lipid interactions as potentially actionable pharmacological targets in obese hypertensive patients, relevant for portfolio positioning of dual-acting cardiometabolic agents. Medical Affairs teams should monitor the adoption trajectory of WWI in clinical practice, as its validation could reshape screening algorithms and patient identification strategies for antihypertensive market development, particularly in Asian populations.

Association of radiation exposure and blood lipid derived indices with hypertension risk in radiation workers.

Revue : Frontiers in Public Health | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-26

Resultats principaux : This study combined a cross-sectional analysis (2,548 subjects) and a retrospective cohort study (484 subjects) among radiation workers who underwent physical examinations at a medical center in Shijiazhuang from October 2021 to March 2025. In the cross-sectional analysis, radiation working years, high working-year group, and industrial radiation exposure were each independently associated with hypertension risk (all p less than 0.05), while HDL-C was protective and elevated TC, TG, LDL-C, and composite lipid indices (FBG/HDL-C, TC/HDL-C, NHHR) were risk factors. Mediation analysis demonstrated that TC, TG, and LDL-C partially mediated the positive association between long working years and hypertension, while HDL-C mediated the inverse association of working years and industrial radiation with hypertension. The cohort analysis confirmed that elevated baseline FBG/HDL-C and TC/HDL-C had the best predictive performance for incident hypertension (superior to conventional lipid indices), with these associations more prominent in males and overweight individuals. Subgroup and sensitivity analyses supported robustness of findings. Primary limitations include the single-center retrospective cohort design and potential selection bias in occupational cohorts.

Implications Medical Affairs : The demonstration that occupational radiation exposure increases hypertension risk via lipid-mediated pathways identifies a clinically distinct risk group for whom concurrent antihypertensive and lipid-lowering therapy may be particularly beneficial, representing a niche positioning opportunity for combination products. The superiority of composite indices (FBG/HDL-C, TC/HDL-C) over conventional lipid biomarkers for hypertension prediction supports pharmaceutical companies in validating composite screening panels as part of risk-stratified therapeutic intervention programs. Engagement with occupational medicine specialists and radiation safety authorities as KOLs could enable Medical Affairs teams to position antihypertensive agents within workplace health promotion programs targeting this growing occupationally exposed population.

Association between the traditional Chinese diet, non-communicable diseases and all-cause mortality: a longitudinal study based on China Health and Nutrition Survey (CHNS).

Revue : European Journal of Nutrition | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-11

Resultats principaux : This longitudinal study utilized data from the China Health and Nutrition Survey (CHNS) between 1997 and 2011, enrolling 11,158 participants aged 18-74 years free from non-communicable diseases (NCDs) at baseline who participated in at least two survey waves, with TCD adherence scored via a validated a priori index categorized as low (0-6), medium (7-11), or high (12-23). Using mixed-effects multilevel logistic regression and Cox proportional hazards models, high TCD adherence was associated with lower risk of cardiovascular disease (OR: 0.73, 95% CI 0.61-0.87) and all-cause mortality (HR: 0.73, 95% CI 0.63-0.87) compared to low adherence. High TCD adherence was initially associated with higher obesity risk (OR: 1.19, 95% CI 1.08-1.32), but this positive association reversed upon standardization for total energy intake, attributing the obesity signal to caloric density rather than the dietary pattern itself. No statistically significant association was identified between TCD adherence and hypertension risk specifically, despite the strong associations with CVD outcomes more broadly. Primary limitations include reliance on 24-hour dietary recalls susceptible to recall bias, the historical study period (1997-2011) that may not reflect contemporary Chinese dietary habits, and the observational design precluding causal inference.

Implications Medical Affairs : The TCD's protective association with CVD and all-cause mortality, even in the absence of a direct hypertension signal, underscores the complementary role of dietary pattern interventions alongside pharmacological antihypertensive strategies and supports Medical Affairs messaging on comprehensive lifestyle co-intervention in cardiovascular risk reduction programs. This evidence base strengthens the rationale for incorporating culturally tailored dietary guidance into hypertension disease management programs, particularly for Asian patient populations where TCD adherence may be more sustainable than Western dietary recommendations. KOL engagement with nutritional epidemiologists and cardiovascular preventive medicine specialists should be prioritized to help integrate traditional dietary pattern evidence into hypertension treatment guidelines and medical education initiatives.

Association between relative fat mass and cognitive impairment in older adults: A cross-sectional study using NHANES 2011-2014 data.

Revue : Medicine (Baltimore) | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-10

Resultats principaux : This cross-sectional study used NHANES 2011-2014 data including 2,730 adults aged 60 years and older with valid anthropometric, cognitive assessment, and covariate data to examine the association between relative fat mass (RFM), derived from height and waist circumference, and cognitive impairment assessed via CERAD word learning and delayed recall tests, animal fluency, and digit symbol substitution tests. After full multivariable adjustment for demographic, socioeconomic, lifestyle, dietary, and clinical factors, higher RFM was significantly associated with increased odds of cognitive impairment (OR=1.05, 95% CI: 1.02-1.09), with a statistically significant linear dose-response relationship. The association was stronger among participants aged less than 70 years and in those with comorbid hypertension or prior stroke, suggesting effect modification by vascular risk burden. Mediation analysis identified hypertension (mediating 10% of the association) and physical inactivity (mediating 9.4%) as partial mediators of the RFM-cognitive impairment relationship, while depression and diabetes showed negligible mediation effects. Principal limitations include the cross-sectional design precluding causal inference, the composite cognitive z-score definition of impairment, and the NHANES sampling frame which may limit generalizability to non-US populations.

Implications Medical Affairs : The mediation of adiposity-driven cognitive impairment through hypertension provides an important additional rationale for aggressive blood pressure control in obese older adults, strengthening the cardioprotective and neuroprotective value proposition of antihypertensive agents beyond traditional cardiovascular endpoints. Medical Affairs teams should actively monitor the emerging hypertension-cognition literature, as this evidence stream may create new market opportunities for positioning long-acting antihypertensives within a cognitive protection narrative targeted at geriatric care settings. Expanding KOL engagement beyond traditional cardiology to include geriatricians, neurologists, and cognitive health specialists would position pharmaceutical companies as scientific leaders in the dementia prevention landscape.

Association of the C-reactive protein-triglyceride glucose index with cardiovascular disease and mortality in the general US population: A NHANES study.

Revue : Medicine (Baltimore) | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-10

Resultats principaux : This study analyzed data from 8,720 adults in NHANES 2001-2010 to evaluate the C-reactive protein-triglyceride glucose index (CTI), a novel composite biomarker calculated as CTI = 0.412 times ln(CRP mg/L) plus ln(TG mg/dL times FPG mg/dL) divided by 2, integrating systemic inflammation and insulin resistance into a single metric. Higher CTI tertiles were significantly associated with worse metabolic profiles and higher prevalence of both hypertension and diabetes (p less than 0.001 for trend). After full multivariable adjustment, the highest CTI tertile (T3 versus T1) was independently associated with increased CVD risk (OR=1.50, 95% CI: 1.18-1.92, p=0.004), cardiovascular mortality (HR=1.54, 95% CI: 1.06-2.25, p=0.024), and all-cause mortality (HR=1.53, 95% CI: 1.29-1.89, p less than 0.001). Restricted cubic spline analysis demonstrated a nonlinear relationship between CTI and both cardiovascular and all-cause mortality, with accelerating risk above intermediate CTI values. Key limitations include the cross-sectional assessment of CVD outcomes limiting causal interpretation, retrospective mortality linkage which may introduce ascertainment bias, and NHANES sampling characteristics that limit geographic and ethnic generalizability.

Implications Medical Affairs : The CTI as an integrated inflammatory-metabolic risk index could serve as a powerful patient stratification tool for identifying hypertensive individuals at highest cardiovascular mortality risk, a concept Medical Affairs should advocate in discussions with clinical guideline committees and payer organizations. The strong and independent association of CTI with both cardiovascular and all-cause mortality in a hypertensive-enriched population supports its potential role as a treatment intensification trigger, providing a scientific basis for Medical Affairs to engage KOLs in designing CTI-stratified clinical trials for antihypertensive and cardiometabolic agents. Pharmaceutical companies could differentiate their portfolios by sponsoring prospective validation studies of CTI as a composite risk biomarker in hypertensive patients undergoing pharmacological treatment.

Non-laboratory-based cardiovascular risk assessment: 10-year absolute risk, predicted heart age and intervention requirements in a Nigerian population.

Revue : BMC Cardiovascular Disorders | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-10

Resultats principaux : This secondary analysis enrolled 3,620 adults aged 30-80 years (51% female) from a population-based cross-sectional hypertension survey in Lagos, Nigeria, applying a non-laboratory-based CVD risk assessment framework to estimate 10-year absolute CVD risk and predicted heart age profiles. A total of 8.7% of the population had a 10-year absolute CVD risk of 20% or greater, with males at significantly higher risk than females (12.3% versus 5.3%, p less than 0.0001). Elevated systolic blood pressure (25.4%) and excess BMI (overweight 29.6%, obesity 17.6%) were the most frequent risk-determining factors; excess BMI was more prevalent in females while elevated systolic BP frequencies were similar across sexes. High excess heart age (5 or more years above chronological age) was present in 42.3% of the population, affecting 45.0% of males and 39.7% of females. Of the subset with CVD risk 20% or greater, antihypertensive treatment alone was the most frequently required intervention (47.2%), followed by antihypertensive in combination with statin and aspirin (26.9%). The cross-sectional design and secondary data nature limit causal inference and may not fully capture the temporal dynamics of risk factor burden in this population.

Implications Medical Affairs : The high CVD risk burden and predominance of antihypertensive therapy requirements in this Nigerian urban population quantify the substantial unmet treatment need in Sub-Saharan Africa, a strategically important and underserved market for antihypertensive portfolio expansion. The high proportion of patients requiring combination antihypertensive, lipid-lowering, and antiplatelet therapy supports the commercial and clinical case for positioning fixed-dose combination products adapted to the resource constraints and comorbidity profiles of African healthcare systems. Engaging Nigerian and broader African cardiovascular KOLs to generate local evidence on treatment access, adherence barriers, and outcomes will be essential for pharmaceutical companies seeking to establish credible scientific leadership and market presence in this high-growth region.

Utility of the Triglyceride and HDL Cholesterol Index in Older Persons in Colombia.

Revue : Health Science Reports | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-09

Resultats principaux : This cross-sectional study included 23,694 older adults aged 60 years and older (57.3% women, median age 69 years) from the nationally representative SABE Colombian survey, evaluating the TG/HDL-c index's predictive capacity for cardiometabolic risk factors including diabetes, metabolic syndrome, and hypertension. The TG/HDL-c index was independently associated with diabetes (adjusted OR per 1 SD: 1.25, 95% CI: 1.13-1.37, p less than 0.001) and metabolic syndrome (adjusted OR per 1 SD: 2.82, 95% CI: 2.46-3.22, p less than 0.001), with excellent discriminatory performance for diabetes (AUC=0.89, cut-off 9.17, sensitivity 0.81, specificity 0.90) and metabolic syndrome (AUC=0.86, cut-off 8.81, sensitivity 0.71, specificity 0.92). In contrast, discriminatory performance for hypertension was limited, with AUC values ranging from only 0.52 to 0.67 across hypertension-related outcomes, indicating poor utility of TG/HDL-c specifically for identifying hypertensive patients. Subgroup and sensitivity analyses confirmed robustness of associations for diabetes and metabolic syndrome. Limitations include the cross-sectional design precluding temporal inference, potential selection bias in an older adult population, and lack of generalizability to younger age groups or non-Colombian Latin American populations.

Implications Medical Affairs : The demonstrated inadequacy of the TG/HDL-c index for hypertension risk discrimination in this large Latin American cohort establishes an important negative finding, clarifying for Medical Affairs that lipid ratio-based biomarkers cannot substitute for blood pressure-specific screening tools in hypertension identification programs. This finding reinforces the necessity of dedicated hypertension screening algorithms based on direct blood pressure measurement in Latin American older adult populations, supporting advocacy for resource-appropriate diagnostic infrastructure within pharmaceutical companies' market access strategies. Opportunities exist for Medical Affairs to partner with Colombian and Latin American KOLs to validate better-performing composite cardiometabolic risk tools that could simultaneously identify patients eligible for combined antihypertensive and metabolic therapy in this high-burden region.
