Exploring the potential of community health workers in type-2 diabetes and hypertension management in Cambodia.

Revue : PloS One | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-23

Resultats principaux : This mixed-method study conducted in Cambodia enrolled 153 active community health workers (CHWs) to assess their knowledge, attitudes, and practices (KAP) regarding type-2 diabetes and hypertension, combined with semi-structured interviews of key informants to identify health system barriers. Approximately 90% of CHWs correctly answered general questions on NCDs, risk factors, and prevention, indicating a solid baseline awareness. However, a notable knowledge gap emerged concerning specific risk factors: only 20-40% of CHWs correctly identified family history or tobacco use as established risk factors. Most CHWs demonstrated positive attitudes and reported engaging in some hypertension-related activities, though implementation was constrained by both financial and non-financial resource limitations. Key informant interviews highlighted the need to restructure CHW roles and systematically assess workload, while well-designed technical training and robust support structures were identified as essential for effective CHW integration into the health system.

Implications Medical Affairs : These findings underscore the critical role of CHWs in expanding hypertension management in low-resource settings, suggesting that targeted training investments could substantially improve disease control at the community level in Southeast Asia, a market segment of growing strategic importance for pharmaceutical companies pursuing access programs. For Medical Affairs teams, this evidence supports engagement with governments and NGOs in Cambodia and similar markets to co-develop training curricula and care pathways that standardize hypertension management through CHW networks. Public health researchers working on CHW integration in low-income countries represent an important KOL advocacy network for raising NCD awareness and amplifying real-world evidence for treatment adherence programs.

Can shortening the waiting time for gestational hypertension and preeclampsia during labor reduce adverse outcomes of conversion to cesarean section?: A retrospective cohort study.

Revue : Medicine (Baltimore) | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-19

Resultats principaux : This retrospective cohort study enrolled 1,188 primiparous women who underwent cervical balloon dilation induction at a Chinese hospital between January 2022 and December 2024, focusing on those with gestational hypertension and preeclampsia (GH-PE). Curve fitting analyses identified a critical inflection point at 3.1 hours for cervical dilation from 2 to 4 cm: shorter durations correlated with higher rates of successful vaginal delivery, while the success rate plateaued beyond this threshold. Among women who underwent emergency cesarean section, those with cervical dilation duration exceeding 3.1 hours had significantly higher incidence of postpartum hemorrhage compared with those under 3.1 hours (OR: 5.97, 95% CI: 1.38-25.78), as well as greater hemorrhage volume. Subgroup analyses were not reported beyond the threshold comparison, and only emergency cesarean conversions were studied. Key limitations include the single-center retrospective design, inclusion of only primiparous women, and reliance on a single induction method, limiting generalizability to multiparous women or alternative labor induction protocols.

Implications Medical Affairs : These findings provide clinically actionable decision thresholds for managing labor in women with hypertensive disorders of pregnancy, a complication of significant maternal-fetal morbidity that directly informs obstetric care protocols and hospital guidelines relevant to antihypertensive product use in peripartum settings. For Medical Affairs, the data reinforce the importance of optimizing time-sensitive interventions in GH-PE patients, creating opportunities to engage with obstetric KOLs on the integration of antihypertensive management into labor decision algorithms. The association between prolonged labor and postpartum hemorrhage risk in this population opens avenues for investigator-initiated studies examining the cardiovascular and hemostatic sequelae of GH-PE management as pharmaceutical research partnership opportunities.

Association of family history of hypertension with preventive attitudes and quality of life in turkish population: A cross-sectional study.

Revue : Medicine (Baltimore) | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-19

Resultats principaux : This comparative cross-sectional study enrolled 152 normotensive participants at a tertiary care hospital in Turkey, equally divided between those with and without a family history of hypertension (FHH), with a mean age of 34 years and 68.4% female composition. The study assessed hypertension-preventive attitudes via the validated Hypertension Prevention Attitudes Scale (HPAS) and cardiovascular-specific quality of life via the Heart Quality of Life Scale (HeartQoL-TR). Participants with FHH demonstrated significantly higher HPAS scores (112.3 +/- 11.1 vs. 107.0 +/- 12.2, P = 0.006), representing a 5.3-point difference suggesting meaningfully greater preventive engagement. Multivariate linear regression confirmed that FHH and higher educational attainment independently predicted higher HPAS scores, while HeartQoL-TR scores did not differ significantly between groups. Notable limitations include the cross-sectional design, reliance on self-reported measures, and single-center recruitment, which preclude causal inference and limit representativeness to the broader Turkish population.

Implications Medical Affairs : The finding that a family history of hypertension independently drives more proactive preventive attitudes in young normotensive individuals has direct implications for patient education strategy, underscoring the potential efficacy of family-based risk communication campaigns targeting pre-hypertension prevention. From a Medical Affairs standpoint, this evidence can inform co-creation of patient empowerment materials with clinical societies, particularly in markets where hypertension awareness remains suboptimal, and supports positioning of screening tools targeting high-risk family clusters. Engagement with cardiovascular KOLs on longitudinal studies exploring whether heightened preventive attitudes in FHH individuals translate into improved blood pressure outcomes would be a high-value research partnership opportunity.

Neighborhood Disinvestment and Racial Disparities in Early Hypertension Onset Among Women.

Revue : JAMA Network Open | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-01

Resultats principaux : This longitudinal cohort study used data from the REGARDS (Reasons for Geographic and Racial Differences in Stroke) study, comprising 15,313 women (7,079 Black and 8,234 White) recruited nationally between 2003 and 2007, with analyses conducted from January to August 2025. Using Weibull accelerated failure time models with age as the time scale, the study quantified racial disparities in hypertension onset and their variation across levels of neighborhood socioeconomic disinvestment (low, moderate, or high) derived from US Census Bureau data. Black women developed hypertension a median of 9.6 years earlier than White women (95% CI: 9.0-10.2), independent of individual-level sociodemographic and health-related factors. This racial disparity persisted across all levels of neighborhood disinvestment: in low-disinvestment neighborhoods, Black women had hypertension onset 9.2 years earlier (95% CI: 8.2-10.3), narrowing only modestly to 8.0 years (95% CI: 6.9-9.1) in highly disinvested neighborhoods. Primary limitations include residual confounding from unmeasured structural factors and the inability to fully capture time-varying socioeconomic exposures over the long follow-up period.

Implications Medical Affairs : The magnitude of the racial disparity in hypertension onset — nearly a decade earlier in Black women regardless of neighborhood context — documents an urgent unmet need for race-targeted early screening and treatment initiation strategies that should be reflected in guideline updates and pharmaceutical company health equity programs. For Medical Affairs, these data provide compelling epidemiological backing for initiatives to expand access to antihypertensive treatments in Black women, supporting evidence-based conversations with payers and policymakers about tailored intervention age thresholds. This study opens strategic research opportunities around earlier pharmacological intervention timing in high-risk populations, including investigator-initiated trials designed around race-stratified age-of-onset endpoints in hypertension management.

Retinal Ischemic Perivascular Lesions in Pulmonary Hypertension: Retinal Biomarkers Informing Systemic Microvascular Dysfunction.

Revue : Translational Vision Science & Technology | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-01

Resultats principaux : This retrospective cross-sectional study enrolled 129 participants at Northwestern University, comprising 65 healthy controls (coronary artery calcium score 0), 18 pre-pulmonary hypertension (pre-PH), and 46 pulmonary hypertension (PH) patients who had undergone macular OCT retinal imaging within 5 years of diagnosis. Mean retinal ischemic perivascular lesion (RIPL) counts were substantially higher in pre-PH (5.78) and PH (3.93) groups compared to controls (2.05), and RIPL prevalence was also markedly elevated (94% pre-PH, 70% PH vs. 48% controls). Although RIPL counts and prevalence were elevated, increased odds of RIPLs did not reach statistical significance after multivariate adjustment, likely due to the small sample size limiting statistical power. Subgroup analysis by PH etiology revealed that PH secondary to lung disease was associated with lower RIPL counts than cardiac, thromboembolic, and idiopathic PH subtypes, suggesting differential systemic vascular involvement. Key limitations include the small single-center sample, retrospective cross-sectional design precluding causal inference, and the inability to assess longitudinal changes in RIPL burden with disease progression.

Implications Medical Affairs : The identification of retinal ischemic perivascular lesions as a potential non-invasive biomarker for systemic microvascular dysfunction in pulmonary hypertension represents an important diagnostic advancement that could facilitate earlier patient stratification and monitoring alongside traditional hemodynamic measurements in clinical practice. Medical Affairs teams focused on pulmonary hypertension therapies should track this emerging biomarker field, as non-invasive retinal imaging could strengthen endpoints for future trials and enhance disease monitoring tools for real-world evidence generation. This data opens conversations with KOLs in pulmonology and ophthalmology regarding collaborative biomarker validation studies, particularly for pre-PH identification where early therapeutic intervention may have the greatest impact on disease trajectory.

Macrophage-Dependent Intercellular Crosstalk in Multiphenotypic Heart Failure With Preserved Ejection Fraction.

Revue : Journal of the American Heart Association | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-23

Resultats principaux : This comprehensive narrative review synthesizes current evidence on the role of cardiac macrophages as central orchestrators of intercellular crosstalk in heart failure with preserved ejection fraction (HFpEF), a condition now recognized as a complex multiphenotypic metabolic syndrome driven by inflammation and metabolic dysregulation rather than a single disease entity. The review details how established cardiovascular risk factors — including hypertension, aging, and cardiometabolic disease — induce profound phenotypic switching and functional reprogramming of cardiac macrophages across different HFpEF subtypes, shifting them toward pro-inflammatory and pro-fibrotic states. Reprogrammed macrophages propagate disease progression by driving inflammation, fibrosis, and endothelial dysfunction through complex bidirectional interactions with cardiomyocytes, fibroblasts, and endothelial cells. The review elaborates on the heterogeneity of cardiac macrophage populations, distinguishing between resident and recruited subsets with distinct functional roles that map onto different clinical HFpEF phenotypes. Key limitations include the inherent heterogeneity of HFpEF as a syndrome, reliance on preclinical models that may not fully replicate human pathophysiology, and the nascent state of targeted macrophage therapies in clinical development.

Implications Medical Affairs : The emerging evidence linking hypertension-driven macrophage reprogramming to HFpEF pathogenesis highlights a novel mechanistic framework with strong implications for the development of anti-inflammatory or immunomodulatory therapies as add-on treatments in hypertensive patients with HFpEF, a large and largely undertreated patient population. Medical Affairs teams can leverage this review to engage with cardiovascular immunology KOLs and position antihypertensive therapies within the broader context of cardiac protection and prevention of adverse remodeling in HFpEF. Future research opportunities include investigating whether existing antihypertensive drug classes differentially modulate macrophage phenotypic switching, which could provide mechanistic differentiation data for portfolio positioning.

Omentin-1 Val109Asp polymorphism and increased coronary artery disease risk in smokers with type 2 diabetes.

Revue : Medicine (Baltimore) | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-19

Resultats principaux : This case-control study enrolled 465 Han Chinese patients with type 2 diabetes mellitus (T2DM), classified as CAD-positive (n=249, coronary stenosis >50% in at least one major vessel) or CAD-negative (n=216), recruited from July 2008 to September 2015, with logistic regression adjusted for age, sex, smoking, hypertension, dyslipidemia, diabetes duration, BMI, and fasting glucose. The Omentin-1 A allele (Val109Asp, rs2274907) frequency was significantly higher in CAD patients (34.5% vs. 27.5%; OR=1.386; P=0.026), and TA/AA carriers had a 50.9% increased CAD risk versus TT homozygotes (adjusted OR=1.509, 95% CI: 1.025-2.222; P=0.037). Stratified analysis demonstrated that smokers carrying the A allele faced further elevated CAD risk (adjusted OR=1.82, 95% CI: 1.02-3.27), indicating a significant gene-environment interaction between this polymorphism and tobacco exposure. No significant subgroup differences were reported by age or sex, and hypertension status was included as a confounder rather than stratification variable. Key limitations include the single-ethnicity cohort, retrospective hospital-based recruitment with potential selection bias, and the inability to establish causality from a case-control design.

Implications Medical Affairs : The Omentin-1 Val109Asp polymorphism as a modifiable-by-lifestyle genetic risk factor for CAD in T2DM patients with hypertension suggests a promising avenue for pharmacogenomic risk stratification in this high-cardiovascular-risk population, where precision medicine approaches are increasingly relevant to treatment decision-making. Medical Affairs teams could use these data to support KOL conversations in cardiology and endocrinology about integrating adipokine genetic profiling into comprehensive cardiovascular risk management protocols for patients with both T2DM and hypertension. The finding that smoking further amplifies this genetic risk strengthens the scientific rationale for multifactorial risk reduction strategies encompassing lifestyle modification alongside pharmacological antihypertensive treatment.

Low thyroid hormone status as a cardiovascular risk factor: A cross-sectional study from NHANES 2007-2012.

Revue : Medicine (Baltimore) | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-19

Resultats principaux : This cross-sectional study analyzed NHANES 2007-2012 data with 1,864 US adult participants, of whom 53 (2.84%) had ischemic heart disease (IHD) defined as self-reported physician-diagnosed coronary heart disease, angina, or myocardial infarction, with hypertension prevalence notably higher among IHD patients (81.13% vs. 47.82%). Serum FT3 and TT3 levels were significantly lower in IHD patients (FT3: 2.80 vs. 3.05 pg/mL, P<0.001; TT3: 94 vs. 110 ng/dL, P<0.001), and after multivariable adjustment, higher FT3 was independently associated with reduced IHD odds (OR: 0.34, 95% CI: 0.13-0.83; P=0.027). Quartile analysis showed participants in the highest FT3 quartile had 76% lower IHD odds versus the lowest quartile (OR: 0.24, 95% CI: 0.07-0.65), and the highest TT3 quartile showed 78% lower odds (OR: 0.22, 95% CI: 0.06-0.60). Restricted cubic spline analyses confirmed nonlinear dose-response relationships for both FT3 and TT3 with IHD risk, suggesting clinically relevant inflection points. Key limitations include the cross-sectional design precluding causal inference, the small number of IHD cases (n=53) limiting statistical power, and potential residual confounding from unmeasured factors.

Implications Medical Affairs : The strong inverse association between thyroid hormone levels — particularly FT3 — and ischemic heart disease risk in a hypertension-enriched US population positions thyroid function as a potentially underutilized cardiovascular risk biomarker warranting integration into routine cardiometabolic risk assessment alongside traditional hypertension monitoring. From a Medical Affairs perspective, these data support advocacy for thyroid function testing as part of comprehensive cardiovascular risk panels in hypertensive patients, creating potential for cross-specialty collaboration between cardiology and endocrinology KOLs. The nonlinear dose-response patterns identified warrant prospective validation and could inform the design of future trials examining thyroid hormone normalization as a cardiovascular prevention strategy in high-risk hypertensive patients.

Dietary intake and hyperuricemia among US adults: A matched case-control analysis of NHANES 2001-2020.

Revue : Medicine (Baltimore) | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-19

Resultats principaux : This frequency-matched case-control analysis used NHANES 2001-2020 data, enrolling 886 individuals with hyperuricemia and 2,171 frequency-matched controls, with dietary intake assessed by 24-hour recall data and multivariable logistic regression applied to examine food group associations with hyperuricemia, a condition closely associated with hypertension and cardiovascular disease. Higher intake of legumes, nuts and seeds (OR 0.74, 95% CI: 0.57-0.96), grain products (OR 0.78, 95% CI: 0.62-0.96), whole-wheat products (OR 0.76, 95% CI: 0.62-0.93), eggs (OR 0.85, 95% CI: 0.77-0.94), and dairy (OR 0.64, 95% CI: 0.51-0.79) were inversely associated with hyperuricemia odds. Conversely, higher consumption of meat, poultry and fish (OR 1.22, 95% CI: 1.12-1.33), sugars, sweets and beverages (OR 1.60, 95% CI: 1.28-2.00), and sugar-sweetened soft drinks (OR 1.11-1.13) were associated with significantly increased hyperuricemia risk. False discovery rate adjustments were applied for multiple comparisons, and most associations remained directionally consistent though some trend tests lost significance. Key limitations include the case-control design with potential dietary recall bias and cross-sectional NHANES data structure precluding temporal causal inferences.

Implications Medical Affairs : Given the well-established comorbid relationship between hyperuricemia, hypertension, and cardiovascular disease, these comprehensive dietary risk findings provide actionable insights for Medical Affairs teams working in the cardiometabolic space, supporting development of lifestyle intervention materials that complement pharmacological management of hypertension and uric acid dysregulation. This dietary risk profile could inform Medical Affairs-led patient education campaigns and support KOL engagement around integrated metabolic risk management protocols addressing both hypertension and hyperuricemia as interconnected conditions requiring co-management. Future research opportunities include prospective dietary intervention studies in hypertensive patients with co-existing hyperuricemia to assess whether dietary modification meaningfully reduces cardiovascular event rates, strengthening the evidence base for a holistic treatment paradigm.

Hypertension and 10-year risk of coronary artery diseases in adult population: Yazd healthy heart project.

Revue : ARYA Atherosclerosis | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026

Resultats principaux : This prospective cohort study enrolled 2,000 adults aged 20-74 years from the Yazd Healthy Heart Project in Iran, with enrollment in 2005-2006 and a 10-year follow-up completed in 2015-2016, excluding individuals with prior coronary artery disease at baseline, with data collected on demographics, anthropometrics, biochemistry, and lifestyle factors. The baseline prevalence of hypertension was 36.1%, reflecting a substantial burden of the condition in this Middle Eastern population. During the ten-year follow-up, CAD incidence reached 16.8% in men, 12.0% in women, and 14.5% in the total population, underscoring a high cardiovascular event rate. Cox regression adjusted for potential confounders demonstrated that hypertension independently increased 10-year CAD risk by 73% in the overall cohort (HR=1.73, 95% CI: 1.23-2.43), with significant associations in both men (HR=1.64, 95% CI: 1.09-2.46) and women (HR=2.16, 95% CI: 1.15-4.05). There was no statistically significant sex-hypertension interaction (P for interaction >= 0.05), and key limitations include potential residual confounding, loss to follow-up over the decade-long period, and limited generalizability beyond the Iranian study population.

Implications Medical Affairs : This long-term cohort study from Iran provides critical epidemiological evidence of hypertension's substantial independent contribution to CAD risk in a Middle Eastern population historically underrepresented in cardiovascular research, reinforcing the global burden of uncontrolled hypertension across diverse populations. For Medical Affairs, these data strengthen the scientific rationale for aggressive hypertension management in Middle Eastern and South Asian markets and provide region-specific evidence valuable for KOL engagement with local cardiovascular societies and health authorities. The numerically stronger effect observed in women (HR=2.16 vs. 1.64 in men) merits dedicated investigation in future studies powered for sex-stratified analyses, representing an actionable research agenda for pharmaceutical partners with interests in cardiovascular outcomes in this population.
