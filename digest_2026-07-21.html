Genistein inhibits oxidative stress and alleviates vascular remodeling in hypertension through the GSK3β/FYN/Nrf2 pathway.

Revue : Chinese Journal of Natural Medicines | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026 Aug

Resultats principaux : This experimental study investigated the anti-hypertensive and vasoprotective effects of genistein (Gen), a phytoestrogenic isoflavone, using spontaneously hypertensive rats (SHR) treated with Gen at 10–20 mg/kg/d over 10 weeks and in vitro vascular smooth muscle cell (VSMC) assays at 10–40 µmol/L. In vivo, Gen significantly reduced systolic blood pressure, aortic wall thickness, and fibrosis, while reversing overexpression of PCNA, MMP2/9, NOX4, and OPN and restoring alpha-SMA expression, indicating attenuation of the synthetic VSMC phenotype. In vitro, Gen dose-dependently inhibited VSMC proliferation (EdU assay) and migration (scratch/Transwell), with concurrent reduction in reactive oxygen species. Network pharmacology analysis identified 52 shared molecular targets, with GSK3β, FYN, and Nrf2 as hub nodes; co-treatment with the pGSK3β inhibitor SNP confirmed the mechanistic cascade, while the Nrf2 inhibitor ML385 did not affect upstream pGSK3β/FYN, establishing Nrf2 as downstream of the GSK3β/FYN axis. Limitations include exclusive reliance on the SHR animal model which does not fully recapitulate human essential hypertension, absence of pharmacokinetic data relevant to human dosing, and lack of direct comparison with approved antihypertensive agents in the same experimental setup.

Implications Medical Affairs : This preclinical study positions genistein as a candidate adjunctive compound acting through oxidative stress reduction via GSK3β/FYN/Nrf2, providing mechanistic rationale for monitoring phytoestrogenic research as potential nutraceutical competition in the antihypertensive space. Medical Affairs teams developing cardiovascular pipeline assets targeting Nrf2 or vascular remodeling should note these findings as preliminary supportive preclinical evidence that may attract investigator interest and inform future collaboration proposals. Translation to clinical proof-of-concept studies will be required before any guideline impact can be anticipated, and KOL engagement in this area should prioritize researchers with expertise in phytochemical-cardiovascular intersections.

Prevalence and associated factors of non-communicable diseases among men: A cross-sectional analysis of Kenya Demographic and Health Survey 2022 data.

Revue : PLoS One | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-20

Resultats principaux : This cross-sectional study analyzed data from 14,439 men aged 15–54 years from the 2022 Kenya Demographic and Health Survey (KDHS) to determine the prevalence and determinants of non-communicable diseases (NCDs) in Kenyan males. The overall NCD prevalence was 9.4% (95% CI 8.7–10.2%), with hypertension emerging as the single most prevalent condition at 3.5% (95% CI 3.1–3.9%), followed by depression (2.2%) and anxiety (1.6%). Multimorbidity was present in 1.9% (95% CI 1.6–2.3%) of participants, with the most common dyad being diabetes and hypertension (0.5%), underscoring the cardiometabolic co-occurrence pattern characteristic of aging Sub-Saharan African populations. Multivariable logistic regression identified age, region, rural-urban residence, ethnicity, education, wealth index, religion, media access, and sedentary lifestyle as significant independent predictors of NCD prevalence. Key limitations include reliance on self-reported disease data (likely underestimating true hypertension burden given low diagnosis rates in low-income settings), a cross-sectional design precluding causal inference, and restriction to men aged 15–54 years which excludes the higher-risk older male population.

Implications Medical Affairs : This study highlights a significant and likely underestimated hypertension burden in Sub-Saharan African men, a population chronically underserved by current screening and treatment programs, directly relevant to antihypertensive market access and patient identification strategies in emerging markets. Medical Affairs teams should leverage this epidemiological evidence to refine geographic expansion priorities in East Africa and support partnerships with regional public health authorities for hypertension screening initiatives and integrated NCD care programs. The identified risk factors — particularly sedentary behavior and low socioeconomic status — provide actionable intelligence for designing KOL-supported community-based programs and advocacy frameworks targeting male-specific health disparities in Kenya and similar LMIC contexts.

Association between COVID-19 infection, elevated C-reactive protein, and neuropsychiatric symptoms in individuals with metabolic and cardiovascular comorbidities.

Revue : Metabolic Brain Disease | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-20

Resultats principaux : This cross-sectional study enrolled 350 participants in Brazil — 114 in a post-COVID-19 group and 236 in a control group — to examine the relationship between prior SARS-CoV-2 infection, elevated C-reactive protein (CRP), and neuropsychiatric symptoms in individuals with cardiometabolic comorbidities including systemic arterial hypertension (SAH), obesity, and diabetes mellitus. The post-COVID-19 group showed significantly higher prevalence of SAH (22.1%, p=0.024), obesity (17.9%, p=0.044), and diabetes (13.3%, p<0.01) compared to controls, and also had significantly elevated serum CRP (p=0.014) correlating with all three comorbidities. Neuropsychiatric burden was markedly higher in the post-COVID group, with significantly greater stress (p=0.02) and depressive symptom severity (p=0.034). Crucially, the joint presence of SAH and obesity was associated with a synergistic amplification of stress, depression, and anxiety severity (p=0.027), suggesting additive inflammatory-neuropsychiatric interactions. Limitations include the cross-sectional design preventing causal attribution, absence of pre-COVID neuropsychiatric baseline measures, use of self-reported comorbidity data, and the relatively small and geographically localized sample from southern Brazil.

Implications Medical Affairs : These findings reinforce that hypertension is not only a risk factor for severe acute COVID-19 but also contributes to amplified neuropsychiatric sequelae in the post-COVID period through sustained systemic inflammation — a clinically important message for Medical Affairs educational content targeting internists, cardiologists, and psychiatrists involved in post-COVID care pathways. Pharmaceutical companies with antihypertensive portfolios should use these data to support advocacy for integrated cardiometabolic-mental health screening in post-COVID clinics, creating an opportunity for MSL-led educational activities and KOL partnerships in this emerging therapeutic interface. Medical research teams should consider supporting investigator-initiated longitudinal studies exploring whether optimal antihypertensive treatment with proven anti-inflammatory properties (e.g., ARBs, ACE inhibitors) can meaningfully reduce neuropsychiatric burden in the post-COVID hypertensive patient population.

Blood pressure polygenic score, cardiorespiratory fitness and odds of dementia: the HUNT Study.

Revue : Age and Ageing | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-02

Resultats principaux : This prospective cohort study utilized data from the population-based HUNT4 70+ sub-study in Norway, including 9,145 participants aged 70 years or older (5,011 females, 4,134 males) to investigate whether genetic predisposition to high systolic blood pressure (SBP), quantified via a polygenic score (PGS), is associated with dementia risk, and whether cardiorespiratory fitness (CRF) modifies this association in a sex-specific manner. Among the study population, 812 females (16.2%) and 570 males (13.8%) had dementia; females in the highest quintile of the BP polygenic score had significantly increased odds of dementia (OR=1.45; 95% CI 1.10–1.90), while no significant association was observed in males (OR=1.03; 95% CI 0.77–1.37). CRF modulated the association in females: low-fitness women in the top PGS quintile had OR=1.53 (95% CI 1.08–2.17), while high-fitness women showed a non-significant OR=1.12 (95% CI 0.78–1.63), suggesting CRF may partially attenuate genetic hypertensive risk for dementia. Subgroup analyses were stratified by sex and fitness, but the study does not provide data on antihypertensive medication use as a potential modifier of the PGS-dementia relationship. Limitations include the cross-sectional dementia assessment at a single time point, the predominantly homogeneous Norwegian population limiting generalizability to other ethnic groups, and potential confounding by uncontrolled lifestyle and treatment variables.

Implications Medical Affairs : This study adds high-value evidence linking blood pressure genetic risk to neurodegenerative outcomes in a sex-specific manner, supporting the narrative that antihypertensive therapy — particularly in genetically predisposed older women — may represent a brain-protective strategy, an argument Medical Affairs can leverage in KOL discussions bridging cardiology and neurology. Companies developing or marketing antihypertensive agents should proactively include this evidence in medical education materials addressing hypertension's systemic consequences beyond traditional cardiovascular endpoints, reinforcing treatment adherence in female patients. The physical fitness-modifying effect opens a compelling collaborative angle between pharmacotherapy and lifestyle medicine, supporting product positioning in combination pharmacotherapy-plus-rehabilitation strategies for elderly female hypertensive patients, and justifying engagement with geriatrics and rehabilitation KOLs.

Heart Failure With Reduced Ejection Fraction Update: A Review of Clinical Trials and New Therapeutic Considerations.

Revue : South Dakota Medicine | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2025 Nov

Resultats principaux : This narrative review published in South Dakota Medicine provides an updated synthesis of the pathophysiology, diagnosis, and management of heart failure with reduced ejection fraction (HFrEF), a condition primarily caused by ischemic heart disease, hypertension, and dilated cardiomyopathy, associated with substantial morbidity and mortality globally. The review summarizes the four pharmacological pillars of evidence-based HFrEF therapy — RAAS inhibitors (ACE inhibitors, ARBs, ARNIs), beta-blockers, mineralocorticoid receptor antagonists, and SGLT2 inhibitors — all demonstrated to reduce mortality and hospitalizations in landmark randomized controlled trials, with SGLT2 inhibitors representing the most recently integrated pillar. Additionally, intravenous iron therapy, implantable cardioverter-defibrillators (ICDs), cardiac resynchronization therapy (CRT), and left ventricular assist devices (LVADs) are highlighted as important complementary interventions for specific high-risk subgroups. Emerging therapies including the AccuCinch Ventricular Restoration System and novel antibody-based approaches are identified as promising pipeline developments under evaluation in ongoing trials. As a narrative review, key limitations include absence of systematic search methodology, potential selection bias in study inclusion, and no meta-analytic quantification of comparative treatment effect sizes.

Implications Medical Affairs : This review reinforces the enduring cornerstone role of RAAS inhibitors — including ACE inhibitors and ARBs — in guideline-concordant HFrEF management downstream of hypertension, providing Medical Affairs teams with a current reference supporting product positioning for antihypertensive agents with proven heart failure prevention credentials. The growing clinical integration of SGLT2 inhibitors and ARNIs signals sustained competitive pressure on traditional ACE inhibitor market share in the HFrEF space, requiring Medical Affairs to sharpen differentiation messaging highlighting specific patient profiles where ACE inhibitors remain the preferred or complementary agent. Strategically, supporting investigator-initiated research into combination regimens — particularly ACE inhibitor or ARB-based therapy combined with SGLT2 inhibitors — in patients transitioning from hypertension to HFrEF would strengthen the evidence base and reinforce product value in the cardiometabolic continuum.

The Effect of Statins on the Prevention of Proliferative Vitreoretinopathy (PVR) Following Vitrectomy or Scleral Buckling for Rhegmatogenous Retinal Detachment (RRD): A Pilot Randomized Controlled Trial.

Revue : Clinical Ophthalmology | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-15

Resultats principaux : This pilot randomized controlled trial enrolled 103 patients with rhegmatogenous retinal detachment (RRD) to evaluate whether post-operative statin therapy (atorvastatin 40 mg daily for 3 months) could prevent proliferative vitreoretinopathy (PVR), a major cause of surgical failure after retinal detachment repair. The primary finding was that PVR occurred in 3.8% of the statin group versus 22% of the untreated group (p=0.005), representing a clinically significant absolute risk reduction of 18.2 percentage points. Subgroup analysis showed the protective benefit was significant exclusively in the vitrectomy with silicone oil tamponade subgroup (p=0.010), while no significant effect was observed in the scleral buckling subgroup (p=0.217). Hypertension was assessed as a potential predictor of PVR alongside other variables including gender, smoking, diabetes, tear characteristics, and lens status, but was not found to be a statistically significant determinant of PVR occurrence. Post-operative visual acuity was significantly worse in patients who developed PVR (p<0.001). Limitations include the pilot design with limited statistical power, single-center enrollment in Iran, short 6-month follow-up, and absence of pharmacokinetic measurement of statin drug levels in the study population.

Implications Medical Affairs : This trial primarily addresses an ophthalmological endpoint (PVR prevention post-vitrectomy) and is relevant to the hypertension monitoring space only insofar as hypertension appeared as a non-significant control variable, limiting its direct applicability to antihypertensive Medical Affairs strategies. The study's capture in hypertension clinical trial searches reflects the broad comorbidity overlap between hypertensive patients and those undergoing retinal surgery rather than a hypertension-specific therapeutic question. Medical Affairs teams may note this paper as peripheral background in cardiovascular-ophthalmic comorbidity discussions, but no immediate strategic action is required from an antihypertensive product perspective.

Cardiac Rehabilitation for Cardiovascular Risk Modification in Patients With Rheumatoid Arthritis and Hypertension: A Randomized Controlled Trial.

Revue : International Journal of Rheumatic Diseases | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026 Jul

Resultats principaux : This single-center randomized controlled trial in Turkey randomized 50 patients with co-existing rheumatoid arthritis (RA) and hypertension (1:1) to a structured 6-week supervised cardiac rehabilitation (CR) program versus usual care, with follow-up at 6, 12, and 24 weeks and analysis by intention-to-treat using linear mixed-effects models. The primary outcome — estimated 10-year cardiovascular risk via Framingham Risk Score (FRS) — showed a highly significant group x time interaction (p<0.001), with a between-group difference of -5.02 FRS points (95% CI -8.60 to -1.44; p=0.007) at week 24 in favor of CR. Secondary outcomes were equally compelling: 24-hour ambulatory systolic blood pressure was reduced by 9.70 mmHg and VO₂max improved by 4.90 mL·kg⁻¹·min⁻¹ in the CR group versus usual care at week 24, both statistically significant. QRISK3 showed a directional but non-significant reduction of -5.77 points (95% CI -12.54 to 1.01; p=0.094), likely due to limited power from the small sample size. Disease activity (DAS28-CRP) remained within the remission range throughout, confirming CR safety in this inflammatory disease context. Limitations include the small sample size (n=50), single-center design, short 6-week intervention, and selection of clinically stable patients with controlled RA, restricting generalizability to more severe or unstable presentations.

Implications Medical Affairs : This RCT provides Level 2 evidence that structured cardiac rehabilitation produces clinically meaningful reductions in ambulatory blood pressure and estimated cardiovascular risk in the challenging comorbid population of RA patients with hypertension — a subgroup frequently underrepresented in rehabilitation program referrals and clinical trial populations. Medical Affairs teams with antihypertensive portfolios should position these findings as supportive of integrated care narratives, where pharmacotherapy and supervised exercise rehabilitation are complementary strategies for cardiovascular risk reduction, particularly in inflammatory disease populations with high unmet need. KOL engagement with rheumatologists, cardiac rehabilitation specialists, and internal medicine physicians can leverage this RCT to expand hypertensive patient pathways into rehabilitation programs, reinforcing the added value of optimal blood pressure control within a multidisciplinary management framework and potentially broadening the addressable patient population for antihypertensive agents in autoimmune-cardiovascular intersections.

Caffeine and Cardiovascular Disease: A Scientific Statement From the American Heart Association.

Revue : Circulation | Tier : 1 | IF estime : >20

Date de publication : 2026-07-20

Resultats principaux : This American Heart Association (AHA) Scientific Statement provides a comprehensive expert synthesis of the evidence on relationships between caffeine — the world's most widely consumed psychoactive drug — and cardiovascular risk factors and diseases including hypertension, lipid metabolism, diabetes, coronary artery disease, heart failure, arrhythmias, and stroke. The central finding for hypertension is that most studies suggest an inverse J-shaped relationship between naturally occurring caffeinated product consumption (primarily coffee) and blood pressure, indicating that moderate consumption does not substantially elevate hypertensive risk and may confer mild protection at habitual doses. Habitual coffee consumption was associated with lower incidence of type 2 diabetes; unfiltered coffee (e.g., French press) was linked to elevated LDL cholesterol, while filtered coffee was not; no consistent relationship was found between caffeine and blood lipids overall. For major cardiovascular outcomes, caffeinated coffee showed no adverse relationship or was associated with lower risk of coronary artery disease, heart failure, and stroke; a dedicated RCT showed caffeinated coffee reduced atrial fibrillation occurrence but increased premature ventricular contraction frequency in regular consumers. High-dose caffeine, particularly from energy drinks, was associated with cardiovascular harm, while data in this category remain limited. The primary limitation of the entire evidence base is the predominantly observational design across included studies, with substantial heterogeneity in caffeine sources, doses, and individual genetic metabolizer status, precluding definitive causal conclusions.

Implications Medical Affairs : This Tier 1 AHA Scientific Statement from Circulation is a landmark publication that will directly influence clinical counseling on dietary caffeine in the context of hypertension management — Medical Affairs teams should immediately prepare KOL-aligned talking points addressing the inverse J-shaped BP relationship to ensure prescribers can accurately contextualize caffeine advice within antihypertensive treatment plans. The nuanced findings may reassure both physicians and patients that moderate coffee consumption is not a major competing driver of hypertension risk, potentially improving treatment adherence messaging by removing a source of patient anxiety and supporting a holistic, non-restrictive lifestyle management dialogue that complements pharmacotherapy. Medical Affairs should proactively monitor whether this AHA Statement catalyzes updates to major hypertension guidelines (ESC/ESH, ACC/AHA) on dietary risk factor recommendations, and position medical education activities and KOL symposia to contextualize these data within evidence-based hypertension management frameworks before guideline updates are published.

Home blood pressure telemonitoring reveals race-specific patterns of target organ damage.

Revue : Journal of Hypertension | Tier : 2 | IF estime : 10–20

Date de publication : 2026-07-03

Resultats principaux : This analysis from the ongoing international UPRIGHT-HTM trial (NCT04299529) enrolled 441 high-risk asymptomatic patients aged 55–75 years with five or more cardiovascular risk factors — 287 Black and 154 White patients — who underwent home blood pressure telemonitoring (OMRON HEM 9210-T) over a median of 34.7 months, generating 252,215 home BP readings and echocardiographic assessment. The median home BP was 127/77 mmHg, with 32.2% meeting 2024 ESC criteria for home hypertension; Black patients received significantly fewer statins and less combination antihypertensive-diabetes combination therapy compared to White patients. For cardiac target organ damage (TOD), echocardiographic parameters including left atrial dimensions, mitral annular s' velocity, and stroke volume showed a steeper relationship with systolic home BP in White patients compared to Black patients, while all participants demonstrated concentric left ventricular remodeling. Conversely, CKD grade — assessed using the race-free 2024 KDIGO eGFR-albuminuria cross-classification — was significantly worse in Black patients and did not correlate with home BP level, suggesting race-specific organ vulnerability pathways independent of BP control. Limitations include the interim/ongoing trial status, reliance on echocardiographic cross-sectional data at a single assessment point, absence of ambulatory BP monitoring for direct comparison, and potential confounding by differential pharmacological treatment between racial groups.

Implications Medical Affairs : This Tier 2 publication from the Journal of Hypertension identifies clinically important race-specific organ damage patterns — predominantly renal in Black patients and cardiac in White patients — that should fundamentally inform Medical Affairs messaging on individualized TOD monitoring protocols and the urgency of intensifying antihypertensive and nephroprotective pharmacotherapy in Black hypertensive patients. The documented undertreatment of Black patients (fewer statins, less combination therapy) represents a critical unmet clinical need and provides Medical Affairs with robust evidence to advocate for equitable access to guideline-recommended antihypertensive combination regimens, particularly in Sub-Saharan Africa and diaspora communities, creating a high-impact framework for equity-focused KOL engagement and patient advocacy programs. Research teams should consider supporting prospective investigator-initiated studies or real-world evidence analyses that specifically evaluate whether race-tailored antihypertensive regimens with renal-protective properties (ACE inhibitors, ARBs, SGLT2 inhibitors) can measurably reduce TOD disparities and improve long-term cardiovascular and renal outcomes in Black hypertensive patients.
