Long-term Complications of Venous Thromboembolism: A Practical Guide.

Revue : Heart Failure Clinics | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-04-08

Resultats principaux : This review article synthesizes current evidence on the epidemiology, pathophysiology, diagnosis, and management of long-term complications of venous thromboembolism (VTE), including deep vein thrombosis (DVT) and pulmonary embolism (PE). The study population encompasses PE and DVT survivors drawn from the broader VTE literature, with findings contextualized against major cardiovascular events such as acute myocardial infarction and stroke. The primary sequelae examined are post-pulmonary embolism syndrome (comprising chronic thromboembolic pulmonary disease, post-PE functional impairment, and chronic thromboembolic pulmonary hypertension, CTEPH) and post-thrombotic syndrome of the lower limbs. Post-thrombotic syndrome reaches severe forms in 5% to 10% of DVT cases, representing a clinically significant and often underappreciated long-term burden. The article underscores that functional impairment following VTE is comparable in magnitude to that observed after stroke or acute myocardial infarction, highlighting the need for sustained post-acute care strategies.

Implications Medical Affairs : CTEPH represents a significant unmet medical need emerging from the PE survivor population, and the acknowledgment of its severity in this review creates positioning opportunities for treatments targeting the pulmonary vascular remodeling pathway in post-VTE hypertension. For Medical Affairs, the functional impairment burden comparable to stroke reinforces the rationale for long-term follow-up programs and real-world evidence initiatives that could demonstrate the clinical and economic value of early CTEPH intervention. Engaging pulmonologists and hematologists around CTEPH identification and treatment pathways within post-VTE clinics represents a high-impact KOL strategy opportunity.

Pulmonary Hypertension: From Molecular Pathways to Patient Care.

Revue : Heart Failure Clinics | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07

Resultats principaux : This review article provides a comprehensive examination of pulmonary hypertension (PH), a heterogeneous condition defined by a mean pulmonary arterial pressure exceeding 20 mm Hg as measured via right heart catheterization. The global prevalence of PH is estimated at approximately 0.6% to 1%, representing a substantial and growing public health burden. The pathophysiology described encompasses multiple mechanisms including endothelial dysfunction, smooth muscle cell proliferation, inflammation, thrombosis, and right ventricular remodeling, driven by both genetic and environmental factors. The review demonstrates that early diagnosis combined with risk stratification and personalized treatment has significantly altered the prognosis and quality of life for PH patients, particularly in pulmonary arterial hypertension (PAH) where disease-modifying therapies have transformed the therapeutic landscape. The authors highlight the heterogeneity of PH subtypes as a critical factor requiring individualized management approaches to optimize clinical outcomes.

Implications Medical Affairs : The review's emphasis on molecular pathways and personalized treatment strategies creates positioning opportunities for agents targeting specific pathobiological mechanisms in PAH, supporting precision medicine narratives in Medical Affairs materials. The demonstrated improvement in prognosis with early diagnosis and risk stratification reinforces the value of comprehensive patient registries and disease monitoring programs that pharmaceutical Medical Affairs teams can co-develop with academic centers. From a KOL strategy perspective, engaging pulmonologists and cardiologists who specialize in PAH subtype characterization and novel therapeutic combinations is essential to maintain scientific leadership in this rapidly evolving space.

Prostacyclin Analogs and Diabetic Retinopathy Outcomes in Patients with Pulmonary Hypertension: A Cohort Analysis.

Revue : Retina | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-01

Resultats principaux : This retrospective, real-world cohort study used the TriNetX Global Collaborative Network with 1:1 propensity score matching to compare 2,584 matched patients with diabetes and pulmonary arterial hypertension receiving prostacyclin analog (PCA)-based dual therapy versus those receiving endothelin receptor antagonists, phosphodiesterase-5 inhibitors, or soluble guanylate cyclase stimulators without PCAs. The primary outcomes were five-year incidence rates of nonproliferative diabetic retinopathy (DR), proliferative DR, and diabetes with ophthalmic complications, analyzed using Cox proportional hazards models and Kaplan-Meier analyses. PCA therapy was associated with significantly lower incidence of nonproliferative DR (24 vs. 42 events; HR=0.59; 95% CI, 0.35-0.97; P=0.0345) and diabetes with ophthalmic complications (65 vs. 99 events; HR=0.67; 95% CI, 0.49-0.91; P=0.0105). No statistically significant difference was observed for proliferative DR (HR=0.63; 95% CI, 0.27-1.43; P=0.2617), potentially due to insufficient statistical power given the lower event count. The retrospective and observational design with residual confounding risk represents a primary limitation, and the authors call for prospective validation of these microvascular protective effects.

Implications Medical Affairs : The demonstration of microvascular protective effects of prostacyclin analogs beyond their established pulmonary vasodilatory action represents a novel therapeutic positioning angle, potentially broadening their clinical value narrative in diabetic patients with comorbid PAH. For Medical Affairs, this real-world evidence can anchor outcomes-based narratives around PCA therapy in polypharmacy cardiometabolic populations, differentiating these agents in formulary access discussions by highlighting systemic microvascular benefits. Future prospective trials specifically designed to assess PCA therapy effects on systemic microvascular endpoints in diabetic PAH patients would constitute a high-priority research collaboration opportunity with ophthalmology and diabetology KOLs.

Development and Usability Evaluation of HOPE: A Patient-Centered mHealth Application for HTN Self-Management in Iran.

Revue : PLoS One | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-17

Resultats principaux : This four-phase study describes the design and evaluation of the HOPE mHealth application for hypertension self-management in Hormozgan Province, Iran, a region with high HTN prevalence and an acknowledged gap in validated digital health tools. Requirements were established from input from 25 cardiologists and 50 patients via Likert-scale questionnaires, and content was developed according to national and international clinical standards. The final application encompasses nine main tabs and 52 sub-tabs covering demographic data, comprehensive patient education, nutrition tracking, BP recording, medication management, and a direct patient-physician dialogue panel. Usability was prospectively assessed in 46 HTN patients over one month at the Hormoz Clinic using the validated Mobile Application Usability Questionnaire (MAUQ), yielding an overall score of 4.32 out of 5 (categorized as very good). The User Interface and Satisfaction dimension scored highest (4.37 out of 5), though no statistically significant association between satisfaction and education level was established, suggesting broad accessibility regardless of education background.

Implications Medical Affairs : The strong usability profile of a rigorously designed mHealth HTN application in an emerging market setting demonstrates the growing viability of digital companion tools as adherence-enhancing components of antihypertensive treatment programs. From a Medical Affairs perspective, co-developing validated digital tools for BP monitoring and medication management represents a differentiation opportunity for antihypertensive portfolios in markets where healthcare access limitations challenge guideline-based control. Future research on long-term clinical impact, specifically on BP control rates and cardiovascular outcomes, would provide the evidence needed to support real-world evidence dossiers and payer value propositions for digitally-enhanced HTN management programs.

Revue : Revista Brasileira de Enfermagem | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-12

Resultats principaux : This cross-sectional study was conducted at a university hospital in Rio de Janeiro State between January 2022 and October 2024, enrolling an intentional non-probabilistic sample of 63 patients with resistant hypertension via telemonitoring. Sociodemographic, clinical, and lifestyle data were collected from medical records, and the Screening for Occult Renal Disease (SCORED) questionnaire was applied to estimate CKD risk. The study population showed a predominance of females (80.3%), a mean age of 66.4 +/- 10.0 years, and a majority of patients with 21 or more years since hypertension diagnosis. A strikingly high proportion, 96.8% of participants, were identified as being at risk for chronic kidney disease. Statistical analysis using Fisher's exact test and Mann-Whitney test identified female sex, advanced age, and longer duration of hypertension as the key factors significantly associated with CKD risk in this resistant hypertension cohort.

Implications Medical Affairs : The near-universal CKD risk burden (96.8%) in this resistant hypertension cohort highlights a profound unmet need for nephroprotective antihypertensive strategies, creating a compelling clinical rationale for positioning agents with established renoprotective profiles in this high-risk subpopulation. For Medical Affairs, evidence from this population supports inclusion of CKD risk screening as a standard component of resistant HTN management algorithms, reinforcing the differentiated value of antihypertensives with dual cardiovascular and renal endpoint data in medical education initiatives. Engaging nephrologists and hypertension specialists around routine SCORED-based or equivalent CKD screening protocols in resistant HTN clinics represents an actionable KOL strategy for expanding the reach of nephroprotective treatment narratives.

Minimal Perceived Discomfort During Oscillometric Blood Pressure Measurement in Children: A Cross-Sectional Study With Implications for Systematic Screening.

Revue : Journal of Clinical Hypertension | Tier : 3 | IF estime : ~3-4

Date de publication : 2026-06

Resultats principaux : This cross-sectional study enrolled 127 Danish children and adolescents aged 5 to 17 years attending the pediatric outpatient clinic at Godstrup Hospital between June and August 2025 to evaluate the perceived discomfort of oscillometric BP measurement as a potential barrier to systematic screening. Each child underwent oscillometric BP measurement with adaptive cuff inflation and rated discomfort using the validated Wong-Baker FACES Pain Rating Scale. Results showed that 63% of children reported no discomfort, 28% reported minimal discomfort, and only 9% reported higher levels of pain, with no child reporting the maximum pain score. Ordinal logistic regression analysis found no statistically significant associations between discomfort scores and age, sex, or prior experience with BP measurement, suggesting that tolerability is uniform across the pediatric age and sex spectrum. The small sample size (n=127) from a single center and the enrollment of clinic attendees rather than a general population sample represent key limitations in terms of generalizability.

Implications Medical Affairs : This study provides reassuring evidence that systematic BP screening in children is feasible, well-tolerated, and not limited by procedural discomfort, effectively removing a key perceived barrier to pediatric hypertension detection programs. From a Medical Affairs perspective, supporting the scientific case for universal pediatric BP screening has strategic value because it expands the diagnosed hypertension pool, thereby increasing the addressable population for antihypertensive interventions in pediatric indications. Engaging pediatric cardiologists, general pediatricians, and guideline committee members in discussions about implementing population-wide oscillometric screening protocols represents a high-impact KOL opportunity with direct influence on future hypertension screening standards.

Identifying MicroRNA Biomarkers in Idiopathic Intracranial Hypertension: A Pilot Study.

Revue : Neurology | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-17

Resultats principaux : This pilot study analyzed serum and CSF samples from participants in the IIH-Weight Trial, a randomized controlled trial comparing bariatric surgery versus community weight management for lowering intracranial pressure (ICP) in idiopathic intracranial hypertension (IIH), recruiting adult women with active IIH between 2014 and 2017. Samples were assessed at baseline (active disease) and at 12 months (remission), with a panel of 40 candidate microRNAs evaluated against comparator groups including participants with obesity or migraine. Five of 40 serum miRNAs showed significantly lower expression in active IIH versus remission. Serum hsa-miR-16-5p demonstrated the highest diagnostic performance for active IIH (AUC=0.951) and differentiated active IIH from both obesity controls (p less than 0.0001) and migraine, while also distinguishing active IIH from remission in CSF (p=0.0354). Both serum and CSF hsa-miR-16-5p correlated significantly with ICP and papilledema grade, and were associated with fatty acid metabolism and lipid biosynthesis metabolites, providing Class III evidence for the potential of serum miRNA as a minimally invasive biomarker for IIH diagnosis and disease monitoring.

Implications Medical Affairs : The identification of serum hsa-miR-16-5p as a candidate biomarker with an AUC of 0.951 for active IIH represents a clinically meaningful advance toward non-invasive disease monitoring, potentially replacing frequent invasive lumbar punctures in a condition with high misdiagnosis rates affecting predominantly young obese women. For Medical Affairs, this biomarker discovery creates opportunities for companion diagnostic development and patient stratification strategies that could support precision treatment trials in IIH, a condition currently lacking FDA-approved pharmacological agents. Engaging neurologists and ophthalmologists who manage IIH patients around biomarker-guided treatment decision-making represents an emerging and strategically important KOL opportunity in this orphan-adjacent disease space.

Socio-demographic and Clinical Determinants of Dyslipidaemia in Hypertensive Patients at the Effia Nkwanta Hospital: A Cross-Sectional Study.

Revue : Scientific Reports | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-17

Resultats principaux : This cross-sectional study enrolled 347 adults with clinically diagnosed hypertension attending Effia Nkwanta Regional Hospital in Ghana to assess the prevalence and sociodemographic and clinical determinants of dyslipidaemia. Fasting serum lipid profiles were measured by enzymatic methods and analyzed using SPSS v26.0 with appropriate nonparametric and chi-square tests. The overall dyslipidaemia prevalence was 58.5%, with 52.7% of affected individuals being female. Statistically significant associations were identified with residential area (p=0.04), income level (p=0.008), history of smoking (p=0.04), BMI (p=0.02), and waist-hip ratio (p=0.02). Paradoxically, obese (OR=0.432, p=0.004) and overweight (OR=0.401, p=0.001) participants were less likely to have dyslipidaemia compared to those with normal weight, a finding that may reflect the well-known obesity paradox or differences in dyslipidaemia phenotype across BMI categories; occupation was significantly associated with both triglyceride and LDL levels, highlighting occupational and lifestyle drivers.

Implications Medical Affairs : The high dyslipidaemia prevalence (58.5%) in a Ghanaian hypertensive population confirms the substantial cardiometabolic risk clustering in sub-Saharan Africa, reinforcing the need for integrated blood pressure and lipid management strategies in emerging markets where combination therapy is underutilized. For Medical Affairs, this evidence base supports the positioning of fixed-dose antihypertensive-statin combinations and comprehensive cardiovascular risk programs in West African markets, where primary care capacity for multi-risk factor management is often limited. Routine lipid screening protocols for hypertensive patients represent an actionable clinical practice gap that Medical Affairs educational initiatives targeting hospital internists and primary care physicians in Ghana and the broader West African region can address.

Links Between Concussion History, Hypertension, and Hypertension Contributing Factors Among Adolescent Football Athletes.

Revue : The Physician and Sportsmedicine | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-17

Resultats principaux : This cross-sectional baseline analysis from a prospective cohort study enrolled 390 adolescent football athletes (mean age 15.8 +/- 1.2 years) from secondary schools to examine whether concussion history is associated with hypertension-range blood pressure, adjusted for anthropometric and behavioral covariates. The main outcome was hypertension-range BP defined as SBP greater than or equal to 130 mmHg or DBP greater than or equal to 80 mmHg, a notably high threshold applied in this adolescent cohort. A substantial 31.4% of athletes (n=121) had blood pressure in the hypertensive range, indicating a high prevalence of elevated BP in this young athletic population. Multivariable logistic regression with 327 complete cases identified body fat percentage as the only variable significantly and positively associated with hypertensive-range BP (AOR=1.06, p=0.03). Concussion history, post-concussion symptom severity, subjective social status, sleep disturbance, waist circumference, and obesity level were not significantly associated with BP, suggesting that concussion-related cardiovascular risk may not manifest during adolescence, though prospective follow-up of this cohort will be required to fully assess longitudinal effects.

Implications Medical Affairs : The 31.4% prevalence of hypertension-range BP among adolescent athletes highlights a markedly under-recognized cardiovascular risk in youth sports populations, underscoring the need for routine BP screening programs in school athletic settings where body composition and lifestyle risk factor management can be initiated early. For Medical Affairs, these findings support non-pharmacological prevention narratives centered on body composition management as the primary modifiable driver of early hypertension in adolescents, relevant to lifestyle intervention programs that could complement antihypertensive portfolios in youth indications. Engaging sports medicine physicians, school health officials, and pediatric cardiologists around evidence-based BP monitoring and lifestyle protocols in youth athletics presents a strategic opportunity to shape emerging screening guidelines at the population level.

Global Evidence on Risk Factors for the Progression of Gestational Diabetes to Type 2 Diabetes: An Umbrella Review of Systematic Reviews and Meta-Analyses.

Revue : Diabetology & Metabolic Syndrome | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-16

Resultats principaux : This umbrella review systematically synthesized 12 systematic reviews and meta-analyses comprising over 400 primary studies and more than 6 million participants to identify risk factors for the progression from gestational diabetes mellitus (GDM) to type 2 diabetes mellitus (T2DM). The study population was women with a prior GDM diagnosis from diverse global settings, with inclusion of reviews published between January 2005 and January 2025. The most consistently reported risk factors for T2DM progression included insulin use during pregnancy, elevated BMI before or after delivery, and a positive family history of diabetes. Glycemic markers including fasting blood glucose, oral glucose tolerance test values, and glycated hemoglobin were also frequently and robustly associated with increased risk across included reviews. Additional confirmed risk factors were advanced maternal age, non-white ethnicity, hypertension, and recurrent GDM, while lactation was consistently identified as a protective behavior, collectively providing a globally applicable risk stratification framework for post-GDM women.

Implications Medical Affairs : The confirmation of maternal hypertension as an independent risk factor for post-GDM T2DM progression among more than 6 million study participants underscores the cardiometabolic interconnectedness of these conditions, creating a compelling evidence base for integrated antihypertensive and metabolic risk management in postpartum women. For Medical Affairs, this provides a strong rationale for positioning antihypertensive agents with favorable metabolic profiles in women of reproductive age, where optimal BP management may deliver benefits beyond cardiovascular protection including reduced diabetes progression risk. Strategic engagement with obstetricians, endocrinologists, and maternal-fetal medicine specialists around comprehensive postpartum cardiometabolic risk screening programs represents a high-value KOL opportunity with direct implications for clinical practice guidelines.

Prevalence, Temporal Trends, Diversity, and Associated Risk Factors of Isolated Cleft Lip With or Without Cleft Palate: A 20-Year Retrospective Hospital-Based Study in Iran (2003-2023).

Revue : Birth Defects Research | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06

Resultats principaux : This retrospective study analyzed 767 isolated cases of cleft lip with or without cleft palate (CL/P) from 620,000 live births at a tertiary Iranian hospital between 2003 and 2023, using medical record data and multivariable logistic regression to identify independent risk factors. The overall CL/P prevalence was 1.24 per 1,000 live births (95% CI: 1.15-1.33), with male predominance (59.7%; ratio 1.59:1). A significant non-linear temporal trend was observed over the study period (p=0.045), with fluctuations peaking in the mid-2010s. Multivariable regression identified maternal smoking at 11 to 20 cigarettes per day (aOR=1.52), extreme maternal obesity (aOR=1.37), pre-pregnancy diabetes (aOR=2.00), maternal hypertension (aOR=1.20; 95% CI: 1.05-1.37), previous preterm birth (aOR=1.44), and use of assisted reproductive technology (aOR=1.42) as independent risk factors for CL/P. The authors estimated that targeting modifiable risk factors such as smoking and obesity through preconception interventions could reduce CL/P incidence by 15-25%, though the single-center retrospective design and potential medical record incompleteness represent limitations.

Implications Medical Affairs : The identification of maternal hypertension as an independent risk factor for congenital CL/P (aOR=1.20) introduces a novel dimension to antihypertensive treatment justification in the periconceptional period, suggesting that optimized preconception BP management may contribute to reducing congenital anomaly risk beyond established cardiovascular benefits. For Medical Affairs, this finding supports positioning of antihypertensives with established safety profiles in women planning pregnancy, reinforcing the importance of BP control as a component of preconception health programs aimed at reducing multifactorial birth defect risk. Engaging maternal-fetal medicine specialists and reproductive endocrinologists in educational programs around preconception cardiometabolic optimization represents an emerging KOL engagement strategy with implications for antihypertensive prescribing in women of reproductive age.

Global, Regional, and National Burden of Respiratory Diseases and Attributable Risk Factors in Adolescents and Young Adults, 1990-2021: A Global Burden of Disease 2021 Analysis.

Revue : Archives of Medical Science | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-04-27

Resultats principaux : This Global Burden of Disease 2021 analysis assessed incidence, mortality, and disability-adjusted life years (DALYs) for respiratory diseases in adolescents and young adults (AYAs, aged 15-39 years) across 204 countries and territories from 1990 to 2021, with results stratified by age, sex, location, and sociodemographic index (SDI). Upper respiratory infections had the highest age-standardized incidence rate (152,791.5 per 100,000), while tuberculosis caused the highest age-standardized mortality rate (6.8 per 100,000). Air pollution was the leading modifiable risk factor, contributing 32.76 age-standardized DALYs per 100,000 for COPD and 62.37 per 100,000 for lower respiratory infections. Notable sex-based disparities were documented, with pulmonary arterial hypertension being more prevalent in female AYAs alongside asthma, while males had higher burdens of pneumoconiosis and lung cancer. Low-SDI regions bore the greatest burden for tuberculosis and lower respiratory infections, while smoking was the primary driver of COPD burden specifically in high-income countries.

Implications Medical Affairs : The finding that pulmonary arterial hypertension disproportionately affects female AYAs, combined with air pollution as the leading modifiable risk factor for respiratory disease burden across 204 countries, reinforces the case for sex-stratified PAH epidemiology data in clinical development programs and underscores the importance of environmental risk factor research in PAH pathogenesis. For Medical Affairs, this GBD data provides a robust epidemiological foundation for investment in early-diagnosis programs and treatment access initiatives targeting young female PAH patients, particularly in markets where disease awareness remains limited. Partnering with pulmonologists and epidemiologists who contribute to GBD research around the intersection of environmental pollution and pulmonary vascular disease represents a high-visibility scientific leadership opportunity that aligns with growing global health advocacy agendas.
