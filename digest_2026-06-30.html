Insights into Matrix Metalloproteinase-2 Genotypes, Smoking, Alcohol Drinking, Hypertension and Renal Cell Carcinoma in Taiwan.

Revue : Anticancer Research | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07

Resultats principaux : This hospital-based case-control study from Taiwan enrolled 118 renal cell carcinoma (RCC) patients and 590 cancer-free controls to investigate the association between two MMP-2 promoter polymorphisms (rs243865 and rs2285053) and RCC susceptibility using PCR-RFLP genotyping. A statistically significant association was found between the rs243865 polymorphism and RCC risk: the TT genotype conferred a markedly elevated risk compared to the CC genotype (OR=4.29, 95% CI=1.56-11.83, p=0.0069), while the CT genotype showed no significant effect (OR=1.31, 95% CI=0.80-2.16, p=0.3530), and the T allele itself was independently associated with elevated RCC risk (OR=1.71, 95% CI=1.15-2.54, p=0.0101). In contrast, rs2285053 showed no significant association with RCC susceptibility in this population. Critically, stratified subgroup analyses revealed significant interactions between the rs243865 genotype and three clinical and lifestyle variables: smoking status (p=0.0175), alcohol consumption (p=0.0173), and hypertension status (p=0.0063), suggesting gene-environment interactions relevant to disease pathogenesis. Limitations include a relatively small sample size from a single Taiwanese center, a cross-sectional case-control design precluding causal inference, and limited generalizability to non-Asian populations.

Implications Medical Affairs : The finding that hypertension status significantly modifies the MMP-2 genotype effect on RCC susceptibility adds a novel pharmacogenomic dimension to hypertension management, suggesting a potential role for antihypertensive therapy in reducing cancer co-risk in genetically predisposed individuals and warranting further exploration. Medical Affairs teams can position this evidence within a cardiovascular-renal-oncology risk reduction narrative, supporting the broader clinical value of sustained blood pressure control beyond purely hemodynamic endpoints. KOL engagement at the intersection of hypertension, genetics, and nephro-oncology may uncover collaborative research opportunities and reinforce scientific leadership in multi-morbidity management.

Improving anxiety screening for adults with hypertension: An integrative review.

Revue : The Nurse Practitioner | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-23

Resultats principaux : This integrative review evaluated 23 studies published between 2014 and 2024, identified through systematic searches of PubMed, Embase, CINAHL, and Scopus databases, using the Johns Hopkins Nursing Evidence-Based Practice model and PRISMA guidelines to assess anxiety measurement and its relationship with hypertension in adult patients aged 18-70. Three principal themes emerged from the review: the clinical necessity of regular anxiety screenings for patients with elevated blood pressure, an increased prevalence of anxiety disorders among hypertensive individuals, and female sex as an independent risk factor for anxiety disorder in this population. The review highlights that anxiety and hypertension share a bidirectional relationship, with each condition potentially worsening the other through neuroendocrine, autonomic, and inflammatory pathways, yet this comorbidity is systematically underaddressed in standard hypertension management protocols. A critical gap was identified in the literature: longitudinal research and randomized controlled trials specifically measuring the influence of anxiety treatment on hypertension rates and outcomes are lacking, limiting actionable clinical guidance. Limitations include heterogeneity in study designs and anxiety measurement tools across included studies, absence of meta-analytic synthesis, and potential publication bias in the databases searched.

Implications Medical Affairs : This review identifies an underaddressed comorbidity that substantially affects treatment adherence and blood pressure control outcomes, reinforcing the value of holistic patient management approaches and opening positioning opportunities for antihypertensive agents with evidence of favorable neuropsychological tolerability profiles. Medical Affairs teams can leverage this evidence to support the development of integrated hypertension-anxiety clinical pathways and patient support programs that address both pharmacological and psychological dimensions of disease management. Strategic KOL engagement with cardiologists, psychiatrists, and nursing leaders should focus on promoting integrated anxiety screening protocols within hypertension care guidelines, with potential co-prescribing and health economics implications.

The Systemic Immune-Inflammation Index and High-Sensitivity C-Reactive Protein to High-Density Lipoprotein Cholesterol Ratio Predict Moderate and Severe Coronary Artery Stenosis in Older Patients with Coronary Heart Disease.

Revue : Clinical Interventions in Aging | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-23

Resultats principaux : This retrospective analysis enrolled 786 older patients with coronary heart disease (CHD) from Chongqing Medical University, stratified into training (70%) and validation (30%) sets and classified by Gensini scores into mild, moderate, and severe coronary artery stenosis (CAS) groups. Both the systemic immune-inflammation index (SII) and the hsCRP/HDL-C ratio were significantly elevated in patients with moderate-to-severe CAS and independently associated with CAS severity in both training and validation datasets, with good model consistency and no multicollinearity. Receiver operating characteristic curve analyses demonstrated that the combination of SII and hsCRP/HDL-C outperformed either biomarker alone for predicting moderate-to-severe stenosis, with the integrated model offering superior discriminative performance. Crucially, hypertension significantly moderated the prognostic utility of SII: SII remained a stable and reliable predictor in hypertensive patients but showed substantially diminished efficacy in the non-hypertensive subgroup, suggesting that the inflammatory milieu in hypertension potentiates SII-mediated atherosclerotic signaling. Limitations include the retrospective single-center design, exclusive enrollment of Chinese elderly patients, and potential confounding from unmeasured comorbidities.

Implications Medical Affairs : The finding that hypertension modifies the predictive value of SII for coronary artery stenosis severity has direct implications for cardiovascular risk stratification protocols, positioning antihypertensive therapy as essential not only for blood pressure control but also for preserving biomarker utility in clinical decision-making. Medical Affairs teams can use this data to reinforce cardiovascular protection messaging for antihypertensive agents, particularly in elderly patients with CHD where inflammatory burden and vascular remodeling intersect. Engaging cardiologists and clinical chemists in prospective validation studies of combined biomarker panels stratified by hypertension status would be a high-value KOL strategy.

Trends and socioeconomic-spatial inequalities in hypertension among Muslim women in India, 2015-2021: evidence from the National Family Health Surveys.

Revue : Frontiers in Public Health | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-11

Resultats principaux : This study analyzed nationally representative data from two Indian National Family Health Surveys (NFHS-4, 2015-16; NFHS-5, 2019-21) to examine hypertension trends and socioeconomic-spatial inequalities among Muslim women aged 15-49 years, using weighted prevalence estimates, concentration indices, logistic regression, and spatial clustering analyses. Hypertension prevalence rose from 12.11% in NFHS-4 to 12.52% in NFHS-5, with a strong and persistent age gradient: women aged 40-49 had approximately 9-fold higher adjusted odds than those aged 15-19 (OR=9.19 in NFHS-4; OR=8.33 in NFHS-5). Paradoxically, wealth increased hypertension risk in NFHS-5, with fourth and richest quintile women having 51% (95% CI: 1.22-1.86) and 37% (95% CI: 1.07-1.74) higher odds respectively versus the poorest, consistent with epidemiological transition patterns, while higher education was independently protective (OR=0.65, 95% CI: 0.50-0.84). Spatial clustering of hypertension was statistically significant and intensifying, with Moran's I rising from 0.111 (NFHS-4) to 0.210 (NFHS-5, p<0.001), identifying geographic hot spots that require targeted policy interventions. Limitations include the cross-sectional nature of each NFHS round preventing causal inference, potential measurement variability across rounds, and possible underascertainment in conservative communities.

Implications Medical Affairs : The rising hypertension burden within a large religious minority population that is underrepresented in global health research points to a significant unmet need for culturally adapted screening and prevention programs, with direct relevance for product access strategies in South Asian markets. The observed wealth-hypertension paradox reflects the ongoing epidemiological transition in India and should inform commercial forecasting models, particularly as urbanization-driven hypertension risk expands beyond historically high-risk demographics. Medical Affairs strategies should prioritize KOL partnerships in public health and community medicine to champion inclusive hypertension management guidelines and sponsor real-world evidence generation in underserved minority populations.

Perioperative systemic therapy versus surgery alone for resectable colorectal peritoneal-only metastases (CAIRO6): a randomised, open-label, phase 3 trial.

Revue : The Lancet. Oncology | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07

Resultats principaux : CAIRO6 was a randomized open-label phase 3 trial conducted at 10 Dutch and Belgian tertiary centers, enrolling 358 patients with resectable colorectal peritoneal-only metastases and randomizing them 1:1 to perioperative systemic therapy (bevacizumab-based CAPOX, FOLFOX, or FOLFIRI regimens) plus cytoreductive surgery with hyperthermic intraperitoneal chemotherapy (CRS-HIPEC) versus upfront CRS-HIPEC alone, with stratification by prior systemic therapy, synchronous vs. metachronous disease, peritoneal cancer index, and HIPEC regimen. After a median follow-up of 41 months, the primary endpoint of overall survival did not differ significantly between arms: median OS was 44 months in the perioperative therapy group versus 39 months in the surgery-alone group (HR=0.85, 95% CI 0.62-1.15, p=0.28), concluding that perioperative systemic therapy cannot be universally recommended. Among the 161 patients who received systemic therapy, 57% experienced major CTCAE grade 3-5 toxicity; hypertension was the most common grade 3-4 adverse event (13 patients, 8%), followed by diarrhea (7%) and neutropenia (7%), reflecting the well-established cardiovascular toxicity profile of bevacizumab. Postoperative major morbidity (Clavien-Dindo grade 3-5) at 90 days was also higher in the perioperative therapy group (36% vs. 26%), with more frequent anastomotic leakage and fascia dehiscence. Limitations include the relatively modest sample size for a phase 3 superiority trial, heterogeneity in chemotherapy regimen selection by investigators, and the absence of a formal biomarker-guided patient selection strategy.

Implications Medical Affairs : The emergence of bevacizumab-induced hypertension as the most frequent grade 3-4 toxicity in this perioperative oncology trial reinforces the need for proactive antihypertensive management in patients receiving anti-VEGF therapy, creating a concrete positioning opportunity for antihypertensive agents within oncology care pathways. Medical Affairs teams should engage oncology KOLs on cardiovascular risk monitoring protocols for perioperative chemotherapy patients and develop evidence-based guidance for managing bevacizumab-induced hypertension that integrates antihypertensive product use. Collaboration with cardio-oncology opinion leaders could enable development of real-world data programs quantifying the prevalence and management of anti-VEGF-induced hypertension across tumor types.

Date de publication : 2026-06-29

Resultats principaux : This national expert consensus document, jointly endorsed by the Portuguese Association of Interventional Cardiology (APIC-SPC) and the Portuguese Society of Hypertension (SPH), provides structured guidance on the use of catheter-based renal denervation (RDN) for blood pressure management in adults with uncontrolled hypertension despite maximally tolerated guideline-directed therapy and lifestyle modifications. The consensus synthesizes evidence from contemporary sham-controlled trials and large real-world registries, which collectively demonstrate sustained blood pressure reductions with both radiofrequency and ultrasound-based RDN systems alongside a consistently favorable safety profile, while acknowledging residual uncertainties regarding long-term clinical outcomes and individual response predictors. Patient selection is rigorously defined within a multidisciplinary hypertension team framework, with RDN explicitly positioned as an adjunctive—not first-line—therapy for resistant hypertension, though carefully selected non-resistant scenarios may also be considered. Key acknowledged limitations include the absence of validated predictors of individual response to RDN and the lack of long-term cardiovascular outcome data from controlled trials beyond blood pressure surrogate endpoints.

Implications Medical Affairs : This national consensus statement formalizes the clinical pathway for renal denervation in Portugal and signals increasing institutionalization of device-based hypertension therapy in Southern Europe, which may progressively redefine the treatment algorithm for resistant hypertension and alter the competitive landscape for pharmacological antihypertensives in this segment. Medical Affairs teams must monitor RDN adoption dynamics to anticipate how pharmacological and device-based therapies will be combined and how prescribing patterns for resistant hypertension drugs may evolve in markets where RDN centers are established. Resultats principaux : This randomized controlled trial enrolled 50 clinically stable pulmonary arterial hypertension (PAH) patients equally allocated to a 6-week integrated mindfulness-based stress reduction (MBSR) plus cardiopulmonary rehabilitation (CR) program or to conventional health education with standard pharmacological management, assessing cardiopulmonary function, hemodynamics, and psychological stress before and after intervention. Implications Medical Affairs : This trial provides evidence that non-pharmacological adjunctive programs meaningfully improve both hemodynamic and psychological outcomes in PAH, a field with high unmet need, and reinforces the concept of multimodal disease management that complements PAH-specific pharmacotherapy and could strengthen patient adherence programs. Medical Affairs teams can leverage these data to develop integrated patient support programs alongside PAH pharmacological portfolios, differentiating through a holistic care model and generating real-world evidence on the combined benefits of lifestyle intervention plus pharmacotherapy. Date de publication : 2026-06-30

Resultats principaux : This retrospective cohort study leveraged the INSPIRE dataset to analyze 15,138 Korean patients undergoing elective or emergency thoracic or abdominopelvic surgery, identifying postoperative cerebral infarction (POCI) in 277 cases (incidence 1.83%, 95% CI: 1.63-2.06%). Multivariable logistic regression identified five independent predictors of POCI: advanced age (aOR 1.65 per 10-year increment, 95% CI: 1.46-1.88), hypertension (aOR 5.92, 95% CI: 4.56-7.68), atrial fibrillation (aOR 4.21, 95% CI: 2.60-6.79), chronic kidney disease (aOR 2.47, 95% CI: 1.56-3.90), and peripheral arterial disease (aOR 2.45, 95% CI: 1.27-4.72), all with p<0.01. Implications Medical Affairs : Hypertension's nearly 6-fold independent risk for perioperative stroke is one of the strongest modifiable risk factors identified in this large surgical cohort, making a compelling evidence-based case for rigorous preoperative blood pressure optimization in surgical candidates and directly supporting antihypertensive product positioning in anesthesia and perioperative care pathways. Medical Affairs teams should engage anesthesiology, neurology, and vascular surgery KOLs to incorporate these findings into perioperative hypertension management protocols and to drive educational programs on preoperative cardiovascular risk assessment. Revue : BMC Geriatrics | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-29

Resultats principaux : This prospective observational study enrolled 86 older hospitalized patients at a Chinese secondary hospital to investigate upstream sociodemographic and chronic disease risk factors—including hypertension, diabetes, living environment, diet, continence, and mobility—for pressure injury (PI) occurrence and staging, going beyond conventional Braden Scale parameters. Resultats principaux : This prospective cohort study from the Guizhou Population Health Cohort Study (GPHCS) followed 3,399 participants (median follow-up 6.42 years), classifying them into four metabolic-obesity phenotypes using BMI and metabolic status: metabolically healthy normal weight (MHNW), metabolically unhealthy normal weight (MUNW), metabolically healthy obesity (MHO), and metabolically unhealthy obesity (MUO), with 826 incident hypertension cases identified. Implications Medical Affairs : This decade-long longitudinal evidence that metabolically healthy obesity is unstable and carries a significant and growing hypertension risk strongly argues for proactive pharmacological and lifestyle intervention before phenotypic deterioration to MUO, supporting early prescribing strategies for antihypertensive agents in obese patients regardless of initial metabolic health status. Revue : The Journal of Nutrition | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-29

Resultats principaux : This individual participant data (IPD) meta-analysis pooled 17,368 participants from eight longitudinal cohorts in Europe and the United States to examine associations between adherence to seven dietary patterns—HEI-2020, AHEI-2010, EAT-Lancet, traditional and continuous Mediterranean Diet (MedDiet), DASH, and MIND—and cognitive outcomes measured by MMSE (global cognition) and Trail Making Test Part B (executive function). Resultats principaux : This retrospective study reviewed all postpartum venous thromboembolism (VTE) cases at the People's Hospital of Xizang Autonomous Region (Tibet) from 2015-2024, comparing 109 high-altitude (HA) and 63 very high-altitude (VHA) patients, with a focus on characterizing altitudinal differences and identifying independent predictors of postpartum pulmonary embolism (PE). The proportion of PE was significantly higher in VHA versus HA groups (p=0.033), and joinpoint regression revealed significant early-phase (2015-2022) escalations in VTE (APC=18.05%), DVT (APC=19.66%), and PE proportion (APC=18.77%, p<0.05) over the decade.
