Left Ventricular and Left Atrial Mechanics in Resistant Hypertension-The Role of Two-Dimensional Speckle Tracking Echocardiography.

Revue : Echocardiography (Mount Kisco, N.Y.) | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06

Resultats principaux : This systematic review examines the clinical utility of speckle tracking echocardiography (STE) in detecting subclinical myocardial dysfunction among patients with resistant hypertension (RH), defined as uncontrolled blood pressure despite three or more antihypertensive agents — a population carrying disproportionately high cardiovascular morbidity and mortality. The primary endpoints evaluated are STE-derived left ventricular (LV) global longitudinal strain (GLS) and left atrial (LA) phasic strain parameters, both of which were consistently found to be impaired in uncontrolled and resistant hypertensive populations, indicating early myocardial dysfunction preceding changes in ejection fraction. Quantitatively, available data confirm that LV GLS and LA strain indices are the most reproducibly altered deformation parameters in RH, while other components such as circumferential strain, radial strain, and twist mechanics remain understudied in this specific subgroup. Subgroup-level findings reveal that the degree of LA phasic strain impairment mirrors the severity of LV dysfunction and correlates with the hypertensive burden. Notable limitations include the absence of multimodality imaging validation across reviewed studies, incomplete exclusion of concurrent coronary artery disease, and the heterogeneity of STE acquisition protocols across centers.

Implications Medical Affairs : The evidence supports positioning STE-derived strain indices as sensitive, guideline-relevant surrogate markers for early hypertensive heart disease, potentially influencing future ESC/ESH monitoring recommendations for resistant hypertension. From a product positioning standpoint, clinical trials for antihypertensive agents could incorporate LV GLS as a secondary endpoint to demonstrate organ-protection benefits beyond blood pressure reduction. KOL engagement with echocardiography specialists and hypertension centers on STE monitoring protocols presents an actionable opportunity to strengthen the clinical narrative for therapies with demonstrated myocardial protective properties.

The Linear Association Between A Body Shape Index and Abdominal Aortic Calcification in Individuals With Hypertension.

Revue : Clinical cardiology | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06

Resultats principaux : This cross-sectional multicenter analysis used data from 1,486 hypertensive participants in the U.S. National Health and Nutrition Examination Survey (NHANES) to examine the relationship between A Body Shape Index (ABSI) — a body composition metric integrating waist circumference, height, and BMI — and abdominal aortic calcification (AAC), a validated cardiovascular risk marker. Multivariable logistic regression demonstrated that each standard deviation increase in ABSI was associated with a 16.2% elevated AAC risk (OR=1.162, 95% CI: 1.025–1.318, p=0.019). Compared with the lowest quartile (Q1), participants in Q3 and Q4 showed significantly higher AAC risk with odds ratios of 1.554 (95% CI: 1.096–2.204, p=0.013) and 1.503 (95% CI: 1.047–2.157, p=0.027), respectively. ROC curve analysis confirmed moderate discriminative ability for AAC detection (AUC=0.625, 95% CI: 0.596–0.654, p<0.001) consistent across sex subgroups. Restricted cubic spline modelling confirmed a dose-dependent positive linear relationship between ABSI and AAC (P for nonlinearity >0.05), though the cross-sectional design limits causal inference and the study population is restricted to NHANES hypertensive participants.

Implications Medical Affairs : The identification of ABSI as an independent predictor of subclinical vascular disease in hypertensive individuals adds a body composition dimension to cardiovascular risk stratification, potentially supporting guideline updates that recommend waist-based indices alongside traditional BP measurement. For pharmaceutical positioning, these findings reinforce the rationale for addressing visceral adiposity as part of comprehensive hypertension management, supporting combination cardiometabolic therapy strategies. Medical Affairs teams can leverage these data in KOL discussions to advance the narrative that optimal hypertension management requires integrated cardiovascular risk factor control beyond blood pressure alone.

Prevalence and Risk Factors for Cerebral Microbleeds in Chinese Patients With Acute Ischemic Stroke.

Revue : Brain and behavior | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06

Resultats principaux : This retrospective hospital-based study assessed 171 consecutive acute ischemic stroke (AIS) patients from northern China (mean age 64.8±13.0 years; 73.7% male) for the presence, burden, and anatomical distribution of cerebral microbleeds (CMBs) using standardized 3.0 Tesla susceptibility-weighted MRI protocols. Overall CMB prevalence was 49.1%, with TOAST subtype variation: large artery atherosclerosis 59.4%, small vessel disease 47.5%, cardioembolism 21.4%, and other etiologies 25.0%. Multivariate logistic regression identified hypertension (AOR=2.07, 95% CI: 1.01–4.24) and severe white matter hyperintensity (AOR=4.38, 95% CI: 1.69–11.35) as independent predictors of CMB presence. Patients with three or more CMBs showed significantly higher small vessel disease prevalence (58.1% vs. 31.7%, p<0.01) and lower LDL-cholesterol levels (1.85 vs. 2.38 mmol/L, p<0.01) compared to those with fewer CMBs. Deep and infratentorial CMBs were associated with small vessel disease and moderate-to-severe white matter hyperintensity, while limitations include the single-center retrospective design, relatively small sample size of 171 patients, and lack of long-term follow-up data.

Implications Medical Affairs : These data solidify hypertension as the dominant modifiable independent risk factor for cerebral microbleed burden in ischemic stroke patients, strengthening the neurological protection argument for effective antihypertensive therapy in stroke-prone populations. From a product positioning standpoint, neurological endpoints such as CMB burden could be incorporated as exploratory endpoints in long-term trials of antihypertensive agents targeting stroke prevention, enhancing differentiation in competitive landscapes. Strategic KOL engagement at the intersection of neurology and hypertension medicine could advance educational programs on the cerebrovascular consequences of uncontrolled blood pressure, particularly in Asian patient populations where burden data are now emerging.

Intravitreal dexamethasone sodium phosphate challenge to predict implant response in refractory diabetic macular edema: a pilot study.

Revue : Arquivos brasileiros de oftalmologia | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-05-25

Resultats principaux : This prospective, non-randomized Phase 2 pilot study enrolled 12 pseudophakic eyes with diabetic macular edema (DME) refractory to anti-VEGF therapy to evaluate the diagnostic accuracy of a short-term intravitreal dexamethasone sodium phosphate (DSP) challenge in predicting anatomical response to a sustained-release dexamethasone implant (Ozurdex 0.7 mg). The primary endpoint was concordance between the anatomical response at Day 3 post-DSP challenge and Day 60 post-implant, defined as a 10% or greater reduction in central macular thickness. The DSP challenge induced significant macular drying by Day 3 (median central macular thickness reduction of -21 µm; p=0.002), and a positive challenge response predicted implant response with a positive predictive value and specificity of 100%, and a negative predictive value of 80%. As a secondary safety outcome, ocular hypertension developed in 16.7% of patients (2/12 eyes), all successfully managed with topical therapy, with no serious adverse events recorded. The study is critically limited by its very small sample size (12 eyes), single-center non-randomized Phase 2 pilot design, short follow-up, and the absence of a control arm, making these results hypothesis-generating only.

Implications Medical Affairs : While primarily an ophthalmology study, the 16.7% incidence of corticosteroid-induced ocular hypertension as a safety outcome highlights the blood pressure monitoring obligations associated with intravitreal steroid therapy in diabetic patients — a population with very high systemic hypertension co-morbidity that warrants cross-specialty vigilance. From a Medical Affairs perspective, this article is only peripherally relevant to cardiovascular hypertension surveillance but reinforces that hypertension management considerations arise across clinical settings, including ophthalmology, particularly in diabetic populations. The extremely limited sample size and pilot design preclude any practice-changing conclusions, and this finding should be filed as a signal requiring validation in adequately powered randomized trials.

Population health outcomes in Qatar 1990-2023: a systematic analysis for the Global Burden of Disease Study 2023.

Revue : EClinicalMedicine | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-05-18

Resultats principaux : This secondary analysis of Global Burden of Disease (GBD) 2023 modelled estimates provides the first comprehensive 33-year longitudinal assessment of population health in Qatar, covering mortality, prevalence, incidence, disability-adjusted life-years (DALYs), years of life lost (YLLs), and attributable risk factor burden across four time periods (1990–2000, 2000–2015, 2015–2023, and 1990–2023). The study population encompasses the entire Qatari national and expatriate population, with uncertainty intervals derived from 250 posterior draws. Communicable disease burden decreased substantially since 1990, driven by immunization coverage and improved maternal care, while non-communicable diseases now account for the majority of mortality and DALYs, led by ischemic heart disease, diabetes mellitus, cancer, chronic kidney disease, and neurological disorders. Hypertension ranks among the five dominant metabolic and behavioral risk factors — alongside high BMI, high fasting glucose, dietary risks, and tobacco use — driving the NCD epidemic in Qatar. COVID-19 temporarily reversed infectious disease burden declines in 2020–2021 before returning to pre-pandemic trajectories by 2023, and the GBD modelled framework carries inherent uncertainty that must be acknowledged when translating estimates to policy.

Implications Medical Affairs : This GBD 2023-based analysis positions hypertension as a top-tier modifiable risk factor driving premature mortality and disease burden in Qatar, providing a robust epidemiological foundation for antihypertensive market development strategies across the Gulf Cooperation Council (GCC) region. Medical Affairs teams can use these government-endorsed, peer-reviewed population data to support formulary submissions, health technology assessments, and payer engagement in a high-income, health-system-investing nation with an identified NCD crisis. Strategic partnership with Qatari KOLs involved in national NCD prevention planning represents a high-priority opportunity to influence hypertension treatment guidelines and ensure product visibility at the national health policy level.

Body composition and cardiometabolic risk reduction following therapy with a multi-ingredient nutraceutical formulation: a real-life clinical study.

Revue : Drugs in context | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-05-15

Resultats principaux : This 90-day prospective, single-arm, real-life observational study enrolled 24 women in perimenopause or postmenopause (aged 45–60 years, BMI ≥27) to assess the effects of a multi-ingredient nutraceutical formulation (Syndrema, Ghimas S.p.A.) designed to simultaneously target body fat, insulin resistance, dyslipidemia, glycemic homeostasis, arterial hypertension, and oxidative stress, administered alongside an isocaloric diet and standardized physical activity regimen. Primary endpoints covering clinical, biochemical, and body composition parameters were assessed at baseline and 90-day follow-up using paired tests. Significant improvements were observed in body weight, BMI, waist circumference, systolic blood pressure, LDL-cholesterol, triglycerides, C-reactive protein, homocysteine, and uric acid after 90 days of intervention. Bioelectrical impedance analysis confirmed reductions in absolute fat mass and fat mass percentage, with additional improvements in liver enzymes and azotemia suggesting systemic metabolic benefits. Critical limitations are the very small sample size (n=24), single-arm non-randomized design without a placebo control, and confounding by concurrent dietary and exercise interventions, which make it impossible to attribute effects specifically to the nutraceutical.

Implications Medical Affairs : This real-life observational pilot provides preliminary evidence that perimenopausal women represent a high-risk population for blood pressure elevation responsive to integrated lifestyle and nutraceutical interventions, strengthening the case for early, proactive hypertension management in the female midlife transition. For Medical Affairs strategy, these findings underscore the perimenopausal population as an underserved segment where adjunctive non-pharmacological approaches may complement antihypertensive pharmacotherapy, opening avenues for differentiated product positioning and patient-centered messaging. Engaging female cardiovascular health KOLs and women's health societies around perimenopause-specific hypertension risk management represents an actionable strategy to build brand presence in this growing demographic segment.

BRinging the Diabetes Prevention Program to GEriatric Populations in Spanish (BRIDGE-S): Assessing Acceptability Among Hispanic/Latino Adults in an Older Adult Center.

Revue : Journal of primary care & community health | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-05-27

Resultats principaux : This mixed-methods study assessed the acceptability of a culturally-tailored CDC Diabetes Prevention Program (DPP) delivered to Spanish-speaking Hispanic/Latino older adults at a community-based Older Adult Center in a large metropolitan area between August 2023 and October 2024. Sessions averaged 15 participants (77% female, mean age 71 years), representing diverse Hispanic origins (primarily Ecuadorian, Mexican, and Puerto Rican), the majority of whom reported chronic comorbidities including hyperlipidemia, hypertension, and type 2 diabetes. Quantitative post-session surveys showed high acceptability and perceived cultural appropriateness across all four interactive, in-person DPP sessions delivered by native Spanish-speaking physicians. Qualitative focus group data confirmed strong receptivity to lifestyle-focused content, a clear preference for in-person OAC-based delivery over virtual formats, and interest in additional topics including medication side effects, natural remedies, and social determinants of diet. Study limitations include the single-site convenience sample design, absence of clinical cardiovascular or metabolic outcome measurement, and restricted generalizability beyond similar OAC settings.

Implications Medical Affairs : This study demonstrates high acceptability of community-based, culturally-adapted cardiometabolic risk prevention programming in older Hispanic adults who carry disproportionate hypertension burden, generating directly actionable insights for patient engagement strategies in underserved minority populations. For Medical Affairs, partnering with community health organizations and Older Adult Centers serving Hispanic/Latino populations offers a concrete channel for hypertension awareness campaigns, medication adherence support programs, and culturally-sensitive health literacy initiatives aligned with pharmaceutical patient engagement commitments. These findings support KOL engagement with primary care physicians and community health workers in Hispanic communities, where structured prevention programs may significantly improve antihypertensive treatment uptake and blood pressure control rates.
