Sleep and Cardiovascular Health Among Women With a History of Hypertensive Disorders of Pregnancy: Pilot Observational Study.

Revue : JMIR Cardio | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-05-26

Resultats principaux : This pilot observational study enrolled 49 postpartum women — 28 with prior hypertensive disorders of pregnancy (HDP) and 21 with normotensive pregnancies — with an average of 4.9 years since delivery, using a wearable device (Oura ring) for 2 weeks of objective sleep monitoring alongside the Pittsburgh Sleep Quality Index (PSQI) and semistructured interviews. Cardiovascular health was assessed using the Life's Essential 8 score, incorporating both self-reported measures (diet, physical activity, nicotine exposure) and objective parameters (BMI, blood pressure, blood lipids, blood glucose, and sleep duration). Sleep quality was suboptimal in both groups, with mean PSQI scores of 7.0 (SD 3.5) in the HDP group versus 5.9 (SD 2.4) in controls (P=0.22), and average objective sleep duration of 6.7 hours (SD 0.8) — below recommended thresholds — with no intergroup difference. Approximately 47% of participants had abnormal sleep timing, and sleep onset variability was high (mean 1.2 hours, SD 0.5) without significant differences by HDP status; the mean Life's Essential 8 CVH score was 70.7 (SD 12.8, moderate range), with diet (mean 37.3) and BMI (mean 50.8) being the poorest-performing components. No significant differences in any CVH component were detected between women with and without prior HDP, suggesting that sleep impairment affects all postpartum mothers equally regardless of pregnancy hypertension history. A notable limitation is the small pilot sample (n=49) and the healthy volunteer bias inherent to feasibility studies, which may restrict generalizability to broader, more diverse postpartum populations.

Implications Medical Affairs : The finding that sleep disruption and suboptimal cardiovascular health are universal among postpartum mothers — irrespective of HDP history — challenges the assumption that HDP survivors carry uniquely elevated post-delivery cardiovascular risk attributable to sleep, which has implications for how current hypertension and obstetric guidelines define post-HDP monitoring protocols. From a Medical Affairs perspective, this study supports broadening cardiovascular risk reduction and blood pressure management interventions to target all mothers in the first decade after childbirth rather than exclusively those with gestational hypertensive diagnoses. The validation of wearable Oura ring data as a feasible, acceptable tool for sleep and cardiovascular monitoring in this population opens a concrete channel for KOL engagement in digital health partnerships and real-world evidence generation relevant to antihypertensive brand strategy.

A multisociety consensus statement on a new common definition and diagnostic criteria for PSVD or NCPF. [Journal of Hepatology]

Revue : Journal of Hepatology | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-05-27

Resultats principaux : This multisociety consensus document represents an internationally coordinated initiative involving hepatology, pathology, and pediatric hepatology societies from the Americas, Europe, and Asia to harmonize nomenclature and diagnostic criteria for non-cirrhotic portal hypertension, historically described under heterogeneous regional labels including idiopathic portal hypertension (IPH), non-cirrhotic portal fibrosis (NCPF), obliterative portal venopathy, and nodular regenerative hyperplasia. Following a structured consensus process with specialized working groups and external Delphi validation, the initiative concluded that the terms porto-sinusoidal vascular disorder (PSVD) and non-cirrhotic portal fibrosis (NCPF) may be used interchangeably when identical diagnostic criteria are applied. Core diagnostic requirements established by the consensus include a high-quality liver biopsy (at least 10 mm), mandatory exclusion of cirrhosis, and systematic ruling out of specific alternative conditions; notably, PSVD or NCPF may be diagnosed even in the absence of clinically overt portal hypertension and may coexist with other liver diseases provided cirrhosis is excluded. Standardized major and minor histologic criteria developed by expert pathologists were externally validated, and an integrated diagnostic scoring system incorporating histology, clinical features, associated conditions, and concomitant etiologies was produced and validated using the Delphi method. A primary limitation of this consensus is the absence of prospective validation data demonstrating clinical impact of adopting the new framework, and real-world uptake across diverse healthcare systems of varying resource levels remains to be established.

Implications Medical Affairs : The establishment of a globally unified diagnostic framework for non-cirrhotic portal hypertension is expected to substantially reduce the heterogeneity that has historically impaired international research collaborations and clinical trial design in this rare vascular disorder, directly facilitating the development of targeted therapies. From a Medical Affairs perspective, this consensus creates an immediate opportunity for pharmaceutical companies with pipeline assets in portal hypertension to engage the large international expert community that contributed to this guideline, facilitating earlier access to relevant clinical trial networks and key opinion leader relationships. The expanded definition — recognizing PSVD/NCPF without overt portal hypertension — may meaningfully increase the addressable patient population in future trials, an important consideration for Medical Affairs when building clinical evidence strategies and market access dossiers in the hepatology space.

Urban-rural disparities in hypertension prevalence, screening, and control in South Kivu Province, eastern Democratic Republic of the Congo.

Revue : Annales de Cardiologie et d'Angeiologie | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-05-26

Resultats principaux : This cross-sectional study conducted during the May Measurement Month campaigns in 2023 and 2024 enrolled 4,012 participants aged 30 years or older from South Kivu Province in the Democratic Republic of the Congo, including 1,036 urban residents of Bukavu and 2,976 rural residents of Kaziba and Katana, with blood pressure, weight, and height measured alongside structured interviews. Hypertension was defined as blood pressure at or above 140/90 mmHg and/or current antihypertensive medication use; rural participants demonstrated a significantly higher hypertension prevalence compared to urban participants (33.4% vs. 27.8%; P=0.001), alongside higher rates of age above 60 years (29.4% vs. 20.4%) and smoking (27.6% vs. 14.0%). Conversely, overweight (32.9% vs. 23.2%), obesity (12.3% vs. 5.3%), physical inactivity (32.9% vs. 11.8%), and a history of diabetes mellitus (5.3% vs. 3.5%) were significantly more prevalent in urban areas (all P less than 0.01). Multivariate analysis identified rural residence as an independent predictor of hypertension (adjusted OR=1.55; P less than 0.0001) after adjustment for age, sex, BMI, smoking, diabetes, and physical inactivity, suggesting that additional environmental and behavioral mechanisms specific to rural Congolese environments — including an older age structure and low detection rates — drive this excess burden. A primary limitation is the cross-sectional design precluding causal inference, with additional risk of selection bias inherent to community-based measurement campaigns that may over-represent health-engaged individuals.

Implications Medical Affairs : These data from Sub-Saharan Africa confirm that the epidemiological burden of hypertension in low- and middle-income countries disproportionately concentrates in rural populations with very low awareness and detection rates, with direct implications for market development strategies and access-to-medicine initiatives for antihypertensive drugs in francophone Africa. From a Medical Affairs perspective, this study underscores the urgent need for simplified hypertension screening algorithms and first-line treatment regimens adapted to resource-constrained rural settings, creating a platform for disease awareness partnerships with local medical associations and international health organizations. The finding that rural residence independently predicts hypertension — beyond classical risk factors — provides actionable intelligence for community health programs and patient support platforms targeting diagnosis rates, where pharmaceutical companies can assume a leadership role in public health co-investment.

Resultats principaux : The Zoi cohort is a newly established prospective longitudinal cohort study based at a dedicated prevention-focused health centre in France, designed to advance personalised prevention through systematic multi-modal screening; this profile describes the first 1,000 participants (67.5% male, mean age 51.1 years, high educational attainment) enrolled as paying customers or through employer-sponsored programmes. Data collection integrates over 500 self-reported items, clinical examinations, an extensive biomarker panel of 196 markers, and multi-modal imaging encompassing vascular, breast, abdominal and pelvic ultrasound, full-body composition, and retinal scans — one of the most comprehensive phenotyping efforts reported in a French prevention cohort. Male sex and older age were significantly associated with disease unawareness (p less than 0.05), reinforcing the role of sex-specific screening gaps in cardiovascular medicine. Longitudinal follow-up is conducted via annual re-evaluations and a dedicated digital application; a key limitation is the healthy volunteer and socioeconomic bias, limiting generalisability to lower-income and less health-conscious population groups where the burden of undiagnosed hypertension is likely even higher.

Implications Medical Affairs : The high rate of undiagnosed hypertension (among the most frequent undetected conditions in 45.6% of apparently healthy adults) found even in this health-conscious, educated cohort underscores a profound detection gap with direct relevance to Medical Affairs disease awareness campaigns and early screening advocacy, particularly for antihypertensive portfolio positioning. Anti-inflammatory effects of oral NLRP3 inhibition with ruvonoflast among individuals at elevated cardiovascular risk.

Revue : Journal of the American College of Cardiology (JACC) | Tier : Tier 1 | IF estime : >20

Date de publication : 2026-05-26

Resultats principaux : This double-blind Phase 1b randomized controlled trial evaluated the anti-inflammatory efficacy and safety of ruvonoflast — an oral NLRP3 inflammasome inhibitor dosed at 225 mg twice daily — versus matching placebo in 63 participants with elevated residual inflammatory risk (hsCRP at least 2.5 mg/L), high ASCVD risk, BMI 30–40 kg/m2, and at least one cardiometabolic comorbidity (dyslipidemia, hypertension, or type 2 diabetes; mean age 52.6 years; 71.4% female; 69.8% white). The primary endpoint — change in hsCRP ratio at Day 28 — was met with a posterior probability greater than 99% for superiority of ruvonoflast over placebo; geometric least-squares mean hsCRP reduction was 82.2% (95% CI 75.9–86.8) with ruvonoflast versus 37.2% (95% CI 7.0–57.6) with placebo, with statistically significant between-group differences emerging from Day 3 onward (p at most 0.001). Key secondary endpoints demonstrated significant reductions in IL-6 and fibrinogen at Day 28 versus placebo (p less than 0.001 for both), while mean percentage body weight reductions were comparable between treatment groups. Four participants (10%) in the ruvonoflast arm discontinued due to transient, reversible treatment-emergent adverse events versus none in the placebo arm; hsCRP returned to baseline 7 days after treatment discontinuation, indicating a class effect requiring continuous dosing. The primary limitations are the short 28-day trial duration, the small sample size of 63 participants, and the absence of hard cardiovascular event endpoints — Phase 2 trials of larger size and longer duration are ongoing to further characterize the therapeutic potential of ruvonoflast.

Implications Medical Affairs : Publication of these Phase 1b results in JACC (Tier 1) will command immediate attention from interventional cardiologists and preventive medicine KOLs, positioning NLRP3 inflammasome inhibition as a next-generation anti-inflammatory strategy for patients with hypertension and residual cardiovascular risk not adequately captured by LDL lowering alone — Medical Affairs should be ready to lead scientific discussions at major cardiology congresses. From a competitive intelligence perspective, ruvonoflast's potent 82% hsCRP reduction surpasses the profile of canakinumab in the CANTOS trial and opens strategic discussions around positioning NLRP3 inhibitors as adjunctive therapy in high-CV-risk hypertensive patients, with Medical Affairs teams needing to develop comparative evidence frameworks and advisory board agendas now. The defined Phase 2 timeline and the eligibility criterion of hypertension as a qualifying comorbidity provide a concrete horizon for Medical Affairs to develop educational materials, patient identification tools, and KOL engagement strategies aligned with potential future regulatory filings.

Psychosocial stress in the first 25 years of life and cognitive function in midlife: the role of inflammation.

Revue : Brain, Behavior, and Immunity | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-05-25

Resultats principaux : This longitudinal analysis examined data from 326 midlife participants (mean age 39.4 years; 56.4% African American; 57.4% female) drawn from two prospective cohorts, investigating whether systemic inflammation mediates the relationship between early-life psychosocial stress (ELS) before age 25 and midlife cognitive function as assessed by the NIH Toolbox Cognition Battery. ELS was quantified using a composite score integrating individual, family, and neighborhood stressors, while systemic inflammation was characterized by measuring 1,034 plasma proteins — including 537 inflammatory markers — using the high-sensitivity Olink Reveal platform. Forty-five proteins were significantly associated with ELS (p less than 0.01), with the strongest effects observed for CXCL17, ISM1, and ADM; nine of these proteins — including LAMP3, SASH3, LPL, and TNFRSF11A — were also independently associated with midlife cognitive performance. Weighted Quantile Sum (WQS) regression demonstrated that the combined inflammatory protein index significantly mediated 35% of the ELS-to-cognition association (p=0.013), and higher ELS was independently associated with poorer cognitive performance overall (p=0.008). A notable limitation is the cross-sectional assessment of proteomic and cognitive data in midlife, which prevents direct causal inference about the temporal sequence of the inflammatory pathway, and the predominantly African American sample may limit generalizability to other demographic groups.

Implications Medical Affairs : The finding that early psychosocial stress drives inflammatory protein profiles that partially mediate midlife cognitive decline — through mechanisms overlapping with those implicated in hypertension pathophysiology, including RAAS activation and HPA axis dysregulation — reinforces a biologically plausible link between chronic stress, systemic inflammation, and cardiovascular risk that should inform holistic hypertension management strategies. From a Medical Affairs standpoint, this research provides a scientific foundation for KOL education on the role of psychosocial risk factor assessment in hypertensive patients, particularly in at-risk minority populations where stress burden and hypertension prevalence are both disproportionately high. This study can be leveraged in Medical Affairs communications to support the value of comprehensive risk factor management beyond blood pressure control, building the evidence base for programs that integrate psychosocial screening with antihypertensive therapy in primary care.

Multi-omics insights into uric acid metabolism: from genetics to epigenetics, transcriptomics, and metabolomics in cardiometabolic disease.

Revue : Biomedical Journal | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-05-25

Resultats principaux : This comprehensive narrative review systematically integrates multi-omics evidence — encompassing genomics, epigenomics, transcriptomics, proteomics, and metabolomics — to elucidate the molecular mechanisms through which hyperuricemia (elevated serum uric acid) contributes to cardiometabolic diseases, including hypertension, type 2 diabetes, chronic kidney disease, metabolic syndrome, and atherosclerotic cardiovascular disease. Mechanistically, uric acid has been shown to induce endothelial dysfunction, oxidative stress, chronic inflammation, insulin resistance, and activation of the renin-angiotensin-aldosterone system (RAAS), collectively worsening cardiometabolic conditions; genome-wide association studies (GWAS) have identified key urate transporter genes — SLC2A9, ABCG2, SLC22A12 — while epigenomic studies demonstrate how DNA methylation, histone modifications, and non-coding RNAs link genetic predisposition to environmental exposures. Transcriptomic and single-cell RNA sequencing analyses reveal tissue-specific regulation of urate transport and inflammatory signaling in the kidneys, liver, adipose tissue, and vasculature, while metabolomic studies link hyperuricemia to perturbations in purine metabolism, redox balance, lipid remodeling, and inflammatory protein networks. The integration of expression quantitative trait locus (eQTL) mapping, causal modeling, network biology, and artificial intelligence-driven analyses now provides powerful tools for biomarker discovery, mechanistic interpretation, and therapeutic target identification. As a narrative review synthesizing primary studies, the absence of new primary data and the largely experimental status of many AI-driven multi-omics integration methods constitute primary limitations, and prospective intervention trials are required to validate causal claims across the omics layers described.

Implications Medical Affairs : The identification of RAAS activation as a direct downstream consequence of hyperuricemia establishes a mechanistic bridge between elevated uric acid and hypertension pathophysiology, providing scientific rationale for monitoring serum uric acid in hypertensive patients treated with RAAS inhibitors and potentially informing combination therapy strategies. From a Medical Affairs perspective, the multi-omics biomarker landscape described — particularly GWAS-defined urate transporters and proteomic markers — supports developing precision medicine frameworks for cardiovascular risk stratification in hypertensive patients with concomitant hyperuricemia, positioning pharmaceutical companies as scientific leaders in the cardiometabolic continuum. This review creates a scientific communications opportunity for Medical Affairs teams to engage cardiologists and nephrologists around the clinical relevance of uric acid management in hypertension, supporting advisory board agendas, KOL publications, and congress symposia focused on this underappreciated intersection of metabolic and cardiovascular medicine.

The mustard operation 50 years on: When surgical success is not enough.

Revue : International Journal of Cardiology | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-05-25

Resultats principaux : This longitudinal narrative case review documents the 50-year clinical trajectory of an individual first diagnosed as a newborn with transposition of the great arteries (TGA) in a 1976 medical documentary and now followed as an adult at the same tertiary Adult Congenital Heart Disease (ACHD) centre, providing a unique historical perspective on the long-term outcomes of the Mustard operation generation. The case illustrates the major late complications characterizing this cohort: arrhythmia, progressive systemic right ventricular dysfunction, and heart failure — arising from the inherent biomechanical vulnerability of the right ventricle supporting the systemic circulation over decades, in contrast to the excellent early surgical outcomes originally reported. The authors highlight that modifiable lifestyle factors and psychosocial stressors — including systemic hypertension — interact critically with congenital cardiac physiology to accelerate ventricular dysfunction and worsen long-term outcomes in this population. An argument is made for a model of lifelong ACHD care extending beyond anatomical repair to encompass sustained lifestyle intervention, cardiovascular risk factor control (including blood pressure management), and psychosocial support across the lifespan. A primary limitation is the single-case narrative structure, which by nature lacks the statistical power of cohort studies and cannot establish prevalence estimates, and the conclusions are drawn from a historically selected and exceptionally well-documented patient whose access to tertiary care may not reflect the broader Mustard operation survivor population.

Implications Medical Affairs : This case underscores the clinical importance of arterial hypertension as a modifiable comorbidity in the rapidly growing adult congenital heart disease population, where even modest blood pressure elevations carry disproportionately severe prognostic implications given the biomechanical vulnerabilities of a systemic right ventricle — an argument for tailored hypertension guidelines and drug selection criteria specific to this population. From a Medical Affairs standpoint, the ACHD population represents an important and underserved niche for antihypertensive therapy development, where standard blood pressure targets and drug class safety data may not apply directly, creating an opportunity for focused KOL education, specialist advisory boards, and labelling expansion discussions with regulatory authorities. This publication supports Medical Affairs investment in longitudinal ACHD registries, patient advocacy partnership initiatives, and physician education platforms that position the pharmaceutical company as a recognized thought leader in cardiovascular comorbidity management — particularly hypertension — in adults surviving congenital heart surgery.
