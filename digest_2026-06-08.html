Reporting changes in right ventricular systolic pressure: insights from Classification and Regression Tree (CART) analysis.

Revue : Echo Research and Practice | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-08

Resultats principaux : This retrospective registry study analyzed 32,656 echocardiogram pairs from 5,934 patients with reported right ventricular systolic pressure (RVSP) in the Stanford CardioShare Registry, applying Natural Language Processing and Classification and Regression Tree (CART) analysis to characterize real-world physician reporting thresholds for pulmonary hypertension monitoring. CART analysis revealed that physicians most commonly determine a meaningful RVSP change based on an absolute threshold of 8 mmHg and whether the change occurred within or outside the normal reference range; RVSP was classified as stable in 48.9%, increased in 12.5%, decreased in 9.9%, and not directly mentioned in 28.7% of pairs. On cross-validation, the CART model achieved accuracy and F1-scores of 83%/79% for detecting increases and 81%/74% for decreases, confirming adequate discriminative performance. A separate duplicate-analysis cohort (210 healthy volunteers and 208 PAH patients) yielded an analytic precision of 8-10% relative imprecision, corresponding to a 15% reference change value given biological variation of 4-5%, with worst imprecision at lower RVSP values. Key limitations include single-institution data, heterogeneity of underlying pulmonary hypertension diagnoses, and the retrospective extraction of physician language via NLP, which may not capture all clinical nuance.

Implications Medical Affairs : This study provides the first data-driven threshold (8 mmHg) standardizing RVSP reporting in pulmonary hypertension monitoring, directly relevant to defining treatment response endpoints in clinical trials involving PAH therapies. Medical Affairs teams should disseminate these standardization findings to echocardiography KOLs and pulmonary hypertension guideline committees to align imaging endpoints with clinically meaningful thresholds. Incorporating this reference change value into clinical trial design and real-world monitoring protocols represents an actionable opportunity to strengthen evidence packages for PAH treatments and support regulatory submissions.

Smartwatch-derived exercise metrics as predictors of early hypertension: a prospective observational study.

Revue : BMC Cardiovascular Disorders | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-08

Resultats principaux : This 12-month prospective observational study enrolled 230 normotensive adults aged 30-60 years who were continuously monitored with consumer smartwatches, with baseline wearable predictors (heart rate variability, resting heart rate, time in moderate-to-vigorous physical activity) computed as the mean of the first 30 days of valid data. Incident hypertension, defined per current European guidelines, developed in 28 participants (12.2%) over the follow-up period, providing a non-trivial event rate for a normotensive cohort. Multivariable logistic regression demonstrated that lower heart rate variability, lower physical activity levels, and higher BMI were each independently associated with incident hypertension. A significant interaction between autonomic variability and physical activity was identified, showing that individuals with both reduced HRV and low physical activity carried the highest predicted hypertension risk, a potentially important subgroup for targeted intervention. Exploratory machine-learning models showed improved statistical discrimination over clinical variables alone; major limitations include small sample size (n=230), single-center design, absence of external validation, and reliance on consumer-grade device accuracy.

Implications Medical Affairs : This study supports the emerging paradigm of wearable digital biomarkers for early hypertension risk stratification, with immediate relevance for patient identification programs and digital health partnerships in preventive cardiology. Medical Affairs teams should monitor this evolving evidence base to inform collaborations with digital health companies and integration of wearable-derived physiological parameters into real-world evidence generation programs for hypertension. Impact of guideline definitions on right ventricular diameter in echocardiography: an automated analysis in controls and patients with pulmonary hypertension.

Revue : Echo Research and Practice | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-08

Resultats principaux : This study compared three guideline-based definitions of right ventricular (RV) diameter measurements — the 2010 ASE/ESC, the revised 2025 ASE, and a novel non-linear centerline-based approach — in 208 healthy volunteers and 221 PAH patients, using custom cardiac contour analysis software. The 2025 ASE definitions yielded diameters 15% lower at the basal level and 20% lower at the mid level compared to 2010 ASE/ESC, resulting in paradoxically high RV enlargement prevalence in healthy males (22% basal, 35.8% mid) under the new definitions. Diagnostic discrimination for PAH was slightly superior with the 2010 definition (AUC 0.937 vs. 0.898 for 2025 ASE; p<0.001), though outcome prediction C-statistics were comparable (~0.59) across both definitions. The healthy volunteer cohort had a median age of 63 years (52% male) while PAH patients were younger (median 48 years, 22% male) with median PVRI of 22 Wood units per square meter. Key limitations include single-center data from a specialized PAH program and the need for prospective validation of the centerline-based approach.

Implications Medical Affairs : These findings have direct implications for PAH clinical trial design, as different guideline definitions may substantially alter RV enlargement classification and patient eligibility, creating inconsistencies across studies if imaging protocols are not pre-specified. Medical Affairs teams should proactively alert KOLs and trial design committees to specify which guideline definition is used for RV measurement to ensure cross-trial comparability in hypertension and PAH studies. Advocating for standardized echocardiographic imaging protocols in PAH clinical programs represents an opportunity for industry leadership in methodology, strengthening the scientific credibility of efficacy and endpoint data.

Revue : Scientific Reports | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-07

Resultats principaux : This 8-year prospective cohort study in 509 Kurdish Iranian adults investigated associations between Nrf2 (rs6721961) and NQO1 (rs1800566) polymorphisms and cardiometabolic diseases using PCR-RFLP genotyping, Cox regression, and generalized structural equation modeling (GSEM). Cox regression demonstrated that homozygous recessive genotypes of either or both polymorphisms were strongly associated with an increased risk of hypertension over time (HR=2.82; 95% CI: 1.4-5.3), with combined Nrf2 GT and NQO1 CT genotypes showing the highest type 2 diabetes risk (HR=3.15; 95% CI: 1.7-5.9). Risk of complications in adulthood following childhood metabolic dysfunction-associated steatohepatitis based on Korean national health insurance data.

Revue : Scientific Reports | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-07

Resultats principaux : This large matched cohort study used the Korean National Health Insurance Service Database (2002-2021) to compare 12,894 children and adolescents (aged 7-18) diagnosed with MASH against 921,141 matched non-MASH controls in a 1:5 ratio, tracking adult-onset complications from age 19 over up to 10 years of follow-up. The MASH group showed significantly higher hazard ratios for adult complications including hypertensive disease, liver fibrosis and cirrhosis, hyperlipidemia, diabetes, cancer, and mental illness, establishing childhood MASH as a multisystem risk factor for adult cardiovascular and metabolic disease. Medical costs in the MASH group were substantially higher than non-MASH controls across multiple disease categories including cerebrovascular disease and liver fibrosis, demonstrating the long-term economic burden of pediatric metabolic liver disease. The study strengths include the large national database, long follow-up period, and matched design; limitations include reliance on diagnostic codes (K75.8), potential miscoding, limited clinical phenotyping, and possible misclassification. Specific hazard ratios for hypertensive disease were not numerically reported in the abstract, limiting direct quantitative interpretation of the hypertension-specific association.

Implications Medical Affairs : The finding that childhood MASH predicts adult hypertension establishes a life-course epidemiological pathway that significantly expands the target population for early cardiometabolic intervention well beyond traditional hypertension risk factors. Date de publication : 2026-06-07

Implications Medical Affairs : The absence of a significant association between hypertension and medicinal herb use in this large Iranian cohort suggests that hypertensive patients in rural Iran do not preferentially use herbal remedies as complementary treatment, which has implications for adherence and drug-herb interaction risk assessment in this setting. Associations of healthy dietary patterns with plasma biomarkers of inflammation and coagulation in adults with and without type 1 diabetes.

Revue : The Journal of Nutrition | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-06

Resultats principaux : This longitudinal analysis from the Coronary Artery Calcification in Type 1 Diabetes (CACTI) study included 563 T1D and 692 non-diabetic adults, followed over three years to examine associations between adherence to three healthy dietary patterns (AHEI-2010, DASH, Mediterranean-style) and plasma biomarkers of inflammation and coagulation including homocysteine, hs-CRP, fibrinogen, and PAI-1. The DASH diet score was significantly inversely associated with homocysteine in both T1D (-0.56%/point increase; 95% CI: -1.01, -0.12) and non-diabetic groups (-0.54%/point; 95% CI: -0.83, -0.24), demonstrating consistent benefit across diabetes status. The DASH score was also associated with lower PAI-1 in non-diabetic individuals (percent change -1.31%; 95% CI: -2.18, -0.43) and T1D subjects (percent change -0.99%; 95% CI: -2.19, -0.22), consistent with anti-thrombotic benefits of the DASH pattern. All three dietary patterns showed significant inverse associations with homocysteine, but associations varied by diabetes status and specific dietary pattern. Limitations include reliance on food frequency questionnaires, the observational design precluding causal inference, and potential residual confounding from unmeasured lifestyle variables.

Implications Medical Affairs : These findings reinforce the mechanistic cardiovascular benefit of the DASH diet in reducing hypertension-associated procoagulant and inflammatory biomarkers, providing scientific support for dietary guidelines as a complement to pharmacotherapy in hypertensive and diabetic populations. From a Medical Affairs perspective, this evidence base strengthens the argument for bundled lifestyle-pharmacotherapy interventions in hypertensive patients with type 1 diabetes, a growing and high-priority comorbid population. Collaboration with nutrition and preventive cardiology KOLs to develop integrated educational programs combining dietary guidance with antihypertensive pharmacotherapy could enhance brand positioning and adherence in cardiometabolic patients.

TKI-associated proteinuria and nephrotic syndrome: a narrative review with clinical illustration in radio-iodine-refractory thyroid cancer.

Revue : Acta Clinica Belgica | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-06

Resultats principaux : This narrative review describes proteinuria and nephrotic syndrome as a significant class effect of VEGF pathway inhibition by tyrosine kinase inhibitors (TKIs), illustrated by a clinical case of radio-iodine-refractory differentiated thyroid cancer patient who developed recurrent nephrotic-range proteinuria during sequential treatment with lenvatinib, sorafenib, and cabozantinib. The underlying pathophysiology is multifactorial, with hypertension, endothelial injury, and direct podocyte damage contributing to glomerular lesions including focal segmental glomerulosclerosis, minimal change disease, and non-thrombotic hyaline glomerular microangiopathy. Reported incidence of proteinuria varies substantially across VEGFR-targeted TKIs in clinical trials, making direct comparative interpretation unreliable without head-to-head evidence. Management strategies include dose reduction, treatment discontinuation, and switching to TKIs with different nephrotoxic profiles; partial or full reversibility of renal damage is possible with early intervention. Limitations include the limited evidence base, reliance on case reports and small series, and heterogeneity in reporting standards across TKI clinical trials.

Implications Medical Affairs : Hypertension is a key upstream driver of TKI-associated renal toxicity, highlighting the critical importance of proactive antihypertensive management in oncology patients receiving VEGFR-targeted therapies to prevent irreversible glomerular damage. This review underscores a niche positioning opportunity for antihypertensive agents with minimal CYP3A4-mediated drug-drug interactions common in TKI co-administration settings. Medical Affairs should engage nephrology and oncology KOLs at the interface of hypertension and cancer care to develop co-management algorithms for TKI-associated hypertension, positioning the company as a scientific leader in this clinically underserved area.

Epidemiology of Cardiovascular Disease in Pregnancy in Japan — Analysis of a Nationwide Perinatal Database 2014-2023.

Revue : Circulation Journal | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-05

Resultats principaux : This retrospective cohort study analyzed 2,189,852 pregnancies in the Japan Society of Obstetrics and Gynecology Perinatal Database (2014-2023), identifying 26,894 (1.23%) complicated by maternal cardiovascular disease, compared to a low-risk population of 1,882,896 pregnancies without medical comorbidities. Pregnancies with CVD had significantly higher rates of hypertensive disorders of pregnancy, pulmonary embolism, and pulmonary edema, establishing hypertension as a leading CVD-associated complication in this nationwide cohort. Maternal death, though rare in both groups, occurred significantly more frequently in CVD-complicated pregnancies (0.03% vs. 0.01%, P<0.001), underscoring a meaningful mortality differential at the population level. CVD-complicated pregnancies required more frequent cesarean delivery, general anesthesia, and labor analgesia, indicating substantially greater obstetric complexity and healthcare resource use. Limitations include the retrospective registry design, potential under-reporting of milder CVD, lack of granular pharmacotherapy data, and absence of long-term maternal cardiovascular follow-up.

Implications Medical Affairs : With 1.23% of Japanese pregnancies complicated by CVD and hypertensive disorders among the leading complications, this registry establishes a significant population health burden demanding targeted antihypertensive management strategies in the obstetric setting. Medical Affairs teams should leverage these national epidemiological data to advocate for updated Japanese pregnancy hypertension guidelines, including evidence on the safety and efficacy of approved antihypertensive agents in pregnancy. Strategic engagement with Japanese maternal-fetal medicine specialists and obstetric cardiologists provides an opportunity to position antihypertensive agents with established pregnancy safety data as the preferred treatment choice in this growing high-risk patient segment.

Predictors of clomiphene citrate response in the treatment of men with testosterone deficiency.

Revue : The Journal of Sexual Medicine | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-05

Resultats principaux : This retrospective study of 292 men with low testosterone (median age 60 years; median baseline total testosterone 264 ng/dL) treated with clomiphene citrate at Memorial Sloan Kettering Cancer Center found that 44% of the cohort had concurrent hypertension, reflecting a high cardiovascular comorbidity burden in this patient segment. Treatment response (defined as total testosterone reaching >=400 ng/dL plus >=200 ng/dL increase from baseline within 12 weeks) was achieved in 136 of 292 patients (47%), with 53% failing to meet response criteria. Multivariable analysis identified higher baseline LH (OR 0.82; 95% CI: 0.71-0.95; P=0.008) and prior androgen deprivation therapy (OR 0.11; 95% CI: 0.01-0.6; P=0.039) as significant independent negative predictors of treatment response. Other high-prevalence comorbidities included hyperlipidemia (46%), prior radical prostatectomy (41%), and diabetes (18%), reflecting a complex cardiometabolic comorbidity profile in this patient population. Limitations include the retrospective design, older study population, short 12-week follow-up, and absence of validated symptom scoring for hypogonadism.

Implications Medical Affairs : The 44% prevalence of hypertension in testosterone-deficient men treated with clomiphene citrate underscores the critical cardiovascular comorbidity burden in this patient segment, reinforcing the need for routine blood pressure monitoring protocols within testosterone optimization care pathways. Medical Affairs teams should develop educational content on cardiovascular risk management in men undergoing hormonal therapies, including appropriate antihypertensive treatment in the context of complex polypharmacy. Engagement with endocrinology and urology KOLs to establish integrated cardiovascular risk management protocols for hypogonadal hypertensive men would strengthen both scientific positioning and commercial opportunities for antihypertensive products in this growing patient segment.

Midlife hypertension and late-life cognition: weighting the LifeAfter90 study.

Revue : Alzheimer's and Dementia | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-01

Resultats principaux : The LifeAfter90 cohort studied 307 ethnoracially diverse adults aged 90+ linked to midlife multiphasic health check-ups, assessing executive function, semantic memory, and verbal memory in oldest-old adults, using stabilized inverse probability of censoring weights to improve population representativeness relative to the target midlife health check-up population. After weighting, LA90 participants were well-matched to the target population on race/ethnicity, hypertension prevalence, and education, enhancing the generalizability of findings to older populations. Midlife hypertension was not significantly associated with executive function (beta=0.03; 95% CI: -0.24, 0.31) or verbal memory (beta=-0.10; 95% CI: -0.37, 0.16) in the oldest-old survivors. However, midlife hypertension was associated with poorer semantic memory scores (beta=-0.19; 95% CI: -0.44, 0.06), suggesting a domain-specific sustained impact on learning and memory that persists into extreme old age. Limitations include survivor bias inherent in oldest-old cohorts (those reaching age 90+ may differ from midlife hypertensives who died earlier), modest sample size (n=307), cross-sectional cognitive assessment, and potential historical misclassification of hypertension diagnosis.

Implications Medical Affairs : This study adds important epidemiological nuance to the well-established hypertension-dementia relationship by demonstrating a selective domain-specific semantic memory impairment persisting into the oldest-old age group, supporting the long-term cognitive value proposition for early and sustained antihypertensive treatment. Medical Affairs teams should leverage these findings to strengthen guideline advocacy and payer communication strategies around the dementia prevention benefit of blood pressure control, an increasingly high-profile area within aging population health priorities. Echocardiographic characterization of combined postcapillary and precapillary pulmonary hypertension in systemic sclerosis with preserved ejection fraction.

Revue : Echo Research and Practice | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-08

Resultats principaux : This retrospective single-center study at Johns Hopkins analyzed 147 adults with systemic sclerosis and heart failure with preserved ejection fraction (SSc-HFpEF) who had echocardiography and right heart catheterization within one year, classifying patients as isolated postcapillary PH (Ipc-PH; n=46) or combined pre- and postcapillary PH (Cpc-PH; n=101) per guideline-defined hemodynamic criteria. Cpc-PH patients exhibited significantly greater right heart remodeling, higher pulmonary pressures, and impaired RV-pulmonary artery coupling compared to Ipc-PH patients. Random forest classification and multivariable logistic regression identified key echocardiographic predictors of Cpc-PH: reduced RV free wall strain (RVFWS), decreased RV/PA coupling ratios (RVFWS/PASP and FAC/PASP), elevated PASP, lower septal e' velocity, and higher systolic LV eccentricity index. In adjusted Cox models for Cpc-PH, elevated LV eccentricity index (HR 1.39), increased RV internal diastolic diameter (HR 2.15), and reduced left and right atrial strain (HR 0.91 and 0.94 respectively) were independently associated with mortality. Limitations include retrospective design, single-center SSc-specialized population, and the within-one-year (not simultaneous) imaging-catheterization pairing.

Implications Medical Affairs : This study advances the echocardiographic toolkit for differentiating pulmonary hypertension phenotypes in SSc-HFpEF, with identified RV-PA coupling and strain biomarkers directly applicable to patient stratification in clinical trials targeting Cpc-PH in autoimmune-associated pulmonary hypertension. Medical Affairs should engage SSc-pulmonary hypertension KOLs at major academic centers to promote understanding of RV-PA coupling and strain-based imaging biomarkers as patient selection and risk stratification tools in clinical practice and trial settings. The identification of mortality-predictive echocardiographic parameters creates an opportunity to support development of imaging-based companion strategies for PAH therapies, differentiating treatment approaches in the heterogeneous SSc-PH population.

Effects of inhaled nitric oxide on ventilation/perfusion mismatch assessed by electrical impedance tomography in intubated patients with moderate-to-severe ARDS: a prospective physiological study.

Revue : Respiratory Research | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-06

Resultats principaux : This single-center RCT enrolled 40 mechanically ventilated patients with moderate-to-severe ARDS (PaO2/FiO2 <=200 mmHg), randomized 1:1 to inhaled nitric oxide (iNO) at clinician-determined dose plus standard mechanical ventilation versus standard ventilation alone, with electrical impedance tomography (EIT) assessment of V/Q matching as the primary endpoint at 24 hours. At 24 hours, iNO significantly improved V/Q matching compared to control, with significantly larger reductions in both EIT-measured dead space and shunt fraction (p<0.001 for both). The median PaO2/FiO2 ratio improved dramatically in the iNO group (from 114.0 to 240.0; p<0.001), a significantly greater improvement than controls (p<0.001), accompanied by a significantly greater reduction in pulmonary artery pressure (p<0.001). No significant between-group differences were observed in inflammatory biomarkers or safety parameters, suggesting a favorable short-term safety profile for iNO. Limitations include small sample size (n=20 per group), single-center design, retrospective registration, and 24-hour follow-up insufficient to assess clinical outcomes such as ventilator-free days or mortality.

Implications Medical Affairs : This RCT provides direct mechanistic evidence that iNO reduces pulmonary artery pressure in ARDS through improved V/Q matching, adding to the physiological rationale for its use in ARDS-associated pulmonary hypertension and supporting the design of future larger outcome trials. The EIT-based V/Q monitoring approach represents an innovative methodology for assessing treatment response in acute pulmonary hypertension, with potential applicability as a surrogate endpoint in critical care clinical studies. Medical Affairs should engage critical care and pulmonary hypertension KOLs around EIT-based endpoints and physiological surrogate outcomes to position the company as a leader in innovative trial design for acute pulmonary vascular conditions.
