Potentials, barriers, and strategies for integrating tuberculosis, diabetes mellitus, and hypertension case management: A scoping review.

Revue : PloS one | Tier : NA | IF estimé : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-26

Résultats principaux : This scoping review screened 7,983 studies and included 126 papers published between January 2005 and January 2025, drawn from MEDLINE, Scopus, ScienceDirect, and EMERALD, focusing on integrated case management models for tuberculosis (TB), diabetes mellitus (DM), and hypertension across diverse health system contexts. The review demonstrates that integrated management of TB, DM, and hypertension can improve access to care, program retention, and early comorbidity detection by streamlining screening, diagnosis, treatment, counseling, and patient self-management support under unified care pathways. Integration was generally well-received by healthcare providers and patients and was found practical to implement in various resource settings, contributing to improved patient outcomes. However, persistent barriers include fragmented health systems, lack of standardized multi-disease protocols, inadequate provider training, limited interoperable health information systems, and insufficient financing mechanisms. Notable limitations include the predominantly observational design of included studies and significant evidence gaps on long-term outcomes and cost-effectiveness across diverse health system contexts.

Implications Medical Affairs : These findings underscore the growing importance of multi-disease care pathways and will likely influence future chronic disease management guidelines toward integrated hypertension screening and management within TB and diabetes programs, expanding the addressable patient population for antihypertensives. From a product positioning perspective, antihypertensive agents with simplified dosing regimens, minimal drug-drug interactions with TB medications, and favorable safety profiles will be competitively advantaged as integrated care programs scale up globally. Medical Affairs teams should proactively engage KOLs at the intersection of infectious diseases, diabetology, and hypertension — particularly in high-TB-burden countries — to position their hypertension portfolio within evolving integrated chronic disease management frameworks.

The Impact of Risk Factors and Comorbidities on Heart Failure in the Aging Population.

Revue : International journal of molecular sciences | Tier : NA | IF estimé : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-11

Résultats principaux : This comprehensive narrative review examined the complex interplay between heart failure (HF) and coexisting conditions — particularly hypertension, diabetes mellitus, and obesity — in the aging population, analyzing how multimorbidity modifies disease pathophysiology, clinical presentation, therapeutic response, and prognosis. The review demonstrates that elderly HF patients with comorbid hypertension experience increased symptom burden, higher rates of recurrent hospitalizations, greater healthcare utilization, and substantially reduced quality of life compared to those without comorbidities. The simultaneous presence of multiple conditions limits the direct applicability of single-disease guideline-directed therapies to older patients, calling urgently for individualized, patient-centered management strategies incorporating multidisciplinary care models. Early identification of comorbidities, tailored pharmacological and non-pharmacological interventions, and longitudinal monitoring are highlighted as essential components of managing the dual burden of HF and multimorbidity. Limitations include the narrative design, heterogeneity in cited study populations, and absence of formal meta-analytic statistical synthesis.

Implications Medical Affairs : This review reinforces that modern hypertension guidelines for elderly patients must account for the multimorbid context, creating an opportunity for Medical Affairs to position antihypertensive agents with dual HF and blood-pressure benefits — such as sacubitril/valsartan or SGLT2 inhibitors — as anchor cardiometabolic therapies in aging populations. From a Medical Affairs standpoint, this publication reinforces the need for portfolio messaging extending beyond blood pressure control to emphasize cardioprotective outcomes in elderly patients with comorbid heart failure, diabetes, and obesity. KOL engagement should focus on geriatric cardiologists and HF specialists in Europe and North America to co-develop consensus-based approaches to integrated cardiometabolic therapy in aging patients.

Joint Associations of Multidimensional Socioeconomic Status and Healthy Lifestyle with Prevalent Hypertension: A Large Population-Based Study in Northwest China.

Revue : Nutrients | Tier : NA | IF estimé : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-09

Résultats principaux : This cross-sectional analysis included 80,218 adults from the Regional Ethnic Cohort Study in Northwest China (2017-2019, mean age 53.9 years) and examined the independent, interactive, and joint associations of multidimensional socioeconomic status (SES) and five healthy lifestyle behaviors with prevalent hypertension, using logistic regression with odds ratios (ORs) and 95% confidence intervals (CIs). Hypertension prevalence was significantly higher in the low-SES group than the high-SES group (45.1% vs. 28.8%), demonstrating a robust socioeconomic gradient in hypertension burden. Each additional healthy lifestyle behavior (non-smoking, moderate alcohol, physical activity, coarse-grain intake, healthy sleep) was associated with 16% lower odds of hypertension (OR: 0.84; 95% CI: 0.82-0.86). Within each SES stratum, participants with 0-1 healthy behaviors had the highest odds of hypertension compared to those with 4-5 healthy behaviors (ORs 1.58-1.62), but even among participants with 4-5 healthy behaviors, low SES remained independently associated with elevated hypertension odds (OR = 1.29, 95% CI: 1.17-1.42 versus high SES). The study is limited by its cross-sectional design, convenience sampling from one region of China, and reliance on self-reported lifestyle data with potential for recall bias.

Implications Medical Affairs : This large Chinese population study conclusively demonstrates that healthy lifestyle alone cannot fully offset the elevated hypertension risk associated with socioeconomic disadvantage, strengthening the rationale for pharmacological antihypertensive therapy as a necessary complement to behavioral interventions across all socioeconomic strata. For Medical Affairs, this evidence supports messaging positioning antihypertensives as essential additions — rather than alternatives — to lifestyle modification in real-world hypertension management, particularly in Asian markets where NCD burden is escalating rapidly. Engagement with public health KOLs in China and Southeast Asia, as well as health economists, can leverage this large-cohort evidence to advocate for integrated hypertension control strategies within national healthcare policies.

Social Determinants of Health/Health Equity and Hypertension Prevalence Among Mississippi Adults.

Revue : International journal of environmental research and public health | Tier : NA | IF estimé : NA

Date de publication : 2026-05-26

Résultats principaux : This cross-sectional study analyzed data from 3,867 respondents to the 2023 Mississippi Behavioral Risk Factor Surveillance System Social Determinants of Health and Health Equity (SDOH/HE) Module, using multivariable logistic regression to examine the association between a composite SDOH/HE measure and hypertension prevalence among Mississippi adults. The SDOH/HE summary measure was constructed from 10 items assessing employment/economic stability, housing security, food assistance, access to quality food and transportation, utilities security, social isolation, social/emotional support, life satisfaction, and mental well-being. Adults experiencing one SDOH/HE risk factor had 47% higher odds of hypertension (AOR = 1.47) compared to those with no SDOH/HE risk factors, while those experiencing four or more risk factors had 2.62-fold higher odds (AOR = 2.62), representing a dose-response-like relationship at the extremes. Associations between 2-3 SDOH/HE risk factors and hypertension did not reach statistical significance, suggesting a non-linear or threshold relationship rather than a simple linear gradient. Study limitations include the cross-sectional design, reliance on self-reported hypertension diagnosis, and inherent sampling biases of behavioral surveillance systems.

Implications Medical Affairs : This evidence from Mississippi — one of the US states with the highest hypertension prevalence and deepest health disparities — demonstrates that social determinants are as clinically important as biomedical risk factors in determining hypertension burden, making a case for guideline updates that formally integrate SDOH screening into hypertension management pathways. From a Medical Affairs perspective, these findings highlight an opportunity to develop and promote patient support programs addressing medication access, adherence, and health literacy in socioeconomically disadvantaged patient populations — differentiating the portfolio through equity-focused value propositions. Medical Affairs and market access teams should collaborate with public health organizations to generate real-world evidence linking SDOH-targeted interventions with blood pressure control outcomes, strengthening the value narrative for their antihypertensive agents in payer negotiations.

Prevalence, Risk Factors, and Preventive Strategies of Hypertension Among Young Adults in the United Arab Emirates.

Revue : International journal of environmental research and public health | Tier : NA | IF estimé : NA

Date de publication : 2026-05-25

Résultats principaux : This cross-sectional study recruited 1,606 participants aged ≥18 years from universities and community settings in the UAE via convenience sampling and administered a validated questionnaire covering sociodemographics, medical and family history, and lifestyle factors to assess hypertension prevalence, risk factors, and prevention strategies. A strikingly high hypertension prevalence of 61.8% was observed — nearly double previous national estimates — raising important questions about population-based versus clinic-based methodology differences. Male gender, age ≥30 years, and family history of hypertension were identified as significant independent risk factors, alongside smoking, alcohol use, sedentary lifestyle, and unhealthy dietary habits, while physical activity and dietary modification were protective. Despite the high prevalence, only 22.1% of participants had controlled blood pressure and only 17.8% adhered to prescribed antihypertensive medication, with 51.5% relying on herbal remedies — indicative of significant under-treatment and health literacy gaps. Limitations include convenience sampling from non-representative settings, self-reported blood pressure and medication data, cross-sectional design, and potentially inflated prevalence due to non-standardized measurement methodology.

Implications Medical Affairs : The critically low rates of blood pressure control (22.1%) and medication adherence (17.8%) in UAE young adults represent both a major public health concern and a significant market opportunity for pharmaceutical companies addressing the Gulf region's growing antihypertensive needs. Medical Affairs should support the development of awareness campaigns and structured adherence programs targeting young adults in the Gulf Cooperation Council, emphasizing the long-term cardiovascular consequences of uncontrolled hypertension in this age group. Engagement with preventive medicine specialists, cardiologists, and university health program directors in the UAE and GCC as KOLs can facilitate data-driven advocacy for systematic hypertension screening in occupational and university settings.

Revue : Wiadomosci lekarskie (Warsaw, Poland : 1960) | Tier : NA | IF estimé : NA

Date de publication : 2026

Résultats principaux : This prospective study at a Ukrainian medical center enrolled 158 STEMI patients divided by BMI into three groups: Group I (n=52, normal weight, mean age 60.83±11.94 years), Group II (n=51, overweight, mean age 62.04±8.55 years), and Group III (n=55, obesity grades I-III, mean age 60.96±11.31 years). Hypertension was the most prevalent risk factor across all BMI groups (88.46-94.55%), followed by dyslipidemia (50.00-76.36%) and smoking (69.23-69.09%). In normal-weight STEMI patients, the presence of dyslipidemia was associated with a 2.08-fold relative risk of acute left ventricular aneurysm (RR=2.08 [0.37;11.62], p=0.029), with concurrent smoking increasing this risk by an additional 25% (RR=1.25, p=0.049). Limitations include the small sample size, single-center design, retrospective complication classification, and wide confidence intervals on several risk estimates reducing statistical precision.

Implications Medical Affairs : With hypertension present in 88-95% of STEMI patients across all BMI groups in this Ukrainian cohort, this study reinforces the paramount importance of aggressive antihypertensive management as a primary prevention strategy for MI complications, supporting inclusion of hypertension control benchmarks in cardiac emergency care protocols. The 8-fold complication risk increase in smoking obese STEMI patients emphasizes the urgent need for comprehensive cardiometabolic risk management — including blood pressure control and smoking cessation — providing a compelling framework for positioning combination antihypertensive-lipid lowering therapy in high-risk patients. Medical Affairs teams should engage Eastern European cardiologists and preventive medicine specialists as KOLs to communicate multi-risk-factor approaches to MI prevention, particularly in regions with high smoking, obesity, and uncontrolled hypertension rates.

Thème 5 : Diuretics et Beta-bloquants (1 articles)

Primary aldosteronism.

Revue : Nature reviews. Disease primers | Tier : NA | IF estimé : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-25

Résultats principaux : This landmark review in Nature Reviews Disease Primers provides a comprehensive update on primary aldosteronism (PA), a condition caused by excessive autonomous aldosterone production from one or both adrenal glands and representing an important, underdiagnosed cause of hypertension with increased cardiovascular and renal morbidity. PA is primarily driven by somatic or germline mutations in aldosterone-driver genes and aberrant adrenal expression of G-protein-coupled receptors, leading to dysregulated aldosterone synthesis. The authors recommend simplified screening — measuring renin and aldosterone in all hypertensive patients — to maximize PA diagnosis rates, given that PA is estimated to affect up to 5-10% of hypertensive individuals but remains severely underdiagnosed in routine practice. For the majority of PA patients who have bilateral adrenal disease, medical therapy consists of dietary sodium restriction plus mineralocorticoid receptor antagonists (MRAs), with aldosterone synthase inhibitors positioned as near-future therapeutic additions; adrenalectomy is reserved for unilateral disease confirmed by adrenal vein sampling. Limitations include the complexity and variability in adrenal vein sampling success rates, limited data from low-resource settings, and the evolving evidence base for emerging biomarkers and functional imaging in PA diagnosis.

Implications Medical Affairs : This high-impact review in Nature Reviews Disease Primers will likely catalyze guideline updates toward broader renin-aldosterone screening in all hypertensive patients, substantially expanding the diagnosed PA population and creating a significantly larger market for mineralocorticoid receptor antagonists such as finerenone and spironolactone. The positioning of aldosterone synthase inhibitors as a near-future therapy class represents a critical strategic inflection point for Medical Affairs, requiring proactive KOL engagement campaigns with endocrinologists, nephrologists, and hypertension specialists to establish product positioning before market entry. Medical Affairs teams should initiate or support real-world evidence studies demonstrating the cardiovascular outcome benefits of early PA diagnosis and MRA therapy to strengthen the scientific narrative and support future guideline adoption of simplified screening protocols.

Association between hemoglobin A1c to HDL cholesterol ratio and hypertension in middle-aged and older Chinese adults: a cross-sectional study.

Revue : BMC cardiovascular disorders | Tier : NA | IF estimé : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-26

Résultats principaux : This cross-sectional study analyzed data from 11,797 middle-aged and older Chinese adults included in the 2011 and 2015 biomarker waves of the China Health and Retirement Longitudinal Study (CHARLS), examining the association between the hemoglobin A1c to HDL-cholesterol ratio (HHR) — a novel composite index integrating chronic glycemic exposure and lipid metabolism — and hypertension prevalence. In the fully adjusted multivariable logistic regression model, each 0.1-unit increase in HHR was associated with significantly higher odds of hypertension (OR = 1.02, 95% CI: 1.01-1.04), after adjusting for sociodemographic, lifestyle, and metabolic confounders. Participants in the highest HHR tertile had 38% higher odds of hypertension compared to those in the lowest tertile (OR = 1.38, 95% CI: 1.24-1.54), demonstrating a robust independent association. Restricted cubic spline analyses revealed a statistically significant nonlinear association between HHR and hypertension prevalence, suggesting an accelerating effect at higher HHR values consistent with a threshold phenomenon. Limitations include the cross-sectional design precluding causal inference, restriction to middle-aged and older Chinese adults limiting generalizability, and potential residual confounding from unmeasured dietary and behavioral variables.

Implications Medical Affairs : The identification of HHR as an independent composite predictor of hypertension integrating glycemic and lipid dimensions offers a potentially useful cardiometabolic risk stratification tool, which may inform future guideline discussions on multifactorial biomarker-based hypertension screening. From a Medical Affairs perspective, this finding supports the cardiometabolic nexus narrative — reinforcing the rationale for therapeutic agents that simultaneously address glycemia, lipids, and blood pressure — and can be used to advance integrated cardiometabolic risk management messaging for relevant portfolio products. Medical Affairs teams should collaborate with endocrinology and cardiometabolic KOLs in China to explore the clinical utility of HHR as a screening tool and to advance research on combined cardiovascular risk reduction strategies.

Revue : AIDS research and treatment | Tier : NA | IF estimé : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-24

Résultats principaux : This systematic review and meta-analysis included 26 studies (10 in developed countries) selected from 915 full-text articles retrieved from PubMed, Web of Science, Embase, Scopus, and Google Scholar, covering English-language research from 2013 to December 2023 on metabolic comorbidities in older people living with HIV (PLHIV). The pooled prevalence of hypertension among older PLHIV was 40.7% (95% CI: 30.2-51.2%), confirming hypertension as the most prevalent cardiometabolic comorbidity in this population, with substantial heterogeneity reflecting geographic and demographic variability. Pooled prevalences of other metabolic comorbidities were: high cholesterol 47.3% (95% CI: 36.3-58.4%), diabetes 13.5% (95% CI: 10.0-17.4%), and obesity 26.1% (95% CI: 20.3-32.4%), with all comorbidities more prevalent in North American studies. Most included studies were concentrated in developed countries, particularly North America, with limited representation from sub-Saharan Africa and Asia where a large proportion of PLHIV reside. Key limitations include significant between-study heterogeneity (I² values not reported in the abstract), the cross-sectional design of most included studies, and the restricted geographic representation of the evidence base.

Implications Medical Affairs : The high pooled prevalence of hypertension (40.7%) among older PLHIV underscores the critical need for systematic antihypertensive therapy integration into HIV chronic care models, creating a niche indication area and a Medical Affairs opportunity to partner with HIV treatment programs to optimize cardiometabolic co-management. Medical Affairs teams should develop targeted communication strategies for infectious disease physicians and HIV specialists who are increasingly responsible for managing cardiometabolic comorbidities in their aging HIV-positive patient populations. This evidence base supports advocacy for mandatory hypertension screening protocols within HIV treatment programs and could serve as a foundation for real-world evidence studies on the efficacy, safety, and drug-interaction profiles of antihypertensives in PLHIV.

Revue : International journal of molecular sciences | Tier : NA | IF estimé : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-13

Résultats principaux : This experimental study in C57BL/6N mice investigated the impact of suboptimal selenium (Se) intake on metabolic risk profiles in animals with pre-existing diet-induced obesity under three dietary conditions: standard diet with adequate Se, Western diet with adequate Se (WD-ASe), and Western diet with suboptimal Se (WD-SOSe). Implications Medical Affairs : While primarily a preclinical study, this evidence on sex-specific selenium-hypertension interactions adds to the scientific narrative around micronutrient modulation of cardiovascular risk, potentially informing future prevention research and supporting sex-disaggregated analyses in hypertension clinical trials. From a Medical Affairs perspective, these findings are most relevant to monitoring the emerging literature on nutritional determinants of cardiometabolic risk and may stimulate scientific discussion on personalized prevention approaches that account for sex differences in metabolic vulnerability. Serum Uric Acid as a Cardiovascular Risk Marker: Differential Effects of Ketogenic Diet and Intermittent Fasting in Postmenopausal Women.

Revue : Nutrients | Tier : NA | IF estimé : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-12

Résultats principaux : This prospective single-center real-world clinical study enrolled 43 postmenopausal women with essential hypertension and obesity at an Italian center and compared the effects of a very low-calorie ketogenic diet (VLCKD), intermittent fasting (IF), and standard dietary advice (free diet) on serum uric acid (UA) levels over 6 months, assessed using both conventional hyperuricemia thresholds (≥6.0 mg/dL) and the cardiovascular risk-oriented URRAH cut-off (≥4.7 mg/dL). At baseline, over half of participants exceeded the URRAH cardiovascular risk threshold, indicating elevated baseline cardiovascular vulnerability in this hypertensive postmenopausal cohort. Only VLCKD produced a statistically significant reduction in UA levels (-1.23 mg/dL, p<0.001), which remained independently significant after multivariate adjustment (β = -0.95 mg/dL, p=0.007); IF and standard dietary advice did not achieve significant UA reductions. URRAH-defined cardiovascular risk decreased significantly only in the VLCKD group (from 55.6% to 22.2%), with one-third of participants transitioning to a lower cardiovascular risk category. Limitations include the small sample size (n=43), single-center observational design without randomization, restriction to postmenopausal women with hypertension and obesity, and absence of long-term follow-up beyond 6 months.

Implications Medical Affairs : These findings suggest that VLCKD can significantly reduce uric acid-mediated cardiovascular risk in hypertensive postmenopausal women, supporting guideline consideration of intensive dietary interventions as complementary — though not substitutive — components of comprehensive hypertension management in this patient population. For Medical Affairs, the identification of uric acid as a modifiable cardiovascular risk marker in hypertensive postmenopausal women provides a scientific rationale for exploring and communicating the potential synergistic effects of pharmacological antihypertensive therapy combined with lifestyle optimization in women-specific hypertension narratives. Medical Affairs teams should engage cardiologists and nutritionists specializing in metabolic cardiovascular risk in postmenopausal women as KOLs to advance the scientific discussion on uric acid as a treatment target and VLCKD as an evidence-based dietary strategy in hypertension guidelines.

Revue : Biomedicines | Tier : NA | IF estimé : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-10

Résultats principaux : This structured narrative review of PubMed and Web of Science literature from January 2000 to December 2025 synthesized evidence on long-term cardiovascular risks associated with three common perinatal endocrine disorders: gestational diabetes mellitus (GDM), hypertensive disorders of pregnancy (HDP), and thyroid dysfunction including postpartum thyroiditis. GDM was associated with a 1.6- to 2-fold increased risk of future cardiovascular disease, HDP with a 1.8-fold increase, and subclinical hypothyroidism with a 2-fold increased risk — with these risks persisting for decades and remaining independent of traditional CVD risk factors including obesity and family history. Despite robust epidemiological evidence, postpartum cardiovascular screening uptake remains below 50%, care for postpartum women is fragmented, and pregnancy history is not yet incorporated into standard cardiovascular risk calculators. Limitations include the narrative review design, potential publication bias, heterogeneity in definitions of HDP and thyroid dysfunction across studies, and the predominance of data from developed country settings.

Implications Medical Affairs : This review makes a compelling scientific case for integrating pregnancy history — particularly HDP — into cardiovascular risk assessment tools and hypertension guidelines, potentially accelerating earlier pharmacological antihypertensive initiation in women with a history of gestational hypertension or preeclampsia. From a Medical Affairs perspective, this evidence supports development of a women's cardiovascular health narrative that positions antihypertensive agents within a life-course approach, targeting women post-pregnancy and in the perimenopausal period as a high-priority screening and treatment population. KOL engagement should target maternal-fetal medicine specialists, cardiologists specializing in women's health, and endocrinologists to advocate for integrated cardio-obstetrics programs that identify and manage elevated hypertension risk following complicated pregnancies.
