Standard modifiable cardiovascular risk factors and acute coronary syndrome free survival

Revue : Atherosclerosis | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-15

Resultats principaux : This retrospective registry analysis drew on 70,953 Caucasian patients with first-time acute coronary syndrome (ACS) enrolled between 2005 and 2021 in the ISACS-TC Registry (NCT04008173), excluding those with prior coronary heart disease, to examine the paradoxical relationship between standard modifiable cardiovascular risk factors (SMuRFs: hypertension, hypercholesterolemia, diabetes, smoking) and short-term mortality. The primary endpoint was 30-day all-cause mortality, with secondary endpoints including age at ACS onset and early in-hospital death within 24 hours, and inverse probability weighting was used to adjust for baseline imbalances. At least one SMuRF was present in 84% of patients, and an increasing SMuRF count was associated with progressively younger age at ACS onset in both women (67.1 vs 61.9 years) and men (62.8 vs 58.9 years, both p<0.001), while paradoxically, 30-day mortality was highest among SMuRF-less patients and lowest among those with multiple SMuRFs (women 16.3% vs 7.7%; men 11.5% vs 4.8%). Diabetes alone conferred excess mortality risk (RR 1.29-1.40), whereas isolated hypertension, hypercholesterolemia, or smoking were each associated with lower mortality risk, and SMuRF-less patients showed a higher rate of early arrhythmic-pattern deaths within 24 hours of admission (36.8% vs 31.7%). The authors attribute the apparent SMuRF paradox to index-event and dilution bias rather than a genuine protective effect of risk factors. Limitations include the retrospective, single-ethnicity (Caucasian) design and the inability to fully exclude residual confounding or undiagnosed risk factors in the SMuRF-less group.

Implications Medical Affairs : This study reinforces that the SMuRF paradox is a statistical artifact rather than evidence against primary prevention, supporting continued guideline emphasis on aggressive management of hypertension and other modifiable risk factors. For Medical Affairs, it strengthens messaging that antihypertensive therapy remains foundational to ACS risk reduction even though SMuRF-less patients present with higher acute mortality, an important nuance for field teams addressing physician skepticism about risk factor paradoxes. This is a useful reference for KOL discussions on risk stratification nuances and could inform future real-world evidence generation distinguishing causal risk reduction from selection bias in cardiovascular outcome studies.

Endothelial c-IAP2 loss amplifies P2X7 receptor-driven inflammation and worsens schistosomiasis-associated pulmonary hypertension

Revue : Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences of the United States of America | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-15

Resultats principaux : This preclinical mechanistic study used a Cdh5-Cre-ER;cIAP1;cIAP2 conditional knockout mouse model combined with a schistosomiasis-associated pulmonary hypertension (Sch-PH) model to investigate the role of endothelial inhibitor of apoptosis protein 2 (c-IAP2) and the purinergic P2X7 receptor (P2X7R) in disease pathogenesis. The primary endpoints were echocardiographic markers of pulmonary hypertension, including pulmonary acceleration time, PAT/pulmonary ejection time ratio, and right ventricular free wall thickness, assessed after Schistosoma egg challenge. Genetic ablation of endothelial c-IAP2 was sufficient to induce PH-like echocardiographic features even without infection, and pharmacological P2X7R inhibition reduced lung endothelial cell apoptosis and attenuated disease progression in the Sch-PH model. A notable subgroup finding was a sex-linked effect, with female mice showing greater susceptibility to c-IAP2-driven PH-like pathology, consistent with the known female predominance of pulmonary arterial hypertension in humans. Limitations include the exclusively murine, preclinical nature of the data, the focus on a parasitic infection-driven PH subtype not directly generalizable to essential or systemic arterial hypertension, and the absence of human validation cohorts.

Implications Medical Affairs : This basic-science finding is not directly relevant to systemic arterial hypertension management but adds mechanistic insight into endothelial apoptosis pathways relevant to pulmonary vascular disease, a distinct but occasionally comorbid condition in cardiovascular drug development. From a Medical Affairs perspective, P2X7R is an emerging druggable target that could inform future pipeline scouting in pulmonary hypertension rather than core antihypertensive positioning. Monitoring this research stream may be useful for teams with assets in pulmonary arterial hypertension, but it has limited near-term KOL relevance for essential hypertension franchises.

Sedentary Behavior, Physical Activity, and Hypertension: Genetic Liability and the Role of Metabolic Factors

Revue : Clinical Cardiology | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06

Resultats principaux : This two-sample Mendelian randomization study used genome-wide significant, LD-independent SNPs as genetic instruments for domain-specific sedentary behaviors (leisure screen time, sedentary behavior at work) and leisure-time physical activity to assess causal associations with hypertension risk, defined using the FinnGen R9 I9_HYPTENSESS endpoint. Two-step Mendelian randomization mediation analysis was used to explore whether body mass index (BMI) and type 2 diabetes (T2D) mediate any observed associations, with pleiotropy-robust estimators and outlier-detection procedures applied for methodological rigor. Genetically proxied leisure screen time was significantly associated with increased hypertension risk (inverse-variance-weighted OR 1.181, 95% CI 1.076-1.296), and mediation analysis suggested BMI and T2D accounted for an estimated 54.7% and 14.7% of this association, respectively. No robust associations were found for moderate-to-vigorous physical activity or workplace sedentary behavior, though the moderate-to-vigorous physical activity estimate trended protective; the authors caution these null results reflect limited instrument informativeness rather than a definitive absence of effect. Limitations include reliance on a single hypertension endpoint definition from FinnGen, the inherent assumptions of Mendelian randomization regarding instrument validity, and the inability to capture short-term behavioral interventions through germline genetic instruments.

Implications Medical Affairs : This Mendelian randomization study lends genetic-causal support to the long-standing epidemiological link between sedentary leisure behavior and hypertension, partially mediated through obesity and diabetes, strengthening guideline rationale for lifestyle counseling alongside pharmacotherapy. For Medical Affairs, this supports positioning antihypertensive therapies as complementary to, rather than a substitute for, lifestyle modification messaging directed at primary care KOLs. It also flags leisure screen time specifically, versus general sedentary behavior, as an actionable target for future patient education materials and digital health partnership discussions.

The role of albumin-globulin ratio in peripheral arterial disease among hypertensive adults: evidence from a large-scale multicenter study

Revue : Frontiers in Endocrinology | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-05-28

Resultats principaux : This retrospective, multicenter cross-sectional study enrolled 28,684 participants from Wuyuan County, Jiangxi Province and Yuexi County, Anhui Province, China between March and August 2018, of whom 24,808 hypertensive adults were analyzed to evaluate the association between the albumin-globulin ratio (AGR) and peripheral arterial disease (PAD). A generalized additive model was used to characterize the AGR-PAD relationship, both as a continuous variable and stratified by AGR quartiles, with multivariable adjustment across three progressively adjusted models. An inverse, dose-dependent association was found, with the fully adjusted model showing 27% (OR 0.73, 95% CI 0.58-0.92, p=0.008) and 35% (OR 0.65, 95% CI 0.51-0.84, p<0.001) lower odds of PAD in the third and fourth AGR quartiles, respectively, compared with the lowest quartile. Subgroup analysis demonstrated that the inverse AGR-PAD association was more pronounced among smokers and individuals with a prior stroke history. Limitations include the cross-sectional design precluding causal inference, geographic restriction to two Chinese counties limiting generalizability, and absence of longitudinal follow-up to assess AGR predictive value over time.

Implications Medical Affairs : AGR is an inexpensive, routinely available biomarker that could enhance vascular risk stratification in hypertensive populations, supporting its consideration in future risk-assessment algorithms or clinical pathway discussions. For Medical Affairs, this finding may be useful for KOL engagement around comorbid PAD screening in hypertensive patients, particularly smokers and stroke survivors, a population relevant to combination cardiovascular risk management strategies. Further prospective validation, ideally in non-Chinese cohorts, would be needed before this biomarker could support label or guideline-level positioning.

Revue : Frontiers in Endocrinology | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-05-29

Resultats principaux : This hybrid study combined a global burden-of-disease analysis (1990-2021) of age-standardized atrial fibrillation/flutter (AF/AFL) prevalence, incidence, mortality, and disability-adjusted life years with a local cohort study using multivariable logistic regression and restricted cubic splines to identify modifiable risk factors among individuals aged 15 to 39 years. Implications Medical Affairs : This analysis reinforces hypertension as the dominant modifiable driver of early-onset atrial fibrillation, a clinically significant finding given the rising cardiovascular burden in adults under 40. For Medical Affairs, it supports messaging around early and aggressive blood pressure control as a strategy to mitigate long-term arrhythmia risk, particularly relevant for KOL discussions bridging hypertension and electrophysiology specialties. Which aldosterone antagonist? Choosing between spironolactone and the novel drug finerenone

Revue : Nederlands tijdschrift voor geneeskunde | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-11

Resultats principaux : This narrative review, published in Dutch with an English abstract, synthesizes clinical trial evidence to guide selection between classic steroidal mineralocorticoid receptor antagonists (spironolactone, eplerenone) and the novel nonsteroidal agent finerenone in patients with chronic kidney disease (CKD), type 2 diabetes, and cardiovascular-kidney-metabolic syndrome. The review's primary focus areas were kidney and cardiovascular outcome data, with key trials discussed including FIDELIO-DKD and FIGARO-DKD for finerenone in CKD/T2DM, CONFIDENCE for finerenone-empagliflozin combination effects on proteinuria, and FINEARTS-HF versus TOPCAT for finerenone and spironolactone respectively in heart failure with preserved ejection fraction. Finerenone demonstrated consistent reductions in kidney and cardiovascular events across FIDELIO-DKD and FIGARO-DKD, and the CONFIDENCE trial showed an additive proteinuria-lowering effect when combined with an SGLT2 inhibitor; in HFpEF, FINEARTS-HF showed cardiovascular benefit with finerenone while the older TOPCAT trial of spironolactone produced mixed, region-dependent results. The authors note that classic steroidal mineralocorticoid receptor antagonists remain valuable specifically for treatment-resistant hypertension, with patient-specific factors such as blood pressure level and hyperkalemia risk guiding the choice between agents. As a narrative review rather than primary research, this article does not generate new outcome data and notes that finerenone uptake remains limited in the Netherlands despite regulatory approval, an access and adoption rather than efficacy limitation.

Implications Medical Affairs : This review is directly relevant to Medical Affairs strategy for nonsteroidal mineralocorticoid receptor antagonist franchises, as it frames finerenone's primary value proposition around CKD and T2DM and HFpEF rather than resistant hypertension, where classic steroidal MRAs retain a niche role. The explicit note on limited finerenone uptake despite approval signals an actionable access and education gap that could inform regional KOL engagement and formulary advocacy in the Netherlands and similar markets. This is a useful reference document for field teams needing a concise comparative positioning narrative between finerenone and spironolactone across overlapping cardio-renal indications.

Model-informed dose optimization of carvedilol and nebivolol in cirrhotic patients: a pilot randomized clinical study

Revue : Scientific Reports | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-16

Resultats principaux : This pilot study combined physiologically based pharmacokinetic (PBPK) modeling with a prospective, open-label, parallel-group randomized clinical trial enrolling 44 cirrhotic patients (Child-Pugh class A or B) with concurrent arterial and portal hypertension, to optimize dosing of the beta-blockers carvedilol and nebivolol. PBPK models were first validated using healthy-volunteer pharmacokinetic data and then extrapolated to simulate exposure across cirrhosis severity stages, after which model-guided doses were administered to patients with monthly monitoring over 3 months for adverse events, blood pressure, heart rate, portal hemodynamics by Doppler ultrasound, and laboratory safety parameters. The PBPK model predicted substantial dose reductions relative to standard dosing: carvedilol 25 mg once daily was reduced to 11.26 mg, 5.52 mg, and 1.99 mg in mild, moderate, and severe cirrhosis respectively, with analogous reductions for nebivolol (10 mg to 4.98 mg, 2.98 mg, and 1.23 mg). Both PBPK-guided regimens were well tolerated and effectively reduced blood pressure and heart rate without significant hepatic or renal toxicity, and portal hemodynamics and platelet counts improved in both arms, with carvedilol producing a greater improvement in portal hemodynamics, albeit with a higher, though still mild, adverse event rate than nebivolol. Limitations include the small sample size (n=44), the short 3-month follow-up, the pilot and exploratory design, and persistent pharmacodynamic differences between Child-Pugh A and B patients despite PBPK-guided pharmacokinetic correction.

Implications Medical Affairs : This study provides clinically actionable, model-informed dosing guidance for two widely used beta-blockers in the difficult-to-treat population of cirrhotic patients with portal and arterial hypertension, an area with limited prior dosing evidence. For Medical Affairs, this supports development of dosing guidance materials or PBPK-based educational tools for hepatology and cardiology KOLs managing this comorbid population, and highlights carvedilol's incremental portal hemodynamic benefit as a possible differentiator. Larger, longer-term confirmatory trials would be a logical next step to support any formal label or guideline updates regarding beta-blocker dosing in hepatic impairment.

Nebivolol Protects the Thoracic and Abdominal Aorta and Their Perivascular Adipose Tissue From the Differential Detrimental Effects of Obesity

Revue : Fundamental & Clinical Pharmacology | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07

Resultats principaux : This preclinical study used male Wistar Hannover rats fed a hypercaloric diet for 14 weeks, with nebivolol (10 mg/kg/day) administered by gavage during the final 4 weeks, to test whether obesity differentially affects thoracic versus abdominal aortic and perivascular adipose tissue (PVAT) function, and whether nebivolol could reverse these changes. Vascular reactivity was assessed via contractility studies measuring superoxide generation, NADPH oxidase activity, and nitric oxide bioavailability in thoracic and abdominal aortic segments and their associated PVAT. Obesity increased superoxide generation in thoracic PVAT and induced vascular hypercontractility in the thoracic aorta via NADPH oxidase-derived superoxide and reduced nitric oxide bioavailability, while in the abdominal aorta, obesity instead induced an endothelium-dependent pro-contractile PVAT phenotype. Nebivolol reversed both obesity-induced abnormalities across both vascular territories, demonstrating an antioxidant-mediated vasoprotective mechanism distinct from its conventional beta-blocking action. As an exclusively rodent preclinical study, the findings require validation in human vasculature and clinical outcome studies before translation, and the short post-obesity treatment window of 4 of the 14 weeks limits direct clinical dose-response inference.

Implications Medical Affairs : This preclinical mechanistic study supports a differentiated vasoprotective, antioxidant-based rationale for nebivolol beyond simple heart-rate and blood-pressure lowering, which could inform competitive positioning against other beta-blockers in obese hypertensive patients. For Medical Affairs, this is supportive mechanistic evidence for scientific platform development and KOL discussions on nebivolol's vascular pharmacology in metabolic syndrome populations, pending human confirmation. It also flags PVAT dysfunction as a potential future biomarker or endpoint of interest for translational research collaborations.

Resultats principaux : This methodological study analyzed a public dataset of 1,879 individuals to test whether integrating natural language processing (NLP) of unstructured clinical text with traditional structured variables (BMI, HbA1c, blood pressure) improves type 2 diabetes risk prediction, using a BERT-based NLP pipeline combined with logistic regression, and validating generalizability across random forest, XGBoost, and neural network classifiers. Implications Medical Affairs : This study confirms hypertension as a robust, independent predictor of type 2 diabetes risk even within advanced multimodal prediction frameworks, reinforcing the clinical rationale for integrated cardiometabolic risk screening rather than siloed disease management. Date de publication : 2026-05-28

Resultats principaux : This systematic review and meta-analysis, registered with PROSPERO (CRD420251270952), searched PubMed, Embase, and Web of Science to identify prospective and retrospective cohort studies examining the association between long-term traffic noise exposure and incident hypertension in adults, ultimately including 11 cohort studies comprising 1,353,481 participants, with study quality assessed via the Newcastle-Ottawa Scale. Implications Medical Affairs : This meta-analysis provides robust, large-scale evidence that environmental noise exposure is an independent and modifiable risk factor for hypertension, supporting its inclusion in updated public health and urban planning guidelines alongside established risk factors. For Medical Affairs, this strengthens the evidence base for environmental risk factor messaging in hypertension prevention campaigns and KOL discussions on non-pharmacological contributors to blood pressure control.
