Association between chronotypes and comorbidities in obstructive sleep apnea: The age effect.

Revue : Pulmonology | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-05-29

Resultats principaux : This cross-sectional study enrolled 671 adults (median age 56 years, 60.2% male) from three outpatient sleep clinics in Greece who underwent polysomnography or home sleep testing and completed the Morningness-Eveningness Questionnaire (MEQ) to assess chronotype. The study's primary objective was to evaluate associations between chronotype and cardiometabolic comorbidities in newly diagnosed obstructive sleep apnea (OSA), with pre-specified stratification by age group. Morning chronotype was significantly associated with higher prevalence of arterial hypertension, dyslipidemia, diabetes mellitus, and cardiovascular disease compared with the evening chronotype (all p less than 0.05), particularly in participants younger than 60 years. Subgroup analysis revealed that these cardiometabolic associations were substantially attenuated in adults aged 60 years or older, suggesting that chronotype acts as an age-modulated clinical marker rather than a universal predictor. In contrast, depression was more frequent among evening chronotype individuals with no significant age-related moderation. A major limitation is the cross-sectional design, which precludes causal inference, combined with reliance on self-reported comorbidities and pharmacological treatment history rather than objective clinical adjudication.

Implications Medical Affairs : Chronotype may serve as a low-cost adjunctive marker for cardiometabolic risk stratification in OSA patients, which could support Medical Affairs teams in positioning antihypertensive agents within sleep medicine clinical pathways and patient profiles. This study adds evidence supporting the integration of circadian phenotyping into cardiovascular risk assessment protocols, potentially broadening the relevant patient population for existing antihypertensive therapies and informing KOL discussions at the intersection of sleep medicine and hypertension. A prospective study co-designed with sleep medicine KOLs to validate chronotype as a treatment decision aid in hypertensive OSA patients would represent a strategically valuable research investment for Medical Affairs.

Right Ventricular End-Ejection Pressure Predicts Mortality in Patients With Pulmonary Hypertension Associated With Heart Failure With Preserved Ejection Fraction.

Revue : Comprehensive Physiology | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06

Resultats principaux : This retrospective study analyzed right ventricular pressure (RVP) waveforms in 80 patients with pulmonary hypertension associated with heart failure with preserved ejection fraction (PH-HFpEF) treated at Yale School of Medicine between 2019 and 2025, with a mean age of 67 years, 65% female, and 80% with combined post- and pre-capillary pulmonary hypertension. The primary objective was to identify hemodynamic predictors of all-cause mortality beyond conventional parameters using LASSO-penalized logistic regression and Cox proportional hazards models. Over a median follow-up of 985 days, 14 patients (17.5%) died; right ventricular end-ejection pressure (RVEEP), a late-systolic measure reflecting RV pressure at pulmonic valve closure, was the only variable consistently retained across cross-validated penalized regression models, achieving a mean AUC of 0.77 for mortality discrimination. In a non-penalized Cox model, higher RVEEP was independently associated with all-cause mortality (hazard ratio 1.82, 95% CI 1.11 to 2.99; p = 0.018), outperforming traditional indices including NT-proBNP alone. A major limitation is the small single-center retrospective design (n = 80), which severely limits statistical power and generalizability, and the observational nature precludes causal conclusions about hemodynamic interventions.

Implications Medical Affairs : RVEEP as a novel prognostic marker in PH-HFpEF could inform patient stratification strategies for clinical trials evaluating pulmonary vasodilators and novel agents targeting right ventricular function, with direct relevance for Medical Affairs teams working on products in the pulmonary hypertension and heart failure space. This study reinforces the scientific rationale for moving beyond standard hemodynamic parameters in PH-HFpEF, and companies could support validation studies incorporating RVP waveform analysis as exploratory endpoints in ongoing or planned trials. KOL engagement with expert hemodynamic centers to prospectively validate RVEEP in larger, multicenter cohorts would position the company as a scientific leader in refining risk stratification for this difficult-to-treat population.

A Randomized Trial to Investigate the Effects of Low-Dose Colchicine in Patients with Hypertension: Rationale and Design of the COlchicine and HypERtENsion Trial (COHERENT).

Revue : Journal of Clinical Hypertension | Tier : Tier 3 | IF estime : ~3-5

Date de publication : 2026-06

Resultats principaux : The COHERENT trial is an investigator-initiated, single-center, prospective, double-blind, placebo-controlled randomized trial from Copenhagen University Hospital designed to assess whether colchicine 0.5 mg daily added to standard antihypertensive therapy reduces arterial stiffness and cardiovascular biomarkers in patients with hypertension but without established cardiovascular disease. A total of 150 participants were randomized 1:1 (mean age 64.5 years, 35% female, 55% current or former smokers), with mean systolic BP 140 mmHg and median high-sensitivity CRP 0.97 mg/L, treated for 6 months. The primary endpoint is change in carotid-femoral pulse wave velocity (PWV) from baseline as a surrogate of arterial stiffness; secondary endpoints include blood pressure, echocardiographic measurements including left ventricular mass, and cardiac and inflammatory biomarkers. Participants were identified through nationwide health registries, lending the study ecological validity, and all vascular and echocardiographic assessments are performed at baseline and 6 months in standardized fashion. Key limitations include the single-center design, modest sample size (n = 150), the surrogate nature of PWV as the primary endpoint, and potential limited generalizability given the Scandinavian population.

Implications Medical Affairs : COHERENT is the first dedicated randomized trial evaluating anti-inflammatory therapy in uncomplicated hypertension, providing direct scientific rationale for companies with colchicine or anti-inflammatory pipeline assets to monitor closely and potentially partner with the investigators. A positive result demonstrating reduction in arterial stiffness and inflammatory biomarkers could trigger guideline discussions about adding inflammation-targeting treatments to standard antihypertensive protocols, fundamentally reshaping the treatment paradigm. Medical Affairs teams should engage with the COHERENT principal investigators as emerging KOLs at the intersection of inflammation and hypertension, and consider designing complementary mechanistic substudies or extension studies with company-sponsored infrastructure.

Subsequent Hypertension's Mediation of the Association Between Sleep Duration Trajectories and New-Onset Cardiovascular Disease: Population-Based Cohort Study.

Revue : JMIR Aging | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-05-28

Resultats principaux : This population-based cohort study analyzed 5,603 participants from the China Health and Retirement Longitudinal Study (CHARLS) to evaluate the relationship between sleep duration trajectories and incident cardiovascular disease (CVD), with hypertension examined as a mediator. Group-based trajectory modeling identified four distinct sleep patterns across time: steady high, inverted U-shaped, steady low, and U-shaped. In fully adjusted Cox proportional hazards models, an inverted U-shaped total sleep trajectory was associated with a 34% higher risk of incident hypertension (HR = 1.34, 95% CI 1.07 to 1.69; p = 0.01) compared to the steady high group. More strikingly, the inverted U-shaped nocturnal sleep trajectory was associated with more than double the stroke risk (HR = 2.10, 95% CI 1.08 to 4.05; p = 0.03). Formal mediation analysis confirmed that hypertension was a significant partial mediator of the relationship between sleep trajectory instability and stroke risk, with findings robust across a range of sensitivity analyses. Limitations include reliance on self-reported sleep duration, a Chinese older adult sample potentially limiting generalizability, and residual confounding inherent to observational design.

Implications Medical Affairs : This study strengthens the mechanistic pathway linking sleep instability to hypertension-mediated stroke risk, reinforcing the scientific rationale for proactive blood pressure monitoring and early antihypertensive therapy in patients with documented sleep disruption. For Medical Affairs teams, these data provide a scientifically grounded argument for expanding the target patient population for antihypertensive therapy to include individuals with objectively identified unstable sleep patterns, a point relevant for payer engagement and guideline development submissions. Collaborative research with sleep medicine experts integrating wearable device data could be proposed to KOLs to prospectively validate the hypertension-mediated stroke risk pathway and generate compelling real-world evidence.

Early-life determinants of cardiometabolic outcomes and accelerated biological ageing in Colombia.

Revue : Aging (Albany NY) | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-05-26

Resultats principaux : This study used data from 3,385 adults aged 60 years or older from the nationally representative SABE-Colombia Health, Well-Being, and Ageing Study to examine associations between five adverse childhood experiences (ACEs) occurring before age 15 and cardiometabolic outcomes (CVD, diabetes, hypertension, obesity) and biological ageing estimated by the Klemera-Doubal Method (KDMAge). Among women, emotional abuse (OR = 1.68), domestic violence (OR = 1.55), scarcity of food (OR = 1.44), and poor health status (OR = 1.66) were each independently associated with significantly elevated odds of CVD. Dose-response associations were identified between cumulative ACEs and CVD in women and hypertension in men. Limitations include the retrospective self-reported nature of ACE assessment with its attendant recall bias, the cross-sectional measurement of biological ageing outcomes, and potential survivorship bias in this older adult sample.

Implications Medical Affairs : The association between early-life adversity and hypertension risk in later life, with dose-response evidence, supports a life-course epidemiological approach to hypertension prevention that Medical Affairs teams could use to strengthen the public health and societal burden narrative for antihypertensive products in Latin American markets. These findings highlight the potential value of upstream screening strategies for hypertension risk in individuals with a documented history of childhood adversity, particularly in conflict-affected populations, which could be incorporated into KOL discussions on early intervention and precision prevention programs. Medical Affairs teams could leverage these data to engage public health stakeholders and payers about the long-term cost-effectiveness of comprehensive antihypertensive treatment in socioeconomically disadvantaged populations in emerging markets.

Impact of gestational diabetes mellitus and hypertensive disorders of pregnancy on adverse pregnancy outcomes: a retrospective cohort study.

Revue : Frontiers in Endocrinology | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-05-13

Resultats principaux : This retrospective cohort study enrolled 240 women who delivered at Dongyang Maternal and Child Health Hospital in China from 2020 to 2024, categorized into four groups based on the presence or absence of gestational diabetes mellitus (GDM) and hypertensive disorders of pregnancy (HDP). Hypertensive disorders of pregnancy were independently associated with a substantially increased risk of a composite of adverse pregnancy outcomes, including preterm birth, low Apgar score, NICU admission, and fetal growth abnormalities (aOR = 3.30, 95% CI 1.41 to 7.70; p = 0.006). Co-existing GDM and HDP demonstrated a dramatically elevated composite risk (aOR = 9.25, 95% CI 2.63 to 32.45; p less than 0.001). Additive interaction analysis confirmed significant positive synergy between GDM and HDP, with an attributable proportion of 0.61, indicating that over 60% of the excess risk in the comorbid group arose from their combined effect rather than additive individual contributions. Restricted cubic spline analysis revealed a nonlinear positive dose-response relationship between systolic blood pressure and composite adverse outcomes, with risk increasing sharply beyond 135 mmHg and accelerating above 160 mmHg, highlighting the clinical importance of tight BP thresholds. Key limitations include the small sample size (n = 240), single-center retrospective design, and limited statistical power for subgroup analyses.

Implications Medical Affairs : The synergistic interaction between GDM and HDP on adverse pregnancy outcomes underscores the critical importance of tight blood pressure control in pregnant women with concurrent metabolic conditions, a niche area where antihypertensive product positioning in maternal health settings could be reinforced with robust mechanistic evidence. The nonlinear SBP threshold data at 135 mmHg provide actionable scientific support for discussions with guideline committees regarding initiation thresholds for antihypertensive treatment in pregnancy, a topic of ongoing debate across major cardiovascular and obstetric societies. KOL engagement in maternal-fetal medicine and obstetric hypertension should be prioritized to leverage this evidence for guideline influence and to develop evidence packages for use by Medical Affairs in obstetric settings.

Huatuo Zaizao Pills improve myocardial ischemia by inhibiting extracellular calcium influx.

Revue : Chinese Journal of Natural Medicines | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06

Resultats principaux : This study combined a double-blind, multicenter, randomized, placebo-controlled clinical trial assessing the efficacy of Huatuo Zaizao Pills (HTZZ), a traditional Chinese medicine formulation, in patients with angina pectoris, with preclinical mechanistic studies in pituitrin-induced acute myocardial ischemia mice and spontaneously hypertensive rats (SHRs). In the clinical arm, HTZZ significantly reduced both the frequency and duration of angina attacks compared to placebo, demonstrating clinically relevant symptom control in angina pectoris patients. In SHRs, HTZZ enhanced vascular elasticity and diastolic function, validating its cardiovascular activity in a hypertensive animal model. Mechanistic investigations using patch clamp electrophysiology, calcium imaging, and tritiated ryanodine binding assays demonstrated that HTZZ induces vasodilation by inhibiting extracellular calcium influx through dual suppression of L-type calcium channels (LTCCs) and ryanodine receptors (RyRs), with long-term administration downregulating LTCC expression at both protein and mRNA levels. Significant limitations include the incomplete reporting of clinical trial parameters in the abstract (sample size, primary endpoint definition, follow-up duration, statistical results), and the uncertain generalizability of traditional Chinese medicine findings to non-Asian patient populations.

Implications Medical Affairs : The dual pharmacological mechanism of HTZZ targeting both LTCCs and RyRs in hypertensive animal models provides a pharmacologically relevant proof of concept for combined calcium channel modulation in coronary vasospasm, which may inform R&D discussions for companies developing novel calcium channel-targeting agents beyond conventional amlodipine-class CCBs. From a Medical Affairs perspective, this study highlights the growing body of evidence for plant-derived calcium channel modulators in hypertension and ischemic disease, which may be relevant for positioning discussions in Asian markets where complementary and traditional medicine integration is clinically common. KOL engagement with integrative cardiology and cardiovascular pharmacology specialists in China and East Asia could identify opportunities for mechanistic validation studies that bridge traditional medicine findings with Western clinical evidence standards.

The endemic nature of chronic kidney disease.

Revue : Deutsche Medizinische Wochenschrift | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-05-29

Resultats principaux : This narrative review synthesizes the global epidemiology of chronic kidney disease (CKD), which caused approximately 1.5 million deaths in 2023 with rising age-standardized mortality trends prompting the WHO and UN to issue a 2025 call for strengthened prevention, early detection, and improved care. Metabolic risk factors, especially hypertension, diabetes, and obesity, are identified as the primary drivers of CKD burden in adults worldwide, while environmental and occupational exposures play an additional role in low-resource settings and contribute to distinct CKD syndromes. Congenital anomalies, hereditary nephropathies, and early-life kidney injury are highlighted as the dominant risk determinants in children, creating a pediatric CKD burden distinct from adult disease. CKD of unknown etiology (CKDu), prevalent in agricultural regions of low-income countries mainly affecting younger adults without classical risk factors, is discussed with heat stress, dehydration, and environmental toxins proposed as potential causal contributors. Secondary endpoint findings relevant to hypertension management include the dose-dependent relationship between blood pressure elevation and CKD progression, reinforcing the nephroprotective rationale for tight BP control in at-risk populations. A limitation is the narrative review design, the language barrier (German), which limits accessibility for the international medical community, and the absence of systematic literature search methodology.

Implications Medical Affairs : The identification of hypertension as the primary modifiable driver of CKD burden globally provides a strong scientific rationale for Medical Affairs teams to position antihypertensive products with established nephroprotective properties at the core of integrated hypertension-CKD management pathways, particularly in nephrology-cardiology junction settings. This review reinforces the need for integrated cardiorenal management approaches and may be cited in discussions with guideline committees considering updates to hypertension guidelines that emphasize nephroprotective antihypertensive strategies and lower BP targets in CKD. Engagement with nephrology KOLs on the hypertension-CKD nexus represents a strategic opportunity to differentiate antihypertensive products by demonstrating renal outcome benefits, especially as the WHO/UN 2025 CKD call-to-action gains policy traction globally.

Revue : BMC Nephrology | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-05-29

Resultats principaux : This real-world evaluation reports first-year outcomes from a personalised, multidisciplinary cardiorenal metabolic (CRM) Hub embedded in primary care in Harrow, North West London, serving a large ethnically diverse population, with 2,641 patients reviewed and 2,300 included in paired pre-post analysis from November 2024 to September 2025. Implications Medical Affairs : This real-world CRM hub model demonstrates that structured, multidisciplinary primary care interventions can achieve clinically meaningful BP reductions in a large, diverse urban population, providing compelling real-world evidence that Medical Affairs teams could leverage in value-based care partnerships with integrated care systems and NHS commissioners. Revue : Scientific Reports | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-05-29

Resultats principaux : This large retrospective cohort study analyzed national health insurance claims data from 1,029,500 Korean adults who underwent lumbar spine surgery between 2009 and 2023, with failed back surgery syndrome (FBSS) defined using two hierarchical mutually exclusive criteria: reoperation or ICD-10 M96.1 (post-laminectomy syndrome) diagnosis. Date de publication : 2026-05-29

Resultats principaux : This large cohort study from the St. Jude Lifetime cohort examined associations between lifestyle behaviors and a comprehensive range of chronic health conditions in childhood cancer survivors, estimating lifestyle-specific population attributable fractions (PAFs) for each condition and comparing them to PAFs for radiotherapy and chemotherapy exposures. Unhealthy lifestyle was significantly associated with higher risks for hypertension, dyslipidemia, diabetes, heart attack, heart failure, valvular disease, joint replacement, anxiety, depression, and impaired physical and mental quality of life in childhood cancer survivors. Date de publication : 2026-05-28

Implications Medical Affairs : The finding that a favorable composite lifestyle profile reduces stroke risk by 30% in treated hypertensive patients, with one-third of this effect mediated through improved BP control and time-in-target-range, provides strong scientific backing for positioning antihypertensive pharmacotherapy as part of integrated lifestyle and pharmacological risk reduction programs. Prevalence and Risk Factors of Non-communicable Diseases in an Adult Tribal Population of Kanyakumari District, India: A Cross-Sectional Study.

Revue : Cureus | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-04-26

Resultats principaux : This community-based cross-sectional study enrolled 420 adults (mean age 44.92 years, 53.6% female) from the tribal population of Pechiparai village in Kanyakumari district, Tamil Nadu, India, using simple random sampling and interviewer-administered questionnaires. The total prevalence of hypertension was 51.4% (n = 216), including 24.3% newly detected cases classified by JNC 7 criteria, indicating that nearly half of all hypertensive cases were undiagnosed at the time of the study. Among hypertensive participants, 51% were current smokers (predominantly beedi users), 57% consumed alcohol, and 75% reported extra dietary salt intake, highlighting the dense clustering of lifestyle risk factors in this population. A strong association was identified between extra salt intake and hypertension (chi-square = 61.1, p less than 0.001), and a significant association was found with family history of NCDs (chi-square = 21.52, p less than 0.001). Diabetes mellitus prevalence was 16.7%, and additional NCD-related comorbidities including hearing problems (61%) and visual impairment (55%) were reported among hypertensive participants. Limitations include the small single-village sample (n = 420), cross-sectional design, self-reported behavioral data subject to recall and social desirability bias, and potential non-representativeness of the broader Indian tribal population.

Implications Medical Affairs : A hypertension prevalence of 51.4% with nearly 24% undiagnosed cases in an underserved Indian tribal population underscores a critical and actionable treatment gap, representing a significant unmet medical need and potential market expansion opportunity for accessible antihypertensive therapies in low-resource Indian healthcare settings. These data support Medical Affairs advocacy for community-based hypertension screening programs in marginalized tribal communities, which could strengthen relationships with public health agencies, NGOs, and government health programs such as India's national NCD management initiatives. KOL engagement with community medicine specialists and government health agencies in South India on NCD burden in tribal populations could position the company as a committed and credible partner in addressing health disparities, enhancing both corporate reputation and access to emerging market healthcare ecosystems.
