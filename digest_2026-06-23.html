A Thematic Analysis of Key Informant Interviews on the Implementation Experience of the HEARTS Hypertension Program in Nigeria.

Revue : Global heart | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-19

Resultats principaux : This qualitative study employed structured thematic analysis of key informant interviews with primary care experts across three Nigerian states (Abuja, Kano, Ogun) to assess implementation of the Nigeria Hypertension Control Initiative (NHCI), launched in 2020 under the WHO HEARTS technical package for cardiovascular disease prevention. The study explored four programmatic domains: service delivery, medication procurement and availability, task shifting, and broader program dimensions. Findings indicate notable improvements in medication access and standardization of hypertension treatment protocols, with high provider satisfaction for the HEARTS-based protocol across all sites. However, significant operational barriers persist, including incomplete staff training, user fees limiting patient access, administrative burdens, transportation costs, and frequent medication stockouts at the facility level. Task shifting to lower-cadre health workers was well-accepted and reduced pressure on senior staff, though it underscored the critical need for ongoing competency-based training programs.

Implications Medical Affairs : This real-world implementation evidence confirms that the HEARTS framework can be operationalized in resource-limited sub-Saharan African settings, offering a validated policy platform for product inclusion and treatment algorithm alignment. From a Medical Affairs perspective, the identified gaps in medication supply chains and training represent strategic entry points for pharmaceutical partnerships with national governments and global health bodies such as Resolve to Save Lives. KOL engagement with Nigerian primary care leaders and national MOH officials should prioritize co-creating solutions addressing stockout prevention and task-shifting competency, which directly support sustained antihypertensive therapy adherence in the NHCI program.

Assessment of Blood Pressure Control Among Hypertensive Patients Attending Primary Healthcare Settings in Qatar: A Retrospective Chart Review.

Revue : Vascular health and risk management | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-16

Resultats principaux : This retrospective multicenter chart review analyzed 2,185 hypertensive patients across Qatar's primary care centers from 2017 to 2020, with a primary objective of estimating the proportion achieving blood pressure control. Over 60% of general hypertensive patients achieved BP control across all three study years (66% in 2018, 69% in 2019, 65% in 2020), and more than 60% of patients with diabetes or chronic kidney disease also reached their respective target BP levels (67.4% in 2018, 71.5% in 2019, 66.8% in 2020). Multivariable logistic regression identified gender, nationality (Qatari vs. non-Qatari), and the presence of comorbidities including dyslipidemia, congestive heart failure, peripheral vascular disease, atrial fibrillation, and chronic kidney disease as significant predictors of BP control. Qatari nationality and dyslipidemia were associated with better control, possibly reflecting differential access to care, adherence, or metabolic treatment. The study's retrospective chart-review methodology introduces inherent limitations in terms of data completeness and potential confounding.

Implications Medical Affairs : This study establishes a real-world effectiveness baseline for BP management in Qatar, demonstrating that despite moderate success, approximately one-third of patients remain uncontrolled, identifying a substantial unmet medical need. From a Medical Affairs perspective, Qatar represents a high-income Gulf market with universal healthcare access yet persistent control gaps, suggesting that therapeutic optimization rather than access is the primary barrier. Strategic engagement with Qatar's Primary Health Care Corporation and local KOLs should focus on combination antihypertensive therapy and personalized risk factor management for the identified high-risk comorbidity clusters.

Effect of Health Literacy on use of a Virtual Coach to Promote Home Blood Pressure Monitoring.

Revue : Journal of clinical hypertension (Greenwich, Conn.) | Tier : Tier 3 | IF estime : ~3.5

Date de publication : 2026-06

Resultats principaux : This preliminary prospective cohort study enrolled 59 individuals from rural primary care practices in Pennsylvania (mean age 58±15 years, 59% female, 71% without a bachelor's degree) to assess the impact of health literacy on engagement with a virtual coaching animation designed to promote home blood pressure monitoring (HBPM). Participants engaged in a mean of 1.03±1.05 agent interactions and performed 5.4±3.6 HBPM sessions per week over 1,487 total participant-weeks across 26 weeks. Virtual coach interactions were significantly associated with greater odds of achieving the target HBPM frequency of 4 readings per week (OR per interaction 1.10, 95% CI 1.01-1.20). Health literacy as measured by the Brief Health Literacy Screen (BRIEF) significantly moderated this association (interaction p=0.01), with individuals of limited or marginal literacy showing a markedly stronger effect (OR 1.31, 95% CI 1.10-1.57). The Newest Vital Sign (NVS) measure did not significantly moderate the relationship (interaction p=0.12), suggesting differential sensitivity across literacy measurement tools.

Implications Medical Affairs : These findings highlight that digital health companion tools for hypertension self-management are particularly efficacious in rural, health-literacy-limited populations — precisely those populations with the highest rates of uncontrolled hypertension — reinforcing the value of integrating digital therapeutics into antihypertensive treatment pathways. From a Medical Affairs perspective, positioning digital coaching as an adherence amplifier for patients on antihypertensive regimens could differentiate product support programs and support value-based contracts with payers. KOL strategy should include rural primary care champions and health literacy researchers to co-develop evidence-based patient engagement protocols aligned with guideline-recommended HBPM standards.

Utilization of evidence-based secondary preventive therapies post-acute coronary syndrome: a heart registry cohort study in Kenya.

Revue : The Pan African medical journal | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-02-09

Resultats principaux : This multicenter retrospective cohort study utilized data from the Kenya Heart Registry across three Kenyan hospitals from 2019 to 2023, enrolling 247 ACS patients (168 STEMI, 79 NSTEMI/UA), of whom 77.7% were male and 39.3% were aged above 65 years, with more than 70% classified as overweight or obese. Pre-hospital medication utilization was suboptimal across all drug classes; however, in-hospital and discharge prescribing rates improved markedly. At discharge, aspirin was prescribed in 91.4% of STEMI and 89.9% of NSTEMI/UA patients, while statins were received by 88.3% and 84.8%, respectively. Multivariate logistic regression identified diabetes as a significant predictor of beta-blocker receipt at discharge (OR=2.59), and hypertension as a strong predictor of ARB prescription (OR=5.28), reflecting appropriate guideline-directed therapy in comorbid patients. Ticagrelor utilization remained low, and the study noted gaps in transition-of-care protocols and long-term adherence support.

Implications Medical Affairs : This evidence from Kenya demonstrates that while in-hospital guideline adherence for beta-blockers and ARBs is improving in low- and middle-income African settings, pre-hospital and long-term secondary prevention gaps remain critical unmet needs. Medical Affairs strategy should prioritize engagement with the Kenya Heart Registry network and East African cardiology KOLs to co-develop evidence-based transition-of-care protocols that reinforce beta-blocker and RAAS blockade continuation post-ACS. The finding that hypertension drives ARB selection in this population provides direct positioning support for combination RAAS-based therapy in post-ACS African patients.

The burden of cardiovascular disease in prostate cancer: prevalence, pathophysiology, and implications for integrated care.

Revue : Supportive care in cancer | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-23

Resultats principaux : This comprehensive review examined the intersection of cardiovascular disease (CVD) and prostate cancer (PCa) in elderly men, exploring shared pathophysiological mechanisms and the cardiovascular sequelae of androgen deprivation therapy (ADT) and androgen receptor signaling inhibitors (ARSI). Common risk factors including metabolic disorders, chronic inflammation, and hormonal abnormalities were identified as driving the co-prevalence of both conditions, while ADT and ARSI treatments were specifically associated with increased rates of hypertension, myocardial infarction, arrhythmias, and metabolic abnormalities that complicate PCa management. Personalized lifestyle interventions including exercise regimens were identified as potentially reducing cardiovascular risk during PCa treatment, alongside pharmaceutical interventions including statins and antihypertensives. Emerging technologies such as wearable blood pressure monitors and telemedicine-based ambulatory blood pressure monitoring (ABPM) were highlighted as tools for real-time cardiovascular monitoring in this patient population. The review identified substantial gaps in clinical guidelines, particularly regarding cardio-oncology integration and biomarker development for shared inflammatory pathways.

Implications Medical Affairs : The identification of treatment-induced hypertension as a major cardiovascular complication of PCa therapy establishes antihypertensive management as a core component of cardio-oncology care, providing a strategic entry point for pharmaceutical positioning in the oncology setting. Medical Affairs should build scientific communication platforms around hypertension monitoring protocols in PCa patients receiving ADT, partnering with cardio-oncology KOLs to develop evidence-based screening algorithms and treatment intensification pathways. The emphasis on digital ABPM solutions opens collaborative opportunities with technology companies and healthcare systems to build integrated cardio-oncology monitoring platforms.

Urolithiasis in patients with cancer.

Revue : Nature reviews. Urology | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-22

Resultats principaux : This systematic review in Nature Reviews Urology examined urolithiasis in the cancer population, evaluating epidemiology, pathophysiology, and management strategies. Urolithiasis rates are increasing globally, driven by rising prevalence of obesity, diabetes, and metabolic syndrome — conditions that also elevate hypertension risk. Cancer patients face unique additional stone-formation risks including fluid and electrolyte imbalances, systemic cytotoxic therapies, tumor lysis syndrome, and anatomical disruption following urinary diversion or nephrectomy. Urolithiasis itself has been linked to elevated rates of renal cell carcinoma, urothelial carcinoma, and bladder cancer, potentially mediated by chronic urinary tract inflammation, recurrent infections, and shared metabolic or environmental risk factors. Management recommendations include percutaneous nephrolithotomy for patients with altered anatomy, with preventive strategies centered on metabolic evaluation, hydration optimization, and control of cancer-specific metabolic perturbations including hypercalcemia and acidosis.

Implications Medical Affairs : The shared metabolic risk substrate of urolithiasis, hypertension, and cancer — particularly obesity, metabolic syndrome, and chronic inflammation — reinforces the case for integrated cardiometabolic risk management in oncology patients. Medical Affairs should note that diuretics commonly used in hypertension therapy can influence urinary calcium and uric acid excretion, creating potential interaction points requiring monitoring protocols in cancer populations on antihypertensive treatment. KOL collaboration with nephro-oncology experts may yield co-development opportunities for integrated cardiometabolic-urological risk assessment tools that complement existing cardiovascular monitoring frameworks.

Supplementation of betaine and creatine to neonatal parenteral nutrition lowers telemetric blood pressure in adult Yucatan miniature pigs.

Revue : The Journal of nutrition | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-22

Resultats principaux : This randomized controlled animal study used 7-day-old normal birthweight female Yucatan miniature pigs (n=24) assigned to sow-fed, TPN-control, or TPN supplemented with betaine and creatine (TPN-B+C), with an additional group of 8 intrauterine growth-restricted (IUGR) neonates on TPN, followed through adulthood (8 months) with telemetric blood pressure measurement before and after a 2-week high-salt dietary challenge. Betaine and creatine supplementation of neonatal TPN significantly reduced mean arterial pressure by 9.5 mmHg and systolic pressure by 7.1 mmHg compared to TPN-control in adulthood (p<0.05, ANOVA). TPN alone did not significantly alter adult blood pressure relative to sow-fed controls, suggesting that deficiency of methyl nutrients rather than TPN per se underlies the programming effect. High-salt challenge increased BP in all groups by 6.2-15.4 mmHg, but neonatal diet did not modify salt sensitivity. Renin-angiotensin system gene expression was not significantly altered, suggesting non-RAAS epigenetic mechanisms.

Implications Medical Affairs : This preclinical study demonstrates that neonatal methyl-nutrient supplementation can durably reprogram adult blood pressure through epigenetic mechanisms distinct from the RAAS, establishing a developmental origins framework for hypertension prevention that merits translational investment. Medical Affairs should track this area as it represents a future preventive-medicine opportunity, where early nutritional interventions in preterm or IUGR neonates could reduce lifetime cardiovascular risk and population-level antihypertensive drug burden. If replicated in human cohorts, these findings could inform neonatal nutritional guidelines and open novel preventive positioning strategies for pharmaceutical-nutritional hybrid products.

Date de publication : 2026-06-09

Date de publication : 2026-06-05

Resultats principaux : This integrative bioinformatics study combined transcriptomic data from PAH lung tissue with GWAS summary statistics and quantitative trait loci (QTL) data for DNA methylation, gene expression, and plasma proteins to identify key genes in idiopathic pulmonary arterial hypertension (PAH), identifying 254 differentially expressed genes.
