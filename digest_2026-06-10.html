Subtypes of Hypertension 2 to 7 Years After First Pregnancy

Revue : Hypertension | Tier : Tier 2 | IF estime : ~10

Date de publication : 2026-06-09

Resultats principaux : This secondary analysis of the NuMoM2b Heart Health Study enrolled 4,302 nulliparous women at 8 US clinical sites (2010-2013), with a follow-up cardiovascular assessment conducted 2 to 7 years post-delivery. Blood pressure was classified into three subtypes: isolated diastolic (systolic less than 130 mmHg but diastolic at or above 80 mmHg), isolated systolic (systolic at or above 130 mmHg but diastolic less than 80 mmHg), and combined systolic-diastolic hypertension. Isolated diastolic hypertension was by far the most prevalent subtype at 15% (n=627), while combined systolic-diastolic occurred in only 4% of the cohort. Women who experienced hypertensive disorders of pregnancy (HDP) had a 3.09-fold higher adjusted risk of combined systolic-diastolic hypertension (95% CI 2.21-4.31) and a 1.65-fold higher risk of isolated diastolic hypertension (95% CI 1.36-2.00) compared to normotensive pregnancies. These associations remained significant after adjustment for age, BMI, race, and time from pregnancy. Subgroup analyses were not reported; the study design is secondary analysis of an existing cohort, and self-reported variables introduce potential misclassification bias.

Implications Medical Affairs : HDP is now established as a robust predictor of long-term hypertension subtypes, particularly isolated diastolic hypertension, which may precede combined systolic-diastolic hypertension and increase long-term cardiovascular risk in younger women — supporting guideline updates calling for mandatory postpartum blood pressure follow-up programs. From a product positioning standpoint, this cohort of young women with post-pregnancy diastolic predominance represents an underserved target segment for antihypertensive agents with favorable tolerability and pregnancy-compatible safety profiles. A priority KOL engagement strategy should target obstetric cardiologists and maternal-fetal medicine specialists at major US academic centers to align on screening protocols and treatment initiation thresholds in this population.

Global trends and geographic variations of hypertension in childhood and adolescence 1990-2021: a systematic analysis of the Global Burden of Disease Study 2021

Revue : Clinical Hypertension | Tier : Tier 3 | IF estime : ~3.5

Date de publication : 2026-06-01

Resultats principaux : This cross-sectional study leveraged data from the Global Burden of Disease (GBD) 2021 database, covering 204 countries and territories, to estimate deaths, DALYs, YLDs, and YLLs attributable to high systolic blood pressure among individuals under 20 years of age from 1990 to 2021. The global DALY rate in 2021 was 2.39 per 100,000 population (95% UI 1.75-2.88), representing a 20.11% decrease since 1990 (95% UI -35.02 to 10.21). In contrast, the YLD rate increased by 22.33% (95% UI 4.16-40.74), suggesting a growing non-fatal burden. Middle East and North Africa (MENA) showed the highest disease burden across all metrics in 2021, while Western Sub-Saharan Africa demonstrated pronounced gender disparities. High-income North America experienced the largest increase in pediatric hypertension disease burden since 1990. A negative correlation was observed between DALY rates and the Socio-demographic Index (SDI), indicating that lower-income regions bear the greatest fatal burden, though wide uncertainty intervals limit definitive conclusions.

Implications Medical Affairs : The rising non-fatal burden of pediatric hypertension, particularly in high-income North America, highlights an urgent unmet need that is likely to generate updated pediatric cardiovascular guidelines and expanded indications for antihypertensive therapy in younger populations. For Medical Affairs, this represents an opportunity to proactively generate pediatric data packages and engage pediatric cardiologists and nephrologists in early dialogue about age-appropriate formulations and dosing. The geographic disparities identified — especially the high burden in MENA and Sub-Saharan Africa — support the development of access programs and KOL networks in these emerging markets.

Heart rate and left ventricular remodeling after repaired coarctation of the aorta: a cross-sectional study

Revue : Clinical Hypertension | Tier : Tier 3 | IF estime : ~3.5

Date de publication : 2026-06-01

Resultats principaux : This cross-sectional study analyzed 57 patients with repaired coarctation of the aorta (CoAo) with a median post-repair follow-up of 11 years; all underwent office and ambulatory blood pressure measurement (ABPM), non-invasive central hemodynamics, and echocardiographic assessment. Left ventricular hypertrophy (LVH) was present in 15.2% (95% CI 4.8-25.6) of patients, and notably none of the 42% who met ABPM criteria for hypertension had simultaneous elevated central blood pressure and LVH. Heart rate-adjusted augmentation index (AIX@75) was inversely associated with posterior wall thickness in diastole (R2=0.40, p less than 0.01), and a model replacing AIX@75 with heart rate improved performance (R2=0.44) — with lower heart rate independently predicting greater posterior wall thickness. Computational simulations showed that a 10% increase in heart rate reduced mean posterior wall thickness and decreased LVH prevalence from 30.9% to 2.4% (OR 0.10; 95% CI 0.01-0.44). The main limitations are the small sample size (n=57) and single-center design, limiting generalizability.

Implications Medical Affairs : These findings position heart rate modulation as a novel mechanistic and potentially therapeutic target for ventricular remodeling in CoAo patients independent of blood pressure control — creating a differentiated argument for beta-blockers in this structural heart disease population that goes beyond simple BP reduction. This data could support a Medical Affairs initiative to investigate beta-blocker use in young adult CoAo patients through investigator-initiated research, partnering with congenital heart disease KOLs at specialized adult congenital heart disease centers. The insight that standard ABPM-based hypertension criteria miss a mechanistic driver of LVH in this population also has implications for how guidelines define and monitor hypertension in adults with repaired congenital heart disease.

Hypertension and its association with behavioral risk factors for non-communicable diseases: insights from the 2021 Vietnam WHO STEPS survey

Revue : BMC Research Notes | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-08

Resultats principaux : This cross-sectional, survey-weighted analysis used nationally representative data from the 2021 Vietnam WHO STEPS survey, including 3,809 adults aged 18-69 years with valid blood pressure measurements. The survey-weighted crude prevalence of hypertension was 26.4%, with higher rates in men than women and a steep age gradient. After multivariable adjustment, alcohol drinking and inadequate fruit and vegetable intake were independently associated with higher odds of hypertension. A cumulative behavioral risk factor index (0 to 4 risk factors: smoking, alcohol, insufficient physical activity, poor diet) showed a dose-response graded association with hypertension odds, with those having all four risk factors having the highest adjusted odds. Subgroup analyses were not conducted; the cross-sectional design prevents causal inference, and self-reported behavioral measures may be subject to social desirability bias.

Implications Medical Affairs : Vietnam is a high-growth pharmaceutical market with a 26.4% hypertension prevalence — suggesting a substantial but likely undertreated population that represents a significant commercial opportunity for antihypertensive agents with strong tolerability and compliance profiles. The identification of behavioral risk clusters aligns with global health authority calls for integrated non-communicable disease prevention strategies, which can be leveraged in Medical Affairs-supported patient education programs and disease awareness campaigns in the region. Engaging Vietnamese hypertension society KOLs and WHO country office representatives would support early market shaping and guideline alignment in this strategically important emerging market.

Blood pressure screening event at Hispanic community organization yields positive screenings and links to primary care

Revue : BMC Public Health | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-08

Resultats principaux : A hypertension screening event was conducted in Hazleton, Pennsylvania, at a monthly community dinner serving a predominantly Hispanic population; 145 individuals were screened, with 140 completing the full survey. Among participants, 58 (41%) had a prior hypertension diagnosis, 54 (39%) lacked a primary care provider, and 59 (42%) had blood pressure in the hypertensive range at the event — including 24 (17%) with newly identified undiagnosed hypertension. After the event, 26 individuals scheduled appointments with the affiliated federally-qualified health center (FQHC). Bilingual materials and community trust were identified as key facilitators; participants requested expanded screenings (e.g., diabetes) and more interpreters. Key limitations include the self-selected, single-event design and lack of long-term follow-up data on BP control or medication initiation.

Implications Medical Affairs : This study reinforces that community-based, culturally tailored screening events are highly effective at identifying undiagnosed hypertension in underserved Hispanic populations — a group with persistently lower hypertension control rates despite similar prevalence to the general US population. For Medical Affairs, this model supports co-investment in health equity initiatives and community outreach programs that can serve as a pipeline from diagnosis to evidence-based treatment with our antihypertensive portfolio. Partnership with FQHCs and community health organizations provides a novel access channel and aligns with payer and policy priorities around health disparities, offering reputational and commercial value.

Revue : Medicine (Baltimore) | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-05

Resultats principaux : This cross-sectional study used multistage stratified cluster sampling to enroll 3,012 permanent residents aged 18 years and older from Qianxinan Buyi and Miao Autonomous Prefecture, Guizhou Province (January 2023 to December 2024). The age-standardized prevalence of cardiometabolic diseases was 26.3%, with hypertension as the most prevalent condition at 21.5%, followed by diabetes (6.8%), coronary heart disease (3.0%), and stroke (1.3%). Cardio- and reno-protective effects of candesartan compared to lisinopril in a rat model of cardiorenal syndrome type 4: implication of oxidative stress and inflammation

Revue : Naunyn-Schmiedeberg's Archives of Pharmacology | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-09

Resultats principaux : This preclinical study used a 5/6 nephrectomy rat model of cardiorenal syndrome type 4 (CRS-4), comparing candesartan (10 mg/kg/day, ARB) versus lisinopril (12.5 mg/kg/day, ACE inhibitor), both administered via gastric gavage for 10 weeks. Both agents significantly improved renal function (reduced serum urea and creatinine), lowered arterial blood pressure, attenuated cardiac hypertrophy, decreased BNP, and reduced oxidative stress (MDA decreased, SOD increased) and inflammatory biomarkers (NF-kB, JAK-2/STAT-3, TGF-beta) compared to the untreated nephrectomy group. Histopathological examination confirmed marked reductions in both renal and cardiac tissue injury in treated animals. Candesartan demonstrated greater reno-protective effects based on renal biomarker and histology results, whereas lisinopril offered slightly superior cardioprotective benefits. Key limitations: animal model may not fully translate to clinical outcomes; single doses tested; short treatment duration of 10 weeks.

Implications Medical Affairs : This preclinical study provides mechanistic evidence for differential organ-protective profiles between ARBs and ACE inhibitors in cardiorenal syndrome — candesartan showing superior renoprotection while lisinopril offers slightly better cardioprotection — which could differentiate product positioning strategies in the growing CKD and CRS patient populations. From a Medical Affairs perspective, this data supports the design of head-to-head real-world evidence studies in CRS patients, which could strengthen the label differentiation of ARB products targeting the nephrology KOL audience. Collaboration with nephrology and cardiorenal syndrome specialists on an investigator-initiated research agenda exploring these differential mechanisms in humans would generate high-value evidence for guideline submissions and payer discussions.

Association between hypertension status and severity and tinnitus: a cross-sectional analysis of the Fasa adult cohort study

Revue : BMJ Open | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-08

Resultats principaux : This observational cross-sectional study analyzed data from 9,775 individuals aged 35-70 years from the Fasa Adult Cohort Study in rural Iran, excluding those with history of cancer, pregnancy, or conditions known to cause tinnitus (stroke, seizures, multiple sclerosis). Hypertension prevalence was 19.3% and tinnitus prevalence was 7.4%. Multivariable logistic regression showed hypertension was significantly associated with higher odds of tinnitus (adjusted OR 1.34; 95% CI 1.10-1.62). Even controlled hypertension was associated with a 27% increased odds of tinnitus versus normotensives (OR 1.27; 95% CI 1.02-1.59), while uncontrolled grade II hypertension had the highest odds (OR 2.08; 95% CI 1.25-3.47), demonstrating a dose-response relationship across severity levels. Subgroup analyses showed stronger associations in females, individuals under 50 years, and those with comorbid hyperlipidemia. Key limitations: cross-sectional design precludes causality; self-reported tinnitus assessment; antihypertensive medications including ACE inhibitors, ARBs, and diuretics were categorized as treatment variables.

Implications Medical Affairs : Hypertension-associated tinnitus represents a novel, underrecognized patient-reported outcome that could be incorporated into the burden-of-disease narrative for inadequately controlled hypertension, supporting more aggressive BP targets as a quality-of-life intervention beyond traditional cardiovascular endpoint reduction. This vascular-auditory connection creates a multidisciplinary engagement opportunity, allowing Medical Affairs to convene joint educational initiatives with ENT specialists and audiologists alongside cardiologists to broaden awareness of the systemic consequences of uncontrolled hypertension. The dose-response relationship between severity and tinnitus risk suggests that effective antihypertensive treatment — particularly to controlled status — may reduce this patient-relevant burden, providing a differentiated communication message for patient advocacy groups and formulary decision-makers.

Revue : Circulation | Tier : Tier 1 | IF estime : ~37

Date de publication : 2026-06-09

Resultats principaux : This landmark clinical practice guideline, jointly endorsed by the AHA, ACC, ADA, and ASN, retires and significantly expands the 2013 obesity guideline, covering the full spectrum of cardiovascular-kidney-metabolic (CKM) syndrome — an interconnected condition encompassing metabolic risk factors (including obesity and type 2 diabetes), chronic kidney disease, and cardiovascular disease. The literature search covered October 2024 to April 2025 across PubMed, EMBASE, Cochrane, and selected databases. The guideline provides staging-based risk thresholds using PREVENT equations to guide initiation and intensification of antihypertensive, lipid-lowering, anti-obesity, and cardio-renal-metabolic therapies (including SGLT2 inhibitors, GLP-1 receptor agonists, and finerenone). It is intended as a living document for continuous update and targets cardiologists, endocrinologists, nephrologists, and primary care clinicians. Subgroup guidance is provided for CKD stages, obesity classes, and diabetes status. Key limitations: predominantly based on RCT evidence that may not fully reflect real-world practice; recommendations for newer agents rely on trials conducted before 2025.

Implications Medical Affairs : This is the single most impactful publication of the week: the 2026 CKM guideline directly reframes antihypertensive therapy as one component of a multidimensional CKM management strategy — requiring Medical Affairs to immediately develop educational materials positioning our products within the new PREVENT-based staging and initiation framework. This guideline creates a competitive landscape shift, as products with proven outcomes across the CKM spectrum (CV, renal, metabolic) will be preferred, making combination therapy messaging and cross-specialty KOL engagement — spanning cardiology, nephrology, and endocrinology — a strategic imperative. Medical Science Liaisons must be briefed within 48 hours on the guideline's classification framework, PREVENT equation thresholds, and our portfolio's evidence alignment, as this will immediately affect prescriber conversations at conferences and in clinical settings.

Revue : Circulation | Tier : Tier 1 | IF estime : ~37

Date de publication : 2026-06-09

Resultats principaux : This AHA/ACC scientific statement details the rationale for adopting outcome-specific PREVENT equations (PREVENT-CVD, PREVENT-ASCVD, PREVENT-HF) to guide initiation and intensification of antihypertensive and lipid-lowering therapies within the CKM syndrome framework. The framework integrates predicted absolute risk with the relative risk reduction expected from treatment for each endpoint, producing a personalized estimate of expected benefit (absolute risk reduction) from pharmacotherapy. The 2025 ACC/AHA hypertension guideline and 2026 dyslipidemia guideline both incorporate PREVENT-based thresholds. The statement includes practical guidance on shared decision-making, addresses gaps in risk communication, and models potential population-level impact of these recommendations on CVD outcomes. Subgroup considerations for patients with CKD, obesity, and diabetes are integrated. Key limitations: PREVENT equations were developed and validated predominantly in US populations; generalizability to global populations of different ethnicities requires further validation.

Implications Medical Affairs : This scientific statement directly governs when and how physicians will initiate antihypertensive therapy, making it essential for Medical Affairs to validate our product's performance against PREVENT-CVD and PREVENT-HF thresholds and develop data packages demonstrating expected benefit within each risk stratum. The expected-benefit quantification approach creates a compelling opportunity to develop patient-level shared decision-making tools that incorporate our antihypertensive agents' specific risk reduction data — tools that MSLs can deploy with cardiologists and primary care physicians to differentiate our products at the point of prescribing. Chronotherapy in hypertension: a systematic review and meta-analysis of antihypertensive dosing timing on cardiovascular outcomes

Revue : Journal of Human Hypertension | Tier : Tier 3 | IF estime : ~3.5

Date de publication : 2026-06-09

Resultats principaux : This updated systematic review and meta-analysis identified 5 studies encompassing 42,075 patients with hypertension, comparing morning versus nighttime (evening or bedtime) administration of antihypertensive drugs on major cardiovascular outcomes. Pooled analyses found no significant differences between morning and nighttime dosing for all-cause mortality (RR 0.99; 95% CI 0.91-1.08), myocardial infarction (RR 1.14; 95% CI 0.98-1.33), major adverse cardiovascular events (RR 1.04; 95% CI 0.92-1.19), or stroke (RR 0.95; 95% CI 0.75-1.20). Results align with the contemporary high-quality TIME and BedMed randomized controlled trials, and contrast with older meta-analyses that favored nighttime dosing. Subgroup analyses were not extensively reported. Key limitations: only 5 studies included in the analysis; some degree of heterogeneity across populations and drug classes; most studies used fixed drug classes rather than class-specific analyses.

Implications Medical Affairs : This meta-analysis effectively closes the long-standing clinical debate about bedtime dosing advantage, providing definitive evidence that physicians can recommend morning or evening dosing based solely on patient preference and adherence — simplifying the prescribing message for our antihypertensive portfolio and removing a common hesitation among prescribers. This finding supports an adherence-centric communication strategy: Medical Affairs messaging can now focus on consistent daily intake regardless of time, which may improve long-term BP control rates and reduce the treatment-related anxiety patients experience when they miss a morning dose. The alignment with the TIME and BedMed RCT conclusions strengthens the credibility of this guidance and provides a clean evidence base for KOL-driven educational materials on hypertension management simplification.

Revue : Journal of the American Heart Association | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-09

Resultats principaux : This retrospective analysis included 603 patients aged 60 years and above (mean age 79.1 years; 48.4% female) newly diagnosed with HFpEF at Huadong Hospital, Fudan University, between July 2019 and June 2024. Overall mortality was 14.2/100 person-years (women 15.4 vs men 13.2). Elecoglipron, an oral small molecule GLP-1 receptor agonist in adults with obesity or overweight (VISTA): a multicentre, phase 2, randomised, placebo-controlled clinical trial

Revue : The Lancet | Tier : Tier 1 | IF estime : ~98

Date de publication : 2026-06-08

Resultats principaux : VISTA was a double-blind, multicentre phase 2 dose-ranging trial conducted across 7 countries (Australia, Canada, Germany, Japan, Taiwan, UK, USA). A total of 310 adult participants (mean age 48.4 years; 73% female; mean BMI 38.2 kg/m2) with obesity or overweight plus at least one weight-related condition without type 2 diabetes were randomized in a 2:3:3:3:3:5 ratio to elecoglipron (5 mg, 15 mg, 50 mg, 75 mg weekly titration, 75 mg biweekly titration) or placebo once daily for 36 weeks. At week 26, estimated mean change from baseline in body weight ranged from -2.6% (5 mg) to -10.5% (75 mg with weekly titration) versus -0.6% with placebo. Proportions achieving at least 5% weight reduction at week 26 ranged from 40.4% to 88.8% with elecoglipron versus 15.6% with placebo. Adverse events were reported by 84-98% of elecoglipron recipients versus 84% placebo, predominantly nausea, constipation, diarrhea, headache, and vomiting. Hypertension was a key eligibility criterion as a weight-related comorbidity. Key limitations: phase 2 dose-ranging study; 36-week duration insufficient for long-term cardiovascular outcomes assessment; primarily obese non-diabetic population.

Implications Medical Affairs : Elecoglipron represents a potential best-in-class oral GLP-1 receptor agonist with meaningful weight loss efficacy that could significantly reduce obesity-driven hypertension in a large patient population — a finding directly relevant to the CKM framework established in today's landmark guideline. The 10.5% weight reduction at the highest dose, combined with the obesity-hypertension connection, suggests that this class of agents will increasingly compete with or complement antihypertensive therapy in overweight hypertensive patients, requiring Medical Affairs to develop a proactive positioning strategy for our antihypertensive portfolio in the era of GLP-1-based weight management. The phase 3 program for elecoglipron will likely include blood pressure as a secondary endpoint, making it essential to monitor this data and prepare Medical Affairs responses addressing how our portfolio addresses residual hypertension risk in patients partially managed on oral GLP-1 agents.
