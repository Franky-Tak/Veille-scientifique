Contemporary achievement of guideline recommendations for blood pressure management after stroke in the CONVINCE trial.

Revue : European Stroke Journal | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-06

Resultats principaux : This pre-specified sub-study of the CONVINCE randomized controlled trial enrolled 3,154 recent ischemic stroke or high-risk TIA patients across multiple European centers, with BP measured at 6-monthly visits from 2017 to 2024. The primary analysis focused on rates of achievement of pre-2022 (<140 mmHg SBP) and 2022 ESO (<130 mmHg SBP) guideline targets using mean follow-up BP derived from all available measurements. Mean SBP during follow-up was 136.6 mmHg (SD 12.8) and mean DBP was 79.3 mmHg (SD 7.8), indicating that the overall treated population remained above the current stricter guideline threshold. A total of 36% of participants failed to meet the pre-2022 guideline target of SBP <140 mmHg, while 70% did not achieve the current 2022 ESO target of SBP <130 mmHg. Multivariable logistic regression identified increasing age (OR 1.026 per year; 95% CI 1.018–1.034), a history of hypertension (OR 1.47; 95% CI 1.24–1.74), and lacunar stroke subtype (OR 1.26; 95% CI 1.06–1.49) as independent predictors of failing to achieve the pre-2022 target. A key limitation is that BP was measured at clinic visits only, which may not fully reflect ambulatory or home BP levels, and the trial population may differ from broader stroke populations in real-world settings.

Implications Medical Affairs : The finding that 70% of post-stroke patients fail to meet current 2022 ESO BP targets (<130 mmHg SBP) underscores a major implementation gap and signals that updated guidelines have substantially raised the bar for what constitutes adequate secondary prevention in this high-risk population. From a Medical Affairs perspective, this data creates a compelling narrative for antihypertensive products demonstrating superiority in achieving intensive BP control post-stroke, particularly in the subgroups identified as highest-risk (elderly, prior hypertension, lacunar stroke). This evidence should drive KOL engagement strategies around guideline adherence challenges and position intensive BP-lowering agents as essential tools in secondary stroke prevention protocols.

Delivering Lifestyle Interventions for Chronic Disease Care Sustainably and at Scale through Virtual Shared Medical Appointments in Primary Care.

Revue : NEJM Catalyst Innovations in Care Delivery | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-05-20

Resultats principaux : This real-world program evaluation from Massachusetts General Hospital (MGH) assessed the Healthy Lifestyle Program, which deployed reimbursable virtual shared medical appointments (SMAs) for hypertension and diabetes management between March 2020 and October 2024. A total of 1,679 unique primary care patients attended at least one virtual SMA across the evaluation period. For the hypertension-specific analysis, 400 patients were enrolled (200 virtual SMA participants, 200 propensity score-matched controls); the overall cohort showed a non-significant trend toward BP improvement (adjusted OR 1.55; 95% CI 0.94–2.58; P=0.086). However, in the pre-specified subgroup of patients with baseline BP >130/80 mmHg (87% of the cohort, n=348), the virtual SMA group achieved a mean 8.7 mmHg reduction in SBP versus 3.9 mmHg in controls, yielding an adjusted difference of -4.7 mmHg (95% CI -8.8 to -0.76; P=0.019). Furthermore, 33% of virtual SMA participants in this subgroup achieved BP control below 130/80 mmHg compared with 16% of controls. Limitations include the observational design, potential selection bias despite propensity matching, and that only short-term (4-month) outcomes were captured without longer follow-up data.

Implications Medical Affairs : The statistically significant 4.7 mmHg adjusted SBP reduction in uncontrolled hypertensive patients through scalable virtual SMAs adds to the body of evidence that behavioral and digital health interventions can meaningfully complement pharmacotherapy in achieving guideline BP targets. For Medical Affairs teams, this data supports the narrative that antihypertensive agents operate most effectively within integrated care models that address lifestyle factors, reinforcing an ecosystem-level value proposition rather than focusing solely on drug efficacy. This study provides actionable insights for KOL strategies centered on care delivery innovation, and could inform payer engagement by demonstrating the economic potential of reimbursable digital health services in reducing uncontrolled hypertension burden.

The CHECK-IT Program - Controlling Hypertension in Black Communities through In-Person and Virtual Interventions.

Revue : NEJM Catalyst Innovations in Care Delivery | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-03-11

Resultats principaux : The CHECK-IT (Convenient Home Evaluation for Cardiovascular Health and Individual Tracking) program was a community-centered intervention launched in June 2023 by the Indianapolis Health Equity, Access, Outreach, and Treatment Collaborative across six IU Health primary care facilities in high-risk Indianapolis neighborhoods with high rates of cardiovascular disease and racial disparity. The 4-month program integrated home BP monitoring, patient education with automated text reminders, community health worker (CHW) support for social determinants of health, and virtual pharmacist-led medication management for uncontrolled BP. CHWs escalated urgent cases to IU Health social work and connected participants with community resources to address food, housing, and economic insecurity. Over the first 18 months of the program, more than 60% of participants who completed the program achieved BP control defined as <140/90 mmHg, with average reductions of 7 mmHg systolic and 4 mmHg diastolic. The study does not report a comparator group, and data on maintenance of BP control beyond the 4-month program period were not collected, limiting conclusions about long-term durability of effect and causal attribution.

Implications Medical Affairs : The CHECK-IT program demonstrates that community-centered hybrid (in-person and virtual) models explicitly designed to address structural racism and social determinants of health can achieve BP control rates exceeding 60% in a population experiencing disproportionate hypertension burden — a result with significant implications for health equity strategy within pharmaceutical Medical Affairs. This model highlights the importance of CHWs and telehealth pharmacist support as force multipliers for adherence and medication titration, creating partnership opportunities for companies with antihypertensive portfolios to support community health infrastructure as part of their real-world evidence and access strategies. The data should inform KOL engagement in health equity science and could anchor differentiation narratives around products positioned in primary care settings serving underrepresented minorities.

Clinical characteristics of childhood essential hypertension before, during, and after the COVID-19 pandemic: a single-center retrospective study.

Revue : European Journal of Pediatrics | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-07

Resultats principaux : This retrospective single-center study from Turkey included 311 children aged 6–17 years diagnosed with essential hypertension between February 2018 and April 2025, and stratified patients into pre-pandemic (26.7%), pandemic (35.0%), and post-pandemic (38.3%) periods. Evaluation included clinical BP measurements, 24-hour ambulatory blood pressure monitoring (ABPM), BMI, antihypertensive medication use, and cardiac and renal assessments. BMI values were significantly higher in the post-pandemic group compared to the pre-pandemic group (p=0.002), reflecting the well-documented adverse effects of pandemic-related lifestyle changes on childhood obesity. Nighttime diastolic blood pressure measured on ABPM was significantly elevated in the post-pandemic group (p<0.001), suggesting an increase in nocturnal BP burden — a marker associated with greater cardiovascular risk. The proportion of patients receiving single or multiple antihypertensive medications increased significantly in the post-pandemic period (p=0.041), indicating a shift toward more severe or treatment-resistant presentations. Age distribution was similar across all three periods. A key limitation is the referral-based clinical population, which precludes generalization to population-level prevalence trends and may reflect changes in healthcare utilization patterns rather than true incidence.

Implications Medical Affairs : The post-pandemic emergence of a more severe pediatric hypertension phenotype — characterized by higher BMI, increased nocturnal BP, and greater medication burden — represents an evolving public health challenge that pharmaceutical companies in the antihypertensive space should monitor closely, as pediatric hypertension remains a largely underserved market with limited labeled indications. This data signals a potential expansion of the addressable patient population for antihypertensive agents in younger age groups, and Medical Affairs teams should proactively engage pediatric cardiologists and nephrologists to develop disease awareness programs and generate pediatric real-world evidence. Longer-term implications include the need for robust registry data and multi-center studies to quantify population-level trends and inform regulatory strategies for pediatric hypertension indications.

Long-Term Health Outcomes in Individuals With Confirmed Versus Unconfirmed Undiagnosed Diabetes Based on Oral Glucose Tolerance Test: Findings From the Tehran Lipid and Glucose Study.

Revue : Journal of Diabetes Research | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026

Resultats principaux : Using data from the Tehran Lipid and Glucose Study, this cohort study enrolled 6,712 participants aged ≥30 years not on glucose-lowering medications, followed for a median of over 10 years. Undiagnosed diabetes was classified as confirmed (both FPG ≥7 mmol/L AND 2-h PG ≥11.1 mmol/L; 3.6% of participants) or unconfirmed (either criterion; 3.3%). Confirmed undiagnosed diabetes was associated with significantly increased risk of incident hypertension (HR 1.26; 95% CI 1.00–1.60), CKD (HR 1.33; 95% CI 1.02–1.73), first CVD event (HR 1.40; 95% CI 1.01–1.93), and hard CVD (HR 1.65; 95% CI 1.04–2.61), while all-cause mortality did not reach significance (HR 1.50; 95% CI 0.96–2.33; p=0.07). Unconfirmed undiagnosed diabetes was only associated with treated diabetes (HR 7.73; 95% CI 6.35–9.44) and first CVD (HR 1.53; 95% CI 1.11–2.12). Isolated FPG elevation was independently associated with both first CVD (HR 2.55; 95% CI 1.50–4.31) and hard CVD (HR 2.43; 95% CI 1.06–6.00), comparable in magnitude to confirmed undiagnosed diabetes. Limitations include the observational design and Iranian population context, which may limit extrapolation to other ethnic groups.

Implications Medical Affairs : This long-term cohort study reinforces the cardiometabolic continuum by demonstrating that undiagnosed diabetes — even at a single-sample confirmatory threshold — confers a clinically meaningful 26% increased risk of incident hypertension over a decade, underscoring the value of integrated metabolic screening strategies in cardiovascular risk programs. For Medical Affairs, this data strengthens the scientific rationale for comorbidity-focused positioning strategies that link antihypertensive treatments to broader cardiometabolic risk reduction, particularly in populations where diabetes and hypertension co-exist at high rates. The elevated isolated FPG association with hard CVD outcomes suggests that pre-diabetic patients represent a target population for proactive antihypertensive intervention and real-world evidence generation.

Pascal selective laser trabeculoplasty (PSLT) in the treatment of ocular hypertension and open-angle glaucoma compared to travoprost eye drops: A non-inferiority clinical trial.

Revue : PloS One | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-08

Resultats principaux : This non-inferiority randomized clinical trial enrolled 30 patients with ocular hypertension (OHT) or primary open-angle glaucoma (POAG), with one eye randomized to Pascal selective laser trabeculoplasty (PSLT) and the contralateral eye to topical travoprost eye drops as the control. Baseline mean intraocular pressure (IOP) was similar in both groups at 18.8 ± 4.0 mmHg (PSLT) and 18.8 ± 4.2 mmHg (travoprost). The primary non-inferiority margin was set at 1.5 mmHg difference in IOP. No significant IOP difference was found between groups at 2, 6, or 9 months; at 12 months, the PSLT group showed significantly lower mean IOP (13.5 ± 2.3 mmHg vs 14.9 ± 2.7 mmHg; p<0.001), demonstrating non-inferiority to prostaglandin therapy. Peak IOP measured by water drinking test also showed similar results between groups. No significant progression was observed in either group regarding visual field or OCT parameters over the study period. Important limitations include the small sample size (n=30 eyes per group), the within-patient design which avoids confounding but may introduce carry-over effects, and the relatively short 12-month follow-up for a chronic progressive disease. Note: this study addresses ocular hypertension (elevated intraocular pressure), which is a distinct entity from systemic arterial hypertension.

Implications Medical Affairs : While this study addresses ocular hypertension rather than systemic arterial hypertension, it was captured in the clinical trials search due to procedural overlap and serves as a reminder that 'hypertension' terminology spans multiple organ systems; Medical Affairs surveillance systems should apply disease-specific MeSH filters to ensure signal-to-noise optimization in automated literature monitoring. From a competitive intelligence perspective, the demonstration that laser-based interventions (PSLT) can match topical prostaglandin analog efficacy in IOP reduction may inform cross-specialty conversations relevant to ophthalmology portfolios. No direct actionability for systemic antihypertensive Medical Affairs; this finding should be flagged for ophthalmic therapeutic area teams if applicable within the organization.

Metabolic Syndrome as a Determinant of Bidirectional Transitions Between Frailty States: Evidence From the Whitehall II Study.

Revue : Journal of Cachexia, Sarcopenia and Muscle | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-08

Resultats principaux : This longitudinal analysis of the Whitehall II cohort included 4,750 participants (mean age 64.6 years; 73.9% men) followed for an average of 6.9 years across Phases 9, 11, and 12. Frailty was classified using Fried's phenotype into four states: robust (0 criteria), pre-frailty 1 (PF-1; 1 criterion), pre-frailty 2 (PF-2; 2 criteria), and frailty (≥3 criteria). Metabolic syndrome (MetS) and its five components were defined per consensus definition; 36.9% of participants had MetS. A multi-state Markov model with death as an absorbing competing state was used to estimate bidirectional transition intensities. The overall MetS composite was not significantly associated with any frailty transition. However, abdominal obesity specifically increased the hazard of transitioning from robust to PF-1 by 27% (HR 1.27; 95% CI 1.09–1.48; p=0.001) and reduced recovery from PF-2 to PF-1 by 28% (HR 0.72; 95% CI 0.52–0.99; p=0.04) and from frailty to PF-2 by 50% (HR 0.50; 95% CI 0.29–0.87; p=0.014). Hyperglycemia reduced recovery from PF-1 to robustness by 28% (HR 0.72; 95% CI 0.59–0.88; p<0.001). Notably, hypertension as an individual MetS component showed no significant associations with frailty transitions, suggesting its effect may be mediated by or confounded by other metabolic factors in this complex syndrome.

Implications Medical Affairs : The dissociation between hypertension as an isolated MetS component and frailty trajectory progression — in contrast to the significant roles of abdominal obesity and hyperglycemia — suggests that BP control alone may be insufficient to prevent frailty in aging cardiometabolic patients, with weight management and glycemic control potentially more impactful targets in this context. For Medical Affairs, this finding supports the scientific rationale for comprehensive cardiometabolic risk management strategies that go beyond BP targets, and could inform medical education initiatives for KOLs in geriatrics and cardiology around integrated treatment approaches. The data also opens opportunities for Real World Evidence programs evaluating whether combination cardiometabolic therapies (e.g., antihypertensives with metabolic benefit) show superior frailty outcomes compared to agents with BP-selective mechanisms.

Lifestyle Factors and Thromboembolic Risk in Atrial Fibrillation: Age-Dependent Effects of Smoking and Alcohol Consumption.

Revue : Journal of Arrhythmia | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-07

Resultats principaux : This retrospective, nationwide, multicenter case-control study used the Japanese Inpatient Clinico-Occupational Database of the Rosai Hospital Group (2005–2022), including 26,915 patients: 5,688 with atrial fibrillation (AF)-related cardioembolic stroke (CS) and 21,227 with AF without CS. Lifestyle factors (smoking status and alcohol consumption) were assessed at admission through structured interviews. Current smoking was significantly more prevalent in AF patients with CS across all age groups from 40–89 years (all p<0.001). Multivariable logistic regression confirmed smoking as independently associated with CS overall and by age group. Daily alcohol intake was associated with CS overall and specifically in patients ≤75 years (p=0.021 and p=0.044 respectively). The CHAD score (incorporating heart failure, hypertension, age >75 years [weighted ×2], and diabetes) increased progressively with age, from 0.66 in the 40–49 year group to 2.94 in those ≥90 years, while smoking and alcohol prevalence declined with age, indicating that lifestyle factors dominate stroke risk profile in younger AF patients with low traditional risk scores. A key limitation is the retrospective design with self-reported lifestyle data, institutional selection bias from the Rosai (occupational) hospital network, and lack of anticoagulation data.

Implications Medical Affairs : The age-dependent contribution of smoking and alcohol to cardioembolic stroke risk in AF patients — particularly relevant in younger individuals with low CHAD scores who may not yet meet anticoagulation thresholds — highlights the critical role of lifestyle modification as a stroke prevention strategy complementary to pharmacological management. For Medical Affairs, this evidence reinforces the need for comprehensive risk stratification tools that integrate lifestyle variables alongside the CHAD score, and could support disease awareness programs targeting cardiologists and general practitioners around holistic AF management including hypertension control. Hypertension remains a key CHAD component and antihypertensive therapy remains essential across all age groups, making this study a useful contextual reference for positioning BP-lowering agents within comprehensive AF stroke prevention strategies.

Interleukin-17A mediates cardiorenal injury in oxalate nephropathy.

Revue : Cardiovascular Research | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-08

Resultats principaux : This preclinical mechanistic study used C57Bl/6N mice fed an oxalate-enriched diet to induce oxalate nephropathy, then examined downstream systemic and cardiorenal consequences. Oxalate exposure induced systemic immune activation, renal fibrosis, and adverse cardiac remodeling including pulmonary congestion with both systolic and diastolic dysfunction. Flow cytometry identified interleukin-17A (IL-17A) as the dominant inflammatory effector, with expansion of Th17 and Th17-like Treg populations in kidney, intestine, and spleen; bulk mRNA sequencing confirmed these signatures in kidney and heart tissue. Elevated plasma IL-17A was also confirmed in human patients with primary hyperoxaluria, lending clinical translational relevance. Gut microbiome analysis by 16S amplicon sequencing showed only mild oxalate-induced alterations, while soluble oxalate directly enhanced Th17 polarization and disrupted mitochondrial respiration in vitro. Antibody-mediated IL-17A blockade in vivo improved kidney function, reduced cardiac fibrosis, decreased neutrophil infiltration, and partially restored cardiac function in oxalate-fed mice. A limitation is the murine model which may not fully recapitulate human CKD progression, and the therapeutic implication of IL-17A blockade requires validation in clinical settings where off-target immunosuppression risks must be weighed.

Implications Medical Affairs : The identification of the oxalate-IL-17A axis as a mechanistic bridge between chronic kidney disease and cardiovascular damage provides a novel immunometabolic pathway that may explain part of the residual cardiorenal risk observed in hypertensive CKD patients despite optimal BP control, opening new avenues for anti-inflammatory therapeutic strategies. From a Medical Affairs perspective, this preclinical evidence supports the growing scientific narrative that cardiorenal protection in hypertensive populations requires targeting of inflammatory mediators beyond renin-angiotensin-aldosterone system (RAAS) inhibition, reinforcing the value proposition of agents with proven anti-inflammatory cardiorenal effects. KOL engagement strategies could leverage this emerging IL-17A pathway to stimulate investigator-initiated research exploring how oxalate burden modifies cardiovascular outcomes in hypertensive CKD cohorts.

Gender disparities in central obesity in Cape Verde: analysis of the 2020 WHO STEPS survey.

Revue : Scientific Reports | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-08

Resultats principaux : This cross-sectional study used secondary data from the 2020 WHO STEPS survey conducted in Cape Verde, an archipelagic nation off the West African coast undergoing nutritional and lifestyle transitions linked to urbanization. Central obesity was defined using waist-to-hip ratio thresholds (≥0.90 for men; ≥0.85 for women). Overall prevalence of central obesity was 25.1% (95% CI: 22.4–28.0%). Predicted probability of central obesity was significantly higher in women (39.4%; 95% CI: 36.6–42.1%) than men (23.2%; 95% CI: 20.2–26.2%), yielding an adjusted marginal effect of 16.15 percentage points (95% CI: 11.84–20.46%; p<0.001). In multivariable logistic regression, key predictors included age ≥60 years (AOR 5.07; 95% CI: 3.34–7.68), primary/basic education (AOR 2.13; 95% CI: 1.42–3.19), and hypertension (AOR 1.72; 95% CI: 1.36–2.18). Fairlie decomposition analysis revealed that only 0.4% of the observed 16.9 percentage-point gender gap in central obesity was explained by the measured covariates, indicating substantial unexplained gender differences, likely rooted in biological, hormonal, and deeply structural social factors not captured in the model. Limitations include the cross-sectional design limiting causal inference and the self-reported nature of some variables.

Implications Medical Affairs : The strong bidirectional association between hypertension and central obesity in this West African population (AOR 1.72) — combined with the finding that over 25% of Cape Verdeans carry central obesity — highlights the growing double burden of cardiometabolic disease in sub-Saharan Africa, a market where pharmaceutical access and awareness remain major unmet needs. For Medical Affairs, this evidence supports scientific engagement strategies focused on the cardiometabolic risk cluster (hypertension + obesity) in African and emerging-market patient populations, and could inform global health equity narratives around antihypertensive portfolio access. KOL development opportunities exist with African epidemiologists working on the WHO STEPS platform to generate further regional cardiometabolic real-world data.

Prediabetes in children and adolescents: what do we need to know?

Revue : Annals of Pediatric Endocrinology & Metabolism | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-30

Resultats principaux : This narrative review synthesizes evidence on pediatric prediabetes, examining definitions, risk factors, screening recommendations, and management strategies for children and adolescents, with particular attention to the limitations of applying adult-based diagnostic criteria to the pediatric population. Pediatric obesity — a growing global concern — increases the prevalence of dysglycemia including prediabetes and type 2 diabetes, which in turn are associated with significant cardiovascular risk factors including hypertension, dyslipidemia, metabolic dysfunction-associated steatotic liver disease (MASLD), and sleep disturbances. Insulin resistance is identified as the key pathophysiological contributor, with the physiological reduction in insulin sensitivity during puberty representing an important developmental modulator that complicates risk stratification. Critically, most youth with prediabetes experience spontaneous normalization of glucose metabolism following puberty as insulin sensitivity recovers, which challenges the clinical utility of the prediabetes label in this age group. General population screening for prediabetes or diabetes is not recommended in youth except in specific high-risk populations. Lifestyle modification remains the primary intervention as pharmacological treatments (including metformin) have not yet demonstrated sufficient efficacy in pediatric prediabetes, representing a key evidence gap. Limitations include the narrative rather than systematic review format and the relatively limited pediatric-specific evidence base.

Implications Medical Affairs : The identification of hypertension as a major cardiovascular comorbidity in pediatric prediabetes reinforces the need for early blood pressure surveillance and management protocols in obese youth with dysglycemia, presenting Medical Affairs with an opportunity to advance scientific evidence on the natural history of hypertension in this emerging pediatric cardiometabolic cohort. The current absence of evidence-based pharmacological interventions for pediatric prediabetes itself represents a substantial unmet medical need, suggesting potential pipeline interest and investigator-initiated research opportunities for compounds with cardiometabolic dual action in younger populations. KOL engagement in pediatric endocrinology and preventive cardiology should be prioritized to build scientific exchange around early-life hypertension prevention within the framework of the cardiometabolic risk continuum.
