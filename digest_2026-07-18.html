Personalised approach to hypertension treatment: protocol for the HYPERMARKER randomised controlled trial.

Revue : BMJ Open | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-16

Resultats principaux : The HYPERMARKER trial is a proof-of-concept, pragmatic, multicentre, adaptive, open-label randomised controlled trial designed to evaluate whether individualised antihypertensive therapy selection, guided by a digital shared decision-making portal integrating patient-specific metabolomic profiles (determined via liquid chromatography-mass spectrometry) and machine learning, improves blood pressure control compared to standard clinician-directed care. The study population comprises adults aged 18 years and older with systolic blood pressure of 140 mmHg or above who have a clinical indication for antihypertensive therapy; 400 participants will be randomised across multiple centres in England, the Netherlands, Germany, and Spain. The primary outcome is change in home systolic blood pressure over a 4-week period following medication changes, assessed remotely via a patient smartphone application linked to a blood pressure monitor. Secondary outcomes include patient-reported adverse effects, quality of life, treatment withdrawal rates, healthcare utilisation, and a health economic analysis; in the second phase, all participants cross over to receive the digital intervention regardless of original allocation. Limitations of this protocol include the open-label design which precludes blinding, the short 4-week primary follow-up period which may not capture long-term blood pressure trajectories, and the proof-of-concept sample size which limits generalisability of efficacy conclusions.

Implications Medical Affairs : The HYPERMARKER trial represents a significant methodological advance that could shift hypertension management from empirical therapy selection to precision pharmacotherapy, with direct implications for guideline revision if results demonstrate superiority of metabolomics-guided prescribing over standard of care. For pharmaceutical companies with antihypertensive portfolios, the ability to stratify patients using metabolomic biomarkers offers a compelling opportunity to reposition existing agents within precision medicine frameworks and support differential therapeutic positioning. Medical Affairs teams should monitor this trial closely as a reference for KOL engagement strategies, scientific exchange activities, and collaborative research programs in the area of personalised hypertension management.

Immunological coagulation dual axis stratification identifies ultra high-risk phenotypes in systemic sclerosis.

Revue : Frontiers in Immunology | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-02

Resultats principaux : This retrospective study enrolled 287 patients with systemic sclerosis (SSc) from a single centre, stratified by organ involvement: 166 with isolated SSc, 85 with concomitant interstitial lung disease (ILD), 18 with pulmonary hypertension (PH), and 18 with both ILD and PH. The study calculated 12 composite indices spanning immune, inflammation, coagulation, and fibrinolysis (IICF) pathways, and applied LASSO-Cox regression to build a multi-index prognostic score. Dual-axis stratification using the Systemic Immune-Inflammation Index (SII) and D-dimer-to-Platelet Ratio (DPR) revealed that patients with concurrent elevation of both indices faced a 7.41-fold higher mortality risk compared to those with dual-low levels (HR 7.41; 95% CI 2.09-26.30; p = 0.002), whereas elevation of either index alone did not reach statistical significance. K-means clustering identified three IICF molecular endotypes that showed incomplete concordance with ILD or PH phenotypes, suggesting immune-coagulation network disequilibrium as the primary driver of adverse outcomes rather than specific organ involvement. The multi-index prognostic score achieved an AUC of 0.747 for mortality prediction, surpassing individual markers such as the Prognostic Nutritional Index (0.693) and Neutrophil-to-Lymphocyte Ratio (0.675); key limitations include the single-centre retrospective design, small PH subgroup sizes (n = 18 each), and absence of external validation.

Implications Medical Affairs : While this study focuses on systemic sclerosis rather than primary arterial hypertension, the findings carry relevant implications for pulmonary hypertension drug development, specifically regarding the role of immune-coagulation axis dysregulation as a targetable mechanism driving adverse outcomes. From a Medical Affairs perspective, the IICF-based stratification framework could inform patient selection biomarker strategies for clinical trials in SSc-associated pulmonary hypertension, an area of high unmet medical need. KOL engagement at the rheumatology-pulmonary hypertension interface may benefit from awareness of this novel precision endotyping approach as it offers actionable prognostic stratification that could support enriched trial designs.

Temporal Trends and Disparities in Hypertension-Related Cardiomyopathy Mortality in the United States, 1999-2023.

Revue : Clinical Cardiology | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07 (Jul)

Resultats principaux : This retrospective analysis of CDC WONDER mortality data identified 153,563 deaths attributable to cardiomyopathy (ICD-10: I42, I42.2, I42.9) with hypertension (ICD-10: I10, I15.0, I15.9) as a contributing cause among US adults aged 25 years or older between 1999 and 2023, using age-adjusted mortality rates (AAMRs) standardised to the US 2000 population with joinpoint regression. Overall AAMR increased from 1.26 per 100,000 in 1999 to 2.22 in 2023, driven by a sharp initial rise from 1999 to 2001 (APC: +30.33%; p < 0.005), followed by a sustained decline. Significant racial disparities were documented: non-Hispanic Black individuals had the highest AAMR at 10.81 per 100,000, more than double that of non-Hispanic White individuals (5.02), with non-metropolitan areas carrying greater burden than metropolitan areas (2.71 vs. 2.26, both declining after 2001). The Southern United States had the highest regional AAMR (4.86), while the Midwest showed a worrying resurgence after 2017 (APC: +4.11; p < 0.005), and adults aged 85 years and older carried the highest crude mortality rate (32.79) with a significant increase after 2015 (APC: +2.44; p < 0.005). Limitations include the ecological design inherent to CDC WONDER analysis, reliance on ICD-10 coding accuracy, inability to control for treatment intensity or blood pressure control status at individual level, and absence of data on antihypertensive medication use.

Implications Medical Affairs : These findings reinforce the substantial and persistent cardiovascular mortality burden attributable to uncontrolled hypertension, especially in racially and geographically underserved US populations, directly supporting the ongoing unmet medical need that antihypertensive therapies must address. From a Medical Affairs perspective, this evidence base directly supports advocacy for equitable access to evidence-based antihypertensive care, particularly for non-Hispanic Black communities and rural Southern populations, and can be leveraged in health equity scientific communications and payer value dossiers. The regional resurgence of mortality in the Midwest post-2017 may signal a quality-of-care gap that targeted KOL engagement programs and medical education initiatives should specifically address.

Association Between Telemedicine Use and Outpatient Visits Among Hypertensive Patients With Disabilities During COVID-19.

Revue : Inquiry | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-17

Resultats principaux : This quasi-experimental study utilised 2019-2020 Korean National Health Insurance Service claims data to evaluate telemedicine utilisation for hypertension management among patients with disabilities during the COVID-19 pandemic, following the Korean government's emergency authorisation of non-face-to-face care from February 24, 2020. Among hypertensive patients with disabilities identified in 2019 (N = approximately 645,213 in the control group), 9,254 accessed telemedicine in 2020, and 1:1 propensity score matching (PSM) yielded 9,170 patients in each group. Multivariable regression showed that telemedicine users had 0.57 fewer outpatient visits compared to controls (0.30 after PSM adjustment), and difference-in-differences (DID) analysis revealed a further reduction of 0.9 outpatient visits in the telemedicine group compared to controls (1.0 after PSM). These results suggest telemedicine effectively substitutes for in-person visits rather than generating additional care, indicating potential efficiency gains in managing hypertensive patients with access barriers. Key limitations include the quasi-experimental rather than randomised design, reliance on administrative claims data which captures visit frequency rather than clinical outcomes such as blood pressure control or cardiovascular events, and the restriction to a disability population during a unique pandemic context that limits generalisability.

Implications Medical Affairs : These findings demonstrate that telemedicine can effectively substitute for in-person outpatient visits in hypertensive patients with disabilities, reducing the access burden for a vulnerable population, reinforcing the role of digital health in supporting adherence and continuity of antihypertensive care. From a Medical Affairs standpoint, this adds to the growing evidence base supporting remote monitoring and telehealth-integrated antihypertensive management strategies, relevant for companies seeking to position their products alongside digital adherence-enhancing solutions. Medical Affairs and Market Access teams should monitor evolving telemedicine regulatory frameworks, as countries formalising non-face-to-face prescribing for hypertension management represent growth markets for adherence-supporting therapeutic combinations.

Use of Digital Clinical Decision Support System Increases Adherence to Provision of Evidence-Based Interventions for Elevated Intracranial Pressure During Simulated Care of Patients with Traumatic Brain Injury.

Revue : Prehospital and Disaster Medicine | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-17

Resultats principaux : This randomised crossover simulation study (N = 24 military clinician pairs) evaluated the effect of a digital clinical decision support (CDS) tool embedded within the US Air Force BATDOK clinical software on adherence to Joint Trauma System (JTS) guidelines for management of elevated intracranial pressure (ICP) in severe traumatic brain injury (TBI) during simulated fixed-wing air transport. The primary outcome of percentage of critical care items completed did not differ significantly between usual care (UC) and CDS-augmented arms (median 83.3% vs. 91.7%; p = 0.58; median difference 4.2%, 95% CI -8.1% to 16.4%). However, the CDS arm demonstrated improved adherence to the tiered sequential guideline intervention framework prescribed by the JTS Clinical Practice Guideline (CPG), suggesting qualitative improvement in guideline-concordant sequencing even without a statistically significant primary outcome difference. The System Usability Scale (SUS) score for the BATDOK CDS platform was 77.6 (SD = 16.0), corresponding to a "good" usability rating, with most participants rating the tool favourably on every item. Key limitations include the small sample size (N = 24), simulation environment not perfectly replicating real-world conditions, and the crossover design's susceptibility to learning and carryover effects between scenarios.

Implications Medical Affairs : Although focused on traumatic brain injury and intracranial pressure rather than systemic arterial hypertension, this study provides valuable translational evidence that digital clinical decision support tools can improve adherence to evidence-based guideline sequencing in acute pressure-management scenarios, a concept directly applicable to hypertension management CDS development. The positive usability findings and trend toward improved tiered guideline concordance have implications for Medical Affairs teams supporting digital health companion tools designed to enhance hypertension guideline adherence at the point of care. KOL engagement around digital clinical decision support for hypertension management may benefit from cross-referencing this military-context proof-of-concept as part of a broader scientific narrative on technology-assisted guideline implementation.

Validity and feasibility of the simplified dietary inflammatory index in Chinese older adults.

Revue : Scientific Reports | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-17

Resultats principaux : This validation study developed and assessed a simplified dietary inflammatory index (S-DII) derived from a 25-item food frequency questionnaire (FFQ25) in a primary sample of 269 community-dwelling older adults in China, with subsequent applicability testing in an independent cohort of 983 older adults. The S-DII showed moderate concordance with a full DII calculated from 24-hour dietary recall data (Pearson r = 0.640, intraclass correlation coefficient = 0.615, p < 0.05), supported by acceptable Bland-Altman agreement. Among inflammatory markers, CRP and IL-1beta were positively associated with higher S-DII scores after covariate adjustment, while TNF-alpha, IL-4, IL-6, and IL-10 showed no significant associations, suggesting partial but not full inflammatory marker validation. In the larger applicability cohort (n = 983), higher S-DII scores were significantly associated with increased odds of hypertension and coronary heart disease after covariate adjustment, demonstrating predictive validity for cardiometabolic outcomes. Limitations include the cross-sectional design which precludes causal inference, the population specificity (elderly Chinese community dwellers from Ningxia) which limits generalisability, and reliance on self-reported dietary intake subject to recall bias.

Implications Medical Affairs : The validation of a simplified dietary inflammatory index that independently predicts hypertension risk offers a practical and scalable screening tool for population-level primary prevention programs, positioning dietary inflammatory potential as a modifiable risk factor that could inform earlier pharmacological intervention decisions. From a Medical Affairs perspective, this data supports the inclusion of dietary pattern assessment in hypertension risk stratification, potentially enriching patient profiling frameworks used to identify candidates for early antihypertensive treatment initiation. Medical Affairs teams could leverage this evidence in scientific exchanges with KOLs focused on lifestyle medicine and preventive cardiology to reinforce the integrative prevention narrative around antihypertensive therapy.

Occupational factors and sex differences after myocardial infarction in working-age adults in Spain: a population-based cross-sectional study.

Revue : BMJ Open | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-17

Resultats principaux : This population-based cross-sectional study used data from the 2020 Spanish European Health Interview Survey to estimate MI prevalence and examine associated risk factors in working-age adults, identifying 80 MI cases among 400 matched participants, corresponding to an estimated 137,646 working-age adults with MI history in Spain and a prevalence of 5.45 per 1,000 population (95% CI: 5.44-5.48). MI prevalence was predominantly male (74.2%; mean age 52.7 years), and lower educational attainment was associated with higher MI odds (OR 0.50; 95% CI 0.25-1.0) while no occupational factors remained independently associated after adjustment. Sex-stratified multivariate logistic regression revealed that hypertension was significantly associated with MI specifically in men (OR 4.11; 95% CI 2.24-7.62), whereas depression (OR 6.75; 95% CI 2.23-20.4), obesity (OR 6.36; 95% CI 1.62-24.9), and COPD (OR 27.7; 95% CI 4.21-749) were more strongly associated in women. No employment-related factors independently predicted MI after full adjustment, suggesting that social determinants and individual comorbidity profiles predominate over occupational exposures. Limitations include the cross-sectional design precluding causality, a relatively small number of MI cases (n = 80), and potential self-report bias for both MI history and risk factor status.

Implications Medical Affairs : The sex-specific finding that hypertension confers a 4-fold higher odds of MI specifically in men in this working-age Spanish population highlights the critical need for sex-differentiated hypertension risk stratification and more aggressive blood pressure management in working-age males. From a Medical Affairs standpoint, this evidence supports incorporating gender-specific cardiovascular risk messaging in scientific communications around antihypertensive treatment initiation thresholds, particularly for male populations where hypertension appears to be the dominant modifiable MI risk factor. KOL strategy in preventive cardiology should emphasise gender-specific comorbidity profiling to identify the male hypertensive subpopulation at highest absolute risk and to prioritise intensive antihypertensive management in this group.

Advancing Heart Ageing in Ischemic Heart Disease: Insights from CMR and ECG Analysis.

Revue : Journal of Cardiovascular Magnetic Resonance | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-17

Resultats principaux : This study estimated biological heart age using cardiac magnetic resonance (CMR) radiomics and ECG features in 2,142 prevalent ischemic heart disease (IHD) cases from the UK Biobank, calculating a heart age gap (HAG) as the disparity between predicted and actual cardiac age. IHD subjects had significantly higher estimated heart age than controls (HAG: 1.55 +/- 5.66 years; p < 0.001), with the radiomics and ECG features linked to heart ageing in IHD characterising a phenotype of structural and electrical remodelling distinct from normal ageing trajectories. Among vascular risk factors (VRFs), adiposity and hypertension were significantly and independently associated with increasing HAG in IHD, while conventional CMR indices (ejection fraction, volumes) explained only a negligible fraction of the IHD-HAG association. The multi-modal approach integrating CMR radiomics with ECG-derived phenotypes captured imaging and electrical features beyond standard CMR measures, suggesting the HAG model provides richer phenotypic characterisation of cardiovascular ageing. Limitations include the observational cross-sectional UK Biobank design, absence of longitudinal HAG trajectory data, and the requirement for prospective clinical validation before deployment as a surrogate endpoint.

Implications Medical Affairs : The identification of hypertension as a significant independent driver of accelerated biological heart ageing in ischemic heart disease, independent of conventional CMR indices, provides mechanistic support for aggressive blood pressure control as a strategy to mitigate cardiac ageing trajectories beyond haemodynamic targets. For Medical Affairs, the HAG framework based on multi-modal cardiac imaging could serve as a compelling novel surrogate endpoint concept for antihypertensive clinical trials, reinforcing the argument that blood pressure reduction benefits extend to preserving myocardial structural and electrical integrity. KOL engagement with cardiac imaging specialists and UK Biobank-affiliated researchers represents an opportunity to co-develop data packages incorporating HAG metrics in antihypertensive outcome studies, particularly in patients with established IHD.

Dyslipidemia across the menopause transition: Mechanisms, trajectories, and opportunities for cardiovascular prevention.

Revue : Maturitas | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-17

Resultats principaux : This narrative review from Mayo Clinic authors synthesises evidence on lipid metabolism changes during the menopause transition, documenting significant menopause-specific increases in LDL-C and triglycerides alongside qualitative HDL-C compositional changes that reduce cardioprotective function, occurring independently of chronological aging with the most pronounced lipid shifts within one year of the final menstrual period. The review evaluates evidence supporting lifestyle interventions, with Mediterranean and DASH (Dietary Approaches to Stop Hypertension) dietary patterns and regular aerobic or resistance exercise identified as effective strategies to mitigate these adverse lipid changes, alongside smoking cessation and alcohol reduction. Statins are confirmed as first-line pharmacotherapy for dyslipidemia management in this population when lifestyle measures are insufficient, and menopausal hormone therapy is noted to favourably affect lipid profiles but is not recommended for dyslipidemia or CVD prevention given inconsistent risk-benefit evidence. The DASH dietary pattern, originally developed to address hypertension, is highlighted as an evidence-based dietary strategy with dual benefit for lipid management and blood pressure control during menopause, linking hypertension prevention directly to this CVD risk window. Limitations of this review include the narrative rather than systematic design introducing potential selection bias, the complexity of separating menopause-specific from chronological aging-related lipid changes, and the limited number of randomised trials specifically in the menopausal transition period.

Implications Medical Affairs : This review highlights the menopause transition as a critical and under-recognised high-risk window for cardiovascular disease onset, where the convergence of dyslipidemia, hypertension risk, and hormonal change creates a compound cardiometabolic risk profile requiring proactive multimodal prevention strategies. Medical Affairs teams supporting antihypertensive products should recognise the peri- and post-menopausal population as a priority demographic for hypertension screening and early treatment initiation, and leverage the DASH diet evidence to position antihypertensive products within comprehensive cardiovascular prevention messaging tailored to women. Collaborative KOL strategy with menopause specialists, women's cardiovascular health advocates, and lipidologists would strengthen integrated prevention communications that frame blood pressure management as central to menopausal CVD risk reduction.

Date de publication : 2026-07-17

Resultats principaux : This large cross-sectional study analysed data from 163,770 Iranians aged 35-70 years enrolled in the PERSIAN (Prospective Epidemiological Research Studies in IrAN) cohort from 18 regions between 2014 and 2020, calculating a weighted dyslipidemia prevalence of 73.2% (95% CI: 71.5-75.0%). Methodological strengths include the large nationally representative sample size (n > 163,000) and prospective population-based design; key limitations include the cross-sectional nature of lipid measurements, potential self-report bias in covariate data, and the regional variability in Iranian population characteristics.

Implications Medical Affairs : The extremely high dyslipidemia prevalence (73.2%) in this large Middle Eastern population, with hypertension as a significant independent correlate, highlights the magnitude of compound cardiovascular risk in MENA region countries and the critical unmet need for integrated cardiometabolic risk management programmes. Effectiveness of Telehealth Low-Carbohydrate Intervention in Preventing Chronic Kidney Disease: A Real-World, Retrospective, Matched Cohort Study.

Revue : Diabetes, Obesity & Metabolism | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-16

Resultats principaux : This retrospective propensity score-matched cohort study used the Komodo Healthcare Map US administrative claims database to compare 11,077 adults enrolled in a telehealth-delivered individualized low-carbohydrate nutrition therapy program (VINT) against 11,077 matched usual-care controls matched on demographic, clinical, and medication covariates over 5 years of follow-up. VINT participation was significantly associated with lower incidence of new-onset CKD (10.1 vs. 15.6 per 1,000 person-years; HR 0.64, 95% CI 0.53-0.77; p < 0.001), CKD stage 3 or above (HR 0.57, 95% CI 0.45-0.73; p < 0.001), and CKD stage 4 or above (HR 0.38, 95% CI 0.18-0.79; p = 0.009). No increased risk of adverse renal safety events including kidney stones, metabolic acidosis, diabetic ketoacidosis, or gout was observed in the VINT group. The population primarily comprised adults with type 2 diabetes and obesity, in whom hypertension is a major comorbidity and a principal driver of CKD progression, making renal protection through dietary lifestyle intervention directly relevant to hypertension-associated nephropathy. Limitations include the retrospective non-randomised design with potential residual confounding despite propensity matching, reliance on insurance claims rather than laboratory or clinical outcome data, and the industry affiliation of the leading authors (Virta Health, the company delivering VINT).

Implications Medical Affairs : The significant reduction in CKD incidence and progression achieved through a telehealth-delivered low-carbohydrate dietary intervention in a metabolic disease population is directly relevant to hypertension management, where CKD represents both a target organ complication and a cardiovascular risk amplifier, reinforcing the value of integrated lifestyle plus pharmacotherapy approaches. From a Medical Affairs perspective, this real-world evidence supports positioning antihypertensive therapies with nephroprotective indications within a broader cardiorenal-metabolic framework, where lifestyle interventions and antihypertensive pharmacotherapy act synergistically to protect renal function in high-risk patients. KOL scientific engagement should explore how renal outcome data from dietary intervention studies can complement and potentially enhance the positioning and clinical narrative of antihypertensive agents with established nephroprotective profiles, particularly in diabetic hypertensive populations.

Evidence map of Tai Chi interventions for older adults.

Revue : Frontiers in Public Health | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-02

Resultats principaux : This evidence map following The Campbell Collaboration methodology synthesised 118 systematic reviews identified from 7,862 records addressing the effects of Tai Chi on health outcomes in older adults (mean age 69.5 years, predominantly female, community-dwelling), with quality assessed using AMSTAR 2. Of 238 identified health effects, 171 were classified as positive, 47 as potentially positive, 13 showed no effect, and 7 were inconclusive, yielding a predominantly favourable profile across most health domains. High-quality evidence supported positive effects of Tai Chi on balance, depression, anxiety, cognitive performance, fall risk reduction, quality of life, well-being, sleep quality, and Parkinson's disease management, establishing a strong evidence base for these outcomes. Critically, the evidence for Tai Chi's effect on hypertension and coronary heart disease was classified as inconclusive or showing no effect, directly challenging popular beliefs about Tai Chi's cardiovascular and antihypertensive benefits. The most widely studied protocol was the Yang-style Tai Chi performed 2-3 times per week for 30-60 minutes per session over 12-24 weeks; quality assessment found heterogeneity with 36 high, 7 moderate, 42 low, and 33 critically-low quality reviews, limiting confidence in some findings.

Implications Medical Affairs : The finding that current evidence does not support a definitive beneficial effect of Tai Chi on hypertension outcomes is scientifically important and directly relevant to Medical Affairs, as it counters the growing perception that mind-body exercises can meaningfully substitute for pharmacological antihypertensive treatment in older adults. Medical Affairs teams should be aware of this evidence map when engaging with KOLs, payers, or patient advocacy groups who may overestimate the blood pressure-lowering potential of Tai Chi, ensuring scientific communications clearly maintain pharmacological therapy as the cornerstone of blood pressure management. This evidence gap also represents a research opportunity: well-designed randomised trials specifically examining Tai Chi as an adjunct to antihypertensive medications in defined older adult hypertensive populations could fill the gap, and Medical Affairs teams could champion such investigator-initiated research to generate evidence that contextualises complementary practice within guideline-directed pharmacotherapy.
