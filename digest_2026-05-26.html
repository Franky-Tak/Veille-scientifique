Association between the triglyceride-glucose index and hypertension: potential role of fatty liver in a cross-sectional study.

Revue : Frontiers in Endocrinology | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-05-08

Resultats principaux : This large-scale cross-sectional study enrolled 115,327 participants who underwent routine health examinations at the Third Affiliated Hospital of Wenzhou Medical University between 2021 and 2025, representing one of the largest datasets examining the TyG-hypertension relationship. The primary endpoint was the association between the triglyceride-glucose (TyG) index, a validated surrogate marker of insulin resistance, and the prevalence of hypertension as defined by 2023 European Society of Hypertension criteria. Each 1-unit increase in the TyG index was associated with significantly higher odds of hypertension (adjusted OR 2.09, 95% CI 2.03–2.15), and simultaneously with much higher odds of fatty liver (adjusted OR 7.05, 95% CI 6.82–7.28). Formal mediation analysis showed that fatty liver statistically accounted for approximately 23.50% (95% CI 21.39%–25.96%) of the total association between the TyG index and hypertension. A notable limitation is the cross-sectional design, which precludes causal inference, and the use of abdominal ultrasonography for fatty liver diagnosis, which carries operator-dependent variability.

Implications Medical Affairs : This study reinforces the role of insulin resistance and non-alcoholic fatty liver disease as mechanistic intermediaries on the pathway to hypertension, with direct implications for guideline updates that may incorporate metabolic markers such as TyG in cardiovascular risk stratification algorithms. For pharmaceutical positioning, drugs that reduce insulin resistance or target hepatic lipid accumulation may demonstrate downstream blood pressure benefits, opening a compelling cardiometabolic story relevant to KOL engagement. Future prospective and interventional studies targeting the TyG-fatty liver-hypertension axis represent a high-priority research agenda worth advancing through investigator-initiated grant support.

Associations of Life's Essential 8 With Mortality Among Individuals With Diabetes and/or Hypertension: Statistical Mediation by Inflammation and Biological Aging.

Revue : Mediators of Inflammation | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026

Resultats principaux : This longitudinal analysis used NHANES data from 2005 to 2018 and included 4,939 individuals with diabetes, 13,298 with hypertension, and 3,303 with both conditions, with mortality ascertained through linkage to the National Death Index. The study evaluated whether the American Heart Association's Life's Essential 8 (LE8) composite cardiovascular health score was associated with all-cause and heart disease mortality in these high-risk groups. Higher LE8 scores were significantly associated with reduced all-cause mortality and heart disease mortality across all disease groups (p less than 0.001), with stronger effects among individuals aged 60 years or younger and those with higher socioeconomic status. Mediation analyses revealed that phenotypic age acceleration (PhenoAgeAccel) accounted for 56.3% of the LE8-all-cause mortality association in the hypertension group, while inflammatory markers (NLR accounting for 31.6%; PIV for 26.9%) were dominant mediators in the diabetes group. Key limitations include the self-reported nature of several LE8 components, the cross-sectional LE8 measurement unable to capture dynamic changes, and potential residual confounding from unmeasured socioeconomic or behavioral variables.

Implications Medical Affairs : These data provide biological mechanistic support for the cardiovascular benefit of comprehensive lifestyle-based interventions in hypertensive patients, suggesting that interventions slowing biological aging could be particularly impactful in this population relative to anti-inflammatory strategies. From a Medical Affairs standpoint, products with documented effects on biological aging biomarkers or inflammatory mediators in hypertension could leverage this framework to strengthen their value proposition in real-world evidence discussions with payers and guideline committees. Engaging KOLs around PhenoAgeAccel as an emerging endpoint in hypertension trials would position the company at the frontier of cardiometabolic precision medicine.

Association of self-reported birth weight and preterm birth with blood pressure measures and risk for hypertension in older women from the Women's Health Initiative.

Revue : Journal of Developmental Origins of Health and Disease | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-05-25

Resultats principaux : Drawing on the large Women's Health Initiative (WHI) observational cohort of post-menopausal women, this study examined associations between self-reported birth weight categories and preterm birth status with prevalent hypertension, incident hypertension, and quantitative blood pressure measures including systolic BP, diastolic BP, and 30-second pulse. After multivariable adjustment, women born weighing less than 6 pounds had significantly higher mean systolic blood pressure and were at increased risk for both prevalent and incident hypertension compared to those born at a normal birth weight of 6 to 7 pounds 15 ounces. In contrast, women born weighing 10 pounds or more had lower mean systolic BP and reduced risk for prevalent and incident hypertension compared to those born at normal weight, suggesting a U-shaped or inverse dose-response relationship. Preterm birth was independently associated with greater risk of prevalent and incident hypertension, though this relationship attenuated when simultaneously adjusting for birth weight, indicating partial overlap between these early-life risk factors. Limitations include reliance on self-reported birth weight and preterm status, recall bias in an older female population, and the restriction of findings to post-menopausal women which limits generalizability to male populations and younger cohorts.

Implications Medical Affairs : These findings from a large prospective cohort add epidemiological weight to developmental origins hypotheses for hypertension, suggesting that birth history should be considered in adult cardiovascular risk assessment and potentially incorporated into hypertension screening guidelines for at-risk populations. Medical Affairs teams can use these data to support educational initiatives with cardiologists and obstetricians emphasizing the lifelong cardiovascular implications of premature birth, which may extend to advocacy for more intensive BP monitoring in adults with low-birth-weight history. This evidence also supports the scientific rationale for earlier pharmacological intervention or intensified lifestyle counseling in women with this phenotypic background, creating an opportunity for differentiated patient-centric messaging.

Reliability and Safety of Smartwatch Blood Pressure and Oxygen Saturation Measurements in Older Adults: Instrument Validation Study.

Revue : JMIR Formative Research | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-05-22

Resultats principaux : This validation study enrolled 50 participants aged 50 to 89 years (mean age 70.6 years, 68% female) to compare blood pressure and arterial oxygen saturation measurements from the Samsung Galaxy Watch 6 against standard ambulatory BP monitoring and pulse oximetry reference devices. Three simultaneous BP measurements were obtained per participant under controlled, resting conditions, and agreement was assessed using paired t-tests and intraclass correlation coefficients (ICC). No significant differences were found between the smartwatch and reference device for systolic BP (p = 0.31) or diastolic BP (p = 0.88), with good agreement for both parameters (ICC = 0.88 for both systolic and diastolic BP). Arterial oxygen saturation showed no significant interdevice difference (p = 0.10) but only moderate agreement (ICC = 0.68), and heart rate comparisons also demonstrated good agreement without significant differences (p = 0.54). Limitations include the small sample size (n = 50), exclusive testing under stable resting conditions which may not reflect ambulatory performance, and the exclusion of patients with decompensated clinical conditions, limiting generalizability to acutely ill or arrhythmic patients.

Implications Medical Affairs : This validation evidence supports the growing case for consumer-grade wearable devices as adjunct monitoring tools in hypertension management, particularly for older adults who are the primary hypertension burden population, and may inform upcoming guideline revisions regarding out-of-office BP measurement modalities. From a product and pipeline perspective, companies developing digital health solutions or combination device-drug hypertension management programs should track this validation literature closely, as regulatory agencies increasingly require real-world accuracy data for wearable-integrated monitoring claims. Effects of Remimazolam Dose and Bolus Injection Rate on Hiccups During Painless Colonoscopy: A Double-Blind Randomized Clinical Trial.

Revue : Drug Design, Development and Therapy | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-05-18

Resultats principaux : This prospective, double-blind, 2x2 factorial randomized controlled trial randomized adult patients undergoing painless colonoscopy to receive remimazolam at either 0.3 or 0.4 mg/kg administered over 10 or 20 seconds, with hiccup incidence as the primary endpoint and haemodynamic parameters as key secondary endpoints. After multivariable logistic regression adjusting for age, sex, BMI, smoking status, hypertension, and diabetes, both the higher remimazolam dose (0.4 mg/kg) and the slower injection rate (20 seconds) independently reduced hiccup incidence without a significant dose-rate interaction. The higher dose regimen was further associated with fewer body movements and reduced need for supplemental boluses, without increasing haemodynamic compromise, suggesting a favorable safety profile for hypertensive patients who are at particular risk from haemodynamic fluctuation during sedation. This study is noteworthy in the hypertension context because hypertension was explicitly controlled for as an adjustment variable, acknowledging its impact on sedation pharmacodynamics and procedural risk. Limitations include absence of detailed reporting of haemodynamic data stratified by hypertension status, and the focus on a single procedural setting that may not generalize to other sedation contexts.

Implications Medical Affairs : While this trial addresses procedural sedation rather than antihypertensive pharmacotherapy directly, its formal adjustment for hypertension status underscores the clinical relevance of blood pressure control as a modifier of procedural sedation outcomes, which may inform anesthetic management guidelines for hypertensive patients. Efficacy of Cabozantinib in Patients With Advanced Renal Cell Carcinoma With Bone Metastasis Assessed by Modified MD Anderson Criteria: An Open-Label Phase 2 Study.

Revue : International Journal of Urology | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-05

Resultats principaux : This multicenter, open-label phase 2 study enrolled 31 adult Japanese patients with advanced renal cell carcinoma (aRCC) and bone metastasis, all receiving oral cabozantinib 60 mg once daily, with primary endpoint of objective response rate (ORR) of bone target lesions assessed by an independent radiology committee using modified MD Anderson criteria. The ORR for bone lesions was 6.5% (90% CI 1.2–18.9%) while the disease control rate was notably higher at 90.3% (90% CI 76.8–97.3%), with a median progression-free survival of 11.9 months and median overall survival of 24.9 months, indicating meaningful disease stabilization. Bone turnover markers generally decreased during treatment, and health-related quality of life measured by EQ-5D-5L remained generally stable throughout the study. Limitations include the small sample size (n = 31), open-label design, exclusively Japanese patient population limiting generalizability, and the absence of a comparator arm, precluding definitive efficacy conclusions.

Implications Medical Affairs : The 32.3% hypertension incidence with cabozantinib highlights the critical need for structured blood pressure monitoring and management protocols in patients receiving VEGF-pathway inhibitors, a gap that oncology-cardiology collaborative care programs could address. Motivational interviewing chatbot improves lifestyle in primary healthcare settings in a pragmatic randomised controlled trial.

Revue : NPJ Digital Medicine | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-05-25

Resultats principaux : This pragmatic, open-label, multicentre randomised controlled trial conducted across three Hong Kong primary healthcare facilities enrolled 627 adults aged 45–75 with or at risk for hypertension or diabetes, randomising them to a 12-week automated motivational interviewing (MI) chatbot plus usual care versus usual care alone, with modified intention-to-treat analysis applied to 460 participants. At 12 weeks, the chatbot group showed statistically significant improvements in physical activity (+576 MET-min/week), fruit and vegetable intake (+0.27 portions/day), and committed action (+0.95 a.u.) compared to usual care alone, with dose-response effects observed among adherent participants. Crucially for hypertension, exploratory subgroup analyses in participants with low baseline physical activity demonstrated strong physical activity improvements (+537 MET-min/week), and hypertensive participants showed a clinically meaningful systolic blood pressure reduction of -5.03 mmHg compared to controls. These lifestyle and BP improvements were sustained at the 9-month follow-up, and no serious adverse events were reported across either group. Limitations include the open-label design which precludes participant blinding, the pragmatic trial setting which reduces internal validity, and the exploratory nature of the BP subgroup analysis which was not a pre-specified primary endpoint.

Implications Medical Affairs : A scalable, AI-driven MI chatbot achieving clinically meaningful SBP reductions of 5 mmHg in hypertensive patients without pharmacological intervention supports the integration of digital therapeutic adjuncts into hypertension management pathways, potentially complementing antihypertensive pharmacotherapy in treatment-naive or pre-hypertension populations. Revue : Journal of Epidemiology and Global Health | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-05-25

Resultats principaux : This hospital-based cross-sectional case-control study conducted in Dakahlia, Egypt, enrolled 140 IBS patients diagnosed using Rome IV criteria and 140 matched non-IBS controls from a healthcare center, comparing the prevalence of metabolic syndrome (MetS) as defined by International Diabetes Federation criteria, with hypertension defined as BP greater than or equal to 130/85 mmHg as one of its five components. IBS patients showed a significantly higher prevalence of metabolic syndrome at 49.3% compared to 17.1% in controls (p less than 0.001), with elevated blood pressure occurring in 57.9% of IBS cases versus 17.9% of controls. Implications Medical Affairs : The markedly elevated prevalence of hypertension (57.9%) in IBS patients relative to controls positions this gastrointestinal population as an underappreciated high-risk group for cardiovascular disease, warranting routine BP screening in GI specialty care settings and representing a potential expanded screening opportunity. Resultats principaux : This narrative review synthesizes evidence linking soil contamination — including heavy metals, pesticide residues, persistent organic compounds, and microplastics in agricultural systems and food chains — to cardiovascular and neurological outcomes including hypertension, atherosclerosis, ischemic stroke, cognitive impairment, and neurodegeneration. Mechanistically, the authors describe how chronic ingestion of soil-derived toxicants promotes oxidative stress, mitochondrial dysfunction, endothelial injury, and neuroinflammation, while disrupting calcium signaling, lipid metabolism, and vascular autoregulation through endocrine-disrupting chemical activity, particularly via aryl hydrocarbon receptor (AhR) activation. The authors propose a plausible pathophysiologic framework in which contaminated soils elevate hypertension and cardiovascular risk at the population level through mechanisms that remain undercharacterized compared to air and water pollution. Key limitations include the narrative rather than systematic review methodology, reliance on heterogeneous evidence from animal models and epidemiological association studies, and the absence of interventional data establishing causal soil-to-disease pathways.

Implications Medical Affairs : This review highlights soil contamination as an emerging upstream environmental determinant of hypertension that has received far less regulatory and scientific attention than air pollution, and represents an opportunity for the pharmaceutical industry to engage in broader environmental health advocacy that aligns with cardiovascular disease prevention messaging. Date de publication : 2026-05-15

Resultats principaux : This study used data from Stony Brook Hospital to assess healthcare encounters during the June 2023 Canadian wildfire smoke event in New York State, combining event-study analysis with a long-term time-series analysis covering January 2014 through July 2023 to examine the effect of wildfire-associated particulate matter (PM2.5) on hospital admissions across cardiovascular, hypertension, respiratory, and asthma categories. Implications Medical Affairs : A 47% increased rate of hypertension hospital visits during acute wildfire smoke events provides compelling evidence that environmental air quality crises translate directly into a hypertension-driven healthcare burden surge, reinforcing calls for proactive patient identification and management protocols during smoke events. Implications Medical Affairs : The finding that hypertension acts as a priming factor amplifying heat-induced subclinical cardiovascular injury — even in treated patients — provides an urgent scientific rationale for heightened BP control targets and monitoring during heatwaves, a message directly aligned with personalized precision medicine narratives for antihypertensive therapy optimization. Revue : Health Promotion Practice | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-05-24

Resultats principaux : This qualitative study used a phenomenological approach informed by the Health Belief Model to examine individual preferences and perceived barriers to participation in chronic disease evidence-based programs (CDEBPs), conducting 38 semi-structured interviews with a highly diverse community sample recruited from multiple communities in Los Angeles County, including individuals with or eligible for programs addressing prediabetes, diabetes, hypertension, and high cholesterol. Participants identified a broad range of factors influencing their willingness to join and complete CDEBPs, including program cost, session duration, total program length, scheduling and timing, delivery location, in-person versus online format, whether the facilitator was a healthcare professional or peer educator, individual versus group class format, who recommended the program, availability of childcare, transportation, and incentives, and the perceived severity of their chronic condition. The perceived seriousness of hypertension and diabetes as chronic conditions was identified as a key motivational driver for program participation, while logistical barriers such as cost, transportation, and scheduling conflicts were the most commonly cited obstacles to completion. No quantitative efficacy or outcome data were reported, as this was a purely qualitative preference-elicitation study. Limitations include the geographic restriction to Los Angeles County communities, potential response bias in the recruitment of community members already somewhat engaged with health programs, and the inability to generalize qualitative findings to other cultural or regional contexts.

Implications Medical Affairs : Understanding that perceived disease severity and logistical access barriers are the primary drivers of CDEBP enrollment and retention in hypertension populations has direct implications for designing patient support programs and medication adherence initiatives that address practical barriers rather than solely focusing on clinical education.
