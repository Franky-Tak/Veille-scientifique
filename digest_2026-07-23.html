Profiles of medication literacy and associated factors among young and middle-aged hypertensive patients in China: A latent profile analysis.

Revue : PloS one | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-22

Resultats principaux : This cross-sectional study enrolled 544 consecutively recruited young and middle-aged hypertensive patients from two tertiary hospitals in China between July and November 2024. Researchers administered five validated instruments including the Revised Chinese Medication Literacy Scale for Hypertensive Patients (C-MLSHP-R), the Perceived Social Support Scale, the General Self-Efficacy Scale, and the Brief Illness Perception Questionnaire. Latent profile analysis identified three distinct medication literacy profiles: low (35.3%), moderate (50.2%), and high (14.5%), demonstrating marked heterogeneity in this population. Multivariate logistic regression identified education level, per capita monthly household income, social support, self-efficacy, and illness perception as significantly associated with literacy profile membership (all p < 0.05). Lower education and income characterized the low-literacy group, while higher social support, self-efficacy, and positive illness perception characterized the high-literacy group; the cross-sectional design precludes causal inference and longitudinal validation is required.

Implications Medical Affairs : These findings reveal that over one-third of young and middle-aged hypertensive patients in China exhibit low medication literacy, directly implicating suboptimal adherence to antihypertensive regimens as a modifiable barrier to blood pressure control. From a Medical Affairs perspective, these data support the development of stratified patient support programs tailored to socioeconomic and psychosocial literacy profiles, particularly targeting the 35.3% in the low-literacy group through foundational medication education initiatives. KOL engagement with Chinese hypertension specialists focused on the patient-education continuum represents a high-value opportunity to advance adherence-improvement strategies aligned with antihypertensive portfolio objectives.

Cardiovascular composite events and the healthy worker effect among noise-exposed workers: a retrospective cohort study.

Revue : Frontiers in public health | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-07

Resultats principaux : This retrospective cohort study enrolled 1,381 noise-exposed workers with normal blood pressure and normal electrocardiogram at baseline in 2020, followed annually through 2023 in China. The primary endpoint was a composite cardiovascular event defined as the onset of hypertension or electrocardiographic abnormality, analyzed using Cox proportional hazards models. Multivariable Cox regression identified systolic blood pressure (HR = 1.012, 95% CI 1.005–1.020, p < 0.001) and smoking (HR = 1.191, 95% CI 1.029–1.377, p = 0.019) as independent risk factors for cardiovascular composite events. Work duration exceeding 120 months showed an apparent protective effect (HR = 0.621, 95% CI 0.466–0.826, p = 0.001) consistent with the healthy worker effect, persisting after exclusion of pre-employment workers (HR = 0.787, p = 0.024). Combined exposure to noise and dust (HR = 1.896, 95% CI 1.219–2.949, p = 0.005) and noise and chemical agents (HR = 1.580, 95% CI 1.013–2.463, p = 0.044) significantly increased hypertension risk specifically; single-center retrospective design and healthy worker effect bias are key limitations.

Implications Medical Affairs : This study highlights occupational noise exposure — compounded by co-exposures to dust or chemicals — as a modifiable environmental risk factor for incident hypertension in working-age populations, broadening the epidemiological target landscape for antihypertensive prevention programs. From a Medical Affairs standpoint, these data strengthen the case for occupational health-focused hypertension screening protocols in high-risk workplaces, an underserved segment of the patient journey where early antihypertensive intervention could meaningfully reduce incident cardiovascular disease. KOL partnerships with occupational medicine and public health specialists represent an actionable engagement strategy to align antihypertensive treatment guidelines with occupational cardiovascular risk management frameworks.

Chronic Kidney Disease Frequently Observed in Adults With Spina Bifida.

Revue : International journal of urology | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07

Resultats principaux : This retrospective study compared adults with spina bifida (group A, n = 34) to patients with pediatric encephalitis or spinal cord injury (group B, n = 23), all aged over 18 years, analyzed between January 2013 and August 2022 at a Japanese tertiary center. CKD-free survival was significantly shorter in the spina bifida group compared to controls (median 48.0 vs. 65.0 years, p = 0.0027), indicating earlier renal deterioration in this population. In the spina bifida group, CKD was significantly associated with pediatric febrile urinary tract infection (p = 0.0018), vesico-ureteral reflux (p = 0.009), current hypertension (p < 0.001), and albuminuria (p = 0.0053) in univariate analyses. Multivariate analysis identified hypertension as the sole independently significant factor associated with CKD in adults with spina bifida, underscoring blood pressure as a critical modifiable driver. Small sample sizes and single-center retrospective design represent important limitations that constrain generalizability.

Implications Medical Affairs : This study reinforces hypertension as the critical independent driver of CKD progression in adults with spina bifida, a vulnerable population with neurogenic bladder dysfunction and chronic urological comorbidities where blood pressure management may be underoptimized. From a Medical Affairs perspective, these data build a compelling nephroprotective narrative for antihypertensive therapies — particularly RAAS inhibitors — in neurological patients with concomitant urological risk factors, extending product relevance beyond classic hypertension populations. KOL engagement targeting nephrologists, urologists, and neurologists managing adult congenital conditions should be pursued, with advocacy for integrating structured blood pressure monitoring into transition-of-care protocols for spina bifida patients.

Blood Pressure Changes During Aging and Menopause Among Forager-Horticulturalists in the Bolivian Amazon.

Revue : American journal of biological anthropology | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07

Resultats principaux : This comparative population study analyzed blood pressure data from 5,570 female and 18,676 male Tsimane forager-horticulturalists from the Bolivian Amazon (ages 15–92) alongside 6,861 US NHANES participants (ages 16–80) using linear regression, logistic regression, and mediation analyses. Post-menopause SBP trajectories accelerated (b = 0.002, p < 0.001) while DBP trajectories declined (b = -0.004, p < 0.001) in Tsimane women, a pattern similar to the US population despite markedly different CVD risk environments. Compared to US women, Tsimane women showed a slightly faster SBP increase with age post-menopause (b = 0.001, p = 0.021) and less dramatic DBP decrease (b = 0.003, p < 0.001), suggesting population-specific but directionally consistent blood pressure aging. Postmenopausal women had lower odds of hypertension with age compared to premenopausal women in both populations, and estradiol did not mediate the blood pressure effects of menopause. Cross-population comparison across markedly different CVD risk environments is a key limitation that requires caution in generalizing results to industrialized settings.

Implications Medical Affairs : This cross-cultural study provides evidence that blood pressure changes associated with menopause may represent a universal biological process rather than a purely environmentally mediated phenomenon, challenging assumptions that menopause-related CVD risk is avoidable through lifestyle modification alone. From a Medical Affairs perspective, these findings support the scientific rationale for initiating antihypertensive monitoring and treatment at the time of menopausal transition across diverse populations, reinforcing blood pressure management as an unmet need in women's cardiovascular health independent of lifestyle context. Future research on whether antihypertensive efficacy or pharmacokinetics differ by menopausal status represents a scientifically differentiated and clinically relevant question for KOL collaboration and clinical trial design.

Mild and non-persistent fasting plasma glucose elevation in the first trimester of pregnancy is not associated with increased risk of gestational diabetes mellitus and adverse pregnancy outcomes.

Revue : Archives of endocrinology and metabolism | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-22

Resultats principaux : This prospective cohort study enrolled 114 pregnant women with first-trimester fasting plasma glucose (FPG) < 100 mg/dL from a Brazilian academic center, comparing three groups: G1 (n=33, early gestational diabetes mellitus with normalized second FPG < 92 mg/dL who underwent OGTT at 24–28 weeks), G2 (n=31, persistent eGDM with second FPG 92–125 mg/dL who received immediate treatment), and G3 control (n=50, FPG < 92 mg/dL throughout). G1 and G3 had significantly lower pre-pregnancy BMI, less chronic hypertension, lower GDM history rates, and lower multiparity compared to G2, confirming that chronic hypertension concentrated in the persistent eGDM subgroup. No significant difference was observed in GDM diagnosis by OGTT between G1 and G3 (27.3% vs. 20.0%, p = 0.594), indicating equivalent metabolic outcomes. G3 gained significantly more weight during pregnancy than G1 and G2 (13.5 ± 6.1 vs. 10.2 ± 7.6 vs. 7.5 ± 6.4 kg; p = 0.001), and all other pregnancy outcomes were similar between G1 and G3. Small sample size and single-center design limit statistical power for secondary outcomes.

Implications Medical Affairs : This study's identification of chronic hypertension as a baseline characteristic concentrating in the persistent early gestational diabetes subgroup reinforces the importance of antihypertensive management in women of reproductive age as part of preconception cardiometabolic risk reduction. From a Medical Affairs standpoint, these data support positioning antihypertensive therapy as a preventive intervention relevant to the gestational metabolic risk continuum, a narrative that resonates with obstetric and endocrinological KOLs managing high-risk pregnancies. Collaborative research with reproductive medicine specialists should explore whether optimized pre-conception blood pressure control mitigates gestational hyperglycemia risk, creating a differentiated clinical message for antihypertensive portfolio positioning in women's health.

Plasma metabolic signatures of healthy dietary patterns and risk of metabolic dysfunction-associated steatotic liver disease and cirrhosis.

Revue : Hepatology international | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-22

Resultats principaux : This large cohort study analyzed 82,259 UK Biobank participants with detailed dietary and metabolomic data to derive plasma metabolic signatures (MSs) for ten healthy dietary patterns using elastic-net regression, primarily comprising fatty acids, lipids, and lipoprotein subclasses (31–116 metabolites per pattern). Across all ten dietary patterns, MSs were consistently and significantly associated with lower risk of metabolic dysfunction-associated steatotic liver disease (MASLD), with hazard ratios ranging from 0.59 to 0.76. MSs for MIND, HPDI, rE-DII, and HLCD dietary patterns were additionally associated with reduced cirrhosis risk (HRs: 0.56 to 0.63). Mendelian randomization supported potential causal relationships between MSs of MED, MIND, HPDI, and EAT-Lancet diets and liver disease, and mediation analyses showed that specific MSs accounted for 20.1%–29.4% of the diet-MASLD association and 25.7%–27.4% of the diet-cirrhosis association. UK Biobank predominantly White British population composition and reliance on self-reported dietary data are key limitations for generalizability.

Implications Medical Affairs : This large-scale mechanistic study demonstrates that lipoprotein and lipid metabolic pathways mediate the protective dietary effect on MASLD risk — pathways that also converge with cardiovascular risk mechanisms relevant to hypertensive patients with metabolic comorbidities. From a Medical Affairs perspective, the MASLD-hypertension-diet nexus reinforces opportunities to position antihypertensive agents with pleiotropic cardiometabolic benefits in the growing MASLD patient population, an increasingly recognized comorbidity in hypertension guidelines. Future KOL-driven translational research exploring whether antihypertensive treatment modifies the metabolic signatures associated with liver and cardiovascular risk represents a scientifically novel and differentiating platform for Medical Affairs.

Occupational-circadian disruption and physical inactivity conjointly amplify fatty liver risk: based on liver ultrasound transient elastography.

Revue : International journal of occupational medicine and environmental health | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-21

Resultats principaux : This cross-sectional study of 653 participants from a Chinese hospital population used liver ultrasound transient elastography (controlled attenuation parameter, CAP) to assess fatty liver disease (FLD), stratifying by work-shift type (floating vs. day shift) and physical activity level. In multivariable-adjusted models, floating shift work independently increased FLD risk (OR = 1.40, p = 0.019), and physically inactive floating-shift workers had the highest FLD risk (OR = 2.01 vs. physically active day-shift workers, p = 0.049), demonstrating a synergistic effect between occupational circadian disruption and behavioral sedentarity. Subgroup analyses identified hypertension (OR = 2.66, p = 0.028), diabetes (OR = 6.21, p = 0.017), older age, and obesity as critical risk amplifiers among floating-shift workers. At moderate physical activity levels (200–400 MET-hours/week), CAP values converged between floating and day-shift workers, suggesting a compensatory activity threshold. Cross-sectional design and single-center recruitment limit causal inference and generalizability.

Implications Medical Affairs : The near-threefold amplification of fatty liver risk by hypertension in circadian-disrupted shift workers highlights cardiometabolic clustering as a critical occupational health challenge, reinforcing blood pressure control as a high-priority intervention in this underserved population. Medical Affairs teams should recognize shift workers with hypertension as a high-risk, frequently overlooked patient subgroup where antihypertensive therapy may confer outsized hepatic and cardiovascular protective benefits beyond blood pressure reduction alone. KOL collaboration with occupational medicine physicians and hepatologists can enable generation of real-world evidence on antihypertensive treatment outcomes in shift-work populations, a novel and commercially differentiated evidence domain.

Uric acid and heart failure.

Revue : Journal of cardiology | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-21

Resultats principaux : This narrative review synthesizes evidence from clinical trials and observational studies — including a US Medicare cohort of approximately 100,000 participants — on hyperuricemia management in patients with heart failure (HF). Hyperuricemia is established as a marker of poor HF prognosis, reflecting disease severity as well as underlying xanthine oxidase activity and oxidative stress that may be therapeutically targetable in selected subgroups. In the large Medicare cohort, patients administered febuxostat showed less aggravation of heart failure compared to those receiving allopurinol, raising the hypothesis of drug-specific cardiovascular properties beyond uric acid reduction per se. The review identifies hypertension and chronic kidney disease as key complications of hyperuricemia warranting uric acid-lowering therapy when lifestyle modification fails to achieve serum uric acid below 8 mg/dL. The authors call for large-scale, high-quality prospective intervention studies on treating hyperuricemia in HF patients, as current evidence remains insufficient for definitive recommendations.

Implications Medical Affairs : This review highlights the bidirectional relationship between hyperuricemia, hypertension, and heart failure — a triad increasingly recognized in the cardiometabolic continuum — reinforcing the need for an integrated management framework in which antihypertensive treatment plays a central role alongside uric acid-lowering therapy. From a Medical Affairs perspective, the differential cardiovascular outcomes observed between febuxostat and allopurinol, combined with the documented contribution of hyperuricemia to hypertension development, create a scientific narrative supporting antihypertensive drug class discussions that account for uric acid effects. Future research priorities should include prospective RCTs investigating whether antihypertensive agents with uric acid-neutral or uric acid-lowering properties offer superior cardiovascular and HF outcomes in hyperuricemic hypertensive patients.

Combined effects of apparent low temperature and PM pollution on circulatory emergency ambulance calls: a time-series analysis in Shijiazhuang, China.

Revue : Frontiers in public health | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-07

Resultats principaux : This time-series analysis included 82,714 cardiovascular emergency ambulance calls (EACs) from Shijiazhuang, China over a 10-year period (2014–2023), assessing daily cardiovascular emergency calls alongside meteorological and PM particulate matter air pollution data using distributed lag non-linear models (DLNM). Apparent low temperature was significantly associated with increased cardiovascular EAC risk with delayed effects, and combined PM-cold exposure further elevated risks under most joint-exposure scenarios. The strongest cumulative joint-exposure effect was observed under the high-PM, low-temperature scenario (risk ratio = 1.146, 95% CI: 1.088–1.207). Subgroup analyses identified hypertensive patients, older adults, and males as the most susceptible subgroups to combined PM-cold exposure, with both synergistic and antagonistic interactions identified at different lag periods. Ecological design with aggregated population-level data precludes individual-level causal inference, and residual confounding from other air pollutants is possible.

Implications Medical Affairs : These findings confirm hypertensive patients as one of the highest-risk subgroups for weather- and air-quality-related cardiovascular emergencies, underscoring the critical importance of consistent antihypertensive treatment adherence during adverse environmental conditions that trigger blood pressure surges. Medical Affairs teams should leverage this epidemiological evidence to support disease awareness campaigns and patient education initiatives focused on seasonal blood pressure management, particularly in high-pollution urban environments where antihypertensive adherence may fluctuate. Collaboration with environmental medicine and cardiovascular epidemiology KOLs can generate real-world evidence on whether optimized antihypertensive control reduces weather-related cardiovascular emergency rates in hypertensive cohorts, a clinically and commercially differentiating evidence gap.

Potential protective effects of Phyllanthus emblica L. extract on high-salt diet-induced hypertension: a combined analysis of gut microbiota and metabolomics.

Revue : Frontiers in pharmacology | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-07

Resultats principaux : This preclinical study used a salt-sensitive rat model to systematically evaluate the effects of Phyllanthus emblica (PE) extract on high-salt diet (HSD)-induced hypertension, assessing blood pressure, inflammatory markers, renal and vascular pathology, intestinal barrier function, gut microbiota composition, and metabolite profiles. High-dose PE extract significantly reduced HSD-induced blood pressure elevation, decreased pro-inflammatory cytokines TNF-alpha and IL-1beta, and improved histological markers of renal and vascular tissue injury. PE extract regulated intestinal tight junction proteins Claudin-2 and ZO-1, indicating improved intestinal barrier function alongside restoration of gut microbiota diversity, particularly increasing beneficial Lactobacillus species. Metabolomic analysis revealed that high-dose PE significantly reversed HSD-induced intestinal metabolite alterations, with eight bile acid metabolites specifically normalized, implicating the gut microbiota-bile acid axis as the central mechanistic pathway. This is a preclinical animal study; human pharmacokinetics and clinical dose equivalence of PE extract remain to be established, limiting immediate clinical translation.

Implications Medical Affairs : This study advances preclinical evidence for the gut microbiota-bile acid axis as a therapeutic target in salt-sensitive hypertension, a mechanistic framework increasingly converging with clinical research on microbiome modulation and blood pressure regulation. From a Medical Affairs perspective, the gut-cardiovascular axis identified here positions natural dietary interventions as scientifically credible adjuncts to antihypertensive therapy, and engagement with nutrition and microbiome research KOLs could shape a differentiated scientific platform for combination prevention strategies. Future translational research prioritizing clinical studies of PE extract or microbiome-modulating strategies alongside standard antihypertensive therapy should be monitored for regulatory and evidence-base development implications.

From guidelines to practice: general practitioners' consensus on indicators for managing selected multimorbidity in primary care in Western Australia.

Revue : Family practice | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-11

Resultats principaux : A two-round reactive Delphi survey was conducted with 21 general practitioners in Western Australia using RAND methodology to develop a unified indicator tool for managing five common chronic conditions: type 2 diabetes mellitus (T2DM), obesity, hypertension, cardiovascular disease, and chronic kidney disease (CKD). Indicators were derived from Royal Australian College of General Practitioners guidelines and covered biophysical markers, lifestyle risks, and pharmacological management. In Round 1, 57 indicators achieved consensus (at least 70% of GPs rating at least 3 on a 4-point relevance scale), with four additional indicators proposed; Round 2 achieved final consensus on 61 indicators. Two indicators addressing pharmacological management of CKD with T2DM did not achieve clarity consensus and require further guideline refinement. GPs highlighted the need for refined lipid management guidelines, prioritization of obesity management for primary prevention, and acknowledged persistent challenges in lifestyle counselling delivery in primary care settings.

Implications Medical Affairs : This study presents the first GP-validated unified indicator tool for multimorbidity management integrating hypertension with T2DM, obesity, cardiovascular disease, and CKD — directly relevant to how antihypertensive drugs are selected, titrated, and monitored in the complex patients who constitute the majority of the primary care hypertension population. Medical Affairs teams should recognize primary care as the primary treatment decision environment for hypertensive patients with multimorbidity and engage GP KOLs and primary care societies to ensure antihypertensive medications feature prominently across multimorbidity care indicators and quality improvement frameworks. Validation of this consensus tool in real-world settings and its effect on hypertension control rates in patients with co-existing T2DM, obesity, or CKD represents a high-value future research direction aligned with Medical Affairs evidence-generation priorities.

Primary prevention of coronary heart disease in women.

Revue : Post reproductive health | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-03

Resultats principaux : This British Menopause Society (BMS) consensus review synthesizes evidence on primary prevention of coronary heart disease (CHD) in women, covering hormone replacement therapy (HRT) and non-estrogen pharmacological therapies including antihypertensives, lipid-lowering agents, aspirin, antidiabetic medications, and selective estrogen receptor modulators (SERMs), as well as diet, lifestyle, and smoking cessation. Observational studies have consistently shown estrogen to have a cardioprotective role in postmenopausal women, and re-analysis of the Women's Health Initiative trial alongside subsequent meta-analyses of RCTs confirmed that timing of HRT initiation is critical — with cardioprotective estrogen benefits primarily observed in women initiating HRT before age 60. The review identifies antihypertensives as key pharmacological agents for CHD primary prevention alongside statins and aspirin, particularly given the accelerated blood pressure rise observed in women post-menopause. Diet, lifestyle modification, and smoking cessation are affirmed as essential non-pharmacological prevention pillars. A key limitation is that specific antihypertensive drug class comparisons in women at the menopausal transition are not systematically reviewed, leaving unresolved questions about optimal agent selection in this group.

Implications Medical Affairs : This BMS consensus statement positions antihypertensives as essential first-line pharmacological agents in the primary prevention of coronary heart disease in women during the menopausal transition — a high-cardiovascular-risk window that is increasingly recognized as a critical intervention opportunity. Medical Affairs teams should leverage this publication to reinforce antihypertensive product positioning in the growing women's cardiovascular health space, particularly for agents with evidence in peri- and post-menopausal populations where the unprotected cardiovascular risk window demands proactive blood pressure management. KOL partnerships with cardiologists and menopause specialists are strategically important to explore optimal sequencing and combination of antihypertensive and hormonal therapies in primary prevention, and to inform updated guideline recommendations for practitioners managing postmenopausal women.
