Prevalence and risk factors of hypertension in rural Bangladesh: A population-based cross-sectional study.

Revue : PloS One | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-10

Resultats principaux : This population-based cross-sectional study enrolled adults aged 18 years and older from 7,384 households across 149 rural villages in Bangladesh in 2017, using a semi-structured questionnaire to collect data on blood pressure, anthropometric measures, socioeconomic status, and lifestyle and behavioral risk factors. The study found that 33.7% of men and 28.6% of women had elevated blood pressure qualifying as either prehypertensive or hypertensive, indicating a substantial and sex-differentiated burden in this rural LMIC setting. Multivariate logistic regression identified age and overweight/obesity as the strongest independent risk factors for hypertension in both genders, while a significant association between tobacco consumption and age was observed specifically in women (p<0.0001). Men aged 45-54 years showed higher levels of physical activity associated with reduced risk (OR: 1.9, 95% CI 1.1-3.1), and individuals in higher socioeconomic status brackets had paradoxically higher rates of overweight and obesity (up to 5.8% in adult women) alongside higher fruit and vegetable consumption. Notable limitations include the cross-sectional design precluding causal inference, the 2017 data collection period potentially limiting current applicability, and the restriction to household heads and their spouses which may not fully represent the broader rural population.

Implications Medical Affairs : These findings from a socioeconomically diverse LMIC context reinforce the need for gender-specific and context-adapted antihypertensive management strategies that go beyond pharmacotherapy alone, which is directly relevant for Medical Affairs when designing evidence generation initiatives in emerging markets. The paradoxical association between higher SES and greater obesity risk in rural Bangladesh suggests that hypertension prevalence in LMICs may accelerate as economic development progresses, creating significant future market expansion opportunities for antihypertensive agents. Medical Affairs teams should leverage these epidemiological data to support KOL engagement and healthcare policy dialogue on integrated NCD prevention programs that can underpin access and reimbursement frameworks in underserved rural markets.

Degree of hypertension and subclinical coronary atherosclerosis in asymptomatic individuals without cardiovascular disease.

Revue : PloS One | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-10

Resultats principaux : This large cross-sectional study analyzed 7,332 asymptomatic individuals (mean age 52.8 plus or minus 7.8 years; 63.8% men) without established cardiovascular disease who voluntarily underwent coronary computed tomography angiography (CCTA) as part of a routine general health examination in South Korea. The hypertension classification was adapted from the 2025 ACC/AHA guidelines, stratifying participants into normal BP (n=2,500), elevated BP (n=969), stage 1 hypertension (n=2,841), and stage 2 hypertension (n=1,022), with CAD assessed by CCTA as normal, non-obstructive (stenosis less than 50%), or obstructive (stenosis 50% or greater). After adjustment for cardiovascular risk factors, stage 1 hypertension was significantly associated with non-obstructive CAD (aOR 1.335, 95% CI 1.156-1.541), while stage 2 hypertension showed significant associations with both non-obstructive (aOR 1.483, 95% CI 1.234-1.784) and obstructive CAD (aOR 1.696, 95% CI 1.194-2.409). No statistically significant association was identified between the elevated BP group and subclinical atherosclerosis, suggesting a threshold effect that becomes clinically meaningful at the stage 1 classification boundary. Key limitations include the cross-sectional design, selection bias from a voluntary health examination population likely healthier and of higher SES, and a single-center Korean cohort that limits generalizability to broader populations.

Implications Medical Affairs : The graded dose-response relationship between hypertension stage and subclinical coronary atherosclerosis beginning at stage 1 thresholds provides compelling clinical evidence supporting early antihypertensive intervention strategies and aligns with the more aggressive 2025 ACC/AHA guidelines, directly reinforcing Medical Affairs positioning of first-line antihypertensive therapies in primary prevention populations. These findings support the scientific narrative for pharmaceutical products targeting early-stage hypertension, strengthening the medical rationale for treatment initiation before the development of symptomatic coronary artery disease in asymptomatic patients. Medical Affairs should use these data in KOL scientific exchanges with cardiologists and hypertension specialists to advocate for CCTA-based risk stratification and earlier blood pressure management as key strategies for reducing cardiovascular event rates.

Psychological distress in hypertension: Prevalence and links to self-care in Gonabad, Iran.

Revue : PloS One | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-10

Resultats principaux : This analytical cross-sectional study was conducted in 2024-2025 in Gonabad, Iran, enrolling 509 hypertensive patients selected through cluster random sampling, with validated inclusion criteria requiring confirmed physician diagnosis of hypertension, informed consent, absence of cognitive impairment, and at least one year of local residency. Participants were evaluated using the DASS-21 (Depression, Anxiety, and Stress Scale) and the Self-Care of Hypertension Inventory (SC-HI V3), with data analyzed using independent t-tests, ANOVA, multiple linear regression, and Pearson's correlation. The prevalence of psychological comorbidities was strikingly high: 62.5% of patients exhibited depression, 75.2% exhibited anxiety, and 59.3% exhibited stress, representing a severe mental health burden within this hypertensive population. Pearson's correlation analysis revealed significant negative associations between depression and self-care (r=-0.314, p<0.001) and between anxiety and self-care (r=-0.330, p<0.001), with the combined variables of depression, anxiety, stress, age, education, and income predicting 15% of the variance in self-care behavior. Key limitations include the cross-sectional design limiting causal inference, the single-city Iranian population which may not generalize to other cultural or healthcare settings, and reliance on self-reported questionnaires subject to response bias.

Implications Medical Affairs : The alarmingly high prevalence of psychological comorbidities among hypertensive patients and their negative impact on self-care adherence underscore the urgent medical need for integrated mental health support programs within hypertension management pathways, representing an opportunity for Medical Affairs to champion patient-centered care programs and medication adherence initiatives. These data reinforce the value proposition of simplified antihypertensive regimens such as fixed-dose combinations that reduce the self-care burden in psychologically vulnerable patient populations, supporting positioning efforts for adherence-enhancing products in LMIC and high-comorbidity settings. Medical Affairs should engage psychiatry and primary care KOLs around the concept of routine psychological comorbidity screening in hypertensive patients, opening new scientific exchange opportunities and supporting guideline advocacy in LMIC healthcare systems.

[Clinical application of echocardiography in the diagnosis and treatment of adult pulmonary hypertension: a Chinese guideline (2026)].

Revue : Zhonghua Nei Ke Za Zhi | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-01

Resultats principaux : This 2026 Chinese clinical guideline was developed under the joint auspices of the Pulmonary Vascular Group of the Chinese Medical Doctor Association of Cardiology, the Chinese Society of Echocardiography, and the Specialized Committee of Cardiovascular Disease Prevention and Rehabilitation, providing a comprehensive standardized echocardiographic pathway for screening, diagnosing, and monitoring adult pulmonary hypertension across China. The guideline was motivated by the significant inter-center and inter-operator variability in echocardiographic parameters and reporting that has historically limited the broad and consistent application of echocardiography in guiding pulmonary hypertension diagnosis and management in China. Stratified recommendations are provided based on hospital capabilities and the expertise of echocardiographic technicians and pulmonary vascular physicians, covering the full disease course from initial screening through diagnosis, risk assessment, treatment target setting, and longitudinal therapeutic response evaluation. The guideline integrates specific echocardiographic workflows and acquisition parameters applicable at different levels of Chinese medical institutions, aiming to establish a unified national standard for echocardiographic evaluation of adult pulmonary hypertension. Limitations include its regional applicability to the Chinese healthcare context, publication primarily in Chinese limiting international adoption, and the potential lag before widespread implementation across the diverse landscape of Chinese hospital settings can be achieved.

Implications Medical Affairs : The publication of the first unified Chinese echocardiographic guideline for pulmonary hypertension represents a significant market-shaping event in one of the world's largest healthcare markets, and Medical Affairs should analyze its imaging thresholds and risk stratification algorithms to align scientific communications with updated Chinese diagnostic standards for PAH therapies. The emphasis on standardized echocardiographic screening at all hospital tiers in China creates strategic opportunities for Medical Affairs to develop regional advisory programs with Chinese pulmonary hypertension KOLs and to support education initiatives around early detection of PH in high-risk populations, including those with comorbid systemic hypertension. These guidelines may accelerate earlier diagnosis and treatment initiation in the Chinese PAH market, potentially expanding the addressable patient population and warranting dedicated medical information programs tailored to the new echocardiographic assessment framework.

Association of Metabolic Score for Insulin Resistance With Resistant Hypertension and Hypertension in Obstructive Sleep Apnea.

Revue : The Clinical Respiratory Journal | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07

Resultats principaux : This retrospective cohort study enrolled 680 adults with confirmed OSA diagnosis between 2020 and 2022, stratifying participants into METS-IR quartiles and conducting multivariate logistic regression analyses adjusted for sex, lifestyle factors, and OSA-related variables to assess the relationship between METS-IR and prevalence of both hypertension and resistant hypertension (RH). In patients with severe OSA, a robust linear dose-response relationship was observed between METS-IR and risks of hypertension and RH (p-trend less than 0.01 for both); conversely, in the non-severe OSA group, these associations were attenuated and no significant linear trend was identified after full adjustment. ROC analysis demonstrated superior discriminative accuracy for METS-IR for both hypertension (AUC 0.745 vs. BMI 0.729 vs. TG/HDL 0.636) and resistant hypertension (AUC 0.754 vs. BMI 0.746 vs. TG/HDL 0.610), with all pairwise differences reaching statistical significance (p<0.05). Post hoc power analyses confirmed adequate study power (greater than 80%) to detect the observed effect sizes across all subgroups including the total cohort, severe OSA subgroup, and non-severe OSA subgroup. Key limitations include the retrospective single-center design, a relatively small sample size for subgroup analyses, and potential residual confounding despite multivariate adjustment.

Implications Medical Affairs : The identification of METS-IR as a superior metabolic marker compared to BMI or TG/HDL ratio for predicting hypertension and resistant hypertension in severe OSA patients highlights a clinically actionable phenotyping tool that Medical Affairs can incorporate into scientific communications targeting antihypertensive product differentiation in the OSA-hypertension comorbidity space. These findings are particularly relevant for Medical Affairs positioning of treatments for resistant hypertension, as they delineate a high-risk severe OSA subpopulation where routine METS-IR screening could facilitate earlier therapeutic intervention and better cardiovascular risk management. Engaging pulmonology and sleep medicine KOLs around integration of cardiometabolic risk scores with OSA severity classifications represents a novel avenue for cross-specialty scientific exchange that reinforces the clinical unmet need in resistant hypertension populations.

Contributions of preeclampsia to preterm delivery: a hypothetical interventional cohort study using incremental propensity scores.

Revue : International Journal of Epidemiology | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-24

Resultats principaux : This causal epidemiology study applied the incremental propensity score intervention (IPSI) approach to two large US population cohorts of singleton pregnancies: 1998-2002 (16.7 million pregnancies) and 2020-2023 (14.2 million pregnancies), to quantify the causal contribution of gestational hypertension and preeclampsia (GHTN/PE) to preterm delivery (PTD). Using IPSI, which models the expected change in population-average PTD risk if each individual's odds of developing GHTN/PE were reduced by a specified increment, halving the individual odds of GHTN/PE in the 1998-2002 cohort would reduce average PTD risk from observed levels (RR=0.969, 95% CI 0.969-0.970). Given the greater GHTN/PE prevalence in the 2020-2023 cohort, halving the odds of preeclampsia would yield an even larger reduction in average PTD risk (RR=0.948, 95% CI 0.947-0.948), demonstrating that the population impact of interventions targeting GHTN/PE has grown in parallel with rising preeclampsia rates over two decades. Sensitivity analyses using bias formulas demonstrated that these conclusions remained robust under assumptions encoding relatively strong unmeasured confounding. Key limitations include the observational nature of the data, reliance on administrative or vital statistics coding for GHTN/PE and PTD diagnoses, and the hypothetical rather than empirically tested nature of the IPSI intervention scenario.

Implications Medical Affairs : This study provides a novel methodological framework for quantifying the population-attributable burden of gestational hypertension and preeclampsia on preterm delivery, with findings that reinforce the growing public health imperative for effective prevention and treatment of hypertensive disorders of pregnancy — a therapeutic area of increasing strategic importance for Medical Affairs. The observed increase in GHTN/PE prevalence and its amplified impact on preterm birth rates across two decades creates a compelling scientific platform for engaging maternal-fetal medicine and obstetric KOLs around therapeutic strategies targeting hypertensive disorders of pregnancy, including emerging pharmacological prevention approaches. These data support advocacy efforts around integrated maternal cardiovascular risk programs and reinforce the scientific rationale for portfolio initiatives addressing hypertension in women of reproductive age.

Recognition, treatment, and control of hypertension in the Danish population-based Lolland-Falster Health Study.

Revue : European Journal of Public Health | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-10

Resultats principaux : This cross-sectional study analyzed data from 7,329 participants with hypertension identified in the Lolland-Falster Health Study (LOFUS), a population-based Danish cohort conducted in a socioeconomically disadvantaged rural region where overall hypertension prevalence was 45%. Among all participants with hypertension, 39% had unrecognized disease; these individuals were significantly younger, had higher educational attainment, and had fewer comorbidities compared to those with recognized hypertension, and larger household size was independently associated with greater odds of remaining undiagnosed. Two-thirds of individuals with unrecognized hypertension were classified as having high 10-year cardiovascular risk and met criteria for antihypertensive pharmacological treatment, quantifying a large and consequential unmet therapeutic need in this population. Among participants with recognized hypertension, 51% had uncontrolled blood pressure despite more than 50% of this group receiving two or more antihypertensive agents concurrently, indicating that therapeutic inertia and suboptimal treatment intensification are major barriers to achieving BP control targets. Key limitations include the cross-sectional design, geographic restriction to one socioeconomically disadvantaged Danish region limiting national generalizability, and reliance on single-visit BP measurements rather than ambulatory monitoring standards.

Implications Medical Affairs : The finding that 39% of hypertensives remain unrecognized and 51% of treated patients remain uncontrolled even in a high-income European setting highlights a substantial and persistent treatment gap, directly reinforcing the Medical Affairs narrative around the need for proactive screening initiatives and intensified therapeutic strategies including early use of combination antihypertensive therapy. These real-world uncontrolled rate data strongly support the value proposition of fixed-dose antihypertensive combinations and simplified once-daily treatment regimens that reduce prescribing complexity and can improve blood pressure control rates even in resource-constrained or disadvantaged healthcare settings. Medical Affairs should deploy these epidemiological findings in scientific engagements with European hypertension KOLs and primary care physicians to advocate for optimized treatment intensification algorithms, particularly in underserved populations where therapeutic inertia is greatest.

Interdisciplinary training on noncommunicable diseases and sustained change in primary health care in the context of health reform, COVID-19, and war in Ukraine: A repeated qualitative study, 2016-2022.

Revue : BMC Public Health | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-10

Resultats principaux : This repeated qualitative study evaluated the impact and long-term sustainability of the WHO Package of Essential Noncommunicable Disease Interventions (WHO PEN) training program across two time points: T1 (December 2018-February 2019) with 74 focus group discussions, and T2 (February-March 2022) with 56 in-depth interviews, conducted among primary health care (PHC) providers across seven pilot regions in Ukraine where the WHO PEN program trained over 10,000 PHC providers between 2016 and 2018. At T1, providers reported meaningfully improved confidence in routine NCD risk assessment, lifestyle counseling, and integrated management of hypertension and diabetes, supported by task shifting and managerial engagement within the broader Ukrainian health reform context. By T2, WHO PEN-facilitated practice improvements were maintained over five years, but their frequency diminished substantially as COVID-19 redirected clinical attention toward established NCD complications, rising patient loads imposed administrative burdens, and the outbreak of war in February 2022 intensified psychosomatic complaints among patients and stressed the health workforce. Deductive thematic analysis was guided by the Dynamic Sustainability Framework and complemented by inductive coding to capture contextual influences on practice sustainability. Limitations include the qualitative design that limits quantitative impact assessment, potential recall bias in provider self-reporting, and the extreme contextual disruption of active war in Ukraine which limits generalizability to more stable health systems.

Implications Medical Affairs : This study demonstrates that structured interdisciplinary training programs aligned with WHO PEN can achieve sustained improvements in hypertension and NCD prevention practices at the primary care level in a LMIC setting even under systemic stress, providing evidence that Medical Affairs teams can leverage when supporting healthcare system capacity-building initiatives in partnership with global health organizations. The documented decline in preventive care frequency under COVID-19 and wartime conditions highlights the fragility of NCD prevention programs under acute system shocks, reinforcing the value of embedded digital tools and simplified clinical protocols that can maintain hypertension management continuity during healthcare crises. Medical Affairs should consider strategic partnerships with WHO and national health ministries in Eastern European and post-conflict health systems to position antihypertensive therapies within integrated NCD care frameworks designed for resilience to healthcare system disruptions.

Waist circumference shows a significant association with the risk of noncommunicable diseases: a cross-sectional study from a large community sample in Southeast China.

Revue : BMC Public Health | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-09

Resultats principaux : This large cross-sectional study recruited 135,352 permanent residents aged 35-75 years from Fujian Province, Southeast China (2018-2021) using multistage stratified cluster random sampling, with physical measurements including height, weight, waist circumference, and blood pressure, and biochemical assessment of fasting plasma glucose and lipid profiles. The observed prevalence of hypertension, type 2 diabetes mellitus, and dyslipidemia in this population was 46.2%, 18.3%, and 16.6%, respectively. After Z-score standardization for direct comparison of effect sizes, every 1-SD increase in waist circumference (WC) was more strongly associated with systolic blood pressure elevation (beta=0.132 vs. beta=0.115 for BMI), fasting plasma glucose (beta=0.130 vs. beta=0.020), and triglycerides (beta=0.176 vs. beta=0.091) than BMI, and WC showed a stronger inverse association with HDL-C (beta=-0.185 vs. beta=-0.147). Poisson regression demonstrated that mixed obesity (elevated both BMI and WC) conferred the highest adjusted risk ratios for hypertension (aRR=1.926), T2DM (aRR=1.574), and dyslipidemia (aRR=1.402), while isolated elevated WC was more strongly associated with T2DM (aRR=1.495) than isolated elevated BMI (aRR=1.143), and mixed obesity nearly doubled the multimorbidity burden (aRR=1.973 for two or more NCDs). Limitations include the cross-sectional design precluding causal inference, the single-province Chinese sample limiting national generalizability, and a noted spurious protective association of smoking likely reflecting healthy survivor bias and residual confounding.

Implications Medical Affairs : The superior discriminative power of waist circumference over BMI for predicting hypertension and cardiometabolic risk in this 135,000-person Chinese cohort supports incorporating WC into routine cardiovascular risk stratification, providing Medical Affairs with strong evidence to advocate for integrated screening programs in educational initiatives targeting Chinese primary care and internal medicine KOLs. The near-doubling of multimorbidity risk in mixed obesity phenotypes with both elevated BMI and WC reinforces the clinical rationale for early pharmacological intervention in hypertensive patients with central obesity, directly supporting product positioning for antihypertensive agents in cardiometabolic high-risk populations. Medical Affairs teams active in the Chinese market should collaborate with endocrinology and public health KOLs on integrated screening programs combining WC and blood pressure measurement, potentially driving earlier treatment initiation and expanding the addressable patient population for antihypertensive therapies.
