Real-World Data-Driven Insights in Obesity: A Comparative Cross-Sectional Analysis of General Practice and a Specialized Obesity Clinic in Belgium — An IMI2 SOPHIA Study.

Revue : Clinical Obesity | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-08

Resultats principaux : This cross-sectional study analyzed real-world data from 208,891 patients in a Belgian general practitioners' morbidity registry (Intego) and 3,605 visitors to a specialized obesity clinic, conducted within the IMI2 SOPHIA framework. The study found that BMI was registered in only 25.6% of general practice records, revealing a critical documentation gap in routine primary care. Among patients with registered BMI (mean 27.1 kg/m2), hypertension prevalence was substantially higher (30.1%) compared to those without BMI registration (17.0%), and dyslipidemia (26.9% vs. 14.7%), prediabetes (17.0% vs. 7.9%), type 2 diabetes (12.7% vs. 4.4%), and sleep apnea (3.8% vs. 1.5%) followed similar patterns. The specialized obesity clinic cohort (mean BMI 39.0 kg/m2) showed even higher complication burdens: hypertension 43.1%, dyslipidemia 44.4%, and sleep apnea 37.9% compared to the general practice BMI-registered subgroup. Group comparisons used chi-square tests (p less than 0.05), but the cross-sectional design and incomplete BMI registration introduce selection bias as key limitations.

Implications Medical Affairs : These findings underscore the underdiagnosis of hypertension in primary care when BMI monitoring is inadequate, directly relevant for Medical Affairs initiatives targeting hypertension awareness and early detection campaigns in general practice settings. The high hypertension burden (43.1%) in specialized obesity settings argues for integrated cardiovascular management programs, potentially positioning antihypertensive portfolios within obesity clinic partnerships and co-management pathways. Engaging obesity-focused KOLs with real-world evidence from large registry analyses may strengthen payer and physician engagement strategies for combination management of cardiometabolic risk.

A comparative analysis of ACE Inhibitors and ARBs as cancer risk factors.

Revue : PLoS One | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-23

Resultats principaux : This retrospective cohort study used electronic health records from Clalit Health Services in Israel, analyzing 423,423 hypertensive adults aged 40 or older free of cancer at baseline (January 1, 2000), comparing 346,405 exclusive ACEI users and 77,018 exclusive ARB users with active follow-up from 2002 through 2024. The primary endpoint was any new cancer diagnosis; secondary endpoints covered the ten most prevalent cancer types, with analysis of cancer incidence rates, odds ratios adjusted for age, sex, and exposure duration, age at diagnosis, and time from treatment initiation to diagnosis. Overall cancer incidence differed between groups, with ACEI therapy associated with lower overall odds of cancer than ARB therapy across the 22-year observation period. Associations varied by cancer type, with lower cancer odds observed among ACEI users for several common malignancies, while no meaningful difference was found for leukemia. The observational retrospective design precludes causal inference, and selection bias in drug assignment, differential follow-up durations, and unmeasured confounders are principal limitations.

Implications Medical Affairs : This large population-based study (over 420,000 patients, 22-year follow-up) contributes important evidence to the ongoing oncological safety debate between RAAS-targeting agents, with immediate relevance for Medical Affairs communications around long-term safety profiles of antihypertensive drug classes. The differential cancer risk by drug class—if confirmed in prospective studies—could influence prescribing preferences and support the positioning of ACEIs in patients with elevated cancer risk, a commercially significant insight for portfolios containing RAAS agents. KOL strategy should prioritize collaboration with oncology-cardiology specialists to design mechanistic and prospective studies that clarify whether these associations reflect true pharmacological class effects or residual confounding.

Revue : Journal of Medical Internet Research | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-23

Resultats principaux : This retrospective cohort study evaluated a 12-week mobile-based chronic disease management program delivered as part of an employee assistance program in Korea, using propensity score-matched cohorts from the Kangbuk Samsung Health Study (HTN: N=90; DM: N=78; obesity: N=132) with follow-up comparing 2019 (baseline) to 2023 outcomes. Key limitations include small propensity-matched cohort sizes, single-company real-world setting limiting generalizability, and inability to blind participants or assessors.

Spatial risk of hypertension in rural China: modelling the effect of dietary, behavioural and climate factors.

Revue : Geospatial Health | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-23

Resultats principaux : This spatial epidemiological study examined hypertension prevalence among rural elderly in China using Pearson correlation analysis and maximum entropy (MaxEnt) modeling across multiple provincial data sets to identify geographic high-risk zones and key modifiable determinants. Grain intake was negatively correlated with hypertension prevalence (r=-0.600, p=0.002), as was poultry consumption (r=-0.504, p=0.010), while alcohol use showed the highest contribution among behavioral variables in the MaxEnt model (39.2%). Climate zones were associated with hypertension prevalence (r=-0.260, p=0.010), with higher risk observed in temperate and some subtropical regions of northern and northeastern rural China. The MaxEnt model demonstrated good discriminatory ability (AUC=0.871), effectively delineating geographic hypertension hot zones primarily in northern and northeastern rural China. Limitations include ecological fallacy risk inherent in area-level aggregate analyses, use of publicly available rather than individual-level data, and limited ability to control for clinical confounders such as medication use or comorbidities.

Implications Medical Affairs : The identification of northern rural China as a hypertension high-risk zone—driven by dietary patterns, alcohol consumption, and climatic conditions—provides actionable geospatial intelligence for targeting public health interventions and pharmaceutical market access strategies in currently underserved regions. The significant contribution of modifiable dietary factors argues for Medical Affairs support of health literacy campaigns aligned with antihypertensive product positioning in rural Chinese populations with high unmet treatment need. KOL collaboration with Chinese public health specialists and geospatial health experts would help validate these findings and co-develop regionally tailored hypertension screening and treatment pathways to expand market reach.

Revue : Frontiers in Public Health | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-03

Resultats principaux : This community-based cross-sectional study surveyed 1,307 adults from 10 communities in Urumqi, China (July-August 2025), using Directed Acyclic Graphs and Propensity Score Matching to examine the association between mental health service (MHS) utilization and diabetes-hypertension (DM-HTN) comorbidity, with adjustment for depression, smoking, alcohol use, and physical activity. After PSM adjustment, MHS utilization was associated with a 41.6% lower odds of DM-HTN comorbidity (OR=0.584, 95% CI: 0.360-0.915), demonstrating a statistically significant protective effect. Depression (OR=1.730, 95% CI: 1.024-2.862), smoking (OR=1.600, 95% CI: 1.177-2.171), and alcohol consumption (OR=1.720, 95% CI: 1.253-2.350) were all positively associated with DM-HTN comorbidity, while moderate physical activity (1-2 hours/session) was protective (OR=0.355, 95% CI: 0.177-0.671). A significant interaction between MHS utilization and depression status was found (OR=0.587), with absolute risk reduction of 3.03% among depressed individuals versus 1.87% among non-depressed, highlighting differential benefit. Limitations include the cross-sectional design precluding causal inference, single-city sampling limiting generalizability, and potential reporting bias for behavioral covariates.

Implications Medical Affairs : The protective association of mental health service utilization on cardiometabolic comorbidity highlights an integrative care model with implications for Medical Affairs strategies promoting holistic hypertension management beyond pharmacotherapy, particularly in populations with comorbid depression. The significant interaction with depression status supports co-positioning of cardiovascular and mental health interventions, relevant for companies with combined psychiatric-cardiovascular portfolios or those developing integrated care programs. Future KOL strategy should promote interdisciplinary care models among psychiatrists, endocrinologists, and cardiologists, reinforcing the importance of behavioral risk factor management in preventing hypertension-diabetes comorbidity progression.

Assessment of Impacts of Clinically Effective Antihypertensive Herbal Medicines on the Activities of Major Drug Metabolizing Enzymes and Transport Proteins.

Revue : Drug Metabolism and Bioanalysis | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026

Resultats principaux : This narrative review systematically analyzed published literature on herbal medicinal products (HMPs) used as antihypertensive agents across multiple world regions, identifying over 50 medicinal plants with documented antihypertensive activity through animal or clinical evidence. Clinical trial-level evidence was available for only a select group of plants including Hibiscus sabdariffa, Allium sativum (garlic), Apium graveolens (celery), Nigella sativa, Linum usitatissimum (flaxseed), and Camellia sinensis (green tea), while the majority of evidence derived from animal models. The review characterized the phytochemical constituents of these HMPs and evaluated their potential to modulate drug-metabolizing cytochrome P450 enzymes and P-glycoprotein drug transport systems, revealing significant variability in interaction potential across plants. Concurrent use of HMPs and conventional antihypertensives was reported in 20-80% of hypertensive patients globally, making herb-drug interactions a clinically significant pharmacovigilance concern. Key limitations include the narrative review methodology with potential selection bias, reliance on animal data for most plants, and substantial pharmacokinetic heterogeneity across patient populations and herbal formulations.

Implications Medical Affairs : The high rate of concurrent HMP-conventional antihypertensive use (20-80% globally) represents a critical safety concern and an immediate opportunity for Medical Affairs to develop HCP education materials on clinically relevant herb-drug interactions for antihypertensive products. Understanding the CYP450 and P-glycoprotein modulating profiles of common herbal antihypertensives should guide pharmacovigilance reporting updates and label revisions for conventional antihypertensives prescribed in populations with high complementary medicine use. Partnering with clinical pharmacologists and ethnopharmacology KOLs to generate structured pharmacokinetic interaction data would support both regulatory compliance and product differentiation in emerging markets where herbal medicine use is prevalent.

Hypertension and gut microbial hydrogenases: a comparison of hydrogen metabolism and etiology.

Revue : Medical Gas Research | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-23

Resultats principaux : This metagenomics study analyzed publicly available gut microbiome data from hypertensive patients versus healthy controls to characterize hydrogenase expression patterns, supplemented by Mendelian randomization (MR) analysis to evaluate potential causal relationships. Compared to controls, the hypertension group showed a 2.3-fold increase in electron-bifurcating [FeFe] group A3 hydrogenases (P=0.0299) and a 55.6% decrease in [NiFe] group 1d hydrogenases (P=0.0097), with hydrogen-sensing hydrogenases increased and hydrogen-uptake hydrogenases decreased overall. After adjusting for age, sex, and lifestyle factors, significant differences were confirmed in [FeFe] group A3, [NiFe] group 1d, and [NiFe] group 1c abundances, which are proposed as potential diagnostic biomarkers for hypertension onset. MR analysis supported a protective effect of hydrogen metabolism against hypertension (OR=0.72, 95% CI: 0.61-0.85, P less than 0.001), suggesting a potentially causal inverse relationship. Limitations include reliance on secondary public datasets, absence of clinical validation cohorts, and inherent constraints of observational metagenomics susceptible to population-level confounding.

Implications Medical Affairs : This mechanistic study opens a novel microbiome-hydrogen metabolism axis in hypertension pathophysiology, with implications for Medical Affairs in monitoring emerging biomarker and microbiome-based diagnostic developments that could complement or precede pharmaceutical management of blood pressure. If validated, hydrogenase-based biomarkers could stratify hypertension risk populations, informing companion diagnostic strategies and precision medicine positioning for antihypertensive portfolios. Medical Affairs should track this emerging research area and consider supporting translational KOL-led studies evaluating whether microbiome-targeted interventions interact with established antihypertensive agents.

Association of Five Dietary Indices with All-Cause Mortality in Stroke Survivors: A National Cohort Study.

Revue : Journal of Stroke and Cerebrovascular Diseases | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-23

Resultats principaux : This national cohort analysis used NHANES 2005-2018 data from 1,212 stroke survivors with complete dietary records to assess associations between five established dietary indices and all-cause mortality, employing Cox proportional hazards models adjusted for multiple sociodemographic and clinical covariates. The Alternative Healthy Eating Index (AHEI) was inversely associated with all-cause mortality: the highest versus lowest quartile conferred a 64.8% reduction in mortality risk (HR=0.352, 95% CI 0.153-0.809, p=0.014), demonstrating a strong gradient effect. The Dietary Inflammatory Index (DII) showed a positive association with mortality; the highest versus lowest quartile was associated with a 47.1% increase in risk (HR=1.471, 95% CI 1.028-2.104, p=0.035), highlighting dietary inflammation as a modifiable prognostic factor. No significant associations with all-cause mortality were found for HEI-2020, the Alternate Mediterranean Diet Score, or the DASH score in this stroke survivor population. Limitations include cross-sectional dietary assessment (single 24-h recall) introducing recall bias, limited ability to track dietary pattern changes over time, and restricted generalizability to non-US stroke populations.

Implications Medical Affairs : The demonstration that anti-inflammatory dietary patterns (high AHEI) reduce all-cause mortality by 65% while pro-inflammatory diets (high DII) increase it by 47% has direct relevance for Medical Affairs messaging linking diet, inflammation, and cardiovascular outcomes in high-risk populations including those with hypertension. These findings support educational initiatives targeting cardiologists and neurologists on dietary co-interventions alongside antihypertensive and cardiovascular pharmacotherapy, reinforcing a holistic risk reduction narrative for products in the cardiovascular secondary prevention space. Investing in KOL engagement with nutrition-cardiology specialists and co-funding dietary intervention trials in hypertension-stroke populations could generate complementary data strengthening product positioning in secondary prevention markets.

Resultats principaux : This cross-sectional study analyzed data from 2,571 reproductive-aged women in the NHANES 1999-2023 database, using weighted multivariable logistic regression, restricted cubic spline modeling, and two-piecewise linear regression to characterize the association between visceral adiposity index (VAI) and hypertension prevalence.
