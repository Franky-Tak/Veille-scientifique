Optimal blood pressure target in patients with uncomplicated hypertension: a target trial emulation study

Revue : Nature Communications | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-05

Resultats principaux : This target trial emulation study used a territory-wide public healthcare database in Hong Kong to analyse 118,271 patients with uncomplicated hypertension, comparing intensive blood pressure management (target below 130/80 mmHg) against standard targets (130-140/80-90 mmHg) in a real-world primary care setting with rigorous methodology designed to approximate a randomised trial. The study employed propensity weighting and clone-censor-reweight techniques to overcome the inherent confounding of observational data, making this one of the most methodologically robust real-world evaluations of BP target thresholds published to date. Patients managed to the intensive target were observed to incur a significantly lower risk of hypertension-related complications, including stroke, myocardial infarction, and heart failure, as well as all-cause mortality, compared to those maintained at standard targets. Critically, no significant increase in serious adverse events — including hypotension, syncope, and acute kidney injury — was observed in the intensive management group, directly addressing the safety concerns that have historically impeded adoption of lower BP thresholds in primary care settings. These findings provide the first large-scale real-world validation from a territory-wide Asian population supporting a BP target of less than 130/80 mmHg, bridging the gap between randomised trial evidence (SPRINT, STEP) and the clinical practice realities of primary care hypertension management.

Implications Medical Affairs : This study provides crucial real-world confirmation of intensive blood pressure guidelines and could catalyse faster adoption of the sub-130/80 mmHg target in primary care guidelines across Asia-Pacific and globally, representing a significant opportunity to expand the treated hypertension population and associated antihypertensive market. From a product positioning perspective, antihypertensive agents with proven efficacy in achieving intensive BP targets — particularly RAAS inhibitors, CCBs, and their combinations — are well placed to benefit from guideline alignment, and Medical Affairs teams should develop primary care KOL engagement programmes leveraging this real-world evidence. Embedding this data in scientific exchange materials for primary care physician education should be prioritised, particularly in markets where the implementation gap between evidence-based intensive targets and clinical practice remains large.

Efficacy and safety of finerenone in patients with chronic kidney disease: an individual participant data pooled analysis (INFINITY)

Revue : Lancet | Tier : Tier 1 | IF estime : 168

Date de publication : 2026-06-05

Resultats principaux : The INFINITY study presents an individual participant data meta-analysis of three landmark randomised, double-blind, placebo-controlled trials of finerenone (a non-steroidal mineralocorticoid receptor antagonist with blood pressure-lowering and anti-fibrotic properties) in patients with chronic kidney disease: FIDELIO-DKD, FIGARO-DKD, and FIND-CKD, collectively enrolling 14,574 participants with mean age 63.7 years (SD 10.6) and mean eGFR 56.4 mL/min/1.73 m2. The primary kidney composite outcome (kidney failure or sustained 57% or more decline in eGFR) was reduced by 24% with finerenone versus placebo (22.3 vs 28.8 events per 1,000 patient-years; HR 0.76, 95% CI 0.68-0.86), and the cardiovascular composite outcome (hospitalisation for heart failure or cardiovascular death) was reduced by 20% (19.1 vs 23.9 events per 1,000 patient-years; HR 0.80, 95% CI 0.70-0.91). Finerenone also significantly reduced all-cause death (HR 0.88, 95% CI 0.79-0.99) and kidney failure alone (HR 0.85, 95% CI 0.74-0.99), establishing comprehensive endpoint benefits spanning kidney, cardiovascular, and overall survival dimensions. Crucially, treatment effects on the composite kidney outcome were consistent irrespective of glycaemic status (diabetic and non-diabetic), CKD aetiology, baseline eGFR category, albuminuria level, and use of SGLT2 inhibitors, demonstrating broad applicability of finerenone across the CKD spectrum. Hyperkalaemia occurred more frequently with finerenone than placebo, though absolute incidence of hyperkalaemia-related hospitalisation remained low, supporting a manageable safety profile in clinical practice settings.

Implications Medical Affairs : The INFINITY Lancet publication represents the most comprehensive evidence platform to date for finerenone as a foundational cardiorenal therapy applicable across the full CKD spectrum regardless of diabetes status or aetiology, significantly expanding the drug's therapeutic positioning beyond diabetic kidney disease and creating a compelling multi-specialty narrative for nephrology, cardiology, and diabetology KOLs. This landmark pooled analysis provides the evidence base for guideline advocacy with KDIGO, ESC, ESH, and national societies, and Medical Affairs teams should proactively submit this data package to relevant guideline committees ahead of anticipated 2026-2027 update cycles. The finding of consistent benefit regardless of SGLT2 inhibitor co-use creates a strong evidence-based rationale for triple cardiorenal protection (RAAS inhibitor + SGLT2 inhibitor + finerenone), enabling a differentiated combination positioning strategy that resonates across specialist prescriber communities.

Finerenone in Patients With Chronic Kidney Disease Due to Glomerular Diseases: A Randomized Clinical Trial

Revue : JAMA | Tier : Tier 1 | IF estime : 157

Date de publication : 2026-06-05

Resultats principaux : This prespecified exploratory subgroup analysis of the FIND-CKD phase 3 randomised, double-blind, placebo-controlled trial evaluated the efficacy and safety of finerenone (10 or 20 mg once daily) in 903 participants with investigator-reported glomerular diseases, conducted across 24 countries, with IgA nephropathy (46.1%), focal segmental glomerulosclerosis (23.8%), and membranous nephropathy (10.0%) as the predominant diagnoses. The patient population had a mean eGFR of 48.8 mL/min/1.73 m2 and a median urinary albumin-to-creatinine ratio of 839.6 mg/g, representing a predominantly non-diabetic CKD population with substantial proteinuria that had previously been unaddressed by mineralocorticoid receptor antagonist therapy. Finerenone significantly attenuated the total eGFR slope decline compared to placebo (-3.50 vs -4.23 mL/min/1.73 m2 per year; difference 0.73 mL/min/1.73 m2 per year, 95% CI 0.22-1.24) and reduced albuminuria at 12 months by 42% (95% CI 35-48%), both clinically meaningful endpoints in progressive glomerular disease management. The risk of the composite outcome of kidney failure or 40% or more eGFR decline was significantly lower with finerenone (7.42 vs 9.60 events per 100 patient-years; HR 0.74, 95% CI 0.57-0.97), translating into a 26% relative risk reduction in hard kidney outcomes in a non-diabetic glomerular disease population. Notably, blood pressure was commonly elevated in this population (hypertension being a universal comorbidity in advanced glomerular disease), and finerenone's blood pressure-lowering mechanisms contributed to its nephroprotective effects through multiple pathways including anti-inflammatory and anti-fibrotic actions beyond mere pressure reduction.

Implications Medical Affairs : This JAMA publication constitutes a landmark evidence base for potential label extension of finerenone into non-diabetic CKD caused by primary glomerular diseases, opening a large and historically underserved patient population for which treatment options have been limited to supportive therapy and immunosuppression. Medical Affairs should develop a specific evidence module targeting nephrology KOLs and IgA nephropathy specialists, as this subgroup represents the most common primary glomerular disease globally and an area of intense therapeutic development interest following the sparsentan and atrasentan approvals. Proactive engagement with KDIGO Glomerulonephritis guideline panelists and national nephrology society scientific committees should be initiated immediately to ensure finerenone data in non-diabetic glomerular CKD is incorporated into upcoming guideline updates expected in 2026-2027.

Safety and Synergy of Finerenone and Empagliflozin in Lowering Blood Pressure

Revue : Hypertension | Tier : Tier 2 | IF estime : 8.3

Date de publication : 2026-06-05

Resultats principaux : This secondary analysis of the CONFIDENCE trial evaluated systolic blood pressure (SBP) changes in 800 participants with CKD and type 2 diabetes randomised to empagliflozin/finerenone combination therapy versus each monotherapy, stratified by baseline SBP categories (below 130 mmHg vs at or above 130 mmHg). Among the 532 participants (66%) with baseline SBP at or above 130 mmHg, combination therapy significantly increased the odds of achieving a 10 mmHg or greater SBP reduction compared to finerenone monotherapy (OR 1.83, 95% CI 1.21-2.76; p=0.004), with a trend versus empagliflozin (OR 1.45, 95% CI 0.97-2.17; p=0.07), demonstrating additive BP-lowering effects in the hypertensive subgroup. The strongest predictor of achieving a BP response was baseline SBP (OR 2.04 per 10 mmHg higher; p<0.0001), while in patients with baseline SBP below 130 mmHg the combination offered no additional SBP benefit, suggesting a threshold-dependent synergy. A key mechanistic finding was that SBP reduction mediated less than 10% of the total urinary albumin-to-creatinine ratio reduction, indicating that the albuminuria-lowering and nephroprotective benefits of combination therapy operate predominantly through blood pressure-independent pathways including direct glomerular and tubular anti-fibrotic mechanisms. Rates of acute kidney injury and hyperkalaemia were similar across SBP subgroups for both combination and monotherapy arms, confirming that the BP-lowering synergy does not introduce additional clinically meaningful safety risks. These exploratory findings, while limited by use of clinic rather than ambulatory BP measurement, provide important mechanistic insights into the multi-pathway cardiorenal benefits of finerenone-empagliflozin combination therapy.

Implications Medical Affairs : The synergistic blood pressure-lowering effect of finerenone-empagliflozin combination in hypertensive CKD patients (SBP above 130 mmHg) provides a powerful, evidence-based rationale for positioning this combination as the preferred strategy in hypertensive CKD-T2D patients who require simultaneous cardiorenal protection and blood pressure control, representing a significant product differentiation opportunity. The finding that albuminuria reduction is largely BP-independent reinforces the complementary multi-mechanistic rationale for the combination, enabling Medical Affairs to develop layered messaging — BP control plus cardiorenal protection — that resonates distinctively across cardiology, nephrology, and diabetology prescriber audiences compared to monotherapy messaging alone. KOL engagement should specifically leverage this nuanced mechanistic evidence to educate opinion leaders that combination therapy's clinical benefits cannot be reduced to blood pressure effects alone, supporting the scientific rationale for early combination initiation in appropriate patients.

Clinical Outcomes in Patients With Hypertension and Renal Artery Stenosis Across Age Categories

Revue : Current Problems in Cardiology | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-04

Resultats principaux : This retrospective cross-sectional study used the National Inpatient Sample (2016-2020) to analyse 5,647,220 nationwide hospitalisations with a primary diagnosis of hypertension, identifying 39,235 (0.69%) with concurrent renal artery stenosis (RAS), stratified by age: under 30 years (0.97%), 30-55 years (11.9%), and over 55 years (87.13%). Renal artery stenosis with hypertension was significantly associated with congestive heart failure and pulmonary oedema across all age groups, with the strongest CHF association observed in the over-55 cohort (OR 4.89, 95% CI 4.62-5.16; p<0.001), followed by 30-55 years (OR 3.56, 95% CI 3.06-4.1; p<0.001), indicating a gradient of age-dependent cardiovascular burden. A notable finding was the comparatively stronger association between RAS-hypertension and pulmonary oedema in the under-30 cohort (OR 3.82, 95% CI 1.1-13.3; p=0.035), exceeding the risk seen in older patients and underscoring the severity of resistant hypertension in young patients with secondary aetiologies. Ischaemic heart disease and arrhythmia associations were confined to the over-55 and 30-55 age groups (IHD OR 2.73 and 2.35 respectively), with no significant association in under-30 patients, reflecting differing underlying pathophysiology and comorbidity profiles across age groups. Limitations include the cross-sectional design, reliance on ICD-10 coding accuracy, inability to capture outpatient RAS diagnoses, and absence of individual treatment or medication data that would allow determination of causality or therapeutic modulation of the observed associations.

Implications Medical Affairs : This nationwide analysis reinforces the clinical significance of secondary hypertension due to renovascular disease and highlights important age-specific complication profiles that can guide antihypertensive prescribing strategies, particularly regarding the use of RAAS inhibitors in RAS patients where careful clinical monitoring is warranted. From a Medical Affairs perspective, the data on the high prevalence of CHF and pulmonary oedema in hypertensive RAS patients across all age groups supports educational initiatives for cardiologists and nephrologists focused on the cardiovascular risks of inadequately managed secondary hypertension. Young patients under 30 with resistant or severe hypertension represent a priority screening population for RAS, and Medical Affairs can leverage this epidemiological data to support diagnostic algorithm education programmes in collaboration with hypertension specialist societies.

Shoulder Arthroplasty Outcomes in Hypertensive Patients: A Propensity Score-Matched Comparison of ARBs and ACE Inhibitors

Revue : Journal of Shoulder and Elbow Surgery | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-04

Resultats principaux : This large retrospective cohort study used the TriNetX Global Collaborative Network to compare periprosthetic complications in 11,603 propensity score-matched patients per group (ARB users vs ACEI users) among hypertensive patients undergoing total shoulder arthroplasty (TSA), with 1:1 matching for age, sex, BMI, HbA1c, smoking status, osteoporosis, rheumatoid arthritis, and the Charlson Comorbidity Index to minimise confounding. ARB usage was significantly associated with reduced dislocation rates at 90 days (OR 0.657; p=0.014), 1 year (OR 0.793; p=0.030), and 2 years (OR 0.668; p=0.002) compared to ACEI users, consistent with emerging hypotheses that ARBs may confer musculoskeletal benefits potentially related to their effects on TGF-beta signalling, connective tissue remodelling, and bone mineral density. Periprosthetic fracture rates were similarly reduced in the ARB cohort at all time points (90 days OR 0.674; 1 year OR 0.709; 2 years OR 0.733), and the risk of shoulder stiffness was also significantly lower at 1 year (OR 0.793; p=0.030) and 2 years (OR 0.778; p=0.013), collectively suggesting a multi-dimensional musculoskeletal protective effect of ARB therapy beyond blood pressure control. Subgroup analyses by arthroplasty type (anatomic total, reverse, hemiarthroplasty) showed the periprosthetic fracture benefit was particularly robust for reverse total shoulder arthroplasty and hemiarthroplasty, though effect sizes were not uniformly reproduced across all procedure types, indicating heterogeneity that warrants further investigation. Study limitations include the retrospective design, inability to verify medication adherence or dosing, potential residual confounding despite propensity matching, and the absence of imaging or functional outcome data beyond the complications analysed.

Implications Medical Affairs : While this study is primarily focused on orthopaedic surgery outcomes, it adds to the growing body of evidence suggesting ARBs may offer pleiotropic musculoskeletal benefits over ACEIs in hypertensive patients, representing a differentiation opportunity for ARB positioning in multimorbid patients of advanced age who frequently require both cardiovascular and orthopaedic management. From a Medical Affairs perspective, this pharmacoepidemiological data could support educational initiatives for orthopaedic surgeons and geriatric medicine physicians regarding antihypertensive selection in patients scheduled for arthroplasty, aligning antihypertensive therapy choice with both cardiovascular outcomes and surgical complication risk. This finding warrants prospective validation in dedicated registry studies, and Medical Affairs could consider supporting investigator-initiated research to explore the mechanistic basis of ARB-related musculoskeletal protection, which would generate further evidence and KOL engagement opportunities.

Doxorubicin-induced early-onset chronic progressive cardiotoxicity, pharmacogenetics and survival among breast cancer patients in Zimbabwe

Revue : South African Medical Journal | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-02

Resultats principaux : This prospective observational study enrolled 50 black Zimbabwean women with breast cancer receiving doxorubicin-based chemotherapy, with 3-monthly echocardiographic monitoring for up to 12 months and vital status tracked at 60 months, making it one of the few longitudinal cardio-oncology studies from sub-Saharan Africa with pharmacogenomic profiling. Early-onset chronic progressive cardiotoxicity (ECPC), defined as a sustained reduction in left ventricular ejection fraction greater than 10 percentage points below 53%, was observed in 10% of participants, and median overall survival was dramatically shorter in ECPC-affected patients compared to those without (11.9 vs 40.8 months), with 5-year overall survival of 0% vs 42% (HR 4.19, 95% CI 1.27-13.79; p=0.018). Calcium channel blocker use emerged as an independent predictor of poor overall survival in multivariate analysis (HR 2.38, 95% CI 1.07-5.32; p=0.034), though the direction of causality is uncertain given that CCBs are primarily prescribed in this context for pre-existing hypertension, which may itself be a competing cardiovascular risk factor. Pre-existing hypertension and tumour histological grade did not independently predict cardiotoxicity in this small cohort (n=50), likely reflecting insufficient statistical power and the confounded relationship between hypertension, CCB use, and baseline cardiovascular risk. Pharmacogenomic risk stratification using the SLC28A3-based scoring system showed no significant predictive value in this African population (HR 1.39, 95% CI 0.46-4.25; p=0.561), highlighting important ethnic differences in pharmacogenomics that limit direct application of European-derived biomarkers to African patients.

Implications Medical Affairs : The observed association between CCB use and poor survival in doxorubicin-treated breast cancer patients likely reflects confounding by indication — hypertensive patients receiving CCBs carry underlying cardiovascular vulnerability — rather than a direct pharmacological harm, but this signal reinforces the importance of proactive cardiovascular risk management in oncology patients and the need for careful antihypertensive selection. This study highlights the growing intersection of cardio-oncology and antihypertensive therapy, an area where Medical Affairs can provide educational value to oncologists and cardiologists regarding the cardiovascular implications of antihypertensive drug selection during cytotoxic chemotherapy regimens. Sponsoring educational symposia on antihypertensive management in cancer patients — particularly in resource-limited settings in Africa where cardio-oncology expertise is scarce — would position the company as a committed scientific partner in an emerging subspecialty with substantial unmet educational need.

Implications Medical Affairs : While this veterinary case has limited direct clinical relevance to human antihypertensive therapy, it provides supplementary evidence supporting the role of amlodipine and related CCBs as adjunct vasodilators in combination therapy for pulmonary arterial hypertension, reinforcing the scientific rationale for CCB use in the niche but important PAH indication subset. High dose versus low dose nitroglycerin infusion in the management of hypertensive acute pulmonary edema in an emergency setting: A randomized controlled trial

Revue : Indian Heart Journal | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-04

Resultats principaux : This open-label, pragmatic, parallel randomised controlled trial was conducted in the emergency department of a tertiary care centre in Eastern India (AIIMS Bhubaneswar), enrolling 64 adult patients (32 per arm) presenting with hypertensive acute pulmonary oedema, defined as systolic BP above 160 mmHg or mean arterial pressure above 120 mmHg, all receiving non-invasive ventilation and diuretics as standard care background therapy. Patients were randomised to high-dose intravenous nitroglycerin (starting 100 micrograms/min, titrated up to 200 micrograms/min) or low-dose infusion (starting 10 micrograms/min, up to 100 micrograms/min), evaluating the hypothesis that more aggressive initial vasodilation would achieve faster haemodynamic stabilisation in acute hypertensive heart failure. The primary endpoint of clinical resolution at 3 hours was achieved in 87.5% of the high-dose group compared to 53.3% in the low-dose group (p=0.003), a statistically and clinically significant difference demonstrating that aggressive initial vasodilation materially improves early outcomes in hypertensive acute pulmonary oedema. Time to 25% reduction in systolic blood pressure was significantly shorter in the high-dose arm (p<0.001), and two patients in the low-dose group required endotracheal intubation compared to none in the high-dose group, suggesting that inadequate initial vasodilation may increase the need for invasive mechanical ventilation. Critically, no episodes of clinically significant hypotension were observed in either group despite the substantial dose differential, providing reassuring safety data for high-dose nitroglycerin use in appropriately selected hypertensive emergency patients, though the small sample size (n=64) and single-centre design limit generalisability of this safety finding.

Implications Medical Affairs : This RCT provides important emergency medicine evidence supporting high-dose intravenous nitroglycerin as the superior strategy for rapid blood pressure reduction and clinical stabilisation in hypertensive acute pulmonary oedema, directly impacting emergency department management protocols and positioning aggressive initial vasodilation as the standard of care. From a Medical Affairs perspective, this data supports educational initiatives for emergency physicians, intensivists, and acute cardiology teams regarding optimal nitroglycerin dosing strategies in hypertensive emergencies, an area where clinical practice frequently remains more conservative than evidence supports. This trial provides actionable evidence for inclusion in acute hypertension management educational modules at emergency medicine and critical care congresses, contributing to differentiated scientific leadership positioning in the acute care hypertension management segment.

Prevalence and Predictors of Silent Myocardial Ischemia in Asymptomatic Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus: A Cross-Sectional Study From North India

Revue : Cureus | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-05-03

Resultats principaux : This cross-sectional observational study enrolled 71 patients with type 2 diabetes mellitus without known coronary artery disease at a tertiary care centre in North India (Sharda University), all undergoing treadmill stress testing using the standard Bruce protocol to detect silent myocardial ischaemia (SMI) — objective evidence of myocardial ischaemia without anginal symptoms, mediated in part by diabetic autonomic neuropathy that blunts anginal warning signals. Silent myocardial ischaemia was detected in 18.31% of participants (13 of 71), a clinically significant proportion that highlights the substantial hidden coronary artery disease burden in asymptomatic T2DM patients who would not typically be referred for stress testing based on symptom presentation alone. Treadmill test-positive individuals showed significantly higher prevalence of sedentary lifestyle (84.6% vs 50%, p=0.011), diabetes duration over 5 years (84.6% vs 44.8%, p=0.004), hypertension (46.2% vs 25.9%, p less than 0.001), smoking (76.9% vs 44.8%, p=0.018), and family history of hypertension (69.2% vs 31%, p=0.005) compared to TMT-negative patients, identifying a multi-factorial high-risk phenotype. Biochemical predictors of SMI included significantly elevated serum urea, creatinine, triglycerides, LDL cholesterol, total cholesterol, and urinary albumin-to-creatinine ratio, alongside reduced HDL, collectively suggesting concurrent cardiorenal metabolic derangement in the silent ischaemia subgroup. Hypertension was the single most statistically significant lifestyle and clinical predictor of TMT positivity (p less than 0.001), confirming its central role as a cardiovascular risk amplifier in T2DM patients and supporting the priority of aggressive blood pressure control as a cornerstone of cardiovascular risk reduction in this population.

Implications Medical Affairs : The identification of hypertension as the strongest predictor of silent myocardial ischaemia in asymptomatic T2DM patients reinforces the critical importance of aggressive blood pressure management as an integral component of cardiovascular risk reduction strategies in diabetic patients, providing strong epidemiological support for positioning antihypertensive agents — particularly those with proven cardiovascular outcome benefits — as indispensable components of T2DM management. This data supports co-positioning strategies in diabetology and endocrinology promotional contexts, where antihypertensive therapy with cardiovascular endpoint data (ACEIs, ARBs, CCBs) can be presented as essential cardiovascular protection tools alongside glucose-lowering agents in T2DM patients with additional risk factors. Engaging endocrinology, diabetology, and internal medicine KOLs with this epidemiological evidence could strengthen the case for systematic cardiovascular screening and early intensive antihypertensive therapy initiation in T2DM patients, particularly in the South Asian population where cardiometabolic risk clustering is disproportionately high.
