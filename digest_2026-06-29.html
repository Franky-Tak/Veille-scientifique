Clinical characteristics of acupoint sensitization in patients with essential hypertension: a cross-sectional matched case-control study.

Revue : Journal of Traditional Chinese Medicine | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : June 2026

Resultats principaux : This cross-sectional matched case-control study enrolled 448 volunteers from China (224 patients with essential hypertension and 224 age- and sex-matched healthy controls) to characterize acupoint sensitization patterns associated with primary hypertension. The primary outcome measures were mechanical pain threshold (MPT) and tenderness threshold (TT) assessed at 16 predefined acupoints using standardized algometric pressure methods. Hypertensive patients demonstrated significantly lower MPT and TT values compared to healthy controls across all measured acupoints (p<0.001), indicating markedly heightened mechanosensitivity at acupuncture sites in the hypertensive population. Receiver operating characteristic (ROC) curve analysis identified six optimal high-sensitivity acupoints for essential hypertension: Quchi (LI11), Taichong (LR3), Fenglong (ST40), Xingjian (LR2), Taixi (KI3), and Renying (ST9), which showed the highest sensitization incidence and degree. Notable limitations include the cross-sectional design precluding causal inference, potential selection bias from voluntary enrollment, and the absence of data on antihypertensive medication status, blood pressure control levels, or ambulatory blood pressure monitoring that would allow for subgroup stratification.

Implications Medical Affairs : This study provides preliminary clinical evidence supporting acupoint sensitization as a measurable physiological correlate of essential hypertension, which may gradually influence integrative medicine guidelines within Traditional Chinese Medicine-adopting health systems, particularly across the Asia-Pacific region. From a Medical Affairs perspective, these findings do not challenge first-line pharmacological antihypertensive therapies but highlight growing scientific momentum around complementary non-pharmacological approaches that could be leveraged in messaging for combination lifestyle-pharmacotherapy programs in key Asian markets. An actionable KOL strategy insight is to engage acupuncture and integrative cardiology specialists in China, Japan, and South Korea to explore whether acupoint sensitization profiling could serve as a supplementary biomarker in future clinical trials evaluating antihypertensives alongside acupuncture as adjuvant therapy.
