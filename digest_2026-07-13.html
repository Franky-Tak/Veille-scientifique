Effect of diltiazem sustained-release capsules on cardiorenal composite outcomes in hypertensive patients with coronary heart disease: A real-world propensity score-matched study.

Revue : Pakistan Journal of Pharmaceutical Sciences | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026 Sep

Resultats principaux : This retrospective cohort study enrolled 302 consecutive patients with coexisting coronary heart disease (CHD) and hypertension treated at the Affiliated Hospital of Jiangnan University (May 2021 to May 2024), comparing add-on sustained-release diltiazem capsules (n=144) against standard guideline-directed therapy alone (n=158). Propensity score matching was applied to balance baseline covariates and minimize selection bias between the two groups. Over a median follow-up of 18 months, the diltiazem group demonstrated a significantly lower cumulative incidence of cardiorenal composite endpoint events compared with controls (16.39% vs. 32.79%, P=0.002). Multivariate Cox regression confirmed that diltiazem therapy was independently associated with a 53.5% reduction in composite outcome risk (hazard ratio 0.465, 95% CI: 0.284-0.760, P=0.002), with the benefit driven primarily by fewer heart failure rehospitalizations. Patients receiving diltiazem also achieved superior combined blood pressure and heart rate control targets (all P<0.05), with adverse event rates comparable between groups. Key limitations include the retrospective single-center design, modest sample size, and exploratory nature of renal endpoints due to low event numbers in this subgroup.

Implications Medical Affairs : These real-world propensity-matched data reinforce the role of heart-rate-lowering calcium channel blockers as a complementary antihypertensive strategy in patients with coexisting CHD, supporting consideration for individualized combination therapy frameworks in updated guidelines. From a product-positioning standpoint, evidence of dual cardiorenal risk reduction with diltiazem SR strengthens the value narrative for CCBs in complex hypertension-CHD populations requiring concurrent blood pressure and heart rate control. A targeted KOL engagement strategy with cardiologists managing this overlap phenotype, combined with advocacy for a prospective confirmatory trial, represents the most actionable Medical Affairs follow-up.

Predictors of Hypertensive Disorders of Pregnancy in Japan: A Population-Based Retrospective Cohort Study Using the Shizuoka Kokuho Database.

Revue : The Journal of Obstetrics and Gynaecology Research | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026 Jul

Resultats principaux : This population-based retrospective cohort study used health insurance claims data from the Shizuoka Kokuho Database in Japan (2012-2020) to identify predictors of hypertensive disorders of pregnancy (HDP) in 6,362 pregnant women followed over a 280-day period. Among participants, 222 (3.5%) were diagnosed with HDP, reflecting the magnitude of this obstetric complication in an aging Japanese population. Multivariable logistic regression identified advanced maternal age (OR 1.05, 95% CI: 1.02-1.07 per year), diabetes mellitus (OR 3.21, 95% CI: 1.22-8.47), preexisting hypertension (OR 2.38, 95% CI: 1.04-5.46), and systemic lupus erythematosus (OR 5.90, 95% CI: 1.41-24.65) as independent predictors of HDP onset. Conditional inference tree analysis further identified maternal age >34 years as the primary risk-stratification threshold, followed by hyperlipidemia and hypertension as secondary splitting variables; polycystic ovary syndrome emerged as an additional risk factor in older women without prior cardiometabolic diagnoses. Study limitations include reliance on administrative claims data with potential coding inaccuracies, single-prefecture geographic scope, and absence of important clinical variables such as BMI and laboratory values.

Implications Medical Affairs : This study quantifies the epidemiological burden of HDP in an aging obstetric population and identifies a high-risk patient segment — older women with preexisting hypertension and metabolic comorbidities — where safe, pregnancy-validated antihypertensive strategies are critically needed. For Medical Affairs, the data support differentiated product positioning for antihypertensives with established safety profiles in pregnancy, particularly in the Japanese market context where advanced maternal age is increasingly prevalent. Collaboration with maternal-fetal medicine and obstetric KOLs, alongside integration into prenatal risk-stratification clinical pathways, represents a high-priority engagement opportunity for the medical team.

Theme 6 : Clinical Trials and Guidelines (1 article)

Bioactive phytochemicals of jasmine oil and their role in managing hypertension: A combined clinical and study.

Revue : Pakistan Journal of Pharmaceutical Sciences | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026 Sep

Resultats principaux : This randomized controlled trial (registration IRCT20230103057033N2) enrolled 300 traffic wardens with stage 2 hypertension in Pakistan between November 2022 and March 2023, randomly assigned to three groups: jasmine oil applied with an electrical massager (Group A), manual jasmine oil massage (Group B), and electrical massager alone (Group C), each receiving 6 sessions per week for 3 weeks (40 minutes per session). Group A demonstrated highly significant reductions in blood pressure and cardiovascular parameters (p=0.0001), Group B showed significant effects (p<0.005), while Group C produced non-significant results (p>0.005), establishing the necessity of the phytochemical component. A complementary molecular docking analysis evaluated four key jasmine oil phytoconstituents (phytosterol, alpha-pinene, benzyl acetate, linalool) against human voltage-gated sodium channel isoforms (Nav1.7, Nav1.8, Nav1.9), finding that Nav1.9 exhibited the most favorable binding affinity, suggesting a plausible ion-channel-mediated mechanism of action. Secondary outcomes including blood cholesterol, respiration rate, heart rate, and oxygen saturation were also improved in the combined treatment group, broadening the cardiometabolic benefit profile. The study is limited by absence of blinding, short treatment duration (3 weeks), reliance on a single occupational subgroup, and incomplete reporting of absolute blood pressure values and standard deviations for each outcome.

Implications Medical Affairs : This pilot RCT provides preliminary clinical evidence that phytochemical-based non-pharmacological interventions may offer adjunctive blood pressure reduction in occupational hypertension settings, though the evidence base remains far below the threshold required to influence clinical guidelines or challenge established pharmacotherapy. The Nav1.9 molecular docking data represents a mechanistic lead worth monitoring, as sodium channel modulation could theoretically complement existing antihypertensive mechanisms; however, robust pharmacokinetic and efficacy data in diverse populations are absent. Medical Affairs teams should track this area to proactively address potential patient-driven enquiries about natural remedies, positioning guideline-recommended antihypertensives as the evidence-based standard while engaging KOLs in high-quality comparative effectiveness research.

Theme 7 : Environment and Risk Factors (2 articles)

Smoking as an independent predictor of long term care certification: The Yamagata cohort study.

Revue : Tobacco Induced Diseases | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026 Jul 10

Resultats principaux : This prospective cohort study from the Yamagata cohort in Japan followed participants for a mean of 7.6 years (SD=1.8) to determine whether baseline smoking status independently predicts long-term care (LTC) certification, a functional disability outcome of major public health relevance. At baseline, the overall smoking prevalence was 12.4% (22.4% in men, 4.6% in women), and LTC certification was issued to 262 individuals (2.6%) during follow-up. Unadjusted Cox proportional hazards analysis identified a significantly elevated risk for current smokers (HR=1.44, 95% CI: 1.03-2.02), which strengthened after adjustment for age and sex (AHR=1.81, 95% CI: 1.23-2.66) and further adjustment for lifestyle, hypertension, diabetes, and dyslipidemia (AHR=1.83, 95% CI: 1.25-2.69). After accounting for nine confounders including stroke and ischemic heart disease, the hazard ratio remained significant (AHR=1.86, 95% CI: 1.27-2.73), confirming smoking as an independent predictor of LTC certification above and beyond its indirect contribution through cardiovascular disease. Former smokers showed no significant difference from never-smokers in adjusted models, and a sensitivity analysis excluding patients with baseline cardiovascular disease yielded consistent results. Limitations include potential residual confounding, self-reported smoking status without biochemical verification, and single-prefecture geographic scope limiting national generalizability.

Implications Medical Affairs : This cohort study provides robust epidemiological evidence that smoking independently accelerates functional decline in older adults, adding a disability-prevention rationale to existing cardiovascular arguments for smoking cessation in hypertensive patients. For Medical Affairs, these data strengthen the scientific case for bundled risk-factor management programs that combine antihypertensive therapy with structured smoking cessation support, enhancing the overall value proposition of cardiovascular risk reduction strategies. Engagement with Japanese public health and geriatric medicine KOLs, and integration into aging-population health policy discussions, presents a clear opportunity to position comprehensive hypertension management as a driver of functional health preservation.

A Nutrient Ratio-Based, Web-Enabled Food Quality Score Is Associated With Weight and Blood Pressure Compared With Leading Nutrient Profiling Systems.

Revue : Current Developments in Nutrition | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026 May 29

Resultats principaux : This cross-sectional study analyzed NHANES 2015-2016 data from 9,971 US adults aged 20 years or older to compare the Nutrient Consume Score (NCS), a web-enabled ratio-based nutrient profiling system (NPS), against four established NPSs (Nutri-Score, Health Star Rating, NOVA Classification, and Food Compass 2.0) in terms of associations with obesity and blood pressure outcomes. Dietary intake was assessed by a single 24-hour dietary recall, and multivariable regression models were adjusted for sociodemographic and health covariates. Higher NCS was significantly associated with lower BMI (-0.64 kg/m2 per 10-unit increase, 95% CI: -0.86 to -0.41; P<0.0001), lower waist circumference (-1.63 cm, 95% CI: -2.23 to -1.02; P<0.0001), lower systolic blood pressure (-1.01 mmHg, 95% CI: -1.67 to -0.35; P=0.0051), and lower diastolic blood pressure (-0.56 mmHg, 95% CI: -0.90 to -0.23; P=0.0026), with effect sizes comparable to established NPSs. Compositional analysis identified sodium-to-potassium and carbohydrate-to-fiber ratios as the primary dietary ratio drivers of blood pressure associations, while calorie-to-weight and saturated-to-unsaturated fat ratios were most strongly linked to weight outcomes. The cross-sectional design precludes causal inference, and reliance on a single dietary recall day introduces measurement error and may not capture habitual dietary patterns.

Implications Medical Affairs : These findings from a large nationally representative US cohort reinforce dietary quality — particularly sodium-to-potassium balance — as a clinically relevant modifiable determinant of blood pressure, supporting non-pharmacological lifestyle interventions as essential complements to antihypertensive pharmacotherapy. For Medical Affairs, the comparability of the NCS with established systems like Nutri-Score and Food Compass 2.0 signals growing scientific and regulatory interest in digital dietary assessment tools that could be integrated into hypertension management apps or patient education programs. Collaborations with nutrition scientists and cardiovascular prevention guideline committees offer a strategic avenue to embed dietary quality scoring into holistic blood pressure management frameworks, strengthening the ecosystem around antihypertensive treatment.
