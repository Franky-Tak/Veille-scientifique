Implications of systolic pulmonary artery pressure trajectories in systemic lupus erythematosus-associated pulmonary hypertension: a CSTAR-PAH cohort study.

Revue : Frontiers in Immunology | Tier : NA | IF estimé : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-09

Résultats principaux : This retrospective cohort study from the CSTAR-PAH registry included 81 patients with systemic lupus erythematosus-associated pulmonary arterial hypertension (SLE-PAH) at a single Chinese center, using group-based trajectory modelling to delineate longitudinal sPAP evolution patterns over follow-up. Four distinct sPAP trajectories were identified: high initial sPAP with rapid decrease (n=17, 20.9%), moderate initial sPAP with slow decrease (n=44, 54.3%), low initial sPAP with slow decrease (n=13, 16.0%), and high initial sPAP with rapid increase (trajectory 4, n=7, 8.6%). Trajectory 4 (rapid progression) was the strongest independent predictor of all-cause mortality after multivariable adjustment (HR=8.843, p=0.037), with significantly worse survival by Kaplan-Meier analysis (log-rank p<0.0001). Key baseline predictors of trajectory 4 included shorter 6-minute walking distance (OR=0.220, p<0.001), elevated BNP (OR=2.159, p<0.001), higher IL-1beta (OR=1.412, p<0.001), and higher TNF-alpha (OR=1.751, p<0.001). Additional independent mortality predictors included elevated SLE disease activity index (HR=1.502), IL-6 (HR=1.031), and systemic inflammation response index (HR=1.828). The study's main limitation is its retrospective single-center design with a small sample (n=81), constraining generalizability to broader SLE-PAH populations.

Implications Medical Affairs : Trajectory-based phenotyping of SLE-PAH using longitudinal sPAP dynamics offers a novel, clinically actionable risk stratification tool that complements traditional biomarkers, providing a rationale for earlier and more aggressive therapeutic intervention in the rapid-progression subgroup. This study supports positioning targeted anti-inflammatory and PAH-specific therapies for the high-risk trajectory phenotype, reinforcing a precision medicine framework for personalized treatment algorithms in autoimmune pulmonary hypertension from a Medical Affairs standpoint. This research creates a strategic KOL engagement opportunity at the rheumatology-cardiology interface and highlights the scientific value of prospective multicenter studies validating trajectory-based endpoints in clinical trial design.

What do Swiss doctors think about cardiovascular risk factors and guidelines?

Revue : Praxis (Bern 1994) | Tier : NA | IF estimé : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06

Résultats principaux : This cross-sectional survey of 224 Swiss physicians examined attitudes toward cardiovascular risk factors and adherence to primary prevention clinical guidelines across multiple specialties. Respondents rated smoking as a more important cardiovascular risk factor than hypertension, diabetes mellitus, or hyperlipidemia, suggesting a relative underestimation of hypertension's relative contribution to cardiovascular morbidity. The vast majority of respondents rated their cardiovascular risk factor knowledge as high, yet a majority expressed skepticism about the evidence base for current primary prevention guidelines. Many physicians considered elevated blood pressure and lipid levels acceptable in older patients, viewing current guideline thresholds as excessively strict for this age group. The primary perceived barriers to guideline implementation were patient non-adherence, medication side effects, and insufficient patient health literacy, rather than the guidelines themselves. The study is limited by its cross-sectional design, convenience sampling, small sample size, and potential responder bias.

Implications Medical Affairs : This survey reveals a significant knowledge-practice gap among Swiss physicians regarding hypertension treatment targets, particularly for elderly patients, which may partly explain persistently suboptimal BP control rates across European cardiovascular cohorts. For Medical Affairs, these findings support targeted educational strategies aimed at realigning physician perception of hypertension risk with evidence-based guideline recommendations, particularly addressing thresholds for older patient populations. KOL-led scientific exchange programs and symposia focusing on outcome data justifying current BP targets represent high-value tactical opportunities for pharmaceutical companies promoting antihypertensive therapies in Western Europe.

Association of I/D and ACE (rs4343) polymorphism with MACE development in hypertensive patients.

Revue : Cardiovascular Journal of Africa | Tier : NA | IF estimé : NA

Date de publication : 2026-04-17

Résultats principaux : This prospective cohort study enrolled 429 adult patients aged 30-70 years from Khyber Pakhtunkhwa, Pakistan, presenting with cardiovascular risk factors or established cardiovascular disease, followed over 12 months for major adverse cardiovascular events (MACE). The ACE I/D genotype distribution was: ID heterozygous 67.6%, II homozygous 28.0%, and DD homozygous 4.4%, broadly consistent with reported South Asian population frequencies. A significant association was found between the ID genotype and ejection fraction (p=0.019) and pulmonary arterial hypertension (p=0.008), while the ACE rs4343 variant showed no significant independent association with MACE occurrence in this cohort. Most participants had a hypertension duration exceeding 10 years (69.2%), and 78.1% were aware of their hypertensive status at enrollment. Genotype and allele frequencies for ACE rs4343 did not differ significantly between case and control subgroups, suggesting limited independent pharmacogenomic utility of this variant for MACE prediction in this population. Limitations include the 12-month follow-up, single-center design, absence of ethnicity-matched controls, and modest sample size limiting statistical power for rare MACE endpoints.

Implications Medical Affairs : This study contributes pharmacogenomic data on antihypertensive therapy in South Asian populations, demonstrating that the ACE rs4343 variant does not independently predict MACE risk, while the I/D polymorphism retains associations with cardiac functional parameters relevant to therapeutic stratification. From a Medical Affairs standpoint, these findings reinforce the need for expanded genetic profiling studies in underrepresented South Asian hypertensive populations, where precision medicine data for cardiovascular prevention remain limited and ACE inhibitor prescribing may benefit from genotype-guided guidance. Future prospective pharmacogenomic investigations in Pakistani and broader South Asian cohorts would provide strategic differentiation opportunities for positioning ACE inhibitors versus ARBs within precision antihypertensive treatment algorithms.

Disease risk perception, health intervention intention and their influencing factors among prehypertensive and hypertensive populations: a cross-sectional study with 1050 participants.

Revue : BMC Public Health | Tier : NA | IF estimé : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-25

Résultats principaux : This cross-sectional study enrolled 1,050 adults (525 prehypertensive, 525 hypertensive) via convenience sampling from community centers and hypertension clinics in Chengdu, China (January-December 2025), using the Health Belief Model (HBM) framework to assess health cognition, lifestyle behaviors, and health intervention intention. Hypertensive patients were significantly older, had more comorbidities, higher perceived disease severity, better lifestyle behaviors, and stronger intervention willingness compared to prehypertensive individuals (all p<0.001). Lifestyle behaviors were positively correlated with intervention intention in both groups (r=0.533, p<0.01), serving as a universal predictor of willingness to engage with preventive health measures. For prehypertensive individuals, lifestyle behaviors (OR=1.533, p<0.001) and health motivation (OR=1.398, p=0.001) were the primary predictors of intervention intention; among hypertensive patients, self-efficacy (OR=1.464, p<0.001) and perceived susceptibility (OR=1.163, p=0.029) were dominant. The prehypertension model demonstrated the highest explanatory variance (Nagelkerke R2=0.849), indicating that early-stage risk perception is a particularly potent driver of preventive behavioral intent. Limitations include convenience sampling from a single Chinese municipality, cross-sectional design precluding causal inference, and limited generalizability beyond urban Chinese populations.

Implications Medical Affairs : These findings demonstrate that lifestyle-focused messaging and motivational health education are the primary drivers of intervention uptake in prehypertensive individuals, a segment often underserved by pharmaceutical-led educational programs, identifying a concrete opportunity for early-stage cardiovascular prevention engagement strategies. From a product positioning perspective, integrating self-efficacy enhancement and disease awareness messaging into patient support programs for diagnosed hypertensives could meaningfully complement pharmacological adherence campaigns and reduce long-term cardiovascular risk burden. This study supports the development of stage-specific educational content for KOL-led community outreach, advocating differentiated messaging strategies for prehypertension prevention versus established hypertension management across primary care networks.

Effect of an early time restricted eating Mediterranean diet compared to naltrexone/bupropion on liver fibrosis in people with cardiometabolic risk factors: study protocol for a randomised controlled trial (MEDFAST trial).

Revue : BMJ Open | Tier : NA | IF estimé : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-25

Résultats principaux : The MEDFAST trial protocol describes a randomized controlled trial comparing an early time-restricted eating Mediterranean diet (eTRE-MD, eating window 8AM-6PM) versus naltrexone/bupropion 32mg/360mg in 70 overweight adults (BMI >27 kg/m2) aged 18-75 years with cardiometabolic risk factors including type 2 diabetes, hypertension, dyslipidemia or obesity, and moderate-to-severe liver fibrosis (LSM 7.0-13.6 kPa by transient elastography). The primary endpoint is the between-group difference in liver stiffness (kPa) at 6 months, with secondary endpoints including liver steatosis, body weight, body composition, cardiovascular risk factors, quality of life, patient satisfaction, and compliance. Hypertension is explicitly included as a key cardiometabolic entry criterion, positioning this trial at the interface of cardiovascular risk management and metabolic-associated steatotic liver disease (MASLD) prevention. The trial addresses a critical comparative evidence gap by providing the first direct head-to-head comparison of structured dietary intervention versus approved weight-loss pharmacotherapy on liver fibrosis outcomes in a multimorbid cardiometabolic population. Protocol limitations include the open-label design (dietary blinding is not feasible), modest planned sample size (n=70), single-center recruitment in the Netherlands, and the 6-month follow-up which may be insufficient to capture divergent fibrosis trajectories.

Implications Medical Affairs : The MEDFAST trial is positioned to generate comparative effectiveness data in a cardiometabolic population where hypertension frequently co-occurs with MASLD and obesity, with findings likely to influence integrated multi-morbidity management guidelines at the intersection of cardiology and hepatology. From a Medical Affairs perspective, this trial reinforces the strategic value of positioning antihypertensive therapies within multi-morbidity frameworks alongside lifestyle interventions, supporting holistic cardiovascular risk reduction narratives that extend beyond BP control alone. The MEDFAST platform creates a scientific engagement opportunity for KOL collaboration around integrated cardiometabolic care, with results that may inform product positioning for therapies targeting the overlapping metabolic, hepatic, and cardiovascular indication space.

Socioeconomic disparities in modifiable risk burden and dementia prevention: A prospective cohort study.

Revue : The Journal of Nutrition, Health and Aging | Tier : NA | IF estimé : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-25

Résultats principaux : This prospective cohort study analyzed 344,793 UK Biobank participants without baseline dementia (2006-2010), followed for a median of 14.1 years, during which 5,515 individuals developed incident dementia. A weighted composite dementia risk score was constructed from the 14 modifiable risk factors identified by the Lancet Commission, assigning weights using log(relative risk) times (1 - communality) to reflect independent attributable contributions. The total population attributable fraction for all 14 modifiable risk factors was 40.70% (95% CI: 35.81%-45.23%), indicating that over 40% of dementia cases may be preventable. Hypertension was specifically associated with dementia risk in the low-SES group only, demonstrating SES-moderated risk that may be masked at the aggregate population level due to differential treatment access. Each 1-point increase in the composite risk score corresponded to a 19% higher dementia risk (95% CI: 17%-21%), with amplified associations in early-onset dementia, younger baseline participants, APOE epsilon4 non-carriers, and low-SES populations. Limitations include observational design, potential healthy-participant bias inherent to the UK Biobank, residual confounding, and limited ethnic diversity.

Implications Medical Affairs : The SES-specific association of hypertension with dementia risk provides a dual cardiovascular-cognitive prevention rationale for prioritizing antihypertensive treatment coverage in low-SES populations, strengthening public health arguments for equitable access to antihypertensive pharmacotherapy. From a Medical Affairs perspective, these data support expanding the clinical value narrative of antihypertensive therapies to include dementia prevention benefits, informing future label discussions and health economic arguments for broader prescribing coverage in high-risk socioeconomically vulnerable populations. Strategic KOL collaborations across hypertension management, geriatrics, and neurology disciplines can leverage these findings to position antihypertensive therapies within neurocardiovascular prevention frameworks and support guideline revision advocacy in relevant medical societies.

Impact of a comprehensive healthy lifestyle score on a broad spectrum of cardiometabolic and related diseases and its incremental value for risk prediction based on the UK Biobank database.

Revue : Journal of Cardiothoracic Surgery | Tier : NA | IF estimé : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-24

Résultats principaux : This large prospective study analyzed 148,155 UK Biobank participants without baseline cardiometabolic diseases using Cox proportional hazards models to assess the relationship between a composite healthy lifestyle score (non-smoking, non-excessive drinking, regular physical activity, healthy diet, and healthy sleep) and 11 cardiometabolic disease outcomes including hypertension, pulmonary hypertension, coronary heart disease, type 2 diabetes, and stroke. Adherence to the healthiest lifestyle category was associated with a 30% lower composite cardiometabolic disease risk versus the very unhealthy group (HR: 0.70, 95% CI: 0.67-0.74), with a clear dose-response gradient across lifestyle score categories. Pulmonary hypertension showed the strongest inverse lifestyle association of all conditions examined (HR: 0.34, 95% CI: 0.24-0.50), while systemic hypertension also demonstrated significant inverse associations. Healthy sleep carried the largest individual weight in the composite lifestyle score (beta=0.342), surpassing diet and physical activity as a preventive factor, highlighting its underappreciated role in cardiometabolic risk reduction. Incorporating the composite lifestyle score into traditional risk prediction models provided modest but statistically significant improvements in discriminative performance. Limitations include observational design with potential reverse causality, reliance on self-reported lifestyle data, and limited ethnic diversity.

Implications Medical Affairs : The dose-response association between comprehensive lifestyle adherence and reduced hypertension risk provides robust epidemiological support for integrating lifestyle modification, particularly sleep hygiene, as a foundational pillar of updated antihypertensive management guidelines alongside pharmacological treatment. These findings present a strategic opportunity for Medical Affairs to position antihypertensive products within an integrated cardiovascular risk reduction narrative combining pharmacological and lifestyle pillars, differentiating scientific communications from purely pharmacocentric messaging. KOL engagement around multifactorial hypertension prevention, with specific emphasis on sleep quality as a newly quantified and actionable risk factor, can strengthen guideline-aligned prescribing behavior and support real-world patient adherence messaging.

Hypertension and cardiometabolic multimorbidity: an analysis of 3.4 million participants from the global May Measurement Month blood pressure screening campaign.

Revue : BMJ Public Health | Tier : NA | IF estimé : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-15

Résultats principaux : This cross-sectional analysis from the May Measurement Month (MMM) global blood pressure campaign included 3,397,746 adult participants across 105 countries screened with triplicate blood pressure measurement and standardized cardiovascular risk factor questionnaires. Among participants, 91,222 (2.7% of total) had cardiometabolic multimorbidity (CMM), defined as co-existence of at least two of three conditions: diabetes, myocardial infarction, and stroke; 56.1% of CMM individuals also had co-existing hypertension. Despite significantly higher blood pressure-lowering treatment rates in hypertensives with CMM (85.6%) versus no cardiometabolic conditions (45.4%), BP control rates below 140/90 mmHg were similar across all subgroups, revealing persistent inadequate control despite high treatment intensity. The burden of hypertension and co-treatment increased progressively with the number of concurrent cardiometabolic conditions, with all-three-condition individuals most likely to have co-existing hypertension and be on antihypertensive therapy. These data document a major global unmet need for improved BP control in multimorbid patients, suggesting therapeutic inertia or inadequate treatment intensification in very high-risk populations. Limitations include cross-sectional design, opportunistic convenience sampling introducing selection bias, and single-visit BP measurement in a non-clinical community screening setting.

Implications Medical Affairs : This uniquely large global dataset demonstrating poor BP control in CMM populations despite high treatment rates provides compelling advocacy evidence for therapeutic intensification and fixed-dose combination antihypertensive strategies, directly supporting forthcoming guideline updates for multimorbid hypertensive patients. From a Medical Affairs perspective, the MMM findings create a powerful scientific narrative for combination antihypertensive products and polypill approaches, particularly in low- and middle-income countries where treatment access exists but therapeutic quality and BP control rates remain suboptimal. These data support KOL-led scientific communications that reframe the cardiovascular risk debate from treatment coverage to treatment quality and control rates, underpinning market access and health economic arguments for combination therapies in cardiometabolic multimorbidity.

Prevalence and Determinants of Uncontrolled Hypertension Among Treated Adults in a Rural Primary Health Care Facility in South Africa: A Cross-Sectional Study.

Revue : Epidemiologia (Basel, Switzerland) | Tier : NA | IF estimé : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-10

Résultats principaux : This cross-sectional study enrolled 103 hypertensive patients receiving follow-up care at a rural community health centre in the Eastern Cape Province of South Africa (August-October 2024), evaluating the prevalence of uncontrolled hypertension and its associated determinants. The mean participant age was 63+/-13 years, 65% were female, and the prevalence of uncontrolled hypertension (SBP >=140 mmHg and/or DBP >=90 mmHg) was markedly elevated at 63.1% (65/103). The median systolic blood pressure was 149 mmHg (IQR: 135-163 mmHg) and median diastolic blood pressure was 84 mmHg (IQR: 76-89 mmHg), indicating a population far from guideline-recommended targets. Low medication adherence assessed by the Hill-Bone Compliance Scale was the strongest independent predictor of uncontrolled hypertension by multivariable logistic regression (OR=4.20, 95% CI: 1.75-10.09, p=0.001). Forgetfulness and non-use of reminder systems were the most frequently reported adherence barriers, suggesting that simple behavioral interventions could yield substantial control improvements. Limitations include small single-facility sample (n=103), cross-sectional design, convenience sampling, and limited scope of the adherence assessment tool.

Implications Medical Affairs : The finding that nearly two-thirds of treated hypertensive patients in a rural South African primary care setting remain uncontrolled, driven predominantly by medication non-adherence, identifies a critical unmet need that pharmaceutical companies can address through patient support programs featuring simplified regimens, community-based reminder systems, and adherence monitoring tools. For Medical Affairs, these data provide a strong evidence base for advocating once-daily fixed-dose combination antihypertensive therapies that reduce pill burden and facilitate adherence in low-resource settings, aligned with WHO essential medicines and global cardiovascular health equity agendas. KOL engagement with African primary care networks and public health advocates can leverage these findings to position adherence-optimized antihypertensive formulations as practical and scalable solutions to hypertension control disparities in sub-Saharan Africa.

Knowledge of Hypertension and Self-Blood Pressure Measurement Skills in Spain.

Revue : Journal of Primary Care and Community Health | Tier : NA | IF estimé : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-25

Résultats principaux : This descriptive cross-sectional observational study enrolled 151 adults with diagnosed hypertension from urban and rural primary care centers in Spain in 2025, assessing hypertension knowledge using the validated Hypertension Knowledge Questionnaire (15 items) and evaluating self-blood pressure measurement skills in routine practice. Most participants were unaware of the diagnostic BP threshold for hypertension (>=140/90 mmHg per European guidelines), and 94% exhibited poor systolic blood pressure control at the time of assessment. Higher educational level was significantly associated with better knowledge of cardiovascular, renal, and cerebrovascular hypertension complications, as well as more accurate self-blood pressure measurement practices, including correct technique and frequency. However, no significant association was found between educational level and overall blood pressure control, indicating that knowledge acquisition alone does not translate into improved clinical BP management outcomes. Sex-based differences were observed, with women demonstrating better diastolic blood pressure control than men, despite no significant difference in hypertension knowledge scores between genders. Limitations include small sample size (n=151), cross-sectional design, convenience sampling from a single Spanish region, and absence of objective medication adherence verification.

Implications Medical Affairs : The fundamental disconnect between hypertension knowledge and blood pressure control in this Spanish cohort challenges the assumption that patient education alone drives clinical outcomes, pointing to a multifactorial management gap involving comorbidities, adherence barriers, and sociodemographic factors that require integrated and personalized patient support strategies. From a Medical Affairs perspective, these findings support investment in structured self-measurement programs and personalized digital health tools within patient support programs, addressing the knowledge-to-action gap as a significant unmet need where pharmaceutical-led adherence programs can add measurable clinical value. KOL collaborations with primary care specialists in Spain and Europe can leverage these data to advocate for differentiated hypertension management protocols combining evidence-based education with pharmacological adherence-enhancing strategies including simplified dosing regimens and digital patient engagement tools.
