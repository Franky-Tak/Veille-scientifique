Associations between systemic inflammation, nutritional status, and cardiometabolic diseases and risk factors among adults living in transitional rural communities in Ecuador.

Revue : PLoS One | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-01

Resultats principaux : This cross-sectional study enrolled 931 adults from ten transitional rural agricultural communities in coastal Ecuador to examine associations between systemic inflammatory markers (high-sensitivity CRP and total IgE) and cardiometabolic diseases including hypertension, type 2 diabetes, metabolic syndrome, and vascular disease, using multivariable regression models adjusted for age, sex, and household clustering. Elevated CRP (≥3 mg/L) was highly prevalent (50.9%) and significantly associated with hypertension (aOR 1.69, 95% CI: 1.19–2.39), type 2 diabetes (aOR 2.53, 95% CI: 1.77–3.64), and metabolic syndrome (aOR 3.24, 95% CI: 2.30–4.58). The association with multimorbidity (≥3–4 conditions vs. none: aOR 5.96, 95% CI: 3.52–10.08, p<0.001) was particularly striking, positioning CRP as a composite marker of cumulative metabolic burden. In contrast, elevated total IgE (≥140 IU/mL) showed no significant association with cardiometabolic outcomes, and CRP associations were attenuated after BMI adjustment, implicating adiposity as the primary upstream mediator of systemic inflammation. Limitations include the cross-sectional design precluding causal inference, reliance on single-point inflammatory measurements, and potential residual confounding from unmeasured dietary and environmental factors specific to the rural Ecuadorian context.

Implications Medical Affairs : These findings reinforce the role of systemic low-grade inflammation — particularly CRP — as a clinically actionable biomarker for hypertension risk stratification in underserved rural populations in Latin America, supporting guideline development that incorporates inflammatory markers in resource-limited settings. For Medical Affairs, this study highlights a substantial unmet need for hypertension screening and treatment in transitional rural communities in emerging economies, representing a strategic opportunity to support patient identification programs and access initiatives in Latin American markets. KOL engagement with cardiovascular epidemiologists and public health specialists working on inflammation-driven hypertension in low-resource settings could reinforce positioning of anti-inflammatory or lifestyle-targeted therapeutic approaches and support publication strategies in global health journals.

Influence of antihypertensive drug classes on circadian blood pressure patterns and left ventricular remodeling - a retrospective study.

Revue : European Journal of Clinical Pharmacology | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-01

Resultats principaux : This retrospective observational study enrolled 500 Chinese adults with essential hypertension from a tertiary care center (2020–2024), analyzing circadian BP phenotypes via 24-hour ambulatory BP monitoring (ABPM) and LV remodeling by transthoracic echocardiography performed within six months of ABPM. Nearly half of patients (48.6%) exhibited non-dipping or reverse-dipping BP patterns, and LV hypertrophy was present in 27.2%; LVMI and relative wall thickness increased progressively with worsening circadian phenotype (p<0.001). RAAS-blocker-based regimens and evening/bedtime dosing were significantly associated with lower nighttime BP, greater nocturnal BP decline, reduced morning BP surge, and higher dipping prevalence versus non-RAAS or morning-only regimens (all p<0.01). In multivariable models, non-dipping (OR 1.89) and reverse-dipping (OR 2.64) patterns, along with higher nighttime SBP, were independently associated with LVH, with partial attenuation after adjustment for nocturnal BP indices. Limitations include the retrospective single-center Chinese design, potential confounding by medication adherence and comorbidities, and inability to establish causality between dosing timing and cardiac structural outcomes.

Implications Medical Affairs : This real-world study provides substantive evidence supporting ABPM-guided chronotherapy in hypertension, demonstrating that RAAS blockers with evening dosing optimize nocturnal BP control and reduce LV hypertrophy risk — findings with direct implications for individualized treatment guidance in evolving hypertension guidelines and prescribing recommendations. From a Medical Affairs perspective, the strong association between circadian BP phenotypes and cardiac remodeling reinforces the clinical message that ABPM should be standard of care for assessing true BP burden, supporting educational programs targeting cardiologists and hypertension specialists around ABPM utility and the clinical relevance of dosing timing. The findings offer a compelling basis for positioning RAAS-based antihypertensive therapies with flexible dosing in the Chinese patient population, and highlight a KOL engagement opportunity around chronopharmacology and cardiac protective endpoints in hypertension management.

Associations of Long-Term Blood Pressure Burden and Variability With Kidney Function Decline.

Revue : Journal of Clinical Hypertension | Tier : Tier 3 | IF estime : 3–10

Date de publication : 2026-06

Resultats principaux : This prospective cohort analysis included 2,821 Swiss adults with preserved kidney function from the CoLaus|PsyCoLaus study, deriving cumulative BP burden and visit-to-visit variability from measurements at baseline and two follow-up visits, with outcomes of annual eGFR slope and incident CKD. Higher long-term systolic BP (SBP) exposure was associated with faster kidney function decline; each 1-SD increase in cumulative SBP correlated with a more negative annual eGFR slope of approximately −0.15 to −0.18 mL/min/1.73 m² per year. Cumulative SBP was independently associated with incident CKD (hazard ratio 1.45, 95% CI: 1.15–1.83), while diastolic BP and BP variability showed weaker and less consistent associations. No evidence of non-linearity or significant effect modification by age, sex, or baseline kidney function was observed, suggesting a consistent dose-response relationship across the SBP spectrum. Limitations include potential measurement error in eGFR calculations, inability to fully account for antihypertensive treatment changes between visits, and the Swiss general population setting which may not generalize to hypertension-enriched clinical cohorts.

Implications Medical Affairs : These findings provide compelling epidemiological evidence that cumulative systolic BP burden is a meaningful predictor of kidney function decline beyond single-visit measurements, reinforcing the importance of sustained, durable BP control for renoprotection and supporting integration of longitudinal BP metrics into clinical decision-making algorithms. For Medical Affairs, this study strengthens the scientific rationale for aggressive and sustained antihypertensive treatment strategies in patients at risk for CKD, offering key evidence to support positioning of therapies with consistent long-term BP-lowering profiles as renoprotective agents in this high-risk population. The data can inform KOL engagement around renal endpoint messaging in hypertension, advocating for longitudinal BP monitoring programs and patient registries as real-world outcomes research tools to demonstrate renoprotective benefit.

The Effect Modification of Systolic Blood Pressure on the Association of Frailty and Cardiovascular Disease in Community-Dwelling Adults.

Revue : Journal of Clinical Hypertension | Tier : Tier 3 | IF estime : 3–10

Date de publication : 2026-06

Resultats principaux : Using data from the China Health and Retirement Longitudinal Study (CHARLS), this analysis included 7,210 community-dwelling adults aged ≥45 years followed over 53,531 person-years, with frailty assessed via a constructed frailty index and 1,663 CVD events recorded across SBP strata (<120, 120–129, ≥130 mmHg). Each SD increase in the frailty index was associated with 28% higher CVD risk in the SBP <120 mmHg group, 54% higher in the SBP 120–129 mmHg group, and 25% higher in the SBP ≥130 mmHg group. Joint analysis revealed that frail individuals with SBP 120–129 mmHg had the highest CVD risk (HR 3.40, 95% CI: 2.54–4.57), identifying the high-normal SBP range as a critical threshold for cardiovascular intervention in frail patients. Sensitivity analyses confirmed a robust interaction between SBP and frailty in relation to CVD risk, with results consistent across multiple analytical approaches. Limitations include the observational longitudinal design, potential misclassification of frailty using a constructed index, and residual confounding from unmeasured socioeconomic and clinical variables in the elderly Chinese population.

Implications Medical Affairs : This study challenges the universal BP target approach by demonstrating that frailty profoundly modifies CVD risk across SBP strata — with frail patients at high-normal SBP carrying the greatest risk — with direct implications for more nuanced, individualized BP target recommendations in hypertension guidelines for older adults. From a Medical Affairs perspective, these findings support the development of geriatric-specific hypertension management frameworks and provide scientific rationale for engaging KOLs in geriatric cardiology to advocate for routine frailty assessment as part of cardiovascular risk stratification in older patients. The data can be leveraged in positioning discussions around well-tolerated antihypertensive agents in frail elderly patients, where the benefit-risk balance is particularly complex and where clinical differentiation based on safety and tolerability profiles is most impactful.

Economic vulnerability or social inequality? A global comparative analysis of their relative impact on chronic kidney disease burden.

Revue : Frontiers in Public Health | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-05-15

Resultats principaux : This 68-country panel study (1990–2023) modeled prevalent CKD cases as a function of social inequality (Palma ratio) and economic vulnerability (unemployment rate), adjusting for diabetes, hypertension prevalence, hospital bed density, and population size. In high-income countries (18 countries, 265 observations at the 10-year lag), a 1-SD increase in the Palma ratio was associated with a 7.54-fold higher CKD case burden (+654%), while unemployment showed no significant association (beta=0.01, p=0.871), yielding a 202:1 absolute effect-size ratio. Bootstrap causal mediation analysis revealed that hypertension mediated 78.1% of the total effect of social inequality on CKD burden (indirect effect=0.82, 95% CI: 0.34–1.28), establishing hypertension control as the critical mediating pathway between structural inequality and kidney disease outcomes. Panel Granger tests suggested that past inequality predicts CKD burden across multiple tested lags (p<0.001), supporting temporal precedence of inequality as a structural determinant. Limitations include the ecological design preventing individual-level causal inference, heterogeneity in CKD and hypertension diagnostic standards across countries, and potential unmeasured country-level confounders.

Implications Medical Affairs : The demonstration that hypertension mediates approximately 78% of the impact of social inequality on CKD burden globally positions antihypertensive treatment at the intersection of chronic disease prevention and health equity, with major implications for public health policies addressing social determinants of cardiorenal disease. For Medical Affairs, this global analysis underscores the importance of hypertension management as a lever for reducing downstream kidney disease burden in high-income countries with widening inequality, strengthening the scientific and societal case for widespread antihypertensive access and patient adherence programs. KOL engagement with health economists and global health researchers can position hypertension therapeutics within health equity frameworks, potentially influencing reimbursement policy and national formulary access decisions.

Antihypertensive drugs and arteriovenous fistula flow rates among patients on maintenance hemodialysis in Egypt.

Revue : Renal Failure | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-05-31

Resultats principaux : This cross-sectional study from Egypt enrolled 79 maintenance hemodialysis patients, comparing AVF Doppler flow parameters between those on antihypertensive therapy (n=58; CCB n=26, beta-blocker n=23) and those without antihypertensive treatment (n=21), with multivariable regression adjusting for age, sex, dialysis vintage, diabetes, calcium level, and cardiac disease. Median AVF flow rate was comparable between CCB users and beta-blocker users (971 [803–1739] vs. 1227 [937–1519] mL/min, p=0.348), and CCB therapy was not independently associated with AVF flow rate in adjusted analyses (beta=−8.41 mL/min; 95% CI: −432.07, 415.25; p=0.968) or other Doppler vascular parameters. Older age was inversely and significantly associated with AVF flow (beta=−14.2, p=0.013), while diabetes showed a non-significant trend toward higher flow (beta=306, p=0.17). CCB use appeared potentially protective against prior AVF failure — a clinically relevant vascular access endpoint in dialysis patients — though this finding did not reach statistical significance, likely due to limited statistical power in this small cohort. Major limitations include the small sample size (n=79), single-center cross-sectional Egyptian design, absence of longitudinal follow-up for vascular access outcomes, and limited generalizability to diverse dialysis populations.

Implications Medical Affairs : While this study does not demonstrate a statistically significant impact of CCBs on AVF flow rates in hemodialysis patients, the potential protective signal against AVF failure warrants confirmation in larger prospective trials, which could meaningfully expand the evidence base for CCB use in the dialysis-dependent hypertensive population — a group with very high cardiovascular and vascular access morbidity. From a Medical Affairs perspective, this study contributes to the safety evidence for CCB use in end-stage renal disease patients typically excluded from pivotal hypertension trials, and the lack of adverse hemodynamic effects on vascular access supports continued CCB use in dialysis-based hypertension management. Engaging nephrologist KOLs around vascular access preservation as a secondary cardiovascular outcome measure in dialysis patients could provide a differentiated educational narrative for CCB positioning in the nephrology and dialysis care setting.

Revue : BMJ Open | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-01

Resultats principaux : This large cross-sectional epidemiological study enrolled 50,778 community-dwelling adults aged ≥60 years across three sites in Xinjiang, China — Kashgar (1,289 m), Hami (738 m), and Turpan (35 m) — using multistage stratified sampling to investigate altitude-hypertension associations in a geographically diverse elderly population. Hypertension prevalence rates were 40.5% at Kashgar, 49.5% at Hami, and 54.5% at Turpan (p<0.001), revealing an inverse association between residential altitude and hypertension prevalence within the mid-to-low altitude range studied.
