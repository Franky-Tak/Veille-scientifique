Blood pressure management in reperfused ischemic stroke.

Revue : The Cochrane Database of Systematic Reviews | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-14

Resultats principaux : This Cochrane systematic review included 9 randomized controlled trials (RCTs) enrolling 4,381 participants, primarily from upper-middle and high-income countries, comparing intensive systolic blood pressure (SBP) management (target < 160 mmHg) versus conventional management (target < 180 mmHg) in adults with acute ischemic stroke treated by systemic thrombolysis or endovascular thrombectomy. The primary outcome of clinical function (dichotomous modified Rankin Scale) showed that intensive SBP management resulted in little to no clinically meaningful difference (RR 0.89, 95% CI 0.80-0.98; I2 = 51%; high-certainty evidence). Regarding neurologic adverse events, intensive management did not increase or reduce the risk of any intracranial hemorrhage (RR 0.99, 95% CI 0.87-1.14; high-certainty evidence) or symptomatic ICH (RR 1.03, 95% CI 0.77-1.36; low-certainty evidence). Critically, intensive SBP management probably increases all-cause mortality (RR 1.19, 95% CI 1.08-1.32; I2 = 0%; moderate-certainty evidence) and probably reduces the proportion of participants achieving favorable neurologic status (RR 0.71, 95% CI 0.51-1.01; moderate-certainty evidence). The review is limited by substantial heterogeneity in target BP thresholds across studies, predominance of data from high-income countries, small subgroup samples, and very low certainty of evidence for symptomatic ICH and other adverse events.

Implications Medical Affairs : This high-certainty evidence that intensive SBP lowering does not improve — and likely worsens — mortality in reperfused stroke patients challenges current practice patterns and may prompt guideline committees (ESO, AHA/ASA) to reinforce or tighten existing conventional targets (< 180 mmHg), directly impacting the acute-stroke indication rationale for aggressive antihypertensive agents. For pharmaceutical companies developing or positioning IV antihypertensives in acute stroke settings, these findings narrow the therapeutic window and reduce the attractiveness of intensive BP-lowering strategies as a differentiated indication claim, making safety profiling and individualization the preferred commercial narrative. Medical Affairs should proactively engage stroke neurology and neuro-intensive care KOLs and guideline working groups to ensure product positioning aligns with emerging post-reperfusion blood pressure protocols, emphasizing titration flexibility over aggressive targets.

Nationwide Population-Based Cohort Study of Advanced Maternal Age and Obstetric Outcomes in Korean Women: Retrospective Cohort Study.

Revue : Journal of Korean Medical Science | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-13

Resultats principaux : This nationwide retrospective cohort study analyzed Korean National Health Insurance claims data from 2015 to 2021, including 1,862,393 women with singleton pregnancies, of whom 33.67% were aged >= 35 years and 5.07% were aged >= 40 years. Receiver operating characteristic (ROC) curve analysis defined maternal cut-off ages for major adverse obstetric outcomes: 35 years for pregnancy-induced hypertension (PIH), 33 years for gestational diabetes mellitus (GDM) and placenta previa, 32 years for placental abruption, and 34 years for preterm delivery and low birth weight. In multivariate logistic regression, advanced maternal age (>= 35 years) was independently associated with a 29% increased odds of PIH (OR 1.29, 95% CI 1.27-1.32) compared to younger mothers, after adjustment for multiple confounders. The composite adverse outcome model identified 33 years as the overall cut-off age (OR 1.57, 95% CI 1.56-1.59; AUC 0.573), with obstetric risks beginning to increase measurably in the early 30s across all outcomes. The study is limited by its retrospective design relying on administrative ICD-10 claims data, which may underestimate mild hypertensive disorders and cannot account for unmeasured confounders such as parity, pre-existing conditions, or assisted reproductive technology use.

Implications Medical Affairs : These population-specific data from nearly 1.9 million Korean pregnancies underscore the increasing burden of gestational hypertension in East Asian populations experiencing delayed childbearing trends, providing a robust epidemiological foundation for preventive maternal cardiovascular strategies. From a Medical Affairs perspective, this study supports positioning antihypertensive agents and preventive cardiology interventions within high-risk obstetric pathways — particularly in markets with aging maternal demographics across East and Southeast Asia — opening dialogue with obstetric and perinatal KOLs. Engaging maternal-fetal medicine specialists and hypertension-in-pregnancy guideline authors in South Korea, Japan, and China to discuss the integration of blood pressure monitoring protocols for women aged 32-35 years could provide an actionable differentiated positioning strategy in the pregnancy hypertension segment.

Fractional Flow Reserve-Guided Renal Artery Stenting in Renovascular Hypertension: 1-Year Results of the FAIR Study.

Revue : JACC: Cardiovascular Interventions | Tier : 2 | IF estime : ~15

Date de publication : 2026-07-13

Resultats principaux : The FAIR pilot study was an investigator-initiated, multicenter, open-label randomized controlled trial that enrolled 107 adults with atherosclerotic renal artery stenosis (ARAS), randomized 1:1 to FFR-guided versus angiography-guided renal artery stenting, with blinded endpoint adjudication. In the FFR-guided group, stenting was performed only when FFR < 0.80, resulting in a stenting rate of 46% versus 100% in the angiography-guided group (P < 0.01), demonstrating that functional guidance substantially reduces unnecessary procedures. At 1-year follow-up, achieved in 93-96% of participants per group, daytime mean systolic blood pressure (DMSBP), composite index of antihypertensive medicines (CIAHM), and renal outcomes were similar between both groups. Patients with FFR < 0.80 who underwent stenting experienced the most substantial blood pressure reductions (median Delta-DMSBP: 13 [IQR 4-21] mmHg at 3 months, 7 [0-16] mmHg at 12 months; all paired P < 0.05) and sustained declines in antihypertensive medication burden (Delta-CIAHM: -2 units sustained at 3, 6, and 12 months; all P < 0.05), with significant eGFR improvement uniquely observed in the FFR-positive subgroup. The study is limited by its pilot sample size (n = 107), open-label design, and 1-year follow-up insufficient to capture long-term renal protective effects or vascular remodeling differences.

Implications Medical Affairs : The FAIR study provides key evidence that FFR-guided renal artery stenting achieves equivalent blood pressure control and renal safety as universal stenting while halving procedure rates, potentially reshaping interventional guideline recommendations for renovascular hypertension and supporting a precision-medicine framework in this patient population. From a Medical Affairs standpoint, this trial reinforces the critical role of antihypertensive pharmacotherapy in the FFR >= 0.80 population (54% of patients in the FFR-guided arm who did not receive a stent), positioning optimized medical management — including RAASi combinations — as the standard of care for functionally non-significant ARAS. Collaboration with interventional cardiologists and nephrologists as KOLs to disseminate FFR-guided selection criteria could sharpen prescribing targeting and support segmentation of resistant renovascular hypertension populations most likely to benefit from intensified pharmacotherapy versus device intervention.

Association of C-Reactive Protein-Triglyceride Glucose Index and TyG Index With Prehypertension and Hypertension.

Revue : Immunity, Inflammation and Disease | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07

Resultats principaux : This cross-sectional study analyzed 8,389 Chinese participants aged >= 45 years from the China Health and Retirement Longitudinal Study, categorizing them into normotension, prehypertension, and hypertension groups to examine associations with the novel C-reactive protein-triglyceride glucose index (CTI) and the established triglyceride-glucose (TyG) index. In multivariate logistic regression after full covariate adjustment, individuals in the highest quartile (Q4) of the TyG index had a 59.2% higher odds of hypertension versus the lowest quartile (OR 1.592, 95% CI 1.354-1.872), and similarly for CTI (OR 1.588, 95% CI 1.356-1.860), both demonstrating independent associations with hypertension and prehypertension. The CTI demonstrated earlier detection ability, maintaining a statistically significant association as early as Q2 (OR 1.201, 95% CI 1.034-1.395), suggesting enhanced sensitivity to lower-threshold cardiometabolic risk. Restricted cubic spline (RCS) analysis revealed a more pronounced non-linear dose-response relationship for CTI (P-nonlinear < 0.001) compared to TyG (P-nonlinear = 0.045), indicating that the integrated inflammation-insulin resistance index may capture risk transitions at lower exposure levels. Both indices showed modest standalone discriminatory power (AUC approximately 0.59), and the cross-sectional design precludes causal inference between cardiometabolic inflammation, insulin resistance, and hypertension development.

Implications Medical Affairs : The CTI's capacity to detect hypertension risk at lower cardiometabolic thresholds than TyG suggests its utility as an early screening biomarker for risk stratification in middle-aged and elderly populations, potentially supporting the development of more sensitive targeted prevention programs in high-burden settings like China. For Medical Affairs, these findings underscore the growing recognition of insulin resistance and systemic inflammation as co-occurring, modifiable drivers of hypertension, expanding the scientific rationale for investigating anti-inflammatory or combined metabolic-antihypertensive therapy strategies. Engaging endocrinology and internal medicine KOLs in China to explore CTI-guided patient identification pathways could provide a differentiated positioning opportunity in the hypertension prevention and risk stratification landscape.

Occupational Noise Exposure Assessed by High-Frequency Hearing Loss and its Association with Hypertension among Air Force Ground Staff.

Revue : Noise & Health | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-30

Resultats principaux : This cross-sectional study enrolled 451 active-duty Air Force ground staff in Taiwan, using 4 kHz pure-tone audiometry as an objective proxy for cumulative occupational noise exposure, categorizing participants into low (< 7.5 dB), medium (7.5-12.5 dB), and high (>= 12.5 dB) hearing loss groups, with hypertension defined as SBP >= 140 mmHg, DBP >= 90 mmHg, or prior diagnosis. The prevalence of hypertension increased progressively across hearing loss categories: 40.9%, 63.5%, and 82.1% in the low, medium, and high groups respectively, demonstrating a striking dose-response gradient. Compared to the low-exposure group, participants in the medium group had significantly elevated adjusted odds of hypertension (aOR 1.90, 95% CI 1.08-3.34), and participants in the high group had nearly five-fold higher odds (aOR 4.81, 95% CI 2.51-9.02), after adjusting for age, BMI, smoking, and physical activity. Each 5 dB increase in the 4 kHz hearing threshold was independently associated with approximately doubling the odds of hypertension (aOR 2.15, 95% CI 1.57-2.94) and with significant increases in both SBP and DBP (adjusted beta: 0.63 and 0.46 mmHg per 5 dB increase, respectively). The study is limited by its cross-sectional design, restriction to a predominantly male military population, potential residual confounding from non-occupational noise sources, and use of 4 kHz audiometry as an indirect rather than direct exposure measure.

Implications Medical Affairs : These findings contribute compelling dose-response evidence linking chronic occupational noise exposure to hypertension risk in military populations, supporting advocacy for occupational health policies that integrate blood pressure screening within military noise surveillance programs — particularly given hypertension prevalence exceeding 80% at the highest exposure levels. For Medical Affairs teams, this data is relevant for positioning antihypertensive therapies within occupational medicine and military health settings, as these underaddressed, high-risk populations may benefit from systematic hypertension detection and treatment protocols linked to audiological surveillance programs. Future research priorities include longitudinal designs quantifying whether occupational noise reduction, hearing protection, or early antihypertensive intervention in noise-exposed workers provide measurable blood pressure benefits — a data generation opportunity that could strengthen health economics dossiers for prevention-oriented pharmaceutical programs.

Emerging Strategies for Cardio-Renal Protection Beyond RAAS Blockade.

Revue : Saudi Medical Journal | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-07

Resultats principaux : This narrative review synthesizes contemporary evidence for cardio-renal protective strategies beyond optimized RAAS blockade in non-diabetic chronic kidney disease (ND-CKD) complicated by resistant hypertension, a high-risk clinical phenotype with unmet need even under maximal renin-angiotensin-aldosterone system inhibition. The review details hemodynamic and tubular benefits of SGLT2 inhibitors that translate into renal and heart failure risk reduction in multiple trial populations with ND-CKD, including data from DAPA-CKD, EMPA-KIDNEY, and related trials. Non-steroidal mineralocorticoid receptor antagonism (MRA) with finerenone is highlighted for its anti-inflammatory and anti-fibrotic signaling mechanisms, with evidence of incremental benefit over RAASi when serum potassium is proactively managed using modern potassium binders (patiromer, sodium zirconium cyclosilicate). GLP-1 receptor agonists are discussed as offering cardiometabolic advantages with emerging kidney-protective signals, particularly in patients with obesity and elevated cardiovascular risk, while renal denervation is proposed as a device-based option in carefully selected resistant hypertension cases. The review proposes a stepwise implementation framework integrating these agents with structured multidisciplinary monitoring, but is limited by its narrative (non-systematic) methodology and lack of formal risk-of-bias assessment or meta-analytic pooling.

Implications Medical Affairs : This review reflects the emerging paradigm shift toward combination cardiometabolic-renal protective regimens in resistant hypertension and ND-CKD, positioning finerenone and SGLT2 inhibitors as complementary agents to standard RAASi — a framing that Medical Affairs teams can leverage when communicating treatment intensification strategies to nephrologists and cardiologists managing complex hypertensive patients. For product positioning, companies developing or marketing non-steroidal MRAs or SGLT2 inhibitors in the resistant hypertension space should adopt the stepwise framework proposed in this review as an educational platform for prescriber engagement, supporting combination therapy sequencing conversations that optimize both BP control and organ protection. The inclusion of renal denervation in the resistant hypertension algorithm alongside pharmacotherapy highlights the growing multidisciplinary treatment landscape and underscores the need for Medical Affairs KOL engagement across cardiology, nephrology, and interventional medicine.

The kidney distal tubule potassium switch, modern diet and hypertension.

Revue : Nature Reviews Nephrology | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-14

Resultats principaux : This authoritative review, produced by leading kidney physiologists from Johns Hopkins, Vanderbilt, Oregon Health & Science University, the University of Lausanne, and the University of Queensland, examines the potassium switch signaling mechanism in the kidney distal convoluted tubule and its central role in diet-induced hypertension. The potassium switch comprises a Kir4.1/Kir5.1 potassium-sensing channel complex coupled to a WNK kinase-induced phosphorylation cascade that targets the thiazide-sensitive sodium chloride co-transporter (NCC), collectively regulating sodium-potassium balance across wide variations in dietary potassium intake. The review presents convergent experimental, genetic, and physiological evidence that low dietary potassium — pervasive in modern high-sodium Western diets — activates the potassium switch to conserve potassium at the expense of increased renal sodium reabsorption, thereby exacerbating salt-sensitive hypertension and associated cardiovascular complications. Critically, the authors argue that low potassium consumption may be as important as high sodium intake in driving the global hypertension burden (affecting approximately 1.28 billion adults), suggesting that public health strategies focused only on sodium reduction are incomplete. The review is inherently limited by its narrative format and substantial reliance on experimental and preclinical data, with limited large-scale human interventional trial data to validate the clinical magnitude of the potassium switch effect across diverse populations.

Implications Medical Affairs : This high-profile review in Nature Reviews Nephrology provides a compelling mechanistic framework — the WNK-NCC potassium switch — that elevates the scientific currency of dietary potassium and thiazide-like diuretics in hypertension management, offering Medical Affairs teams a powerful educational narrative about the intersection of modern dietary patterns and blood pressure physiology. For product positioning, the mechanistic alignment between the potassium switch and thiazide diuretic pharmacology (NCC inhibition) provides a renewed scientific rationale for communicating the complementary action of thiazide/thiazide-like agents alongside dietary potassium optimization, strengthening guideline-consistent messaging to primary care and hypertension specialist KOLs. Engagement with leading nephrologists and cardiovascular physiologists around the WNK kinase inhibitor research agenda — an emerging therapeutic class — could generate Medical Affairs-supported investigator-initiated studies examining whether potassium supplementation augments thiazide effectiveness in defined salt-sensitive hypertensive subgroups, positioning the company at the frontier of precision hypertension research.

Prediction model for aortic dissection, aortic aneurysm, and peripheral artery disease.

Revue : Hypertension Research | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-14

Resultats principaux : This large-scale prospective cohort study leveraged the DeSC Japanese health database encompassing 1,082,369 participants aged 20-74 years without prior cardiovascular disease, using flexible parametric survival models to derive 5-year risk prediction equations for aortic dissection (AD), aortic aneurysm (AA), and peripheral artery disease (PAD). During follow-up, 756 AD, 2,230 AA, and 4,131 PAD events occurred across derivation and validation cohorts (50/50 random split), providing substantial statistical power. The models incorporated routine health check-up data including age, sex, BMI, blood pressure, lipid profile, glucose, smoking status, physical activity, and medication use, achieving Harrell's C-index of 0.781 (95% CI 0.761-0.801) for AD, 0.812 (0.799-0.825) for AA, and 0.701 (0.690-0.711) for PAD in internal validation — representing good to excellent discrimination. Royston D statistics of 1.695 for AD, 1.975 for AA, and 1.222 for PAD, combined with good calibration, confirm model validity for clinical risk stratification. The study is limited by its restriction to the Japanese population which may limit cross-ethnic generalizability, reliance on internal validation only without an independent external cohort, and absence of imaging data to detect subclinical vascular disease at baseline.

Implications Medical Affairs : These validated risk prediction models incorporating blood pressure as a key predictor underscore hypertension's pivotal role in the pathogenesis of major vascular events beyond coronary disease — aortic dissection, aneurysm, and PAD — supporting the extension of antihypertensive treatment rationale to a broader spectrum of cardiovascular-vascular end-organ outcomes relevant for value dossiers. From a Medical Affairs perspective, such models could be used as pharmacoeconomic tools to demonstrate the downstream cost-benefit of blood pressure control in reducing the burden of catastrophic vascular events, strengthening reimbursement arguments and guideline advocacy for earlier antihypertensive intervention in intermediate-risk patients. Partnering with Japanese and Asia-Pacific cardiovascular epidemiology KOLs to externally validate and adapt these models for other ethnic populations could generate high-quality supplemental real-world evidence supporting antihypertensive drug market access strategies in the Asia-Pacific region.

Revue : Scientific Reports | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-14

Resultats principaux : This cross-sectional study enrolled 860 hypertensive patients from three rural counties in Shanxi Province, China, using multi-stage cluster random sampling, with subjective cognitive decline (SCD) assessed via the SCD-Q9 questionnaire (threshold score >= 5) within a Health Ecological Model (HEM) framework spanning individual, behavioral, working/living, social, and policy layers. The prevalence of SCD in this rural hypertensive population was strikingly high at 80.58%, signaling a substantial cognitive burden associated with uncontrolled or poorly managed hypertension in a resource-limited rural setting. In the Individual Characteristics layer, advanced age (OR 1.042 per year, 95% CI 1.010-1.074), family history of hypertension (OR 2.352, 95% CI 1.550-3.570), hypertensive complications (OR 3.049, 95% CI 1.680-5.534), and moderate hypertension risk level (OR 1.847, 95% CI 1.149-2.968) were independently associated with higher odds of SCD. The study is limited by its cross-sectional design precluding causal conclusions, reliance on self-reported SCD rather than objective neuropsychological testing, and limited generalizability beyond rural Chinese populations.

Implications Medical Affairs : The extraordinarily high prevalence of SCD (80.58%) in this rural hypertensive cohort identifies an urgent, underaddressed public health dimension — uncontrolled hypertension and associated lifestyle factors appear to drive a substantial cognitive burden in aging low-resource populations, suggesting that antihypertensive treatment may yield substantial benefits beyond traditional cardiovascular endpoints. For Medical Affairs, these findings reinforce the developing evidence base linking blood pressure control to cognitive preservation, providing context for positioning antihypertensives within a broader brain health framework and expanding KOL engagement to neurologists and geriatricians alongside cardiologists and primary care physicians. Longitudinal studies examining whether intensified antihypertensive management reduces the incidence or progression of SCD in comparable rural populations — potentially as an exploratory endpoint in existing trials or real-world evidence programs — represent a high-impact research agenda for Medical Affairs-supported investigator studies.

Multimetallic exposure and risk of hypertension: A multi-model analysis based on the China Southwest prospective cohort.

Revue : Ecotoxicology and Environmental Safety | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-14

Resultats principaux : This prospective cohort study analyzed data from 3,749 participants enrolled in the Guizhou Population Health Cohort Study with a median follow-up of 6.63 years, during which 806 incident hypertension cases were identified, enabling longitudinal characterization of environmental metal mixture effects on blood pressure outcomes. Multiple advanced statistical methods were applied — Cox proportional hazards modeling, factor analysis, weighted quantile sum (WQS) regression, quantile g-computation (qgcomp), and Bayesian kernel machine regression (BKMR) — to disentangle the independent and combined effects of ten blood metals (Fe, Zn, Mg, K, As, Mn, Na, Sr, Se, and others). Factor analysis identified three exposure patterns: Factor 1 (eight essential metals) was inversely associated with hypertension (HR 0.920, 95% CI 0.855-0.990), Factor 2 (Mn alone) was positively associated (HR 1.205, 95% CI 1.120-1.297), and Factor 3 (Sr-As) showed no significant effect. WQS regression demonstrated that each 1-percentile increase in the Factor 1 mixture index corresponded to an 11.8% hypertension risk reduction (RR 0.882, 95% CI 0.788-0.988), with potassium (K) as the primary protective contributor, while BKMR identified manganese (Mn) as the dominant risk-driving element (posterior inclusion probability PIP = 0.807) with an overall low-dose beneficial, high-dose harmful pattern. The study is limited by potential measurement error in blood metal concentrations, difficulty disentangling dietary from environmental metal sources, reliance on a single geographic cohort from Southwest China, and incomplete characterization of exposure timing.

Implications Medical Affairs : These findings highlight complex, non-linear relationships between dietary and environmental metal mixtures and hypertension incidence — with manganese consistently identified as the dominant modifiable risk driver and potassium as the primary protective element — providing novel environmental context to complement traditional cardiovascular risk stratification frameworks. For Medical Affairs, this multi-exposure framework aligns with growing global interest in environmental determinants of hypertension and supports the scientific argument for personalized risk stratification incorporating environmental exposures alongside traditional risk factors, broadening the narrative for hypertension prevention in exposed populations. Collaboration with environmental health, occupational medicine, and nephrology KOLs in China could help identify industrially exposed populations at metal-related hypertension risk where pharmacological intervention studies and environmental remediation programs could be co-designed.

Impact of Physical Activity on Stroke Risk in Middle-Aged and Older Adults.

Revue : Brain and Behavior | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07

Resultats principaux : This prospective cohort study analyzed 4,380 stroke-free Chinese adults aged >= 45 years from the China Health and Retirement Longitudinal Study (CHARLS) over a 7-year follow-up period, during which 300 participants (6.8%) experienced a first-ever stroke diagnosed by a physician. Physical activity was classified into sedentary behavior, active, and highly active categories using the International Physical Activity Questionnaire Short Form, and total activity was quantified in MET-min/week. Highly active physical activity was independently associated with a 48% reduction in first stroke risk compared to sedentary behavior (adjusted HR 0.52, 95% CI 0.30-0.92; adjusted OR 0.45, 95% CI 0.36-0.55), with a non-linear flipped J-shaped dose-response relationship observed — minimum risk occurring at approximately 13,045 MET-min/week. Subgroup analyses demonstrated stronger protective effects of high physical activity in men, individuals without pre-existing heart disease, overweight individuals, and those with hypertension. Mediation analyses indicated that the protective association was substantially mediated through improvements in blood pressure, HDL cholesterol, BMI, and the triglyceride-glucose index, with blood pressure reduction emerging as a principal mediating pathway — though the study is limited by its reliance on self-reported stroke diagnosis and physical activity assessment, precluding objective exposure measurement.

Implications Medical Affairs : The demonstration that blood pressure reduction is a principal mediator of the physical-activity-to-stroke-risk pathway provides mediation-level evidence that exercise functions as a core non-pharmacological antihypertensive strategy, reinforcing its inclusion in clinical hypertension management guidelines alongside pharmacotherapy and strengthening the integrated prevention narrative for Medical Affairs teams. This evidence is particularly actionable for positioning antihypertensive pharmacotherapy as part of a comprehensive cardiovascular risk reduction package in physically active but insufficiently controlled hypertensive patients, supporting combination messaging for lifestyle and drug intervention programs. KOL engagement should target stroke neurologists, preventive cardiologists, and sports medicine specialists in China — where stroke burden is disproportionately high — to communicate additive benefits of structured exercise counseling alongside antihypertensive treatment in high-risk middle-aged populations.

Revue : Frontiers in Public Health | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Resultats principaux : This cross-sectional study analyzed 2,717 records from the 2021 China General Social Survey (CGSS 2021), examining whether subjective noise pollution perception (low vs. high) was associated with the prevalence of cardiovascular diseases — including hypertension, dyslipidemia, heart disease, and stroke — in a nationally representative sample of Chinese adults. Implications Medical Affairs : While this study does not demonstrate a statistically significant independent association between subjective noise perception and hypertension after adjustment, the heart disease finding adds to a growing body of evidence linking psychosocial environmental stress to cardiac pathology, providing contextual evidence relevant to Medical Affairs teams communicating environmental cardiovascular risk to public health and occupational medicine stakeholders. Impact of Obesity on the Association Between Salt Intake and Blood Pressure in Adult Men With Hypertension Who Participated in the Remote Lifestyle Intervention.

Revue : Journal of Obesity | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026

Resultats principaux : This observational study analyzed data from 1,095 adult men with hypertension (median age 57 years; 67.2% with obesity defined as BMI >= 25 kg/m2) participating in a remote lifestyle modification program in Japan, measuring daily salt intake via validated urinary sodium excretion self-monitoring devices (expressed as g NaCl/day) and tracking home systolic blood pressure (SBP). The study is limited by its restriction to Japanese men, precluding generalizability to women and other ethnicities, the 3-month follow-up duration, and potential measurement variability in home urinary sodium devices.

Implications Medical Affairs : This real-world confirmation of obesity-amplified salt sensitivity provides actionable evidence for stratifying dietary sodium reduction counseling in hypertensive patients by obesity status, directly informing personalized lifestyle modification guidelines and supporting Medical Affairs communications around individualized blood pressure management approaches. For product positioning, these findings strengthen the pharmacological case for combination therapies that concurrently reduce weight and blood pressure in obese hypertensive patients — including SGLT2 inhibitors and GLP-1 receptor agonists — whose mechanisms of action align with simultaneously addressing salt sensitivity and adiposity.
