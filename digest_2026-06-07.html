Frailty and sex differences in blood pressure control in older adults.

Revue : Scientific Reports | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-07

Resultats principaux : This cross-sectional study conducted at two major hospitals in Vietnam (2023-2024) enrolled 1,038 older adults with hypertension (mean age 73 years, 326 females). Frailty was defined by a Clinical Frailty Scale score of 4 or higher, and suboptimal blood pressure (BP) as systolic BP 140 mmHg or higher or diastolic BP 90 mmHg or higher. The overall prevalence of frailty was 28.6%, significantly higher in females (35.3%) than males (25.6%), and the overall suboptimal BP rate was 26.7%. In females, frail participants had a markedly higher suboptimal BP rate (33.9%) compared to non-frail (20.9%), while no significant difference was observed among males. After full multivariate adjustment, the odds ratio of frailty on suboptimal BP was 2.01 (95% CI 1.08-3.76) in females versus 0.92 (95% CI 0.59-1.44) in males, yielding a female-to-male ratio of odds ratios of 2.18 (95% CI 1.01-4.70). Key limitations include the cross-sectional design precluding causal inference and the single-country population limiting generalizability.

Implications Medical Affairs : These findings establish a compelling sex-specific frailty-hypertension interaction that should inform upcoming geriatric hypertension guidelines, calling for differentiated BP targets based on frailty status and sex. From a product positioning standpoint, antihypertensive agents with favourable pharmacokinetic profiles in frail elderly populations could be differentiated by highlighting their benefit in this underserved subgroup. Engaging geriatric cardiologists and gerontologists as KOLs around frailty-aware BP management represents a high-priority opportunity for medical affairs strategy.

Hypertension, use of antihypertensive medications and breast cancer survival among Black women.

Revue : Breast Cancer Research | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-06

Resultats principaux : This prospective cohort study examined 2,474 Black Women's Health Study participants with invasive breast cancer, using Cox proportional hazards models adjusted for clinical, lifestyle, and cancer treatment factors to assess associations of hypertension and antihypertensive medication use with breast cancer-specific death. The hazard ratio for untreated hypertension vs. no hypertension was non-significant (HR 1.17, 95% CI 0.75-1.82), while treated hypertension showed a non-significant inverse association (HR 0.81, 95% CI 0.60-1.10). Critical subgroup analyses revealed that for ER-positive cases, treated hypertension was strongly protective (HR 0.53, 95% CI 0.34-0.83), whereas for ER-negative cases, untreated hypertension more than doubled the risk of cancer-specific death (HR 2.19, 95% CI 1.09-4.39). These receptor subtype-specific findings suggest that antihypertensive medications may exert anti-tumour effects in ER-positive disease. Limitations include the observational design preventing causal claims and the absence of disaggregated antihypertensive drug class data in primary analyses.

Implications Medical Affairs : The differential survival benefit of hypertension treatment by hormone receptor subtype in Black women opens important questions about pleiotropic anti-tumour effects of antihypertensive drug classes, which may inform cardio-oncology co-management guidelines. From a product positioning perspective, this data creates an opportunity to explore anti-cancer properties of specific antihypertensive agents — notably ACE inhibitors, ARBs, and beta-blockers — in ER-positive breast cancer, potentially supporting investigator-initiated research. KOL strategy should target cardio-oncologists and breast cancer specialists who can design the randomized trials needed to establish causality in this high-risk population.

The interplay of adolescent obesity and smoking on hypertension in early adulthood: insights from TLGS.

Revue : BMC Endocrine Disorders | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-06

Resultats principaux : Using data from the Tehran Lipid and Glucose Study (TLGS), 985 adolescents (mean age 15.01 years) were followed for a median of 15.05 years, examining the independent and joint effects of adolescent obesity and smoking on incident adult hypertension (BP 140/90 mmHg or antihypertensive use). Ninety-five participants developed hypertension during follow-up. After adjustment, adolescent overweight/obesity was independently associated with adulthood hypertension (HR 1.73, 95% CI 1.11-2.71), while smoking alone was not statistically significant after full adjustment (HR 1.15, 95% CI 0.63-2.09). Joint analyses showed that overweight/obese non-smokers had significantly elevated HTN risk compared to normal-weight non-smokers (HR 1.77, 95% CI 1.10-2.84), and a potential synergistic interaction between obesity and smoking was observed. The study is limited by self-reported smoking status and restriction to an Iranian non-Western population, which may limit generalizability.

Implications Medical Affairs : The 73% elevated hypertension risk conferred by adolescent overweight/obesity over 15 years provides strong evidence for weight management as the highest-priority hypertension prevention intervention beginning in childhood, with direct implications for public health guidelines. This data set supports pharmaceutical company advocacy for integrated school-based or primary care prevention programs targeting adolescent weight, potentially positioning prevention as a core corporate social responsibility pillar. Engaging paediatric cardiologists, endocrinologists, and preventive medicine KOLs around life-course cardiovascular risk reduction represents a timely medical affairs strategy.

Associations of cumulative exposure and dynamic trajectories of the combined atherogenic and frailty index with incident cardiometabolic multimorbidity: a longitudinal analysis based on CHARLS.

Revue : Cardiovascular Diabetology | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-06

Resultats principaux : This longitudinal analysis from the China Health and Retirement Longitudinal Study (CHARLS) enrolled 7,995 participants followed for a median of 9 years, with 4,483 participants included in the longitudinal trajectory substudy. The composite atherogenic-frailty index (AIPFI, calculated as AIP x FI) was the primary exposure, with incident cardiometabolic multimorbidity (CMM) as the outcome; 747 (9.3%) incident CMMs occurred. Participants in the highest AIPFI quartile had a 2.46-fold increased risk of CMM (95% CI 1.77-3.43) compared to Q1. Longitudinal trajectory analysis identified that cluster 2 (persistently high and increasing AIPFI) was associated with HR 1.54 (95% CI 1.19-2.01). Restricted cubic splines confirmed a significant positive nonlinear relationship between AIPFI and CMM risk. SHAP analysis identified hypertension, heart disease, and AIPFI as the three most influential CMM predictors. Limitations include the observational design and residual confounding from unmeasured variables.

Implications Medical Affairs : The designation of hypertension as a top SHAP-identified predictor of cardiometabolic multimorbidity underscores the multiplicative burden when atherogenic dyslipidemia and frailty co-exist with elevated blood pressure, directly supporting medical affairs communication on the compounding cardiovascular risk in incompletely treated hypertensive patients. This data argues for integrated risk assessment tools combining lipid, frailty, and BP metrics in clinical practice, which could be incorporated into product differentiation messaging for combination therapies. Engaging internists and geriatric cardiologists as KOLs around longitudinal multimorbidity risk trajectories represents a strategic priority for medical affairs.

Identifying high-risk and actionable profiles for prevention in hypertensive patients with concurrent dyslipidemia: a population-based retrospective study.

Revue : BMC Cardiovascular Disorders | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-06

Resultats principaux : This retrospective population-based study analyzed data from 5,018 hypertensive patients recruited from 110 hospitals across China, comparing those with concurrent dyslipidemia versus those without. The dyslipidemia group exhibited significantly higher proportions of stroke, angina pectoris, and atrial fibrillation (p less than 0.05), alongside greater use of beta-blockers and antiplatelet agents. Multivariate logistic regression identified BMI, family history of hypertension, stroke, angina pectoris, atrial fibrillation, obstructive sleep apnea (OSA), and family history of coronary heart disease as independent factors associated with hypertension-dyslipidemia comorbidity. The combination of genetic predisposition (family history of HTN and CAD) and metabolic excess (high BMI) appeared particularly potent. Family history of hypertension and coronary heart disease emerged as the most consistent genetic risk amplifiers in this large multicenter dataset. Limitations include the retrospective design and potential hospital-based selection bias.

Implications Medical Affairs : Defining specific actionable risk profiles for hypertension-dyslipidemia comorbidity in a large 110-hospital Chinese dataset provides real-world evidence directly applicable to precision medicine approaches and combination cardiovascular risk management protocols. From a product positioning perspective, these findings strongly reinforce the clinical rationale for fixed-dose antihypertensive-statin combination therapies targeting the high-BMI, family-history-positive patient segment. KOL engagement should focus on cardiologists and internists specializing in cardiovascular prevention who can implement three-tier prevention strategies in hypertensive-dyslipidemic populations.

Comprehensive epidemiological analyses of urinary stone diseases in eastern China: a regional population-based study.

Revue : BMC Public Health | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-06

Resultats principaux : This prospective cohort study from the CHERRY database enrolled 966,481 participants from Yinzhou, China (2009-2022) and investigated the epidemiology and risk factors for urinary stone disease (USD). Annual percentage change analysis showed a 5.3-6.8% annual increase in USD incidence (p less than 0.05) between 2014 and 2021. Cox proportional hazards regression confirmed hypertension as an independent risk factor for USD alongside smoking, alcohol use, high BMI, diabetes, and hyperlipidemia, while regular exercise was protective. Limitations include geographic restriction to a single eastern Chinese region and reliance on electronic health records with potential coding variability.

Implications Medical Affairs : The confirmation of hypertension as an independent contributor to the rapidly increasing urinary stone disease burden in China adds another dimension to the cardiorenal metabolic syndrome narrative, supporting the argument that uncontrolled hypertension drives multi-organ complications. This evidence reinforces the value of RAAS inhibitors with nephroprotective properties in hypertensive patients, linking BP management to kidney stone prevention as a secondary benefit relevant for product differentiation. KOL engagement opportunities exist at the intersection of nephrology and cardiology, particularly with Chinese opinion leaders who can translate this population-level evidence to clinical practice guidelines.

Effect of relative handgrip strength and back muscle strength on the incidence of hypertension: 18-year follow-up in a population-based cohort study.

Revue : Journal of Human Hypertension | Tier : Tier 3 | IF estime : 3-5

Date de publication : 2026-06-06

Resultats principaux : This population-based prospective study followed 3,038 non-hypertensive subjects aged 40-69 years for 18 years in Korea, examining whether body-weight-normalized handgrip strength (HGS) and back muscle strength (BMS) predict incident hypertension. Using Cox proportional hazards modeling, relative BMS below -1.5 SD (normalized to BMI and waist circumference) was significantly associated with higher hypertension incidence (HR 1.264, p=0.050 for BMI-BMS; HR 1.405, p=0.003 for WC-BMS). Weak HGS by AWGS criteria independently predicted hypertension (HR 1.679, p=0.002), and weak HGS below -1.5 SD showed consistent associations with HRs of 1.476 to 1.530 (p less than 0.001 to 0.008) across relative metrics. Stepwise dose-response trends were observed across relative strength quartiles for both muscle groups (trend p less than 0.001). Absolute (non-normalized) muscle strength showed weaker associations, underscoring the importance of weight-adjusted metrics. Limitations include the single-country (Korean) cohort and the 18-year follow-up period without intermediate assessments.

Implications Medical Affairs : This robust 18-year prospective evidence establishes body-weight-normalized muscle strength as a modifiable independent predictor of hypertension, lending scientific support for physical fitness screening as a hypertension prevention tool that may complement pharmacological approaches in upcoming guidelines. From a product positioning perspective, these data strengthen the case for combining antihypertensive pharmacotherapy with structured exercise programs, potentially differentiating agents well-tolerated during physical activity. Medical affairs should engage sports medicine specialists, rehabilitation physicians, and preventive cardiologists as KOLs to build multidisciplinary hypertension prevention platforms.

Relationship between medication class and ambulatory blood pressure profile in the chronic renal insufficiency cohort (CRIC) study.

Revue : Journal of Human Hypertension | Tier : Tier 3 | IF estime : 3-5

Date de publication : 2026-06-06

Resultats principaux : This cross-sectional analysis of 1,499 patients from the CRIC study compared associations between antihypertensive medication classes and ambulatory blood pressure monitoring (ABPM) phenotypes: controlled hypertension, white coat effect, sustained hypertension, and masked uncontrolled hypertension (MUCH). RAAS inhibitor (RASi) use was independently associated with lower odds of sustained hypertension (OR 0.60, 95% CI 0.42-0.84) and nocturnal hypertension (OR 0.71, 95% CI 0.55-0.91). Beta-blocker use was positively associated with MUCH (OR 1.48, 95% CI 1.12-1.96) and nocturnal hypertension (OR 1.33, 95% CI 1.04-1.70). Calcium channel blocker use was also associated with nocturnal hypertension (OR 1.36, 95% CI 1.07-1.73). Diuretics showed no significant associations with ABPM phenotypes after Bonferroni correction. The cross-sectional design limits causal conclusions, and these findings require longitudinal confirmation.

Implications Medical Affairs : The finding that RASi agents are associated with superior ABPM profiles — including lower nocturnal hypertension and masked uncontrolled hypertension — in a large CKD cohort provides powerful data to support RASi preferential use in nephrology guidelines and clinical protocols. From a product differentiation standpoint, this evidence reinforces organ-protective RASi positioning in CKD patients, directly addressing the clinical challenge of nocturnal and masked hypertension that standard office measurements miss. Medical affairs should prioritize engaging nephrologists and hypertension specialists in KOL activities centered on ABPM-guided treatment selection as a quality-of-care differentiator.

Hyperuricaemia as a cardiovascular risk factor.

Revue : Atencion Primaria | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-06

Resultats principaux : This retrospective observational cohort study from Vic Hospital Consortium in Barcelona (2019-2024) analyzed 159,235 uric acid measurements from 60,481 patients and 3,517 hospital discharges due to cardiovascular disease in 2,919 patients. Hyperuricemia was significantly associated with higher hypertension prevalence (82.7% vs. 61.7%) and diabetes mellitus (36.9% vs. 28.0%), but not with dyslipidemia or smoking. In logistic regression predicting cardiovascular events, uric acid levels yielded an odds ratio of 1.080 (95% CI 1.053-1.108) per unit, alongside age (OR 1.053) and female sex (protective, OR 0.488). Higher uric acid was also associated with older age in the full population. Subgroup analysis showed that in admitted cardiovascular patients, hyperuricemia was specifically linked to hypertension and diabetes as comorbidities. Limitations include the single-center retrospective design, reliance on hospital discharge codes, and absence of longitudinal follow-up for cardiovascular event recurrence.

Implications Medical Affairs : This real-world hospital database confirms the strong clustering of hyperuricemia with hypertension in patients admitted for cardiovascular events, supporting the clinical utility of uric acid measurement as a complementary cardiometabolic risk stratification tool in hypertensive patients. From a Medical Affairs perspective, this evidence supports communication strategies that highlight xanthine oxidase inhibitors or urate-lowering therapies as potential adjunctive cardiovascular risk reducers in the hypertensive-hyperuricemic patient subgroup. KOL engagement with primary care physicians and cardiologists around gout-cardiovascular risk co-management represents an actionable near-term opportunity.

Prevalence and prognostic role of untreated moderate-to-severe tricuspid regurgitation: a systematic review and meta-analysis.

Revue : European Journal of Heart Failure | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-06

Resultats principaux : This systematic review and meta-analysis searched PubMed from inception through August 2025, ultimately including 106 studies (107 comparisons) with 961,136 patients examining the prognostic role of untreated moderate-to-severe tricuspid regurgitation (TR). The overall prevalence of untreated moderate-to-severe TR was 13.5% with wide variation across subgroups. Moderate-to-severe TR was associated with significantly higher all-cause mortality vs. none-to-mild TR (unadjusted HR 2.07, 95% CI 1.89-2.26, p less than 0.001), confirmed in adjusted models and across subgroups including pulmonary arterial hypertension patients. A stepwise increase in mortality was observed with increasing TR severity. Cardiovascular mortality (HR 1.92, 95% CI 1.64-2.26) and heart failure hospitalization (HR 1.63, 95% CI 1.44-1.84) were also significantly elevated. Limitations include substantial heterogeneity across studies and potential unmeasured confounding.

Implications Medical Affairs : The confirmed two-fold excess mortality from untreated moderate-to-severe TR, including in pulmonary hypertension subgroups, strengthens disease burden communications supporting timely intervention in this population and may drive updates to structural heart disease and pulmonary hypertension management guidelines. From a Medical Affairs perspective, this meta-analysis reinforces the narrative that inadequately controlled pulmonary hypertension leads to right-sided structural complications with severe prognostic consequences, supporting positioning of effective PAH therapies as tools for preventing tricuspid valve decompensation. KOL engagement should target structural heart disease interventionists and pulmonary hypertension specialists to build coordinated right heart management programs.

Revue : Maternal and Child Health Journal | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-06

Resultats principaux : This retrospective descriptive analysis used 2007-2020 North Dakota birth records for AI/AN and White women with hypertensive disorders of pregnancy (HDP). Overall HDP prevalence increased nearly three-fold from 4.4% to 12.1% over the study period, with significant increases in both AI/AN (6.6% to 13.6%, p less than 0.001) and White women (4.1% to 11.9%, p less than 0.001). Limitations include reliance on birth certificate data without clinical severity stratification and the inability to track long-term maternal outcomes.

Implications Medical Affairs : The near tripling of HDP prevalence over 13 years and the substantial racial disparities in obstetric outcomes highlight a critical public health emergency in maternal hypertension that calls for targeted, culturally responsive antihypertensive management programs. From a Medical Affairs perspective, this evidence supports advocacy for equitable access to evidence-based HDP therapies and positions antihypertensive agents as critical tools for reducing the racial disparity in maternal morbidity, particularly in rural and indigenous populations. Trends in mortality and disability-adjusted life years (DALYs) attributable to subarachnoid hemorrhage in South Asia (2000-2023).

Revue : Neurosurgical Review | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-06

Resultats principaux : This ecological trend analysis used Global Burden of Disease 2023 database data for five South Asian countries (India, Pakistan, Bangladesh, Nepal, Bhutan) across 2000-2023. Age-standardized SAH mortality rates increased by 29.5% (from 3.2 to 4.2 per 100,000), with an average annual percent change (AAPC) of 1.2% (95% CI 1.0-1.4); absolute deaths rose from 26,951 to 65,635. The four key risk factors identified as driving SAH mortality in South Asia were hypertension, smoking, household air pollution from solid fuel, and BMI. Gender disparities narrowed but persist, with females maintaining higher age-standardized mortality rates. Limitations include ecological design and reliance on GBD modeled estimates.

Implications Medical Affairs : Hypertension's prominent role in the dramatically rising subarachnoid hemorrhage burden across the South Asian subcontinent — representing over 1.9 billion people — defines a massive unmet need for effective blood pressure control that has direct implications for market access and public health partnership strategies in this region. From a Medical Affairs perspective, this GBD data supports urgent disease awareness campaigns around hypertension as a preventable cause of stroke in South Asia, potentially justifying expanded access programs and partnerships with local health authorities. Revue : Scottish Medical Journal | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-06

Resultats principaux : This cross-sectional study analyzed NHANES 2021-2023 data from 3,179 women aged 18-80 years, examining associations between systemic inflammation (hs-CRP quartiles), sex hormones, and vitamin D. Women in the highest hs-CRP quartile had significantly higher hypertension and diabetes prevalence compared to the lowest quartile. After multivariable adjustment for adiposity and confounders, hs-CRP remained positively associated with anti-Mullerian hormone (AMH, beta=0.212, p=0.007) and estrone (beta=0.142, p=0.048), while inversely associated with FSH (beta=-0.194, p=0.039) and LH (beta=-0.191, p=0.019). Implications Medical Affairs : The co-occurrence of elevated inflammatory markers with hypertension and reproductive hormonal dysregulation in women highlights the complexity of female-specific cardiovascular risk, supporting sex-differentiated approaches to hypertension evaluation and management. From a Medical Affairs perspective, this NHANES-based evidence supports the development of women's cardiovascular health communication platforms that integrate inflammatory, hormonal, and metabolic risk factors as interconnected drivers of hypertension. CPAP therapy may improve HFpEF-like features in coronary artery disease with obstructive sleep apnea: A randomized controlled trial.

Revue : Sleep Medicine | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-04

Resultats principaux : This secondary analysis of the RICCADSA cohort included 177 revascularized coronary artery disease (CAD) patients with nonsleepy obstructive sleep apnea (AHI 15 or higher events/hour), randomized to CPAP (n=89) or no CPAP (n=88). HFpEF-like features, defined by a composite of 5 echocardiographic and biomarker criteria, were present in 71.2% of all participants at baseline. Among baseline HFpEF-positive patients, improvement at 12 months occurred in 38.7% with CPAP vs. 20.3% without (p=0.023). Higher baseline NT-proBNP was inversely associated with improvement (OR 0.37, p=0.002). Hypertension was one of the key comorbidities adjusted for in the model. Limitations include the post-hoc exploratory nature, the restriction to revascularized CAD patients, and the non-sleepy OSA phenotype limiting generalizability.

Implications Medical Affairs : The demonstration that CPAP reverses HFpEF-like features in CAD-OSA patients — where hypertension is a prevalent comorbidity — has significant implications for hypertension guidelines recommending integrated cardiovascular management that addresses sleep-disordered breathing as a contributor to cardiac dysfunction. From a Medical Affairs perspective, these data reinforce the importance of OSA screening in hypertensive patients and support positioning comprehensive cardiovascular management pathways (including CPAP) as complementary to antihypertensive pharmacotherapy. Engaging cardiologists, pulmonologists, and sleep medicine specialists as KOLs around the OSA-hypertension-HFpEF continuum represents an actionable interdisciplinary medical affairs strategy.

Valsartan alleviates acetaminophen-induced hepatotoxicity by activating fibroblast growth factor 21 in acute liver injury in mice.

Revue : Biochemical Pharmacology | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-04

Resultats principaux : This preclinical study using a murine model of acetaminophen (APAP)-induced acute liver injury investigated the hepatoprotective mechanism of valsartan (Val), an ARB with established antihypertensive efficacy. Val treatment significantly reduced hepatic necrosis and oxidative stress and improved survival in APAP-challenged mice. The hepatoprotective effects were mediated through fibroblast growth factor 21 (FGF21) activation via the PGC-1alpha/Nrf2 antioxidant pathway, a mechanism distinct from its AT1R-mediated antihypertensive action, as Val maintained efficacy after AT1R silencing. FGF21 knockdown markedly attenuated Val's liver-protective effects, partially restored by FGF21 reconstitution. Pharmacological inhibition of the PPARalpha pathway suppressed FGF21 activation by Val, confirming the PPARalpha/FGF21/PGC-1alpha/Nrf2 axis as the hepatoprotective mechanism. Limitations include the exclusively preclinical nature with uncertain clinical translation and reliance on knockdown rather than knockout models.

Implications Medical Affairs : The discovery of an AT1R-independent hepatoprotective mechanism for valsartan via FGF21/PGC-1alpha/Nrf2 signalling reveals a potentially novel indication for ARBs beyond hypertension and heart failure, opening avenues for drug repositioning strategies in acute and chronic liver disease. From a Medical Affairs perspective, this preclinical finding supports the initiation of investigator-sponsored research exploring the hepatoprotective properties of valsartan and related ARBs in APAP-induced liver injury and potentially NASH/MASLD, adding scientific depth to the benefit-risk profile of this drug class. Engaging hepatologists and clinical pharmacologists as KOLs in ARB hepatoprotection research represents a strategically valuable long-term investment for a company with an ARB portfolio.

Resultats principaux : This post-hoc exploratory analysis of the ENCHANTED2/MT randomized trial enrolled 816 acute ischemic stroke patients with elevated BP randomized within 3 hours post-successful endovascular thrombectomy (EVT) at 44 Chinese hospitals to intensive (BP less than 120 mmHg) or less intensive (140-180 mmHg) systolic BP targets. EQ-5D-3L data were available in 685 patients. At 90 days, both EQ-5D index (0.66 vs. 0.72) and EQ VAS (73.01 vs. 76.84) were significantly lower in the intensive BP group. Adjusted mean differences favored less intensive treatment: 0.06 (95% CI 0.01-0.11, p=0.017) for EQ-5D index and 3.49 (95% CI 0.38-6.60, p=0.028) for EQ VAS. Causal mediation analysis confirmed that the HRQoL impairment with intensive BP lowering was mainly indirect via worse modified Rankin Scale outcomes. Subgroup analyses were exploratory only. Limitations include the post-hoc design, single-ethnicity (Chinese) population, and the small absolute magnitude of EQ-5D differences relative to standard minimal clinically important difference thresholds.

Implications Medical Affairs : These patient-reported outcome data add a crucial HRQoL dimension to the functional harm already documented with intensive BP targets after EVT, providing converging evidence that supports less aggressive post-thrombectomy BP management (140-180 mmHg) in upcoming acute stroke guidelines. From a Medical Affairs perspective, this evidence challenges the paradigm of aggressive BP lowering in the acute post-stroke period and supports positioning of antihypertensive agents that can achieve stable moderate BP reduction without overshooting into potentially harmful ranges, offering a clinical differentiation opportunity. Engaging stroke neurologists and interventional neuroradiologists as KOLs around post-EVT BP protocols represents a high-value medical affairs strategy given the rapidly expanding use of thrombectomy in acute ischemic stroke worldwide.
