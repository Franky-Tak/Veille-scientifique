Prevalence and determinants of alcohol use disorder and its association with adherence to antihypertensive therapy among adult hypertensive patients in a hospital setting, Northwest Ethiopia.

Revue : Annals of Medicine | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-07

Resultats principaux : This hospital-based cross-sectional study enrolled 400 hypertensive outpatients at the University of Gondar Comprehensive and Specialized Hospital in Northwest Ethiopia (mean age 44.9 +/- 12.5 years, 100% response rate). The prevalence of alcohol use disorder (AUD) was 12.2%, comprising hazardous drinking (8.0%), harmful use (2.5%), and alcohol dependence (1.7%). Multivariable logistic regression identified male sex (AOR = 3.60; 95% CI: 1.30-9.94), cigarette smoking (AOR = 8.56; 95% CI: 3.89-18.82), and comorbidities (AOR = 3.87; 95% CI: 1.75-8.56) as independent predictors of AUD. Overall, 42.2% of participants were non-adherent to antihypertensive therapy, and alcohol dependence was associated with nearly fourfold higher odds of poor medication adherence compared with social drinking. Key limitations include the cross-sectional design precluding causal inference, single-center recruitment limiting generalizability, and reliance on self-reported adherence measures that may underestimate true non-adherence rates.

Implications Medical Affairs : This study underscores the dual burden of AUD and antihypertensive non-adherence in sub-Saharan African populations, suggesting that guideline bodies should incorporate routine alcohol screening as a standard component of hypertension management protocols in resource-limited settings. Companies marketing antihypertensive agents in LMIC settings should consider partnering with health systems to develop integrated screening programs, as uncontrolled hypertension due to poor adherence represents a substantial unmet clinical need. Engagement with KOLs in global health and implementation science could help design culturally adapted adherence support programs specifically targeting male smokers and patients with multiple comorbidities.

Evaluation of a sleep sound analysis smartphone app for home obstructive sleep apnea screening among community-dwelling Chinese adults with hypertension.

Revue : Sleep & Breathing | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-07

Resultats principaux : This randomized cross-over trial enrolled 180 Chinese adults with hypertension from a hospital clinic and community health center in Haikou, China (median age 51.0 years, 72.2% male), comparing a sleep sound analysis smartphone app (Snail Sleep) to the STOP-BANG questionnaire (SBQ), each followed by home sleep apnea testing with portable polysomnography. The prevalence of obstructive sleep apnea (OSA) was strikingly high at 91.7%, including 28.3% moderate and 37.2% severe OSA (mean AHI 28.6 +/- 23.02). An optimal app-generated apnea index (APP-AI) cutoff of 7.1 yielded 72.0% sensitivity, 85.5% specificity, a positive likelihood ratio of 4.96, and AUC of 0.831 (95% CI 0.773-0.890) for detecting moderate-to-severe OSA (AHI >= 15), outperforming both the original SBQ (AUC 0.728) and the BMI-adjusted SBQ (AUC 0.754). Participants reported high satisfaction with the app-based approach, and intention to pursue treatment remained high among those receiving abnormal screening results. Limitations include the single-center design, male predominance limiting generalizability to women, and absence of gold-standard in-lab polysomnography for validation.

Implications Medical Affairs : The extraordinarily high OSA prevalence (91.7%) in this hypertensive Chinese cohort reinforces the well-established bidirectional relationship between OSA and treatment-resistant hypertension, a key consideration when positioning antihypertensive portfolios in patients with inadequate BP control. Companies developing CPAP or pharmaceutical treatments for OSA-related hypertension should take note of validated digital screening tools as potential enablers of earlier diagnosis and streamlined treatment pathway entry. Medical Affairs should engage KOLs in cardio-respiratory medicine to advocate for integrated OSA screening protocols in hypertension clinics, supported by the strong diagnostic performance demonstrated in this trial.

Preliminary Report of a Nationwide Prospective Cohort of Congenital Diaphragmatic Hernia in Korea.

Revue : Journal of Korean Medical Science | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-06

Resultats principaux : This multicenter prospective cohort study (K-CDH registry) enrolled 60 newborns with congenital diaphragmatic hernia (CDH) across 6 Korean tertiary centers from March 2023 to December 2024, including centers equipped with experienced neonatologists, pediatric surgeons, and ECMO support. Non-isolated CDH was identified in 27% of cases including three genetic syndromes; 27% required ECMO with a 44% mortality rate among those requiring ECMO support. Multivariable analysis identified initial arterial PaCO2 and early severe pulmonary hypertension (PH) as independent predictors of mortality or ECMO requirement, consistent with international literature. Overall survival was 85%, exceeding many international benchmark figures. Among 57 surgically repaired infants, 40.4% required patch repair, and 53% received at least one pulmonary vasodilator including inhaled nitric oxide (25 infants) and milrinone (22 infants). Limitations include the interim nature of the analysis with limited sample size, potential selection bias toward severe cases at tertiary centers, and absence of long-term neurodevelopmental outcome data.

Implications Medical Affairs : Although this study addresses neonatal pulmonary hypertension secondary to CDH rather than primary systemic arterial hypertension, it carries relevance for pharmaceutical companies with pulmonary vasodilator portfolios targeting neonatal and pediatric indications, given the 53% pulmonary vasodilator utilization rate documented. The establishment of the K-CDH national registry provides a structured platform for Medical Affairs to engage with neonatal KOLs in Korea to generate real-world evidence supporting optimal pulmonary vasodilator treatment protocols. Collaboration with the K-CDH consortium could identify patients for investigational pulmonary hypertension therapy trials and strengthen product positioning in the neonatal intensive care setting.

Impact of folic acid supplementation on cardiovascular risk among individuals with hemorrhagic stroke: A prospective study.

Revue : Medicine (Baltimore) | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-03

Resultats principaux : This prospective observational study enrolled 799 patients with hemorrhagic stroke and H-type hypertension (total homocysteine >= 10 micromol/L) at a Chinese center, prescribed daily enalapril (10 mg) plus folic acid (0.8 mg) and followed for a median of 9.6 months. The primary composite endpoint included cardiovascular mortality, nonfatal myocardial infarction, and nonfatal stroke; overall, 5.0% developed CVD events and 2.6% experienced recurrent hemorrhagic stroke. Patients with high adherence (>= 70%) showed dramatically reduced risk of CVD events (HR: 0.16; 95% CI: 0.06-0.45), recurrent hemorrhagic stroke (HR: 0.21; 95% CI: 0.07-0.67), and all-cause mortality (HR: 0.14; 95% CI: 0.05-0.41) versus low-adherence patients. Subgroup analyses stratified by sex, age, BMI, blood pressure, cholesterol, and homocysteine level were broadly consistent in directional findings. Key limitations include the non-randomized design preventing true causal conclusions about the regimen's efficacy independent of adherence effects, single-center Chinese hospital setting, and potential residual confounding from unmeasured variables.

Implications Medical Affairs : These data extend the evidence base for the enalapril-folic acid fixed-dose combination in secondary cardiovascular prevention among H-type hypertensive patients with prior hemorrhagic stroke, strengthening its clinical value proposition in stroke-prone Asian populations where hyperhomocysteinemia is highly prevalent. Medical Affairs teams promoting ACE inhibitor-based antihypertensive therapies should leverage these findings to build the clinical case for screening homocysteine levels in hypertensive patients and incorporating folic acid supplementation into secondary prevention protocols. KOL engagement should target neurologists and cardiologists specializing in stroke prevention who could advocate for homocysteine-driven patient stratification and optimal combination antihypertensive adherence programs.

Leisure screen time, cardiometabolic pathways, and frailty: A two-step Mendelian randomization analysis.

Revue : Medicine (Baltimore) | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-03

Resultats principaux : This two-sample Mendelian randomization (MR) study used summary-level GWAS data from large international consortia to examine the causal association between genetically proxied leisure screen time (LST) and frailty risk, with mediation analysis via cardiometabolic factors. After radial MR outlier correction, higher genetically predicted LST was significantly associated with increased frailty risk (IVW OR = 1.157; 95% CI: 1.118-1.197; P = 5.42x10^-17), with broadly consistent directionality across sensitivity estimators (MR-Egger, weighted median, weighted mode). Two-step MR analyses demonstrated that BMI, hypertension (HTN), coronary artery disease (CAD), and type 2 diabetes (T2D) each partially mediated the LST-frailty relationship, accounting for 11.4%, 12.7%, 7.2%, and 7.8% of the total effect respectively. Although heterogeneity was present in the initial analysis, residual heterogeneity and directional pleiotropy were not evident after radial MR correction. Important limitations include reliance on summary-level data precluding individual-level analyses, potential residual pleiotropy despite sensitivity corrections, and restriction to populations predominantly of European ancestry limiting cross-ethnic generalizability.

Implications Medical Affairs : This MR analysis provides genetic-level causal evidence that leisure screen time contributes to frailty risk partly through hypertension as a mediating pathway, strengthening the scientific rationale for lifestyle interventions targeting sedentary screen-based behavior in hypertension management guidelines. Medical Affairs should leverage these findings to support the positioning of antihypertensive therapy within comprehensive cardiometabolic risk reduction frameworks, particularly given the growing recognition of hypertension as a gateway to frailty in aging populations. Engagement with geriatric cardiology KOLs could advocate for frailty screening in hypertensive patients and position antihypertensive interventions as upstream preventive strategies against frailty syndrome.

Association between a high total cholesterol-to-high-density lipoprotein cholesterol ratio and hyperuricemia among patients with hypertension: A cross-sectional study from central Uganda.

Revue : The Journal of International Medical Research | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-07

Resultats principaux : This cross-sectional study enrolled 277 consecutively recruited hypertensive patients (aged >= 18 years) from the Uganda Heart Institute between August and October 2025. Hyperuricemia prevalence was 32.1% (95% CI: 26.9-37.9%), with significantly higher rates among patients with TC/HDL ratio >= 5 (53.8%) versus < 5 (25.5%) (p < 0.001). After multivariable adjustment, a high TC/HDL ratio remained independently associated with hyperuricemia (adjusted OR = 2.83; 95% CI: 1.31-6.09; p = 0.008). The TC/HDL ratio demonstrated moderate discriminative ability for hyperuricemia at an optimal cutoff of 4.33 (sensitivity 58%, specificity 71%, AUC = 0.68; 95% CI: 0.61-0.75). No subgroup analyses specifically addressing sex or age interactions were reported. Limitations include the cross-sectional design restricting causal inference, single-center tertiary hospital recruitment introducing potential selection bias toward more severe disease profiles, and the relatively small sample of 277 participants.

Implications Medical Affairs : The 32.1% hyperuricemia prevalence in Ugandan hypertensive patients highlights a clinically significant comorbidity that may compound cardiovascular risk and constrain antihypertensive drug selection, particularly regarding thiazide diuretics that elevate serum uric acid. Medical Affairs teams should incorporate these findings into educational materials addressing metabolic monitoring and drug selection during antihypertensive therapy in sub-Saharan African settings, emphasizing alternatives to uricosuria-exacerbating agents in patients with atherogenic dyslipidemia. KOL engagement with African cardiologists and nephrologists could support the development of continent-specific hyperuricemia screening algorithms integrated into hypertension management guidelines.

Effects of Left Bundle Branch Pacing and Right Ventricular Pacing on Blood Pressure and Cardiac Function in Patients With Bradyarrhythmia Complicated With Hypertension.

Revue : British Journal of Hospital Medicine | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-16

Resultats principaux : This retrospective single-center study included 1060 patients with bradyarrhythmia and hypertension treated at Xinhua Hospital, Shanghai (January 2020 to December 2023), divided into left bundle branch pacing (LBBP, N=260) and right ventricular septal pacing (RVSP, N=800) groups. Postoperatively, the LBBP group demonstrated significantly lower systolic blood pressure (SBP) and pulse pressure (PP) compared to the RVSP group (both p < 0.05), with a significant time x group interaction for both hemodynamic parameters. Significant time effects were observed for LVEF, cardiac output (CO), and cardiac index (CI) in both groups; the group effect was significant specifically for CI (p < 0.05). The paced QRS duration was significantly narrower with LBBP throughout follow-up, and the ventricular pacing percentage was slightly lower in the LBBP group (p < 0.05). The incidence of postoperative adverse events was comparable between groups, indicating equivalent safety profiles. Limitations include the retrospective observational design with potential selection bias, single-center Chinese tertiary hospital setting, large group size imbalance (800 vs. 260), and absence of blinded outcome assessment.

Implications Medical Affairs : This study demonstrates that LBBP offers meaningful hemodynamic advantages over RVSP in hypertensive patients with bradyarrhythmia, including statistically significant reductions in systolic blood pressure and pulse pressure that could synergize with pharmacological antihypertensive therapy in this challenging patient population. Medical Affairs teams operating in the cardiac device or antihypertensive therapy space should monitor the accumulating LBBP evidence base as it may justify physiological pacing as an adjunctive blood pressure management strategy. Collaboration with electrophysiology and heart failure KOLs to investigate LBBP within comprehensive hypertension management protocols could differentiate cardiovascular care strategies and create cross-specialty educational opportunities.

Multidimensional correlates of adherence to cardiovascular treatment among patients with multimorbidity from tribal communities in South India: an exploratory cross-sectional study.

Revue : BMJ Open | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-07

Resultats principaux : This exploratory cross-sectional study, guided by the Consolidated Framework for Implementation Research (CFIR), enrolled 200 participants with multimorbidity from tribal communities in South India (mean age 64.6 +/- 9.71 years; 57.5% female; 80% tribal origin; 84.5% with no formal education). Hypertension was the predominant comorbidity (84%), followed by diabetes (56.5%) and chronic kidney disease (24%). Self-reported non-adherence to CVD medications (missing at least one pill in the prior week) was reported by 60% of participants. Independent correlates of non-adherence in multivariable logistic regression included lower education level (OR 8.27; 95% CI: 1.96-35.01; p=0.004), co-existing diabetes (OR 2.94; 95% CI: 1.04-8.33; p=0.04), and high pill burden (OR 5.04; 95% CI: 1.88-28.84; p=0.03), while social support (OR 0.85; 95% CI: 0.7-0.97; p=0.013) and positive illness perception (OR 0.95; 95% CI: 0.91-0.99; p=0.05) were protective. Limitations include reliance on self-reported adherence subject to social desirability bias, small sample size of 200 participants, single tribal community recruitment, and the cross-sectional design precluding causal inference.

Implications Medical Affairs : This study reveals a striking 60% medication non-adherence rate in hypertensive tribal communities in India, underscoring the critical implementation gap between clinical guidelines and real-world cardiovascular care in underserved populations with high educational barriers and pill burden. From a Medical Affairs perspective, simplified fixed-dose combination antihypertensive regimens represent a compelling value proposition for reducing pill burden, identified here as the most potent modifiable predictor of non-adherence, and should be prioritized in educational advocacy targeting rural Indian health systems. KOL strategy should engage implementation scientists and community health program leaders in India to co-develop culturally sensitive adherence interventions and generate real-world evidence supporting simplified combination antihypertensive regimens in this vulnerable patient segment.

Residual Cholesterol Inflammatory Index: A Predictor of Cardiovascular-Kidney-Metabolic Syndrome; Insights from NHANES and CHARLS Cohorts.

Revue : Anatolian Journal of Cardiology | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-07

Resultats principaux : This cross-sectional study analyzed data from two nationally representative cohorts, NHANES (US, n=10,669) and CHARLS (China, n=9,496), to evaluate the association between the residual cholesterol inflammatory index (RCII, a composite of residual cholesterol and C-reactive protein) and cardiovascular-kidney-metabolic (CKM) syndrome risk. In fully adjusted multivariate models, each 1-unit increment in RCII was associated with a 4% increase in CKM risk in NHANES (OR=1.04; 95% CI: 1.03-1.06; p<0.001) and a 3% increase in CHARLS (OR=1.03; 95% CI: 1.02-1.05; p<0.001). Quartile analysis revealed a strong dose-response gradient; CHARLS individuals in the highest quartile (Q4) exhibited a 9.60-fold higher CKM risk compared to Q1 (95% CI: 7.83-11.80). Restricted cubic spline models confirmed a nonlinear upward-sloping association in both datasets. Subgroup analyses in CHARLS revealed significant RCII-CKM interactions stratified by hypertension, diabetes, stroke, and smoking status, whereas NHANES showed significant interaction by race. Limitations include the cross-sectional design precluding temporal inference, cross-cultural heterogeneity between US and Chinese populations, and the lack of longitudinal validation of RCII as a clinical biomarker.

Implications Medical Affairs : RCII as a composite biomarker integrating residual atherogenic lipids and systemic inflammation offers a practical risk stratification approach for identifying patients at elevated CKM syndrome risk, a population in which hypertension is a frequent co-driver, supporting the use of intensified antihypertensive and metabolic therapies. Medical Affairs teams promoting lipid-lowering and anti-inflammatory therapeutic strategies should monitor RCII validation studies closely, as it may emerge as a complementary biomarker to LDL-C in comprehensive cardiovascular risk profiling. KOL engagement should target cardiologists, endocrinologists, and nephrologists who manage patients at the intersection of metabolic, cardiovascular, and renal disease, where antihypertensive positioning increasingly intersects with cardiorenal metabolic portfolios.

Metabolic dysfunction-associated steatotic liver disease and metabolic dysfunction-associated alcohol-related liver disease represent distinct clinical phenotypes and fibrosis risk: insights from a population-based Polish cohort.

Revue : Polish Archives of Internal Medicine | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-06

Resultats principaux : This population-based cross-sectional study analyzed 2456 randomly selected adult residents from Bialystok, Poland, as part of the Bialystok PLUS prospective cohort. The prevalence of metabolic dysfunction-associated steatotic liver disease (MASLD) was 22.72%, while metabolic dysfunction-associated alcohol-related liver disease (MetALD) accounted for 2.08% of the population. MASLD was predominantly associated with obesity and diabetes mellitus, whereas MetALD showed a significantly higher prevalence of arterial hypertension and dyslipidemia. Significant liver fibrosis was more frequent in MetALD (17.8%) and MASLD (15.6%) compared to controls (5.1%; p<0.001 and p=0.005, respectively), with cirrhosis observed in 4.44% of MetALD, 0.58% of MASLD, and 0.12% of control participants (p=0.001). Risk factors for significant fibrosis were analyzed across subgroups. Limitations include the cross-sectional design and single-city recruitment from Bialystok, potentially limiting extrapolation to broader Central and Eastern European populations with differing metabolic and alcohol consumption patterns.

Implications Medical Affairs : The significantly higher prevalence of arterial hypertension in MetALD patients compared to MASLD underscores the importance of comprehensive cardiovascular risk assessment, including antihypertensive therapy, in patients with alcohol-related metabolic liver disease who carry an amplified cardiometabolic risk burden. Medical Affairs teams must account for the emerging MetALD disease category when evaluating antihypertensive prescribing decisions, noting that hepatic metabolism of many antihypertensive agents may be impaired in patients with significant liver fibrosis or cirrhosis, necessitating tailored dosing considerations. KOL strategies targeting hepatologists and cardiologists across Central and Eastern Europe could leverage these epidemiological data to promote integrated liver-cardiovascular risk assessment protocols, positioning antihypertensive therapies within multidisciplinary MASLD and MetALD care pathways.
