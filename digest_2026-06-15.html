Prenatal paraben exposure and pregnancy complications: The moderating role of psychological anxiety.

Revue : Ecotoxicology and Environmental Safety | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-05-09

Resultats principaux : This prospective cohort study enrolled 934 mother-infant pairs from a tertiary hospital in Ningxia, China, to investigate the individual and combined effects of prenatal paraben (PB) exposure on pregnancy complications, specifically hypertensive disorders of pregnancy (HDP), gestational diabetes mellitus (GDM), and gestational anemia. Maternal serum concentrations of five parabens (methylparaben, ethylparaben, propylparaben, butylparaben, hexylparaben) were quantified using high-performance liquid chromatography-tandem mass spectrometry, and pregnancy-related anxiety was assessed using the Pregnancy-related Anxiety Questionnaire. The primary findings showed that ethylparaben (EtP; OR=1.13, 95% CI: 1.01-1.28) and butylparaben (BuP; OR=1.28, 95% CI: 1.01-1.62) were significantly associated with increased HDP risk, while quantile g-computation analyses confirmed a positive association between combined PB exposure and GDM risk. A significant interaction was identified between pregnancy-related anxiety and EtP exposure, with BuP remaining significantly associated with pregnancy complications specifically among women reporting anxiety; mixture exposure was also associated with elevated gestational anemia risk (95% CI: 1.06-6.87). Notable limitations include the single-center design restricting generalizability, the cross-sectional serum measurement at a single time point, and the potential for residual confounding from unmeasured socioeconomic and dietary variables.

Implications Medical Affairs : These findings expand the epidemiological understanding of hypertensive disorders of pregnancy by implicating ubiquitous environmental endocrine disruptors as modifiable risk contributors, reinforcing the case for preconception and antenatal counseling as part of a preventive hypertension strategy. From a Medical Affairs positioning standpoint, gestational hypertension as an environmental-chemical-attributable condition opens opportunities to engage with maternal-fetal medicine KOLs in discussions about the long-term cardiovascular sequelae of HDP and the role of antihypertensive therapy in the postpartum period. The identification of psychological anxiety as an effect modifier introduces a psycho-environmental framework for hypertension risk in pregnancy that warrants further investigation through interdisciplinary research partnerships.

Impact of toxic metal exposure on blood pressure: Insights into individual and combined effects with Bayesian kernel machine regression.

Revue : Ecotoxicology and Environmental Safety | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-05-07

Resultats principaux : This cross-sectional study surveyed 336 adult residents of Shaanxi Province, China (June to August 2022), comparing hair concentrations of six toxic metals (arsenic, cadmium, chromium, mercury, lead, nickel) between 199 hypertensive and 137 normotensive participants using inductively coupled plasma mass spectrometry. Chromium (Cr) and lead (Pb) concentrations were significantly higher in hypertensive participants (p<0.05), with multivariate linear regression confirming that Cr was positively associated with systolic blood pressure (beta=12.8, 95% CI: 2.8-22.7) and with hypertension risk (OR=2.1, 95% CI: 1.1-4.0). Bayesian kernel machine regression (BKMR) analysis of the mixed-metal exposure identified Pb as having the highest posterior inclusion probability (PIP=0.984), indicating its dominant role in the mixture-level hypertension effect. Dose-response analyses using Restricted Cubic Spline revealed nonlinear relationships for both Cr and Pb, while As and Cd appeared protective in the combined-exposure model, a finding likely reflecting complex mixture interactions rather than true protective effects. Limitations include the cross-sectional study design preventing causal inference, the use of hair as exposure proxy reflecting chronic rather than acute exposure, and the relatively small sample size limiting subgroup analysis power.

Implications Medical Affairs : The identification of chromium and lead as primary drivers of hypertension risk in a metal mixture context provides novel environmental etiology evidence supporting early antihypertensive intervention strategies in industrially exposed populations, particularly in rapidly industrializing regions of Asia. Medical Affairs teams should engage occupational medicine and environmental health KOLs to co-position antihypertensive therapy within a broader pollution-attributable cardiovascular disease narrative, which may be relevant for health technology assessment submissions in regions with high industrial metal exposure. This evidence also supports advocacy for population-level blood pressure screening programs in occupationally or environmentally exposed communities as a market access rationale for antihypertensive agents in high-risk, pollution-exposed patient segments.

Air pollution and adverse birth outcomes: A systematic review of fine critical windows.

Revue : Environmental Research | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-03-25

Resultats principaux : This PRISMA-compliant systematic review (PROSPERO: CRD42024523345) searched PubMed, Embase, and CENTRAL for studies examining critical gestational windows of outdoor air pollutant exposure (PM2.5, PM10, NO, O3) in relation to adverse pregnancy outcomes including prematurity, low birth weight, gestational diabetes, pre-eclampsia, eclampsia, gestational hypertension, and stillbirth. After screening 3,631 articles, 20 studies were included; all were conducted exclusively in China or the United States and used distributed lag models (DLM or DLNM) to identify time-specific exposure-response associations. The most consistent finding was for preterm birth, where weeks 17-28 of pregnancy (end of second trimester) were repeatedly identified as a critical window of vulnerability to air pollutants across multiple studies and pollutant types. Results for gestational hypertension and pre-eclampsia were highly heterogeneous across studies, and no included studies addressed intrahepatic cholestasis of pregnancy or stillbirth, representing major evidence gaps. Key limitations include geographic restriction to two countries, high between-study methodological and outcome heterogeneity, reliance on a single statistical approach (DLM/DLNM), and the inability to perform quantitative meta-analysis.

Implications Medical Affairs : The identification of week 17-28 as a specific critical window for air pollution-related adverse pregnancy outcomes, including hypertensive disorders of pregnancy, strengthens the case for targeted maternal blood pressure monitoring during the second trimester in high-pollution urban environments. Medical Affairs teams should engage with maternal-fetal medicine and environmental health KOLs to explore whether current antihypertensive guidelines should incorporate pollution-exposure risk stratification, particularly for gestational hypertension prevention during identified critical windows. Future prospective studies stratified by specific air pollutant type and geographic context could be proposed as investigator-initiated research collaborations to define optimal pharmacological intervention windows in pollution-exposed pregnant populations.

Associations between repeated measures of exposure to phthalates, phenols, parabens, and their mixtures and metabolic syndrome in midlife women in Mexico city.

Revue : Environmental Research | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-03-25

Resultats principaux : This longitudinal study from the ELEMENT cohort (Mexico City) included 422 midlife women with repeated urinary measurements of phthalate metabolites, phenols, and parabens obtained at two timepoints: 2008-2011 (mean age 36.7 years) and 2019-2023 (mean age 48.5 years), with metabolic syndrome (MetS) and its components — hypertriglyceridemia, hypertension, abdominal obesity, HDL deficiency, and hyperglycemia — assessed at the second visit. Multiple phthalate metabolites from Visit 2 were associated with higher odds of abdominal obesity (OR range: 1.67-2.46) but paradoxically with lower odds of hypertension (OR range: 0.70-0.78), and mixture analysis confirmed that a one-quartile increase in Visit 2 phthalate mixture was associated with 0.61 times lower odds of hypertension (95% CI: 0.38-0.97). In contrast, the phenol mixture from Visit 2 was associated with 1.40 times greater odds of hyperglycemia (95% CI: 1.03-1.89), and Visit 1 phthalate exposure was associated with lower odds of HDL deficiency (OR range: 0.77-0.84). Subgroup analyses by MetS component revealed inconsistent and sometimes opposing directionalities across different chemical classes, suggesting complex non-monotonic relationships. Key limitations include the single-cohort design, potential differential exposure misclassification across time, and inability to control for all behavioral and dietary confounders in a single statistical model.

Implications Medical Affairs : The paradoxical inverse association between phthalate exposure and hypertension risk in midlife women challenges simplistic linear models of environmental chemical effects on cardiovascular risk, and signals the need for exposome-aware design in future hypertension clinical trials. Medical Affairs teams should monitor this rapidly evolving field of environmental endocrinology, as understanding complex mixture effects on blood pressure regulation may eventually illuminate mechanisms underlying treatment resistance or differential antihypertensive responses across patient subpopulations. This study argues for the inclusion of environmental exposure biomarkers in the baseline characterization of patients enrolled in antihypertensive trials to better interpret between-subject variability in treatment outcomes.

Addition of high-sensitivity troponin to the T-Amylo score for the diagnosis of transthyretin cardiac amyloidosis in acute heart failure: TnT-Amylo.

Revue : International Journal of Cardiology | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-03-14

Resultats principaux : This prospective single-center study at the Instituto Cardiovascular de Buenos Aires enrolled 138 consecutive patients aged 60 years or older hospitalized with acute heart failure (AHF) between 2022 and 2024, to evaluate whether integrating high-sensitivity cardiac troponin T (hs-TnT) into the T-Amylo clinical risk score could improve diagnostic accuracy for transthyretin cardiac amyloidosis (ATTR-CM), a condition that frequently co-occurs with hypertensive heart disease. All patients underwent bone-tracer scintigraphy, with ATTR-CM defined by grade 2 or higher myocardial uptake and exclusion of plasma cell dyscrasia; ATTR-CM prevalence in the cohort was 15.9%. The original T-Amylo score showed good discrimination (AUC=0.93), while hs-TnT alone yielded an AUC of 0.80, with median hs-TnT significantly higher in ATTR-CM versus non-ATTR-CM patients (68 vs. 29 ng/L; p<0.001). In the clinically important intermediate-risk group (n=87, 63% of the cohort), incorporation of hs-TnT reclassified 15.7% to high risk (of whom 72.7% were confirmed ATTR-CM) and 84.3% to low risk (only 5% ATTR-CM), with the combined TnT-Amylo model achieving 72.3% sensitivity and 94.9% specificity, and a net reclassification improvement of 135%. Limitations include the single-center design, small sample size, potential for spectrum bias, and absence of external validation.

Implications Medical Affairs : While not directly focused on hypertension pharmacotherapy, this study is relevant because ATTR-CM is a clinically underdiagnosed condition frequently mistaken for hypertensive heart disease, and incorrect attribution can result in prolonged antihypertensive monotherapy without appropriate amyloid-directed treatment. Medical Affairs teams managing antihypertensive portfolios should be aware that biomarker-augmented diagnostic tools are reshaping differential diagnosis in heart failure, and KOL engagement should include cardiologists skilled in the differentiation between hypertensive cardiomyopathy and infiltrative cardiomyopathies. The very high specificity (94.9%) of the TnT-Amylo model could support earlier initiation of ATTR-CM-specific therapies and help avoid inappropriate continuation of antihypertensive monotherapy in patients with unrecognized amyloid cardiomyopathy, emphasizing the need for diagnostic precision in elderly hypertensive heart failure patients.

Intermittent fasting and cardiometabolic responses under cardiac stress: a systematic review of preclinical studies.

Revue : Life Sciences | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-04-13

Resultats principaux : This PROSPERO-registered systematic review (CRD42023466215) searched multiple databases and included 32 preclinical studies involving 451 predominantly male animals to assess the effects of intermittent fasting (IF) on cardiovascular outcomes under various cardiac stress models, including atherosclerosis induction, hypercholesterolemic diet, doxorubicin-induced cardiotoxicity, pulmonary arterial hypertension, myocardial infarction, and ischemia-reperfusion injury. Approximately 80% of included studies reported that IF improved cardiovascular outcomes, including reductions in blood pressure, cardiac hypertrophy, myocardial fibrosis, and ischemic injury, while also enhancing vascular and metabolic function across diverse cardiac stress models. Mechanistic analyses identified downregulation of pro-inflammatory pathways (NF-kappaB, IL-1beta, TNF-alpha) and activation of cardioprotective pathways (SIRT3, AMPK) as key mechanisms, with downstream improvements in mitochondrial function, lipid metabolism, and cardiac energy homeostasis. Cardioprotective benefits were found to be dependent on fasting duration and regimen type, with time-restricted feeding and alternate-day fasting protocols showing different magnitudes of cardiovascular protection. Significant limitations include the restriction to preclinical data only, a male-predominant animal model reducing translational applicability to female patients, high heterogeneity in IF protocols and cardiac stress models across studies, and the absence of a quantitative meta-analysis due to methodological variability.

Implications Medical Affairs : The robust preclinical evidence that multiple IF regimens reduce blood pressure and attenuate cardiac hypertrophy under stress provides a strong mechanistic rationale for adequately powered randomized clinical trials exploring IF as a lifestyle adjunct to antihypertensive pharmacotherapy. Medical Affairs teams should proactively engage with cardiometabolic KOLs and nutrition scientists to design and support investigator-initiated trials examining IF in hypertensive populations stratified by cardiovascular risk profile, generating real-world evidence that could inform guideline updates and product messaging around lifestyle co-interventions. The convergence of IF-activated AMPK/SIRT3 pathways with the mechanisms of some antihypertensive and cardioprotective agents (e.g., SGLT2 inhibitors) represents a scientifically compelling rationale for combination lifestyle-pharmacology research programs that Medical Affairs could champion to differentiate product strategy.
