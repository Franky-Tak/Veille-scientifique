Genetics of hypertension in populations affected by the Aral Sea ecological crisis: A case-control study.

Revue : PloS one | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-06

Resultats principaux : This population-based case-control study enrolled 801 participants from Karakalpakstan, Uzbekistan — an ecologically stressed region characterised by high environmental salinity due to the Aral Sea crisis — including 621 patients with essential hypertension and 180 normotensive controls. Nine polymorphic variants were genotyped using multiplex qPCR with allele-specific probes, and associations were assessed by logistic regression under a log-additive model with Bonferroni correction (corrected threshold p<5.6e-3). Six variants showed statistically significant associations after correction: AGT C521T (rs4762; OR=0.12, 95% CI 0.08-0.16, p=2.99e-50), NOS3 T(-786)C (rs2070744; OR=0.27, 95% CI 0.21-0.34, p=2.50e-27), AGTR2 G1675A (rs1403543; OR=0.52, 95% CI 0.42-0.64, p=4.76e-9), CYP11B2 C(-344)T (rs1799998; OR=0.47, 95% CI 0.36-0.60, p=8.97e-9), AGT T704C (rs699; OR=0.68, 95% CI 0.54-0.86), and GNB3 C825T (rs5443; OR=1.66, 95% CI 1.27-2.18). Stratified analyses by sex, age group (<60 vs ≥60 years), and BMI (<25 vs ≥25 kg/m²) were performed, revealing consistent associations. The study is limited by its single-site design in an environmentally specific region with high salinity exposure, reducing generalisability to other hypertensive populations.

Implications Medical Affairs : The strong associations of RAAS genes (AGT, AGTR2, CYP11B2) with essential hypertension in this environmentally stressed Central Asian population reinforce the pharmacogenomic rationale for RAAS-targeting agents and support precision medicine approaches in genetically and environmentally predisposed groups. Medical Affairs teams can leverage these findings to engage KOLs in pharmacogenomics and environmental cardiology, positioning this work within a broader narrative on gene-environment interactions as determinants of antihypertensive treatment response. This study also highlights a research gap in Central Asian hypertension registries and may justify investigator-initiated studies exploring RAAS inhibitor efficacy in genetically defined subpopulations from ecologically compromised regions.

Effect of the interaction between noise exposure and AGT gene polymorphisms in steelworkers on essential hypertension.

Revue : International archives of occupational and environmental health | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-06

Resultats principaux : This case-control study of 725 male steelworkers in Tangshan, China (224 essential hypertension cases, 501 controls) investigated gene-environment interactions between occupational noise exposure and angiotensinogen (AGT) gene polymorphisms (A-20C and T174M). Environmental noise and high-temperature exposure were objectively measured; associations were analysed by binary multifactorial logistic regression, crossover analysis, and generalised multifactor dimensionality reduction (GMDR). Noise exposure independently increased EH risk 1.636-fold (95% CI 1.127-2.375); the AGT T174M TM genotype increased risk 1.802-fold vs TT (95% CI 1.208-2.689); combined T174M-noise interaction conferred 2.02-fold elevated risk (OR=2.020, 95% CI 1.064-3.835) vs unexposed TT carriers. The three-factor GMDR model (T174M-A-20C-noise) achieved the strongest predictive association. Limitations include the male-only, single-industry design, potential noise-dose misclassification, and inability to control fully for dietary and medication confounders.

Implications Medical Affairs : The synergistic interaction between occupational noise and AGT polymorphisms identifies a high-risk industrial subgroup, relevant for Medical Affairs advocacy around workplace cardiovascular prevention and targeted screening programmes. These data support positioning RAAS-modulating antihypertensives as a pharmacogenomically grounded first-line choice in genetically predisposed industrial workers, potentially strengthening product differentiation in occupational health formularies. Engagement with KOLs in occupational medicine and pharmacogenomics is warranted to develop screening algorithms integrating genetic and environmental risk for early antihypertensive intervention in industrial populations.

Integrated Care Models for HIV, Diabetes and Hypertension in Sub-Saharan Africa: A Systematic Review of Effectiveness, Implementation and Real-World Applicability.

Revue : Journal of the International AIDS Society | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07

Resultats principaux : This PRISMA 2020-compliant systematic review synthesised 16 studies from 8 sub-Saharan African countries (2016-2026) evaluating integrated care models — one-stop clinics, nurse-led services, adherence clubs, and decentralised drug distribution — for co-management of HIV, diabetes, and hypertension. HIV outcomes were strong: retention in care was generally above 80% and viral suppression above 90% across integrated models. In contrast, hypertension and diabetes retention was lower (70-78%), and improvements in blood pressure and glycaemic control were inconsistent and frequently suboptimal. Key implementation barriers were NCD drug stockouts, workforce shortages, fragmented health information systems, stigma, and long waiting times; personnel costs dominated programme expenditure. Limitations include heterogeneous study designs, inconsistent outcome definitions, limited economic evidence, and concerns about scalability and sustainability given donor dependence and supply-chain fragility.

Implications Medical Affairs : The persistent shortfall in hypertension control within HIV-integrated care programmes represents a major unmet need, directly relevant to Medical Affairs advocacy for dedicated NCD components within global health architectures and integrated care pathways. This evidence supports positioning fixed-dose combination antihypertensives as tools to reduce polypharmacy burden and simplify prescription in resource-limited settings where NCD control currently lags far behind HIV outcomes. Medical Affairs teams can use these findings to engage global health KOLs and policy makers on the case for investment in hypertension-specific supply chains, task-shifting protocols, and affordable treatment access strategies across Sub-Saharan African health systems.

Visit-to-Visit Systolic Blood Pressure Variability Predicts Long-Term Cardiovascular Events After Coronary Artery Bypass Grafting.

Revue : Journal of clinical hypertension (Greenwich, Conn.) | Tier : Tier 3 | IF estime : 3-10

Date de publication : 2026-07

Resultats principaux : This prospective single-centre observational study enrolled 120 consecutive patients undergoing isolated CABG (2020-2024) within a structured Transitional Care Service System, with a median follow-up of 46 months and a landmark cohort of 116 patients. Systolic blood pressure variability (SBPV) was quantified by the coefficient of variation (CV) at scheduled office visits; patients were stratified at the median SBP-CV (8.4%) into low- and high-variability groups. MACCE occurred in 19 patients: the high-variability group experienced significantly more events (26.3% vs 6.8%, log-rank p=0.005). After multivariable adjustment for age, diabetes, number of measurements, and mean SBP, high SBPV remained an independent predictor of MACCE (HR=2.85, 95% CI 1.12-7.24, p=0.028), whereas mean SBP was not predictive. Key limitations include small sample size (n=120), single-centre design, absence of ambulatory BP data, and reliance on office measurements to characterise variability.

Implications Medical Affairs : Visit-to-visit BP variability as an independent predictor of post-CABG cardiovascular events — independent of mean BP level — shifts clinical focus toward BP stability as an emerging therapeutic target with implications for guideline revision and drug labelling differentiation. Medical Affairs can leverage this evidence to position antihypertensive agents with proven BP variability-reduction properties (long-acting CCBs, ARBs) in the post-surgical cardiovascular risk management space, supporting product differentiation in this high-stakes setting. KOL engagement in cardiac surgery and transitional care medicine should focus on developing consensus protocols for SBPV monitoring post-CABG, creating demand for antihypertensives with demonstrated variability-reducing profiles as quality-of-care standards.

Ultra-processed food intake and risk of renal cell carcinoma in the NIH-AARP Diet and Health Study: a prospective cohort analysis.

Revue : Lancet regional health. Americas | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-29

Resultats principaux : This prospective cohort analysis of 480,347 NIH-AARP participants (median age 62.6 years, 59.6% male, 91.3% White) with follow-up from 1995 to 2018 assessed NOVA-classified ultra-processed food (UPF) intake via food-frequency questionnaire, with cancer diagnoses linked to cancer registries. Multivariable Cox models were used; 3,330 RCC and 1,216 clear-cell (cc-)RCC diagnoses were ascertained. Higher UPF intake was independently associated with elevated RCC risk (HR=1.16, 95% CI 1.05-1.29, p-trend=0.0006) and cc-RCC risk (HR=1.38, 95% CI 1.17-1.62, p-trend<0.0001). Associations were attenuated but persisted after adjusting for BMI, diet quality, or hypertension status, suggesting that hypertension partially mediates the UPF-RCC pathway alongside obesity. Key limitations include the predominantly White cohort, single baseline dietary assessment, potential FFQ measurement error, and the observational design precluding causal inference.

Implications Medical Affairs : Hypertension's role as a partial mediator of the UPF-RCC association broadens the risk-reduction narrative for antihypertensive therapy beyond cardiovascular endpoints into oncological territory — an emerging but communication-sensitive area requiring careful Medical Affairs framing. This evidence may support long-term benefit framing in risk stratification programmes targeting patients with unhealthy dietary patterns and concurrent hypertension, reinforcing the value of sustained BP control for broader health outcomes. Medical Affairs teams should monitor prospective mechanistic studies to determine whether antihypertensive treatment modifies UPF-associated RCC risk before incorporating this finding into KOL scientific exchange materials.

Prevention of atherosclerotic coronary artery disease in tactical athletes.

Revue : American journal of preventive cardiology | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-05-22

Resultats principaux : This state-of-the-art review synthesised evidence on ASCVD risk in tactical athletes (TAs) — military service members, firefighters, and law-enforcement officers — a high-performance population in which sudden incapacitation carries serious individual and public safety consequences. Traditional ASCVD risk factors including hypertension, obesity, insulin resistance, and sleep disturbance are highly prevalent in TAs and commonly undertreated despite generally high baseline physical fitness. Unique occupational stressors (heat exposure, inhalational hazards, performance-enhancing drug use) contribute additional cardiovascular risk; sudden cardiac death is the leading cause of duty-related mortality across all TA disciplines. The authors advocate a paradigm shift from symptom-triggered evaluation to systematic risk-factor detection, aggressive risk-factor control, and selective non-invasive atherosclerosis imaging in high-risk TAs. As a narrative review, it carries no original data, is susceptible to selection bias in included studies, and lacks TA-specific RCT evidence to guide specific treatment thresholds.

Implications Medical Affairs : The high prevalence of undertreated hypertension in tactical athletes — despite their high fitness — represents an under-recognised target for Medical Affairs, particularly for agents proven safe during physically demanding activities. Positioning antihypertensives in this population requires evidence of compatibility with peak cardiovascular performance, creating differentiation opportunities for agents with neutral or beneficial exercise-performance profiles in occupational cardiology formularies. Medical Affairs KOL engagement strategies targeting occupational and military cardiology networks can raise hypertension treatment rates through institutional occupational health programmes and disease awareness campaigns tailored to high-performance populations.

Circadian regulation of cardiovascular function: from physiology to clinical implications.

Revue : Genome medicine | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-06

Resultats principaux : This comprehensive review examines circadian rhythms in cardiovascular physiology — from molecular clock networks and clock-controlled gene regulation to systemic cardiovascular homeostasis including blood pressure diurnal patterns, heart rate variability, endothelial function, and vascular tone. Circadian disruption arising from ageing, genetic predisposition, or modifiable environmental and lifestyle factors (shift work, sleep disruption, irregular eating patterns) was linked to increased risk of hypertension, atherosclerosis, heart failure, and arrhythmias through mechanisms including inflammation, mitochondrial dysfunction, and metabolic dysregulation. The review discusses inflamm-ageing as a promoter of atherosclerosis and acute cardiovascular events, and desynchrony between central (suprachiasmatic nucleus) and peripheral circadian clocks as a disease-amplifying mechanism. Circadian regulation of genes implicated in cardiovascular ageing is reviewed alongside therapeutic strategies including chronotherapy, time-restricted eating, and timed physical activity. As a narrative review based on correlative mechanistic evidence, the manuscript provides no original clinical trial data and synthesises heterogeneous molecular and clinical evidence without formal meta-analytic methods.

Implications Medical Affairs : Chronotherapy — optimising antihypertensive dosing timing based on circadian blood pressure patterns — is a scientifically grounded differentiating feature for long-acting antihypertensives with once-nightly dosing profiles that Medical Affairs can actively develop for KOL-facing scientific exchange programmes. The evidence linking circadian disruption (shift work, irregular sleep) to hypertension provides Medical Affairs with robust scientific context for patient education programmes addressing modifiable environmental and lifestyle risk factors alongside pharmacotherapy. Investment in collaborative research on circadian pharmacodynamics — exploring how antihypertensive dosing time affects BP variability and cardiovascular event risk — could generate proprietary differentiation data for long-acting agents in an increasingly competitive market.

Revue : Diabetes, obesity & metabolism | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-06

Resultats principaux : This longitudinal study used CHARLS 2011-2015 data (n=3,965 overweight/obese adults, BMI ≥24.0 kg/m²) to develop and internally validate a predictive nomogram for cardiometabolic multimorbidity (CMM), with external validation in 8,180 overweight/obese US HRS participants. Implications Medical Affairs : Hypertension's retention as an independent predictor of cardiometabolic multimorbidity in a cross-nationally validated nomogram reinforces its central role in risk stratification for the growing overweight and obese older adult population — a prime target demographic for antihypertensive therapy. Medical Affairs can use this validated tool in disease awareness programmes framing hypertension as a gateway condition to broader cardiometabolic burden, strengthening the case for early and sustained treatment across both Asian and Western markets. Breast cancer and coronary artery disease: insights from a national inpatient sample analysis.

Revue : Future cardiology | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-06

Resultats principaux : This retrospective analysis of the 2016-2019 National Inpatient Sample included 589,940 unweighted breast cancer hospitalisations (2,949,699 weighted), 1:1 propensity-score-matched to controls, with multivariable logistic regression adjusting for hypertension, diabetes, CKD, smoking, obesity, dyslipidaemia, and prior MI. Implications Medical Affairs : While breast cancer per se was not independently associated with increased CAD risk after propensity matching, hypertension's role as a key confounder highlights the necessity of rigorous cardiovascular risk management in breast cancer survivors, compounded by cumulative cardiotoxic treatment burden. Revue : PLOS global public health | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-06

Resultats principaux : This hybrid type 2 effectiveness-implementation cluster RCT (NCT06163859) randomised 12 primary healthcare facilities in Namobuddha Municipality, Nepal (1:1) to intervention, where 34 Female Community Health Volunteers (FCHVs) delivered bi-monthly group hypertension sessions over three months to adults aged 30-75 with elevated blood pressure. Implications Medical Affairs : This cluster RCT demonstrates the feasibility of community health worker-led hypertension programmes in rural LMIC settings, establishing adherence and knowledge gains as intermediate endpoints that precede clinical BP improvement — a finding directly relevant to pharmaceutical patient support programmes in similar markets. Revue : CVIR endovascular | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-06

Resultats principaux : This case report describes a 57-year-old cirrhotic male presenting with one month of progressive hepatic encephalopathy refractory to lactulose and rifaximin, in whom CT and scrotal ultrasound identified a spontaneous portosystemic shunt from inferior mesenteric vein branches through the scrotal venous plexus to the left testicular vein — a complication of portal hypertension. Medical Affairs monitoring of hepatology-cardiology intersections can note portal hypertension complications as illustrative of the heterogeneous presentations under the 'hypertension' umbrella, supporting the importance of condition-specific therapeutic approaches in cross-specialty KOL communications. Hypertension and Pneumonia: A Retrospective Analysis on the Length of Stay.

Revue : Cureus | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-04

Resultats principaux : This retrospective analysis of 18,141 hospitalisation records compared extended length of stay (LOS ≥6 days) across three groups: patients with pneumonia and concomitant hypertension (PXG), pneumonia without hypertension (P0G), and hypertension without pneumonia (0XG). A statistically significant reduction in the proportion of patients with extended LOS was found in the PXG vs P0G cohort — an unexpected finding given the additional comorbidity burden of hypertension in the pneumonia group. Subgroup analysis revealed that the protective LOS effect was specifically driven by patients aged >65 years with comorbid hypertension. Authors hypothesise that antihypertensive medication use, lifestyle modifications, and higher baseline healthcare contact frequency in treated hypertensive patients may explain the shorter observed LOS. Limitations are substantial: retrospective administrative design, potential ICD coding inaccuracies, inability to control for antihypertensive drug class or adherence, and residual confounding from unmeasured health-seeking behaviours.

Implications Medical Affairs : The paradoxical finding of shorter hospitalisation in hypertensive pneumonia patients — likely mediated by more regular healthcare contact and antihypertensive management routines — provides indirect evidence of a systemic health benefit associated with treated hypertension that Medical Affairs can incorporate selectively into adherence and disease management campaigns. Prospective research should investigate whether specific antihypertensive drug classes (e.g., ACE inhibitors with immunomodulatory properties) mediate this protective LOS association, representing a potential product differentiation opportunity supported by Medical Affairs investigator-initiated research funding.
