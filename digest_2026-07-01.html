Potassium Imbalance in Patients with Hypertension and Acute Haemorrhagic Stroke in a Tertiary Level Hospital of Bangladesh.

Revue : Mymensingh Medical Journal | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026 Jul

Resultats principaux : This cross-sectional descriptive study enrolled 100 patients (mean age 59.5 plus or minus 15 years, 58% male, 42% female) with hypertension and CT-scan-confirmed acute haemorrhagic stroke at Mymensingh Medical College Hospital, Bangladesh, between December 2019 and May 2020. The primary endpoint was the prevalence and pattern of serum potassium imbalance among hypertensive patients admitted with haemorrhagic stroke. Results showed that 34% of participants had potassium imbalance, with hypokalemia (K+ below 3.5 mmol/L) present in 31% and hyperkalemia (K+ above 5 mmol/L) in 3%. Patients with potassium imbalance tended to display lower ICH scores, suggesting a trend toward less severe stroke presentations in this subgroup, although the association between potassium imbalance and haemorrhagic stroke severity was not statistically significant. Key limitations include the small, single-centre sample (n=100), short six-month recruitment window, cross-sectional design precluding causal inference, absence of medication history adjustment, and lack of a normotensive control group for comparison.

Implications Medical Affairs : These preliminary findings from Bangladesh highlight electrolyte dysregulation — specifically hypokalemia — as a potentially modifiable cofactor in hypertensive haemorrhagic stroke patients, reinforcing the importance of electrolyte-sparing antihypertensive regimens in high-risk populations. From a Medical Affairs perspective, this evidence supports scientific messaging around antihypertensive agents with favorable potassium profiles (potassium-sparing diuretics, ARBs, ACE inhibitors) that may reduce hypokalemia-associated stroke risk, offering a differentiation angle in regional portfolio positioning. Larger prospective studies are warranted to confirm this association, representing an opportunity for KOL engagement and investigator-initiated study support in South and Southeast Asian cardiovascular research networks.

Potential synergistic influence of magnesium and vitamin D supplementation on blood pressure reduction: a narrative review.

Revue : Nutrition Research (New York) | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026 Jun 06

Resultats principaux : This narrative review, conducted by authors from Complutense University of Madrid and Harvard Medical School, evaluated randomized clinical trials published over the past 15 years identified through systematic searching of PubMed, ClinicalTrials.gov, and the WHO trials registry, focusing on the joint effect of magnesium and vitamin D supplementation on blood pressure outcomes. Magnesium supplementation was consistently associated with a statistically significant reduction in systolic blood pressure compared to placebo across the included trials, while vitamin D supplementation demonstrated potential for reducing both systolic and diastolic blood pressure, particularly among individuals with type 2 diabetes or overweight or obesity. A plausible complementary and potentially synergistic biological mechanism was proposed, as magnesium is required for vitamin D transport, conversion, and receptor activation. No head-to-head randomized trials specifically testing the magnesium-vitamin D combination on blood pressure were identified, representing the most critical gap in the evidence base. Limitations include heterogeneous dosing regimens, patient populations, and outcome measurement methods across included studies, as well as the narrative design which precludes quantitative synthesis and introduces selection bias risk.

Implications Medical Affairs : The potential complementarity of magnesium and vitamin D in blood pressure reduction, if confirmed through dedicated combination RCTs, could support the integration of micronutrient optimization strategies as adjuncts to first-line antihypertensive pharmacotherapy, particularly in metabolically at-risk patients. For Medical Affairs, this evidence provides a scientifically grounded messaging opportunity around holistic hypertension management, appealing to preventive cardiology and nutrition KOLs who advocate for dietary supplementation alongside pharmacological treatment. The explicit identification of absent combination trial data constitutes a clear gap for investigator-initiated study support, positioning pharmaceutical companies as enablers of rigorous evidence generation in the lifestyle-pharmacotherapy interface.

Hypertension and left ventricular geometry: Diagnosis, prognosis, and management.

Revue : EXCLI Journal | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026 Jun 02

Resultats principaux : This comprehensive narrative review from Rush University Medical Center examined the full spectrum of hypertensive left ventricular (LV) remodeling, encompassing concentric remodeling, concentric hypertrophy, and eccentric hypertrophy as classified by LV mass index and relative wall thickness primarily measured via echocardiography. Key determinants of LV geometric changes were synthesized, including cumulative blood pressure exposure, sex differences (women exhibiting higher rates of concentric hypertrophy relative to men), obesity, pericardial adiposity, metabolic comorbidities, and obstructive sleep apnea as independent contributors to LV mass. Prognostic data confirm that LV geometric patterns carry independent predictive value for major adverse cardiovascular events, heart failure, and mortality beyond blood pressure levels alone. The review details emerging AI-augmented electrocardiographic and echocardiographic approaches that may improve early detection and phenotypic differentiation of hypertensive LV remodeling. Evidence for regression of LV hypertrophy through intensive blood pressure control (targeting systolic BP below 120 mmHg), renin-angiotensin system blockade, and lifestyle interventions is reviewed, emphasizing the critical window prior to irreversible myocardial fibrosis. Limitations include the narrative design, absence of formal meta-analytic quantification, and the heterogeneous and evolving evidence base for AI-based cardiac imaging tools.

Implications Medical Affairs : The independent prognostic significance of LV geometric patterns beyond blood pressure values reinforces the scientific rationale for incorporating LV hypertrophy regression as a primary surrogate endpoint in antihypertensive clinical trial programs, strengthening trial design credibility and regulatory discussions. For Medical Affairs, this review supports differentiated scientific messaging for antihypertensive agents — particularly RAS inhibitors and calcium channel blockers — that demonstrate superior LV hypertrophy regression, enabling compelling scientific exchanges with cardiology and cardiac imaging KOLs. The emerging role of AI-enhanced echocardiographic and ECG tools as prognostic markers presents a forward-looking scientific communication opportunity and a potential area for cross-industry partnerships to sponsor validation studies enhancing the treatment monitoring framework in hypertensive heart disease.

Oxalate Nephropathy-Induced Acute Kidney Injury following Use of an Organic Hair-Treatment Product in an 8-Year-Old Child: A Case Report.

Revue : Case Reports in Nephrology and Dialysis | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026 Apr 28

Resultats principaux : This case report from Maternity and Children Hospital, Al-Ahsa, Saudi Arabia describes an 8-year-old previously healthy girl who developed severe oliguric acute kidney injury (AKI), arterial hypertension, facial edema, and abdominal distension within 48 hours of a single topical application of an unidentified organic hair-treatment product. Renal biopsy confirmed the diagnosis of oxalate nephropathy with acute tubular necrosis and extensive calcium oxalate crystal deposition, establishing secondary hyperoxaluria from percutaneous or ingested oxalate as the causative mechanism. The patient was managed conservatively with cautious fluid administration, intravenous furosemide, electrolyte correction, antihypertensive therapy, and a low-oxalate diet, achieving complete renal function normalization within one month with no evidence of recurrence at subsequent follow-up visits. This case identifies environmental cosmetic product exposure as a novel cause of secondary hyperoxaluria and AKI with associated hypertension in a pediatric patient, a mechanism rarely reported in Saudi Arabian literature. Limitations include the single-case design, absence of chemical composition analysis of the implicated product preventing identification of the specific oxalogenic compound, and the inability to quantify long-term subclinical renal injury risk.

Implications Medical Affairs : This case highlights the cardiovascular-renal consequences of unregulated cosmetic product exposure and reinforces secondary hypertension as a complication of acute nephrotoxic environmental exposures, a clinically important etiology that may be underrepresented in hypertension management guidelines. For Medical Affairs teams active in the renal and pediatric hypertension space, this report provides educational content supporting disease awareness campaigns emphasizing secondary causes of hypertension and the importance of thorough environmental and product-exposure history in pediatric BP workups. The regulatory gap regarding unlabeled and uncontrolled cosmetic products represents an advocacy opportunity for pharmaceutical and healthcare industry stakeholders to support policy initiatives improving product safety standards and thereby reducing iatrogenic renal-hypertensive emergencies.

Applying the extended health belief model to understand salt-reduction behaviors among Saudi adults: a cross-sectional study.

Revue : Frontiers in Public Health | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026 Jun 15

Resultats principaux : This cross-sectional survey recruited 522 Saudi adults and applied an extended Health Belief Model (HBM) framework to assess the psychosocial determinants of salt-reduction behaviors in a population where sodium intake substantially exceeds WHO recommendations. While 85.1% of participants identified high salt intake as a risk factor for hypertension, only 15.3% correctly identified the WHO daily salt limit (below 5 g/day), revealing a significant awareness-to-technical-knowledge gap. High habitual salt use during cooking was reported by 55.2% of participants and frequent salty snack and fast food consumption was observed across all demographic subgroups, with suboptimal dietary practices universal. Multivariable analysis identified behavioral intention (beta=0.291), self-efficacy (beta=0.248), and cues to action (beta=0.234) as significant positive predictors of favorable salt-reduction dietary practices. A persistent awareness-behavior gap was confirmed, where theoretical awareness of harm did not translate to consistent behavioral modification. Limitations include the cross-sectional design precluding causal inference, self-reported dietary data susceptible to social desirability bias, and the Saudi-specific sample limiting generalizability to other high-sodium consumption settings.

Implications Medical Affairs : The robust awareness-behavior gap in sodium reduction documented in Saudi adults confirms that information-only health campaigns are insufficient and validates the need for structured behavioral intervention frameworks targeting self-efficacy, environmental cueing, and motivational reinforcement as drivers of sustained dietary change. These psychosocial determinants are directly relevant to the design of patient support programs accompanying antihypertensive therapies in Gulf Cooperation Council markets, where lifestyle co-management is an increasingly critical component of BP control strategy. Medical Affairs teams can leverage these findings to co-develop evidence-based, HBM-aligned patient education materials with dietetics and preventive cardiology KOLs, building a differentiated value proposition for antihypertensive treatment packages that integrate structured dietary behavior change support.

Prevalence and determinants of metabolic syndrome among patients with type 2 diabetes mellitus in the Buea Health District, South West Region, Cameroon.

Revue : The Pan African Medical Journal | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026 Feb 17

Resultats principaux : This hospital-based cross-sectional study recruited 139 patients with type 2 diabetes mellitus (T2DM) using systematic sampling in the Buea Health District, South West Region of Cameroon, between February and May 2024, with a mean age of 59.5 plus or minus 10.95 years. Using International Diabetes Federation (IDF) criteria, the prevalence of metabolic syndrome among T2DM patients was 64.0%, and 56.8% applying National Cholesterol Education Program Adult Treatment Panel III (NCEP-ATP III) criteria. Metabolic syndrome prevalence was significantly higher in women (56.81%), and central obesity, hyperglycemia, and hypertension were the most frequently observed components, with female patients demonstrating a higher burden across all three. Multivariable logistic regression identified female sex, older age, alcohol consumption, absence of self-glucose monitoring, and poor glycemic control (HbA1c) as independent determinants of metabolic syndrome (all p below 0.05). Subgroup analyses revealed that patients with poor glycemic control had substantially higher rates of metabolic syndrome compared to those with adequate control. Limitations include the small single-district sample (n=139) limiting national or sub-Saharan African generalizability, the cross-sectional design, and reliance on self-reported lifestyle data susceptible to recall bias.

Implications Medical Affairs : The 64% prevalence of metabolic syndrome among T2DM patients in Cameroon — with hypertension as a cardinal component — underscores the compounding cardiovascular risk burden in sub-Saharan Africa where both conditions are frequently undertreated and underdiagnosed, creating an urgent unmet medical need. For Medical Affairs, this evidence supports positioning antihypertensive agents with glucose-neutral or glucose-beneficial profiles within the cardiometabolic continuum narrative, particularly for markets where T2DM-hypertension comorbidity is the dominant cardiovascular risk pattern. The marked female predominance of metabolic syndrome and the association with poor glycemic control point to targeted patient access and health equity programs, with opportunities for KOLs in endocrinology, cardiology, and public health in Central and West Africa to co-develop adapted clinical guidance addressing resource-limited healthcare systems.
