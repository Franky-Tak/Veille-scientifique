Echocardiographic Probability of Pulmonary Hypertension, Cardiac Structural Alterations and All-Cause Mortality in Maintenance Hemodialysis Patients: A Single-Center Retrospective Cohort Study With Competing Risk Analysis.

Revue : Clinical Cardiology | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07

Resultats principaux : This single-center retrospective cohort study enrolled 749 maintenance hemodialysis (MHD) patients from a tertiary hospital in China (May 2010 to May 2022), applying a Fine-Gray competing risk model with renal transplantation as the competing event to minimize survivorship bias. The primary endpoint was all-cause mortality; over the follow-up period, 274 deaths and 64 renal transplantations occurred. Intermediate or high echocardiographic probability of pulmonary hypertension (PH), older age, increased left atrial diameter, and right ventricular diameter (RVD) were independently associated with all-cause mortality in multivariate analysis. Subgroup analyses by vascular access type revealed that age and high PH probability predicted mortality in tunneled cuffed catheter (TCC) patients, while age, intermediate/high PH probability, and RVD were significant predictors in arteriovenous fistula/graft (AVF/AVG) patients; no survival difference was observed between the two vascular access groups. A nomogram was constructed to support individualized cardiovascular risk stratification; however, the study is constrained by its retrospective single-center design, potential selection bias, and limited generalizability beyond the Chinese university hospital setting.

Implications Medical Affairs : This study highlights the prognostic value of routine echocardiographic PH screening in the chronic kidney disease and dialysis population, which may inform future cross-specialty guideline updates bridging nephrology and cardiology and create positioning opportunities for therapies targeting cardiopulmonary complications in end-stage renal disease. From a Medical Affairs perspective, the demonstration that PH severity independently drives mortality in hemodialysis patients—across both vascular access types—points to an underexplored indication space where differentiated cardiovascular agents could be evaluated in nephrology-cardiology combined outcomes studies. Engaging KOLs in both nephrology and pulmonary hypertension through targeted symposia and co-authorship of real-world evidence publications would reinforce credibility and open investigator-initiated research avenues around nomogram-driven cardiovascular risk stratification in dialysis patients.

Epidemiological Trends in Mortality From Acute Myocardial Infarction With Essential and Secondary Hypertension: CDC WONDER Database Analysis, 2000-2024.

Revue : Clinical Cardiology | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07

Resultats principaux : This large-scale, population-based epidemiological analysis drew on the CDC WONDER database (2000–2024) to characterize mortality trends among US adults aged 25 years and older who died from acute myocardial infarction (AMI, ICD-10 I21) with essential hypertension (ICD-10 I10) or secondary hypertension (ICD-10 I15.0/I15.9) as a contributing factor. A total of 842,646 deaths were identified, with age-adjusted mortality rates (AAMR) declining significantly from 17.63 per 100,000 in 2000 to 12.26 in 2024, reflecting an overall average annual percentage change (AAPC) of -1.71%. Despite this long-term downward trend, joinpoint regression identified alarming upward inflections in the Southern US region (APC: +11.17%, p=0.004) and metropolitan areas (APC: +7.71%, p=0.038) during 2018–2021, as well as among Non-Hispanic Black individuals (APC: +9.45%, p=0.053) and adults aged 65–74 years (APC: +7.15%, p=0.039) over the same period. Males consistently showed a higher mean AAMR (18.50) compared to females (11.82), with pronounced state-level geographic heterogeneity observed across the study period. Limitations include reliance on death certificate data, potential ICD-10 coding inconsistencies, and the ecological study design, which precludes causal inference at the individual level.

Implications Medical Affairs : The identification of persistent and widening mortality disparities in hypertension-related AMI among Non-Hispanic Black individuals and Southern US residents between 2018 and 2021 provides critical epidemiological grounding for targeted product access and patient support programs in high-burden, historically underserved communities. The post-2018 reversal of progress in specific subgroups likely reflects disruptions in chronic disease management, including reduced antihypertensive adherence during the COVID-19 pandemic, and reinforces the strategic importance of real-world evidence studies examining treatment continuity and long-term cardiovascular outcomes in vulnerable populations. Medical Affairs can leverage these data to brief payers, health systems, and public health KOLs on the residual unmet need in hypertension-driven AMI mortality reduction, supporting value-based messaging and formulary positioning in high-risk demographic segments.

Associations Between Obesity-Related Comorbidities and Weight Loss After Bariatric Surgery: A Retrospective Analysis.

Revue : Journal of Obesity | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026

Resultats principaux : This retrospective analysis of 601 bariatric surgery patients in Poland examined the prevalence of obesity-related comorbidities and their relationship to total weight loss over a 1-year postoperative period, using multivariable linear regression adjusted for age, sex, and baseline BMI. Hypertension was the most prevalent comorbidity at baseline, affecting 71.2% of the study population, underscoring the near-universal burden of elevated blood pressure in candidates for bariatric surgery. After adjustment, the presence of metabolic syndrome (beta=2.405; 95% CI: 0.195–4.615) and COPD (beta=6.522; 95% CI: 1.094–11.951) were independently and positively associated with greater 1-year total weight loss (%WL), while osteoarthritis and gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD) were associated with lower weight loss outcomes. Hypertension alone was not independently associated with weight loss magnitude after adjustment, suggesting that its effect is mediated through metabolic syndrome overlap. The study is limited by its retrospective single-center design, 1-year follow-up duration, restriction to a Polish population, and potential unmeasured confounders including medication changes and dietary compliance.

Implications Medical Affairs : The extraordinarily high prevalence of hypertension (71.2%) in bariatric surgery candidates confirms that this population represents a major segment for antihypertensive pharmacotherapy, requiring proactive medication management protocols—particularly dose adjustment or de-escalation—in the postoperative period as blood pressure improves. Medical Affairs teams should engage bariatric surgery KOLs and multidisciplinary obesity centers to co-develop evidence-based antihypertensive management algorithms that address the transition from maximum pharmacological therapy to potentially reduced medication burden following significant weight loss. These data also present an opportunity to study long-term cardiovascular outcomes in hypertensive patients who undergo bariatric surgery, positioning antihypertensive agents within integrated cardiometabolic treatment pathways and supporting product differentiation through real-world evidence generation in this complex patient population.

Lifestyle modifications in individuals with blood pressure beyond treatment target despite pharmacological treatment: design and baseline characteristics of the MeDiPA randomized controlled trial.

Revue : Blood Pressure | Tier : Tier 3 | IF estime : 3–5

Date de publication : 2026-07-18

Resultats principaux : The MeDiPA (Hypocaloric Mediterranean Diet or Physical Activity) randomized controlled trial was designed to evaluate whether structured lifestyle interventions—specifically a hypocaloric Mediterranean-style diet or an increased physical activity program—can help patients with uncontrolled hypertension achieve the recently endorsed European BP target of below 130/80 mmHg. Sixty-six participants (56% women) were enrolled and randomized across three arms: diet (n=21), physical activity (n=22), and control (n=23) at Oslo University Hospital, with study visits at baseline and at 3, 6, and 12 months. The baseline characteristics paper reports a mean age of 59 years, mean BMI of 32.0 kg/m2, and mean office BP of 137/90 mmHg; 61% of participants had BP exceeding 140/90 mmHg at enrollment, and 83% met at least three metabolic syndrome criteria, reflecting a high cardiometabolic risk phenotype. All participants were required to be on at least two antihypertensive agents with BMI 25–40 kg/m2 and abdominal obesity criteria; patients with BP ≥160/100 mmHg, type 1 diabetes, or recent cardiovascular events were excluded. The primary outcome is change in 24-hour ambulatory BP after six months, with exploratory outcomes including metabolic parameters and quality of life; however, as this is a design and baseline characteristics report, no efficacy outcomes are yet available, and the small, predominantly Norwegian, highly educated sample (83% higher education) substantially limits generalizability.

Implications Medical Affairs : The MeDiPA trial addresses a major real-world clinical gap: whether lifestyle modification provides additive BP benefit to patients already receiving guideline-directed pharmacotherapy, a question directly relevant to the new European recommendation of a BP target below 130/80 mmHg that leaves many treated patients undertreated. From a Medical Affairs perspective, the trial's outcomes—expected within the next 12 months—will be pivotal for shaping positioning messages around antihypertensive agents in combination with structured lifestyle programs, particularly in the growing segment of metabolically complex, obese hypertensives who require multi-modal management. Engaging preventive cardiology and hypertension KOLs ahead of results publication, and participating in the dissemination strategy through advisory boards or congress symposia, would position the company as a leader in evidence-based, integrated hypertension management aligned with the latest European guidelines.
