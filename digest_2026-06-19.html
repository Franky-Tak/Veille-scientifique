The role of metabolic health in neurostructural and cognitive alterations in bipolar disorders.

Revue : Psychological Medicine | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-18

Resultats principaux : This observational case-control study enrolled 163 participants — 76 with bipolar disorder (BD) and 87 healthy controls — at a Canadian academic psychiatric centre to investigate how metabolic dysfunction mediates brain structural and cognitive changes in BD. Using principal component analysis across ten cardiometabolic variables, the investigators found that obesity, hypertension, insulin resistance, and dyslipidemia were the dominant contributors to the composite metabolic health index. The MetSy-associated risk cluster significantly predicted accelerated brain ageing as measured by machine-learning BrainAGE (beta = 0.75 +/- 0.29, p = 0.011) and impaired cognitive performance (beta = -0.19 +/- 0.09, p = 0.037), independently of psychiatric medications, symptom burden, and illness duration. Mediation analyses revealed that metabolic dysfunction — including hypertension as a key component — accounted for approximately 30% of the association between BD and higher BrainAGE and 25% of the association between BD and worse cognitive outcomes. Primary limitations include the cross-sectional design precluding causal inference, the relatively small sample of 163 participants at a single centre, and the absence of longitudinal brain imaging data to assess the reversibility of these findings with cardiometabolic treatment.

Implications Medical Affairs : This study reinforces the need for systematic hypertension and metabolic syndrome screening in psychiatric populations, broadening antihypertensive therapy positioning beyond cardiovascular prevention to encompass neuroprotection and cognitive preservation in patients with comorbid BD. From a Medical Affairs standpoint, these findings support developing cross-specialty communication initiatives targeting psychiatrists, neurologists, and cardiologists around the cerebrovascular consequences of untreated hypertension in psychiatric comorbidity — a high-prescriber segment often overlooked in traditional hypertension campaigns. The data provide a compelling basis for supporting outcomes research examining antihypertensive agents' impact on cognitive trajectories in bipolar populations, potentially differentiating products with established end-organ protection profiles in scientific congresses and KOL engagements.

Factors Associated With Evidence of Self-Measured Blood Pressure Adoption in an Urban Safety-Net Health System.

Revue : Journal of Clinical Hypertension (Greenwich) | Tier : Tier 3 | IF estime : 3–5

Date de publication : 2026-06

Resultats principaux : This cross-sectional study analysed electronic health records of 15,937 hypertensive patients cared for by 283 providers across 12 clinics in an urban safety-net health system in San Francisco, California. The primary endpoint was documented self-measured blood pressure (SMBP) adoption, defined as at least one patient-reported blood pressure value entered in the EHR from the hypertension registry. Only 26% of patients (n = 4,186) had documented SMBP adoption, representing a major implementation gap relative to current guideline recommendations. In multivariable hierarchical logistic regression, patients aged 75 years and older were significantly more likely to have documented SMBP adoption (aOR 2.37, 95% CI 1.62–3.55), as were those with an activated patient portal (aOR 1.36, 95% CI 1.25–1.49), while patients experiencing homelessness were significantly less likely (aOR 0.72, 95% CI 0.60–0.87). Variance decomposition attributed 75% of SMBP adoption variability to patient-level factors, 19% to clinics, and 6% to providers, identifying clinic-level workflows as a primary modifiable implementation lever; major limitations include the cross-sectional retrospective design, reliance on EHR documentation as a proxy for actual monitoring behaviour, and the restricted generalisability from a single safety-net system.

Implications Medical Affairs : The 74% non-adoption rate among guideline-indicated SMBP users reveals a critical blood pressure monitoring implementation gap in underserved populations, with direct implications for connected-device programs and digital health partnerships that pharmaceutical companies can co-develop to improve hypertension management and medication adherence. For Medical Affairs, these data provide the evidentiary basis for health equity-focused SMBP education campaigns targeting front-line clinicians at safety-net institutions, particularly addressing homeless and digitally disconnected populations who represent the highest-risk, most under-monitored hypertensive cohorts. The 19% clinic-level variance offers a tangible lever for KOL-led quality improvement programs, supporting the positioning of comprehensive hypertension management platforms — combining pharmacotherapy with structured blood pressure monitoring — as essential tools for equitable cardiovascular outcomes.

The Medium and Long-Term Effects of COVID-19 on the Non-Dipper Blood Pressure Pattern.

Revue : Cardiovascular Journal of Africa | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-03-13

Resultats principaux : This single-centre retrospective study from Ankara, Turkey evaluated ambulatory blood pressure monitoring (ABPM) data collected between January 2023 and February 2024 in 50 COVID-19-positive and 42 COVID-19-negative patients originally recruited during the early pandemic (January 2020 – March 2021). The primary endpoint was the prevalence of the non-dipper BP pattern, characterised by a nocturnal systolic BP dip of less than 10%, which is associated with increased cardiovascular and renal end-organ risk. COVID-19-positive patients demonstrated significantly lower daytime systolic BP (121.62 +/- 11.65 vs. 133.36 +/- 15.56 mmHg; p less than 0.001) and nighttime systolic BP (109.3 +/- 11.00 vs. 122.86 +/- 14.96 mmHg; p less than 0.001), with a markedly higher prevalence of the non-dipper pattern (36.0% vs. 11.9%; p = 0.008). Subgroup analysis was not reported, and hypertension was present in similar proportions in both groups (36.0% COVID-19-positive vs. 38.1% COVID-19-negative), suggesting that the non-dipper effect was not explained by differential hypertension prevalence at baseline. Critical limitations include the small sample size in both groups, the retrospective observational design with significant selection bias risk, absence of data on COVID-19 severity and treatments received, and the inability to exclude pre-existing non-dipper patterns prior to COVID-19 infection.

Implications Medical Affairs : These findings identify COVID-19 survivors as a distinct, previously under-recognised hypertension phenotype characterised by the non-dipper pattern, suggesting that long-term ABPM surveillance should be integrated into post-COVID care pathways — a new patient population for antihypertensive market development. From a Medical Affairs perspective, the non-dipper phenotype may respond differentially to chronotherapeutic dosing strategies (bedtime versus morning administration), providing a scientific platform to engage cardiologists and internists on personalised antihypertensive management for post-COVID patients. This study provides the rationale for larger, properly controlled longitudinal investigations to confirm the durability of post-COVID non-dipper patterns and evaluate whether specific antihypertensive drug classes preferentially restore normal circadian BP rhythmicity in these patients, constituting a high-value future research priority.

Co-exposure to phthalates and their alternatives in early pregnancy and the subsequent risk of pregnancy complications: A nested case-control study.

Revue : Ecotoxicology and Environmental Safety | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-18

Resultats principaux : This nested case-control study conducted within a Chinese birth cohort enrolled pregnant women with gestational complications — including gestational hypertension (PIH), gestational diabetes mellitus (GDM), premature rupture of membranes, and others — matched to controls by fetal sex, maternal age, and sampling season, with urinary phthalate metabolites quantified in the first trimester. Conditional logistic regression demonstrated that higher tertiles of individual phthalate exposure were significantly associated with increased odds of GDM (ORs 1.40–1.76 and 1.38–2.98 for 2nd and 3rd tertiles), PIH (ORs 1.41–1.67 and 1.36–2.05), and insufficient amniotic fluid (ORs 2.17–3.17 and 1.97–2.54). In quantile g-computation models assessing mixture effects, combined phthalate exposure was associated with elevated ORs for GDM (OR 1.32, 95% CI 1.09–1.74), PIH (OR 1.49, 95% CI 1.04–1.78), and IAF (OR 1.73, 95% CI 1.06–2.47), with MBP and DEHP metabolites identified as the dominant contributors to joint effects. Associations varied by fetal sex, indicating potential sex-specific susceptibility windows for phthalate-induced gestational hypertension. Key limitations include reliance on spot urine samples that capture short-term exposure variability, the single-country context limiting generalisability, and the absence of data on other concurrent environmental exposures that may confound the observed phthalate-PIH association.

Implications Medical Affairs : The demonstration that prenatal phthalate exposure — a pervasive endocrine-disrupting environmental factor — substantially increases gestational hypertension risk (OR 1.49) positions environmental toxicology as a new preventive frontier in hypertension management, broadening Medical Affairs communications beyond pharmacological strategies to encompass environmental risk reduction during pregnancy. These findings support interdisciplinary KOL engagement with maternal-fetal medicine specialists, toxicologists, and public health researchers to articulate environmental exposure burden as a modifiable hypertension risk factor complementary to traditional pharmacological approaches in pregnant populations. From a research investment perspective, this study provides the rationale for investigating whether antihypertensive pharmacotherapy selection or dosing requires adjustment based on environmental exposure profiles in pregnant populations, particularly in regions with high phthalate exposure burden.

Job strain and ischemic heart disease: the balance of methodological bias and implications for prevention.

Revue : Scandinavian Journal of Work, Environment and Health | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-18

Resultats principaux : This letter-to-the-editor by Lavigne-Robichaud et al. critically appraises the methodology of a recent systematic review by Bonde et al. on job strain and ischemic heart disease (IHD) risk, arguing that the published pooled relative risk estimate (RRE) of 1.14 substantially underestimates the true effect due to multiple identified sources of downward bias. The authors identify nondifferential exposure misclassification, overadjustment for intermediate cardiometabolic risk factors including hypertension, and collapsing of passive and active non-high-strain exposures into a single reference category as primary drivers of underestimation in existing meta-analyses. The widely cited IPD-Work Consortium population attributable fraction (PAF) of 3.4% for job strain on IHD is argued to be a significant underestimate; a prospective cohort study specifically designed to address these methodological limitations estimated a substantially higher PAF of 18.2% of incident IHD attributable to job strain. Sex-related differences further attenuate pooled estimates as women develop IHD at older ages relative to the working-age follow-up period, and the use of job-exposure matrices introduces additional non-differential misclassification that biases estimates toward the null. The publication is a commentary rather than original research, limiting its ability to resolve the methodological debate; it does not provide primary data reanalysis and relies on critical appraisal of existing meta-analytic literature.

Implications Medical Affairs : A contested PAF of psychosocial workplace stress on IHD — potentially as high as 18.2% — represents a substantial population-level cardiovascular burden extending well beyond conventional hypertension risk factors, with meaningful implications for how Medical Affairs frames cardiovascular risk reduction in occupational health and corporate wellness settings. Antihypertensive products used in working-age adults with job stress-related hypertension can be repositioned within broader cardiovascular risk reduction narratives resonating with occupational health physicians and employers, leveraging psychosocial risk factor data to support early treatment initiation conversations. Future outcomes research incorporating validated job strain exposure measures alongside antihypertensive treatment adherence and blood pressure response data would meaningfully strengthen the Medical Affairs evidence base and support KOL advocacy for psychosocial screening tools embedded in hypertension disease management programs.

Combined AGT M235T-ACE I/D genotype and risk of essential hypertension in Vietnamese adults: a hospital-based case-control study.

Revue : BMC Cardiovascular Disorders | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-17

Resultats principaux : This hospital-based case-control study enrolled 471 Vietnamese Kinh adults — 238 with essential hypertension and 233 normotensive controls — at the University Medical Center in Ho Chi Minh City, Vietnam, to investigate the combined contribution of renin-angiotensin system (RAS) gene polymorphisms to hypertension susceptibility in a Southeast Asian population. The primary exposure was the combined AGT M235T-ACE I/D TT/DD genotype, identified in 15.55% of hypertensive cases versus 6.44% of controls, conferring an adjusted odds ratio of 3.26 (95% CI 1.53–6.92, p = 0.002) in multivariable logistic regression controlling for age, sex, obesity, diabetes, high-salt diet, smoking, alcohol misuse, physical inactivity, and family history. In an exploratory six-category genotype model using (MM+MT)/II as the reference, the TT/DD combined genotype was the only category significantly associated with hypertension (adjusted OR 3.46, 95% CI 1.24–9.70, p = 0.018), suggesting a synergistic gene-gene interaction effect beyond individual polymorphisms. The findings are particularly noteworthy given the chronic underrepresentation of Southeast Asian populations in genetic epidemiology studies of hypertension, and the dual RAS polymorphism potentially implicating pharmacogenomic sensitivity to both ACE inhibitors and ARBs in this high-risk group. Substantial limitations include the hospital-based design introducing selection bias, lack of replication in external independent cohorts, imprecise genotype effect estimates driven by small subgroup numbers, and absence of functional genomic data elucidating the biological mechanism underlying the interaction.

Implications Medical Affairs : The identification of a dual RAS polymorphism more than tripling hypertension risk in Vietnamese adults highlights clinically significant pharmacogenomic implications for ACE inhibitor and ARB responsiveness in Southeast Asian populations — an area where current clinical evidence remains limited and where Medical Affairs could pioneer targeted real-world outcomes research. These findings create a compelling basis for engaging cardiogenomics KOLs and supporting regional pharmacogenomics studies in Southeast Asian markets to evaluate whether TT/DD carriers demonstrate differential blood pressure responses to RAS-targeting antihypertensive therapies. For product positioning, demonstrating antihypertensive efficacy and safety in genetically defined high-risk Asian populations would significantly differentiate therapies in rapidly growing emerging markets where this combined polymorphism prevalence may carry substantial population-level clinical significance.

Peri-operative management of a child with catecholamine-secreting neuroblastoma and Dravet syndrome undergoing tumour resection.

Revue : Anaesthesia Reports | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-16

Resultats principaux : This case report from Madrid, Spain describes the complex perioperative management of a 6-year-old child diagnosed with a catecholamine-secreting presacral neuroblastoma combined with Dravet syndrome — a rare and demanding simultaneous combination of catecholamine-mediated haemodynamic instability and refractory epilepsy requiring a highly tailored anaesthetic approach. Pre-operative stabilisation utilised amlodipine (a calcium channel blocker), stepwise alpha-blockade with phenoxybenzamine followed by atenolol for beta-blockade, and volume expansion to manage catecholamine-induced systemic hypertension and reduce the risk of intraoperative haemodynamic crises. Intraoperatively, severe hypertensive crises occurred during tumour manipulation, complicated sequentially by preload-related hypotension after prone positioning and arterial bleeding-induced hypotension, underscoring the extreme and bidirectional haemodynamic volatility of catecholamine-secreting paediatric tumours. Antihypertensive therapy was required for several months postoperatively before successful discontinuation, and the child had returned to normal lifestyle 18 months after surgery, confirming that catecholamine-driven hypertension is reversible upon complete tumour resection. The primary limitation is the intrinsic lack of generalisability from a single paediatric case report, and the absence of standardised evidence-based protocols for perioperative antihypertensive management in catecholamine-secreting neuroblastoma specifically limits the broader clinical recommendations that can be derived.

Implications Medical Affairs : This case reinforces the established role of combined calcium channel blockers and alpha/beta-adrenergic blockade in managing extreme secondary hypertensive emergencies of catecholamine-driven aetiology, demonstrating the therapeutic flexibility of these antihypertensive classes across rare paediatric indications relevant to Medical Affairs scientific platform materials. From a strategic perspective, this report highlights an opportunity for Medical Affairs to support physician education on perioperative hypertension protocols in rare secondary causes, particularly within paediatric oncology — a niche but scientifically credible arena for KOL engagement at the intersection of cardiology and paediatric anaesthesiology. Although a single case, it contributes to the sparse evidence base for managing paediatric hypertensive crises and provides the scientific justification for supporting multicentre registry initiatives or consensus guideline development for antihypertensive management in secondary paediatric hypertension.

Benefits Beyond Hypertension Control: Current and Evolving Treatment Strategies for Prevention of Cardiovascular Diseases.

Revue : Current Hypertension Reviews | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-15

Resultats principaux : This narrative review, authored by a multidisciplinary panel of Indian clinicians and pharmaceutical medical affairs professionals, synthesised 101 publications from 2004 to 2025 — including 20 systematic reviews and meta-analyses, 27 trials, 24 research articles, 10 guidelines, 18 review articles, and 2 editorials — to characterise the cardiovascular disease (CVD) continuum originating from uncontrolled hypertension and evaluate pharmacological and non-pharmacological management strategies. Hypertension affects over one billion people globally, with fewer than half diagnosed and receiving treatment, and suboptimal management is particularly prevalent in low- and middle-income countries including India, a major unmet medical need. Guideline recommendations favour initial monotherapy at BP thresholds of 130–140/80–90 mmHg using ACE inhibitors, ARBs, calcium channel blockers, diuretics, or beta-blockers, with single-pill combination therapy of two or more complementary agents increasingly preferred for adherence improvement. Importantly, beta-blockers are identified as inferior to other first-line antihypertensive drug classes for reducing stroke, myocardial infarction, and all-cause mortality, while emerging agents — including non-steroidal mineralocorticoid receptor antagonists, endothelin receptor antagonists, and anti-aldosterone drugs — demonstrate meaningful efficacy and acceptable safety profiles particularly in resistant hypertension. The review is limited by its non-systematic methodology, potential author bias given pharmaceutical industry co-authorship, heterogeneity of incorporated studies, and the absence of formal quality assessment of the underlying evidence base.

Implications Medical Affairs : The explicit mapping of the full CVD continuum — from uncontrolled hypertension through atherosclerosis to myocardial infarction, stroke, and cardiovascular death — provides Medical Affairs with a structured, evidence-anchored scientific narrative to position antihypertensive therapies as disease-modifying interventions rather than symptomatic blood pressure control tools. The documented inferiority of beta-blockers for CV outcomes compared to other first-line agents creates a clear competitive differentiation opportunity for ACE inhibitor, ARB, and CCB manufacturers in markets where beta-blocker prescribing remains prevalent, particularly across South and Southeast Asia. For KOL strategy and pipeline investment, the review spotlights non-steroidal MRAs and endothelin receptor antagonists as priority evidence-generation areas in resistant hypertension — the most treatment-refractory, highest-risk, and commercially underserved hypertensive patient segment.

Explainable and interpretable models for predicting early-onset hypertension in the Tlalpan 2020 cohort.

Revue : Frontiers in Digital Health | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-02

Resultats principaux : This nested case-control study, derived from the Tlalpan 2020 prospective cohort of clinically healthy adults followed over 10 years in Mexico City, applied DSRegPSOP — a novel symbolic regression algorithm — to develop transparent interpretable mathematical models predicting early-onset hypertension from demographic, clinical, biochemical, lifestyle, and sleep-related variables. Addressing class imbalance through oversampling and SMOTE-based strategies, the models achieved predictive performance — measured by accuracy, sensitivity, specificity, F1-score, and AUC-ROC — comparable to state-of-the-art black-box machine learning algorithms while generating compact, clinically interpretable mathematical expressions. Clinically meaningful predictors of early-onset hypertension identified included energy drink consumption, anxiety, family history of hypertension, high-fat diet, and sedentary lifestyle, representing a convergence of psychosocial and modifiable lifestyle risk factors amenable to targeted prevention. The cohort's Latin American urban composition provides epidemiologically relevant context for hypertension risk modelling in a high-burden, understudied geographic and demographic region. Primary limitations include the absence of external validation on independent cohorts beyond the Tlalpan 2020 cohort, the single-country design restricting generalisability, and the inherent limitations of case-control designs in establishing the precise temporality of risk factor exposures preceding hypertension onset.

Implications Medical Affairs : The identification of energy drink consumption, anxiety, and sedentary lifestyle as key early-onset hypertension predictors provides Medical Affairs with scientifically grounded, actionable risk stratification targets that align with youth-directed cardiovascular prevention campaigns and expand the hypertension scientific narrative beyond traditional dietary sodium and weight messaging. Explainable AI models applied to hypertension risk prediction represent an emerging clinical decision support category in which pharmaceutical companies can demonstrate health ecosystem value by co-developing or endorsing validated risk scoring tools that funnel high-risk patients toward earlier antihypertensive intervention — directly supporting treatment initiation conversations in primary care. For KOL strategy, this study creates high-value engagement opportunities at the intersection of preventive cardiology, digital health, and epidemiology, particularly for building Medical Affairs presence in Latin American scientific communities and positioning companies as leaders in early hypertension identification through algorithmic screening programs.

Arginine vasopressin: A promising therapeutic target for metabolic syndrome.

Revue : The Journal of Pharmacology and Experimental Therapeutics | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-05-08

Resultats principaux : This narrative review comprehensively examined the accumulating evidence linking arginine vasopressin (AVP) to metabolic syndrome (MetS) and its core components — including hypertension, insulin resistance, dyslipidemia, and visceral obesity — which collectively affect approximately 20–25% of adults globally and carry substantially elevated cardiovascular morbidity and mortality. Epidemiological data reviewed demonstrate that copeptin, the stable C-terminal fragment of the AVP precursor and its validated clinical surrogate, correlates significantly with MetS prevalence and its individual components across multiple large population-based cohort studies. Experimental evidence from animal models and cell-based mechanistic studies supports causal relationships between AVP signalling through V1a, V1b, and V2 receptors and key pathophysiological processes underlying hypertension, insulin resistance, and visceral fat accumulation central to MetS. The review proposes that selective V1a receptor antagonism could simultaneously address multiple MetS components including hypertension, representing a mechanistically distinct therapeutic target compared to current single-component pharmacological strategies. The major limitation is the predominance of preclinical and observational epidemiological data, with a notable paucity of interventional clinical trial evidence in humans, rendering the therapeutic implications speculative and requiring controlled proof-of-concept studies before any practice change can be considered.

Implications Medical Affairs : Positioning AVP and copeptin simultaneously as a cardiometabolic biomarker and therapeutic target creates a novel translational framework bridging hypertension, diabetes, and obesity management — three converging disease areas where integrated Medical Affairs messaging can resonate with endocrinologists, cardiologists, and metabolic disease KOLs managing the full cardiometabolic patient profile. The absence of clinical trials on AVP-targeted therapy in MetS represents a first-mover opportunity for pharmaceutical companies to invest in proof-of-concept Phase II studies that could establish competitive market leadership in a mechanistically differentiated cardiometabolic drug class with a strong preclinical rationale. Targeted engagement with cardiometabolic and nephrology KOLs around copeptin as a precision biomarker for identifying vasopressin-driven hypertensive patients could accelerate clinical translation and support compelling pipeline differentiation strategies for companies with V1a receptor-targeting assets in development.
