Secular trends in society-, school-, and individual-level factors related to physical activity and their associations with elevated blood pressure among 0.6 million children and adolescents.

Revue : Chinese Medical Journal | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2025-11-26

Resultats principaux : This large-scale cross-sectional study analyzed 642,386 Chinese children and adolescents aged 7 to 18 years across three national survey cycles from 2010, 2014, and 2019 using a multistage stratified cluster sampling design (Chinese National Survey on Students Constitution and Health). The primary endpoint was prevalence of elevated blood pressure (EBP) defined by updated Chinese age- and sex-specific reference standards, with secondary analyses examining multilevel ecological factors at society, school, and individual levels using multilevel logistic regression and population-attributable fractions. EBP prevalence rose significantly from 13.6% in 2010 to 17.9% in 2019, while paradoxically the prevalence of inadequate physical activity (defined as less than 1 hour per day) decreased over the same period, suggesting a decoupling of individual-level PA behaviors from EBP trajectories. In 2010, inadequate PA was significantly associated with EBP with an odds ratio of 1.17 (95% CI: 1.04-1.21), but this association attenuated substantially from 2010 to 2019 as obesity emerged as the dominant individual-level driver. Society-level factors such as lack of exercise facilities increased EBP risk while urban residence was protective; school-level deficits in physical education and sports competitions combined with unhealthy dietary habits at the individual level also contributed to EBP, with societal and school-level ecological factors demonstrating increasing relative contributions to EBP over time, independent of obesity. The study is limited by its cross-sectional design, which precludes causal inference, and by the self-reported nature of PA and dietary data collected via questionnaire, with potential recall and social desirability biases.

Implications Medical Affairs : This study challenges the prevailing paradigm that individual-level physical inactivity alone drives pediatric hypertension, demonstrating that upstream societal and school-level environmental factors are gaining relative importance over the decade studied — a finding that may reframe public health guidance and inform how pharmaceutical companies align product strategies with lifestyle modification pathways in pediatric and young-adult populations. For a pharmaceutical company operating in the hypertension space, these secular trends documenting a rise in EBP prevalence from 13.6% to 17.9% over nine years highlight a growing future patient burden as today's hypertensive children become tomorrow's at-risk adults, reinforcing the long-term market relevance of antihypertensive portfolios and supporting the case for early prevention and disease awareness partnerships with public health agencies. Medical Affairs teams should leverage these data to engage KOLs across adolescent medicine, public health, and school health policy disciplines, positioning the company as a science leader in early-life cardiovascular risk — an increasingly strategic space as global pediatric hypertension guidelines evolve toward broader screening recommendations.

Dyslipidemia in Saudi Arabia: A Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis of Population-Based Studies (2000-2025).

Revue : Journal of Epidemiology and Global Health | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-06

Resultats principaux : This systematic review and meta-analysis synthesized data from 27 observational studies published between January 2000 and March 2025 examining dyslipidemia in Saudi Arabia, following the PRISMA 2020 framework across six databases (PubMed, Scopus, Web of Science, Embase, Cochrane Library, Google Scholar), with quality assessment using the Joanna Briggs Institute checklist for prevalence studies. Among the 12 studies eligible for meta-analysis, the pooled prevalence of dyslipidemia was 43.0% (95% CI: 41.8-44.1%) with considerable heterogeneity (I squared = 97.2%), while individual study estimates ranged widely from 20.3% to 82.4%, reflecting variations in diagnostic criteria, age groups, and study populations across the 25-year period. Secondary narrative synthesis revealed alarmingly low rates along the care continuum: awareness of dyslipidemia was as low as 19%, treatment coverage was below 30%, and lipid control was typically achieved in less than 20% of diagnosed individuals, indicating a massive care gap. Key cardiovascular risk factors statistically associated with dyslipidemia in cross-sectional analyses included obesity, diabetes, hypertension, older age, male sex, and physical inactivity; adolescents and young adults also showed elevated dyslipidemia burden in narrative-only studies, suggesting early onset of cardiometabolic risk. The study is limited by the high heterogeneity across included studies (I squared = 97.2%), the predominantly cross-sectional design of primary studies precluding causal inference, the absence of a prospectively registered protocol, and potential publication bias toward higher-prevalence estimates in population health surveys.

Implications Medical Affairs : The extremely high prevalence of dyslipidemia (43%) combined with profoundly low awareness (19%) and control rates (less than 20%) in Saudi Arabia defines a massive treatment gap in one of the highest-income, fastest-growing pharmaceutical markets in the Middle East — pharmaceutical companies with cardiometabolic or lipid-lowering portfolios have compelling unmet-need positioning opportunities anchored to robust epidemiological evidence. The strong epidemiological comorbidity linkage between dyslipidemia and hypertension in this population directly supports cross-portfolio medical strategy, enabling Medical Affairs to position combination cardiovascular risk reduction approaches — simultaneously addressing lipid disorders and blood pressure — with KOLs managing hypertensive patients at elevated cardiovascular risk. Medical Affairs should leverage these meta-analytic data to engage the Saudi Food and Drug Authority, clinical cardiology and endocrinology societies, and regional KOLs in advocacy for national dyslipidemia screening programs and updated treatment guidelines, building scientific leadership while simultaneously expanding market access and brand recognition in the Gulf Cooperation Council region.

Total Care Strategies in Atrial Fibrillation Practice: Lifestyle Intervention and Risk Factor Management.

Revue : Journal of Cardiology | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-05

Resultats principaux : This narrative review from the Jikei University School of Medicine in Tokyo synthesized current evidence on lifestyle interventions and cardiovascular risk factor management as integral components of total care strategies for atrial fibrillation (AF), covering a comprehensive range of modifiable risk factors including alcohol, caffeine, exercise, obesity, hypertension, diabetes, and obstructive sleep apnea. The review evaluates alcohol reduction evidence (strict limitation to three or fewer drinks per week reduces arrhythmia recurrence risk), moderate caffeine consumption evidence (recent trials confirm no increased AF risk, making routine restriction unnecessary), and aerobic exercise data (regular moderate exercise is beneficial, whereas extreme endurance training may paradoxically increase AF risk). Obesity is identified as a driver of atrial fibrosis through epicardial adipose tissue accumulation, with weight loss programs achieving at least a 10% body weight reduction shown to reverse atrial structural disease progression — a finding with significant implications for cardiometabolic therapeutic positioning. Hypertension is described as the most prevalent comorbid risk factor for AF, with strict blood pressure control demonstrated to significantly mitigate irreversible structural atrial remodeling; additionally, novel pharmacotherapies for diabetes including sodium-glucose cotransporter-2 inhibitors and glucagon-like peptide-1 receptor agonists are shown to provide substantial structural and electrophysiological benefits beyond glycemic control alone. Obstructive sleep apnea treated with continuous positive airway pressure is shown to improve success rates of both medical therapies and catheter ablation; the study is limited by its narrative review design, which lacks a systematic search methodology and is subject to author selection and interpretation bias, and no quantitative effect sizes are reported for most interventions reviewed.

Implications Medical Affairs : This review explicitly positions hypertension as the single most prevalent and structurally impactful risk factor in atrial fibrillation — creating a high-value Medical Affairs opportunity to extend antihypertensive product messaging beyond blood pressure reduction to encompass atrial structural cardioprotection, atrial remodeling prevention, and AF risk mitigation, particularly with cardiologist and electrophysiologist audiences. The inclusion of SGLT2 inhibitors and GLP-1 receptor agonists as providing meaningful structural AF benefits expands the cardiometabolic competitive landscape and opens dialogue for Medical Affairs teams with cardiometabolic portfolios to engage electrophysiology KOLs, positioning these agents as structural cardiac protectors within the rapidly growing AF management space. For KOL strategy, Medical Affairs should integrate this total care evidence base into symposia, advisory boards, and CME programs targeting cardiologists, electrophysiologists, and hypertension specialists, emphasizing the company's scientific leadership at the intersection of hypertension, metabolic disease, and arrhythmia — a convergence area increasingly reflected in international cardiology guidelines.
