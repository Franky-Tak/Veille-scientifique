Correlation analysis between cumulative blood pressure load and arterial stiffness in midlife: a 36-year follow-up study

Revue : Zhonghua Xin Xue Guan Bing Za Zhi | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-24

Resultats principaux : The Hanzhong Children's Hypertension Cohort (Shaanxi, China) enrolled 4,623 children aged 6-18 years in 1987, with 1,845 adults completing 36-year longitudinal follow-up through 7 assessments; arteriosclerosis was detected in 27.4% (n=506) of participants at the final follow-up at a median age of 49 years. Multivariate logistic regression revealed a clear dose-response relationship between cumulative diastolic blood pressure (DBP) load and arteriosclerosis risk, with the high-load group showing a 15.39-fold increased risk (OR=15.39, 95% CI: 9.34-25.37) relative to the no-load reference group. Cumulative mean arterial pressure load showed a similarly strong gradient (high-load OR=12.55, 95% CI: 8.28-19.02), and cumulative systolic BP load also increased risk progressively (high-load OR not as extreme but statistically significant). Restricted cubic spline analysis confirmed a non-linear positive correlation between all three cumulative BP load metrics and midlife arteriosclerosis, with associations remaining consistent across subgroups defined by sex, age, smoking, alcohol, BMI, and medication use. The study's major limitation is potential survival bias in a 36-year cohort, and cumulative BP load was calculated area-under-curve based, which may not fully capture episodic hypertensive spikes.

Implications Medical Affairs : This landmark 36-year prospective cohort demonstrates that cumulative diastolic blood pressure burden from childhood is the single strongest predictor of midlife arteriosclerosis, reinforcing guideline imperatives for early and sustained antihypertensive therapy targeting diastolic control. From a Medical Affairs perspective, this evidence supports positioning long-acting antihypertensives that achieve durable diastolic BP reduction as superior in long-term vascular outcomes, providing differentiation arguments for agents with proven sustained efficacy. The findings create compelling KOL engagement opportunities around childhood BP screening programs and longitudinal BP monitoring, which could shape future guideline revisions and expand the market rationale for early antihypertensive intervention.

Effects of CPAP therapy on blood pressure and vascular endothelial function in hypertensive patients with moderate-to-severe obstructive sleep apnea

Revue : Zhonghua Xin Xue Guan Bing Za Zhi | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-24

Resultats principaux : This retrospective study enrolled 109 hypertensive patients with moderate-to-severe obstructive sleep apnea (OSA) from Tianjin Kanghui Hospital (October 2023-December 2024), comparing 54 patients receiving CPAP therapy (>=4h/night, >=5 nights/week) with 55 controls receiving only standard antihypertensive medication over 24 weeks. CPAP therapy significantly improved brachial artery flow-mediated dilation (FMD) compared to controls (7.15+/-1.15% vs. 4.08+/-1.46%, p<0.001), indicating meaningful restoration of vascular endothelial function. Ambulatory blood pressure monitoring demonstrated significantly lower mean 24-hour systolic (120.96+/-5.27 vs. 124.71+/-3.15 mmHg) and diastolic BP (77.36+/-6.99 vs. 81.60+/-3.67 mmHg) in the CPAP group, along with significantly greater nighttime blood pressure dipping ratios. The defined daily dose of antihypertensive drugs was significantly lower in the CPAP group (1.60+/-0.86 vs. 2.26+/-0.87, p<0.001), suggesting CPAP therapy reduces the pharmacological burden for BP control. Study limitations include the retrospective design, single-center setting, relatively short follow-up of 24 weeks, and the predominantly male cohort (86%), limiting generalizability to female OSA-hypertension patients.

Implications Medical Affairs : This study demonstrates that treating comorbid sleep apnea with CPAP not only improves blood pressure control but also measurably reduces antihypertensive drug burden, establishing OSA as an important modifiable contributor to resistant hypertension that should be systematically screened in guideline-recommended hypertension workups. From a Medical Affairs perspective, this evidence supports co-promotion strategies between cardiovascular and respiratory therapeutic areas, particularly positioning antihypertensives that synergize with CPAP therapy in OSA-related hypertension management. KOL engagement should emphasize restoration of the nocturnal blood pressure dipping pattern as a clinically meaningful endpoint, potentially supporting label expansion strategies for antihypertensives specifically validated in OSA-comorbid hypertension.

Reliability and device objectivity in oscillatory blood pressure measurement: a measurement error analysis to inform clinical decision making

Revue : BMC Cardiovascular Disorders | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-14

Resultats principaux : Sixty-five participants completed four laboratory visits each with standardized and un-standardized blood pressure measurements using two certified devices (Boso Medicus and Welch Allyn) across two testing days, enabling systematic quantification of intra-day, inter-day, and inter-device measurement errors. Un-standardized measurements with insufficient pre-measurement rest caused clinically relevant systematic BP elevations of approximately 10 mmHg in both devices, which resolved within a standardized 10-18 minute rest period. The Boso Medicus device consistently measured higher BP values by approximately 13 mmHg compared to the Welch Allyn monitor, a clinically significant inter-device discrepancy that could lead to false-positive hypertension diagnoses based on device alone. Although measurements were reproducible inter-day within each device, substantial random measurement errors were observed, particularly when pre-measurement rest was insufficient. These findings underscore the necessity of multiple BP assessments across different time points and standardized conditions to reduce the risk of diagnostic misclassification and unnecessary treatment escalation.

Implications Medical Affairs : This rigorous measurement error analysis directly challenges the validity of single-reading hypertension diagnoses and highlights systemic inter-device variability that can artificially inflate hypertension prevalence statistics cited in clinical guidelines and epidemiological reports. From a Medical Affairs perspective, these findings support advocating for multi-measurement standardized BP protocols in clinical practice guidelines, which could influence trial design requirements for antihypertensive efficacy endpoints and regulatory submission standards for BP-lowering drugs. Companies with digital health or validated ambulatory BP monitoring solutions may find this data particularly valuable for KOL discussions around the clinical superiority of home BP monitoring and 24-hour ABPM in confirming diagnoses before initiating pharmacotherapy.

Blood Pressure Variability is Associated With Lower Cognitive Function: An Elderly Cohort Study in Hong Kong

Revue : Journal of Clinical Hypertension | Tier : Tier 2 | IF estime : 10-20

Date de publication : 2026-07

Resultats principaux : This prospective cohort study included 573 community-dwelling older adults in Hong Kong (mean age 72 years, 96.2% women) with a mean follow-up of approximately 8 months and a mean of 19 blood pressure measurements per participant, with cognitive function assessed using the Montreal Cognitive Assessment (MoCA). Higher systolic blood pressure variability (BPV), defined as standard deviation of multiple readings categorized by machine learning, was independently associated with increased risk of mild cognitive impairment (MCI) after full adjustment (OR=1.16; 95% CI: 1.03-1.31 per unit increase in systolic BPV). Participants classified as high BPV showed a 5.6-fold higher risk of MCI compared to the low BPV group, with the association being most pronounced among those with lower baseline cognitive levels. Diastolic BPV was not significantly associated with MCI, suggesting systolic variability is the primary driver of cognitive risk in this population. Key limitations include the predominantly female cohort (96.2%), the relatively short follow-up period, and the cross-sectional nature of cognitive assessment limiting causal inference.

Implications Medical Affairs : This study adds compelling prospective evidence that systolic blood pressure variability, independent of mean BP levels, constitutes an independent determinant of cognitive decline in older hypertensive patients, with direct implications for the selection of antihypertensive regimens that minimize BP fluctuations. From a Medical Affairs perspective, agents demonstrating superior reduction in blood pressure variability, particularly long-acting calcium channel blockers and angiotensin receptor blockers, may gain a clinically differentiated positioning narrative around cognitive protection in aging hypertensive populations. This represents a strategic KOL engagement opportunity around blood pressure smoothing as a therapeutic goal, potentially driving future investigator-initiated studies on BPV monitoring as a routine outcome metric in hypertension management guidelines.

Evaluation of the Molecular Docking of Potential Targets and the Time-Dependent Myocardial Effects of Omeprazole in Normotensive and Spontaneously Hypertensive Rats Subjected to Cardiac Ischemia and Reperfusion

Revue : International Journal of Molecular Sciences | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-30

Resultats principaux : This experimental study compared the cardiac effects of intravenous omeprazole (10 mg/kg) administered either before cardiac ischemia or before reperfusion in a cardiac ischemia-reperfusion model using 12-16 week-old male normotensive Wistar rats (NWR) and spontaneously hypertensive rats (SHR), with groups including SHAM, saline-control, pre-ischemia omeprazole, and pre-reperfusion omeprazole. Administration of omeprazole prior to cardiac ischemia significantly increased the incidence of ventricular arrhythmias, atrioventricular block, lethality, and serum CK-MB levels in both NWR and SHR compared to controls. In contrast, administration of omeprazole immediately before cardiac reperfusion did not exacerbate cardiac injury and was associated with attenuation of electrophysiological instability across both rat strains. Molecular docking analysis identified potential cardiac targets mediating these time-dependent effects, suggesting mechanistic pathways involving endothelial dysfunction and nitric oxide bioavailability. Histopathological findings corroborated the biochemical outcomes, with worse myocardial injury scores in the pre-ischemia omeprazole group; study limitations include the animal model design and inability to directly translate drug kinetics to human clinical settings.

Implications Medical Affairs : This preclinical study raises pharmacovigilance signals regarding the timing of proton pump inhibitor (PPI) use in acute coronary syndrome patients, a clinically relevant concern as PPIs are routinely co-prescribed with antihypertensives and antiplatelet agents in high-cardiovascular-risk populations. From a Medical Affairs perspective, these findings could inform prescribing guidance for gastric acid management in perioperative cardiac settings, reinforcing the importance of post-ischemia rather than pre-ischemia PPI administration timing when reperfusion therapy is planned. Engagement with cardiology and gastroenterology KOLs around drug-drug interaction awareness and safety labeling discussions would be warranted to translate these animal model findings into clinical risk communications and potential label updates for PPIs prescribed to high-cardiovascular-risk patients.

Dietary Determinants of Mental Well-Being Among Cardiometabolic High-Risk Adults in Hungary

Revue : Nutrients | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-26

Resultats principaux : This cross-sectional analysis used data from the European Health Interview Survey (EHIS) 2019 and included 2,785 Hungarian adults with at least one cardiometabolic risk factor (obesity, hypertension, or hypercholesterolemia), assessing mental well-being with the WHO-5 Well-Being Index. Overall, 25.9% of participants had poor mental health (WHO-5 score <=50), representing a substantial psychological burden in this cardiometabolic high-risk population. Weighted multivariable logistic regression showed that low vegetable intake (OR=1.15), low fruit intake (OR=1.55), low fruit juice intake (OR=1.26), low fish intake (OR=1.17), and inadequate water intake (OR=1.38) were each independently associated with higher odds of poor mental health after adjustment for sex, education, income, physical activity, and alcohol use. The findings highlight a consistent pattern in which poor dietary quality across multiple food categories compounds poor mental well-being in patients already burdened by cardiometabolic conditions. Study limitations include the cross-sectional design precluding causal inference, reliance on self-reported dietary data subject to recall bias, and the single-country setting limiting generalizability.

Implications Medical Affairs : These findings reinforce the concept of integrated cardiometabolic and mental health management, demonstrating that dietary intervention programs targeting hypertension and other cardiometabolic conditions may simultaneously yield meaningful mental health benefits in high-risk populations. From a Medical Affairs perspective, this data supports the development of comprehensive patient support programs that combine antihypertensive pharmacotherapy with structured dietary counseling, potentially improving patient adherence, quality of life, and overall cardiovascular risk reduction simultaneously. KOL engagement opportunities exist at the intersection of cardiovascular prevention and mental health, which may inform future guideline revisions incorporating mental well-being assessment as a secondary treatment target in hypertension management, particularly in Central and Eastern European populations.

Does hydrochlorothiazide-associated hyponatremia in hypertensive individuals have a genetic origin?

Revue : Clinical and Experimental Hypertension | Tier : Tier 2 | IF estime : 10-20

Date de publication : 2026-07-15

Resultats principaux : This study enrolled 95 hypertensive patients from Tekirdag Namik Kemal University nephrology clinic who received HCTZ-containing antihypertensive therapy for at least one month, comparing 62 hyponatremic (serum sodium <135 mEq/L) with 33 non-hyponatremic individuals; next-generation sequencing was performed on the SLC12A3 gene. A total of 947 variants were identified in the SLC12A3 gene, the majority classified as variants of uncertain significance; however, several specific variants were strongly concentrated in hyponatremic patients. The c.282+492G>A variant was present in 85.7% of hyponatremic patients and conferred a 9.2-fold increased risk of hyponatremia (OR=9.243; 95% CI: 2.259-37.818; p=0.002), while c.282+499G>C was found in 87.0% and c.505+375G>A in 92.3% of hyponatremic patients. These findings suggest that SLC12A3 gene variants significantly predispose hypertensive patients to HCTZ-induced hyponatremia, and clinical and biochemical parameters alone are insufficient to predict this adverse event. Key limitations include the small sample size (n=95), single-center setting, variants of uncertain significance classification for most identified variants, and the cross-sectional nature that prevents longitudinal risk assessment.

Implications Medical Affairs : This pharmacogenomic study identifies SLC12A3 gene variants as potential predictive biomarkers for hydrochlorothiazide-induced hyponatremia, opening possibilities for pre-prescribing genetic screening to prevent a common and potentially serious adverse event with one of the world's most widely used antihypertensives. From a Medical Affairs perspective, this evidence supports the development of companion pharmacogenomic testing services for HCTZ prescribing, particularly in populations with known advanced age or female sex risk factors, differentiating companies that invest in precision diuretic therapy from those relying on empirical prescribing. These findings warrant multi-center prospective validation studies and engagement with regulatory authorities regarding the inclusion of SLC12A3 genotyping as a recommended pre-prescribing screening measure, potentially reshaping safety labeling for HCTZ-containing formulations.

Socioeconomic gradients in hypertension prevalence and management: a cross-sectional study

Revue : BMC Public Health | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-15

Resultats principaux : This nationwide cross-sectional study analyzed data from the 2023 Korea Community Health Survey, including 231,584 adults for educational attainment analyses and 228,608 for household income analyses; hypertension-related outcomes included diagnosed hypertension, pharmacotherapy, lifestyle modification, and self-management education. The overall prevalence of diagnosed hypertension was 31.3%, with a high pharmacotherapy rate of 95.9% among hypertensive patients, but only 23.8% engaging in lifestyle modification and 15.5% receiving self-management education. Multivariable logistic regression revealed a dose-response SES gradient: participants with primary school education or less had 1.79-fold higher odds of diagnosed hypertension (95% CI: 1.71-1.88) and 2.04-fold higher pharmacotherapy odds, but significantly lower engagement in lifestyle modification (OR=0.47; 95% CI: 0.43-0.50) and self-management education (OR=0.34; 95% CI: 0.31-0.38). Socioeconomic gradients were significantly steeper among females and younger adults under 65 years, highlighting sex- and age-specific disparities in hypertension management equity. Key limitations include the cross-sectional design precluding causal inference, reliance on self-reported hypertension diagnosis and management data, and potential residual confounding by unmeasured SES-related variables.

Implications Medical Affairs : This large national study demonstrates a critical public health gap: while pharmacotherapy reaches lower socioeconomic groups with hypertension at high rates, lifestyle modification and self-management education remain severely underutilized, limiting the full effectiveness of antihypertensive treatment in the most vulnerable populations. From a Medical Affairs perspective, these findings underscore the value of simplified treatment regimens, particularly once-daily fixed-dose combinations that reduce patient burden, alongside patient-centered digital support tools accessible to lower-SES hypertensive populations who are less likely to engage with traditional educational programs. KOL engagement should champion health equity in hypertension management, advocating for community-based education programs and simplified antihypertensive regimens that maximize adherence among socioeconomically disadvantaged patients.

Geographical disparities and spatial non-stationarity in stroke prevalence across China: a Bayesian analysis

Revue : BMC Neurology | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-15

Resultats principaux : This Bayesian spatially varying coefficient analysis used data from 19,713 adults across 28 provinces in the 4th China Health and Retirement Longitudinal Study (CHARLS 2018), standardized to the 2020 National Census for age, sex, and residence type distribution. Province-level stroke prevalence showed marked geographic disparities ranging from 1.89% (95% BCI: 0.92-3.61%) to 8.64% (95% BCI: 6.98-10.63%), with a distinct North-high, South-low spatial gradient and significant positive spatial autocorrelation (Moran's I=0.428, p<0.001). Hypertension showed the strongest and most geographically consistent association with stroke, with province-level odds ratios ranging from 2.39 to 3.38, surpassing diabetes, dyslipidemia, smoking, and physical inactivity in effect size. Local cluster analysis identified high-high clusters in Northeast and North China and low-low clusters in South and East China, corresponding to known regional disparities in cardiovascular risk factor prevalence. The marked spatial non-stationarity of risk factor associations highlights the inadequacy of uniform nationwide stroke prevention strategies, though the cross-sectional design and self-reported stroke diagnosis introduce limitations.

Implications Medical Affairs : This Bayesian spatial analysis provides granular provincial evidence that hypertension is the dominant modifiable risk factor for stroke across China, with its attributable burden varying geographically, strongly calling for region-specific hypertension control policies and targeted antihypertensive treatment programs in high-burden northern provinces. From a Medical Affairs perspective, the identification of high-burden geographic clusters offers concrete opportunities for precision market segmentation strategies and focused KOL development in provinces with the highest hypertension-attributable stroke burden in China. Partnerships with Chinese health authorities and hypertension professional societies to design region-tailored prevention programs could accelerate market penetration and formulary inclusion for antihypertensive portfolios in this strategically important market.

Ergothioneine attenuates cardiac fibrosis induced by a high-fat diet in spontaneously hypertensive rats

Revue : Journal of Nutritional Biochemistry | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-15

Resultats principaux : This experimental study evaluated ergothioneine (ERG, 50 mg/kg daily for 10 weeks) on high-fat diet (HFD)-induced cardiac fibrosis in spontaneously hypertensive rats (SHR), using fibrosis markers including hydroxyproline, alpha-smooth muscle actin, and galectin-3 as primary outcomes. ERG treatment significantly attenuated HFD-induced cardiac remodeling, reducing fibrosis marker levels and favorably modifying extracellular matrix composition by decreasing collagen I content and the collagen I/collagen III ratio. Mechanistically, ERG suppressed SMAD-2 expression, indicating inhibition of TGF-beta-driven profibrotic signaling, while upregulating mitochondrial antioxidant defense (Sod2) and reducing pro-oxidative Nox4 expression. ERG supplementation also prevented HFD-induced increases in the neutrophil-to-lymphocyte ratio, a validated biomarker of systemic stress and immune burden, while not significantly affecting HFD-induced metabolic hormone changes. Limitations include the preclinical animal model design, absence of comparison with standard antifibrotic pharmacotherapy, and the lack of functional cardiac endpoints such as echocardiographic parameters.

Implications Medical Affairs : This preclinical study provides mechanistic evidence that ergothioneine, a dietary antioxidant selectively accumulated in mammalian tissues, can attenuate obesity-amplified cardiac fibrosis in a hypertensive model by modulating TGF-beta signaling and oxidative stress, suggesting a potential adjunctive supplementation strategy for hypertensive patients with metabolic syndrome. From a Medical Affairs perspective, the TGF-beta/SMAD pathway emerges as a therapeutically relevant target in hypertension-related cardiac remodeling, and these findings may support the development or repositioning of anti-fibrotic agents as adjunctive therapy alongside standard antihypertensives in hypertensive heart disease populations. KOL engagement around nutraceutical-pharmacological combination strategies in hypertensive patients consuming Western-type diets could support future Phase II investigator-initiated trials of ergothioneine supplementation alongside antihypertensive therapy in patients with metabolic syndrome and early cardiac fibrosis.

Revue : JAMA Cardiology | Tier : Tier 1 | IF estime : >20

Date de publication : 2026-07-15

Resultats principaux : This systematic analysis from the Global Burden of Disease Study 2023 (GBD 2023) estimated ischemic heart disease (IHD) mortality attributable to 12 modifiable risk factors across all US states from 1990 to 2023, using vital records, Bayesian meta-regression, and the Burden of Proof framework with 95% uncertainty intervals. In 2023, 473,000 IHD deaths (95% UI: 414,000-510,000) were recorded in the US, representing a 58.7% decrease in age-standardized rate since 1990, with greater relative improvement in females (24.5% decrease 2010-2023) than males (19.0%). High systolic blood pressure was the leading modifiable risk factor for IHD death in 2023, accounting for 47.2% (95% UI: 36.4-57.0%) of IHD deaths, followed by dietary risks (38.6%; 95% UI: 17.2-56.8%) and high LDL-cholesterol (28.5%). High BMI attribution increased by 54.5% (95% UI: 41.8-66.3%) since 1990 and high fasting plasma glucose increased by 38.8%, while smoking and particulate matter pollution showed the greatest reductions. Overall, 88.7% of IHD deaths remained attributable to modifiable risk factors in 2023, with persistent state-level variability highlighting ongoing geographic disparities in cardiovascular risk management.

Implications Medical Affairs : This landmark GBD 2023 analysis in JAMA Cardiology establishes hypertension as the single largest modifiable contributor to ischemic heart disease mortality in the US, accounting for nearly half of all IHD deaths in 2023, providing compelling evidence for aggressive blood pressure control as the most impactful single cardiovascular prevention strategy available. From a Medical Affairs perspective, these high-impact data provide exceptional payer-facing value: a product demonstrating superior SBP reduction can be positioned as preventing a proportional share of the 88.7% of IHD deaths attributable to modifiable risk factors, strengthening formulary positioning and health-economic value dossiers. The state-level granularity of these findings creates opportunities for targeted KOL development and market access strategies in high-burden states, where region-specific hypertension control rates lag behind national averages.

Multiple 24-Hour Urinary Sodium and Potassium Excretions and Type 2 Diabetes Incidence

Revue : Diabetes Care | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-15

Resultats principaux : This prospective cohort study included 3,173 adults from three Harvard cohorts (mean age 62.2 +/- 10.0 years, 69% women) without major chronic diseases at baseline, with 2-4 twenty-four-hour urine collections per participant providing objective biomarker-based estimates of dietary sodium and potassium intake, and Cox proportional hazards models used for analysis. Over a median follow-up of 13.6 years, 161 participants (5.1%) developed type 2 diabetes; risk was 2.66-fold higher (95% CI: 1.57-4.51) in the highest versus lowest sodium excretion quartile. Limitations include the predominantly female, older, health-professional sample with potential residual confounding despite comprehensive covariate adjustment, and the relatively small event count limiting power for subgroup analyses.

Implications Medical Affairs : This large prospective study using objective urinary sodium biomarkers provides some of the strongest available evidence linking high dietary sodium intake to type 2 diabetes risk, extending sodium's established cardiovascular harms to glucose metabolism dysregulation and reinforcing the dual importance of sodium reduction for both hypertension and diabetes prevention. From a Medical Affairs perspective, this evidence supports positioning sodium restriction and low-sodium dietary programs as cornerstones of integrated cardiometabolic risk prevention, potentially complementing antihypertensive therapies prescribed to patients with both hypertension and diabetes risk. KOL engagement around the metabolic consequences of high dietary sodium beyond blood pressure can broaden the scientific narrative for antihypertensives, particularly RAAS blockers and diuretics also prescribed in metabolic syndrome contexts, potentially informing combination therapy guidelines.

Intradialytic Renal Rehabilitation and Mortality: Results from the Registry of Active Renal Rehabilitation in Dialysis Patients (REVEAL-D)

Revue : Clinical Journal of the American Society of Nephrology | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-10

Resultats principaux : The REVEAL-D registry study retrospectively evaluated 708 adult dialysis outpatients at 10 Japanese institutions over a 3-year follow-up, comparing 228 patients who received intradialytic renal rehabilitation (supervised exercise plus nutritional and lifestyle guidance >= 1 session/week for >= 1 month) with 480 controls, using Cox regression and propensity score matching. Three-year survival was significantly higher in the rehabilitation group (84.6% vs. 75.6%, p=0.007), with deaths in 14.9% of the rehabilitation group versus 23.1% of controls (p=0.01). Adjusted Cox regression showed a trend toward lower mortality with rehabilitation (adjusted HR=0.66; 95% CI: 0.44-1.00), remaining directionally consistent after propensity score matching (HR=0.65; 95% CI: 0.40-1.05). Sex-stratified analyses suggested stronger association in men (adjusted HR=0.49; 95% CI: 0.27-0.89) than women (adjusted HR=0.98; 95% CI: 0.54-1.77), though the interaction was not statistically significant (p=0.13). No adverse rehabilitation-related events were reported; limitations include the retrospective registry design, potential for residual confounding, and Japanese single-country setting limiting global generalizability.
