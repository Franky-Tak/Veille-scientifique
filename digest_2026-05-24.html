Relationship between hypertensive disorders of pregnancy and postpartum mood and anxiety symptoms: P4 study.

Revue : Archives of Women's Mental Health | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-05-23

Resultats principaux : This observational longitudinal cohort study recruited 314 women between 2013 and 2018 following singleton births and categorized them into normal blood pressure (n=238, 76%), gestational hypertension (n=17, 5%), and preeclampsia (n=59, 19%) groups. Primary endpoints included prevalence and severity of mood and anxiety symptoms at 5 years postpartum using the Edinburgh Postnatal Depression Scale (EPDS >12), the 7-item Generalized Anxiety Disorder Scale (GAD-7 ≥10), and the Posttraumatic Stress Diagnostic Scale (PDS-5 ≥28). Key results showed that 10.8% of women had mood or anxiety symptoms at 5 years postpartum, with no significant difference between preeclampsia and normotensive groups (10.2% vs. 10.1%, p=0.20); however, preeclamptic women were significantly more likely to recall childbirth as traumatic (5.5% vs. 0.4%, p=0.01). Longitudinal analysis revealed a significant increase in symptom prevalence over time (from 4.5% at 6 months to 5.4% at 2 years to 10.8% at 5 years, p<0.001), with prior mental illness history and significant recent life stressors being the strongest predictors (p<0.001 and p=0.01, respectively). Notable limitations include the small gestational hypertension subgroup (n=17), potential selection and attrition bias, and reliance on self-reported survey instruments rather than clinician-confirmed psychiatric diagnoses.

Implications Medical Affairs : These findings do not establish hypertensive disorders of pregnancy as an independent long-term risk factor for postpartum psychiatric morbidity, which may redirect clinical screening protocols toward universal psychosocial risk assessment rather than HDP-specific surveillance, with implications for guideline updates on postpartum care pathways. From a Medical Affairs perspective, these results underscore the importance of comprehensive maternal mental health screening beyond obstetric complications, representing an opportunity to support guidelines that incorporate prior psychiatric history and life stressors as primary triggers for structured postpartum follow-up. The findings provide an actionable insight for KOL engagement around integrated maternal cardiovascular-mental health programs, positioning evidence-based psychosocial risk stratification tools as drivers for patient support initiatives and interdisciplinary advocacy.

Metabolic score for insulin resistance and cancer mortality: Effect modification by hypertension in a nationwide cohort study.

Revue : Medicine (Baltimore) | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-05-22

Resultats principaux : This retrospective cohort study analyzed 17,347 US adults from the National Health and Nutrition Examination Survey (NHANES) 1999-2018, with follow-up through December 31, 2019, to examine the association between the Metabolic Score for Insulin Resistance (METS-IR) and cancer-specific mortality using weighted Cox proportional hazards models. Each 1-unit increase in METS-IR was independently associated with a 3% higher risk of cancer-specific mortality (P=0.01), demonstrating an approximately linear dose-response relationship confirmed across both continuous and categorical analyses. Subgroup analyses identified a significant statistical interaction between hypertension status and METS-IR: stratified analyses demonstrated that hypertension significantly amplifies the association between insulin resistance and cancer mortality, acting as an effect modifier rather than merely a confounder. Sensitivity analyses confirmed the robustness of findings across alternative modeling approaches and after exclusion of early follow-up deaths. Notable limitations include the cross-sectional nature of baseline METS-IR assessment, reliance on administrative cancer diagnoses without histological verification, and potential residual confounding from unmeasured dietary, genetic, and medication variables.

Implications Medical Affairs : The identification of hypertension as an effect modifier of the METS-IR-cancer mortality relationship highlights the additive cardiometabolic burden in hypertensive patients, reinforcing the scientific rationale for positioning antihypertensive therapies that also improve metabolic parameters within integrated cardiovascular-oncology risk reduction frameworks. For Medical Affairs, this evidence strengthens messaging around the multisystem consequences of uncontrolled hypertension and metabolic dysfunction, presenting an opportunity to engage KOLs in cardiology, endocrinology, and oncology on developing joint clinical risk-stratification pathways that extend beyond blood pressure targets. These findings support research collaboration proposals examining whether effective antihypertensive treatment modifies the METS-IR-cancer mortality link in future prospective studies.

Integrating Nutritional Status in Machine Learning Predictive Models for Cardiovascular Risk: A Pilot Study.

Revue : Studies in Health Technology and Informatics | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-05-21

Resultats principaux : This pilot cross-sectional study was conducted in 210 hypertensive adults aged 60 years and older from an elderly Thai population to evaluate machine learning models for cardiovascular risk stratification when nutritional status variables were integrated alongside traditional predictors including age, sex, systolic blood pressure, smoking status, diabetes mellitus, and body mass index. Model performance was assessed using rigorous 5-fold cross-validation, and the random forest model achieved the best overall performance with an accuracy of 67.14 ± 10.47% and an F1-score of 0.57, indicating moderate predictive capability but substantial variability across validation folds. No formal subgroup analyses were reported given the pilot nature of the study and the limited statistical power afforded by the sample size of 210 participants. The moderate F1-score reflects the inherent complexity of cardiovascular risk stratification in an elderly hypertensive population using a limited predictor set. Principal limitations include the small sample size, cross-sectional design precluding causal inference, single-center Thai population limiting generalizability, and absence of external validation in an independent cohort.

Implications Medical Affairs : Although preliminary, this pilot study highlights the growing integration of machine learning approaches in hypertension risk stratification and suggests that incorporating nutritional status variables into traditional cardiovascular risk models may modestly enhance predictive accuracy in elderly hypertensive populations, warranting further investigation. From a Medical Affairs standpoint, these findings support engagement with digital health innovators and clinical decision-support developers to explore validated, data-driven tools that could complement traditional guideline-based risk calculators in primary care and geriatric cardiology settings. The modest performance metrics underscore the need for larger, multicenter validation studies, presenting an opportunity for pharmaceutical companies to co-sponsor real-world evidence generation initiatives linking nutritional status, antihypertensive management, and cardiovascular outcomes in diverse elderly populations.

Starch coating on low-sodium salt via fluidized bed granulation: Sensory improvement and health benefits.

Revue : Carbohydrate Polymers | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-04-26

Resultats principaux : This experimental food science study developed and evaluated a novel low-sodium salt formulation (NovLSS) using soluble and pre-gelatinized starch derivatives to coat potassium chloride (KCl) via fluidized bed granulation technology, aiming to mask the bitterness and astringency that limit consumer acceptance of KCl-based salt substitutes used in sodium reduction strategies for hypertension prevention. The coating technology modulated interfacial release kinetics, slowing potassium ion diffusion and burst release to attenuate bitter oral perception, with NovLSS formulated as a blend of 30% coated KCl and 70% NaCl to maintain palatability while reducing sodium content. In a mouse model of high-salt diet-induced hypertension, NovLSS prevented blood pressure elevation and associated renal injury compared to high-salt controls, and also reduced total serum sodium levels. Mechanistic analyses showed NovLSS modulated gut microbiota composition, protected intestinal barrier integrity, promoted short-chain fatty acid production, and suppressed inflammatory markers in treated animals. Key limitations include the exclusive use of a murine model rather than human clinical trial data, which substantially limits direct translation of blood pressure and renal protection findings to clinical hypertensive populations, and the absence of formal human dose-response data for sodium reduction efficacy.

Implications Medical Affairs : This research provides a scientific basis for dietary sodium reduction strategies using palatable KCl-based salt substitutes as a nonpharmacological approach to hypertension prevention and management, aligning with current guideline recommendations for lifestyle modifications that complement pharmacotherapy. The gut microbiota and anti-inflammatory mechanistic findings represent emerging pathways that could differentiate this salt-reduction intervention in patient education programs and in KOL-targeted scientific exchanges at the intersection of hypertension and nutritional medicine. The translational gap from murine to human data represents a compelling opportunity to advocate for or co-sponsor clinical nutrition trials evaluating NovLSS efficacy on blood pressure endpoints, supporting the evidence base for complementary non-pharmacological hypertension management strategies.

The global burden of calcified aortic valve stenosis: epidemiology, risk factors, disparities, and future perspectives — a narrative review.

Revue : Atherosclerosis | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-05-24

Resultats principaux : This narrative review comprehensively summarized the current epidemiology, risk factors, pathophysiology, sex differences, and racial/ethnic disparities in calcific aortic valve stenosis (CAVS), the most common valvular heart disease requiring intervention worldwide, affecting an estimated 9.6 to 12.6 million people globally according to Global Disease Burden data and contributing to over 100,000 deaths annually. The review identified hypertension as a classical cardiovascular risk factor that significantly increases CAVS disease burden alongside dyslipidemia, obesity, and smoking, while valve-specific factors such as elevated lipoprotein(a) and valve calcification are recognized as independent accelerators of disease progression. Sex-based data revealed differences in valvular and myocardial remodeling patterns between men and women, together with documented disparities in diagnosis and access to intervention, while racial and ethnic variations in CAVS epidemiology and management were identified as areas of substantial unmet need. The anticipated rise in disease burden driven by an aging global population and persistent cardiovascular risk factor prevalence underscores the absence of targeted medical therapies proven to prevent onset or halt CAVS progression, representing a critical unmet medical need. Principal limitations include the narrative rather than systematic methodology, potential for publication bias, absence of formal pooled quantitative analysis, and the rapidly evolving CAVS management evidence base that may be incompletely captured.

Implications Medical Affairs : The absence of disease-modifying pharmacological therapies for CAVS, combined with its rapidly rising global burden in which hypertension plays a recognized contributing role, represents a critical unmet medical need that could anchor Medical Affairs advocacy for accelerated drug development programs targeting shared cardiovascular risk pathways, including lipoprotein(a)-lowering and anti-calcification strategies. For product positioning, antihypertensive agents with favorable effects on dyslipidemia or vascular inflammation could be highlighted in CAVS-adjacent scientific narratives in KOL-targeted educational initiatives, reinforcing the systemic cardiovascular value of aggressive risk factor modification beyond blood pressure targets alone. These findings support prioritizing real-world evidence and patient registry development to better characterize the hypertension-CAVS risk continuum, which would inform both guideline advocacy and future randomized trial design for CAVS prevention.

Interactive effects of green-space exposure and air pollution on cardiovascular disease risk: a study based on the CHARLS and CLHLS databases.

Revue : BMC Public Health | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-05-23

Resultats principaux : This large-scale epidemiological study utilized two major Chinese cohorts (CHARLS 2015 and CLHLS 2014) to investigate the combined and interactive effects of green-space exposure and multiple air pollutants on cardiovascular disease risk, with hypertension included as part of a composite cardiometabolic risk score. Green-space exposure was quantified using the Normalized Difference Vegetation Index (NDVI) and PM2.5, NO2, and SO2 exposures were assessed using the China High-resolution Air Pollutant (CHAP) dataset, with generalized linear models applied for all primary analyses. Green-space exposure was independently associated with lower cardiovascular disease risk (OR=0.867, 95% CI: 0.796-0.944, P=0.001), while NO2 showed the strongest adverse cardiovascular association with each IQR increase corresponding to a 25.9% higher CVD risk (OR=1.259, 95% CI: 1.152-1.377, P<0.001); associations for PM2.5 and NO2 remained significant in multi-pollutant models (OR=1.179 and 1.165, respectively). A critical interaction finding showed that at low pollution levels, green space conferred clear cardiovascular protection, but at high pollution levels, greater green-space exposure paradoxically amplified the adverse cardiovascular effects of pollutants. Key limitations include the cross-sectional design limiting causal inference, ecological air pollution assignment at the area rather than individual level, potential residual confounding, and geographic restriction to a Chinese population.

Implications Medical Affairs : The paradoxical amplification of air pollution cardiovascular harms by green-space exposure under high-pollution conditions challenges simplistic urban greening narratives and highlights the complexity of environmental cardiovascular risk mitigation, with direct implications for how hypertension prevention guidelines incorporate environmental risk factor messaging. From a Medical Affairs standpoint, these findings reinforce the scientific case for environmental risk factor monitoring as part of comprehensive hypertension patient management, creating opportunities to develop patient education materials on residential environment and blood pressure control that extend beyond conventional lifestyle recommendations. The central role of NO2 as the dominant cardiovascular risk pollutant supports targeted KOL engagement with environmental health specialists to advocate for joint cardiovascular-environmental health guidelines and research partnerships examining pharmacological cardiovascular protection in high-pollution-exposed hypertensive populations.

The cross-sectional and prospective associations between time-restricted eating and cardiometabolic health in community-dwelling adults: A systematic review and meta-analysis of observational studies.

Revue : European Journal of Clinical Nutrition | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-05-23

Resultats principaux : This pre-registered systematic review and meta-analysis (PROSPERO CRD42023390410) searched four major databases and included 12 cross-sectional and 6 cohort observational studies to investigate the associations between time-restricted eating (TRE, defined as an eating window of 12 hours or less per day) and cardiometabolic markers in community-dwelling adults. Meta-analyses of cross-sectional studies demonstrated no significant associations between TRE and abdominal obesity (OR=0.96, 95% CI: 0.86-1.07, I²=59%), metabolic syndrome (OR=1.04, 95% CI: 0.90-1.19, I²=32%), elevated blood pressure/hypertension (OR=1.04, 95% CI: 0.97-1.12, I²=0%), reduced HDL cholesterol (OR=0.94, 95% CI: 0.83-1.06, I²=78%), elevated triglycerides (OR=1.06, 95% CI: 0.99-1.14, I²=0%), or prediabetes/type 2 diabetes (OR=1.04, 95% CI: 0.96-1.12, I²=50%). The hypertension-specific pooled estimate was precisely null (OR=1.04, I²=0%), indicating no meaningful real-world observational association between TRE and blood pressure risk across included studies. Prospective associations could not be meta-analyzed due to insufficient data from the 6 cohort studies, and substantial heterogeneity (I² up to 78%) limited confidence in some outcomes. Key limitations include exclusive reliance on observational studies susceptible to confounding, variable TRE definitions across studies, reliance on self-reported dietary data, and inability to draw causal conclusions from cross-sectional evidence.

Implications Medical Affairs : The null finding for TRE and blood pressure in observational real-world data suggests that time-restricted eating should not yet be incorporated into hypertension management guidelines as an evidence-based dietary intervention, supporting a conservative scientific posture that prioritizes established strategies such as sodium reduction, the DASH diet, and weight management. For Medical Affairs, this provides scientific grounding for messaging that clearly distinguishes between evidence-based and investigational dietary approaches in antihypertensive patient education, ensuring KOLs and healthcare providers communicate accurate benefit-risk profiles to patients interested in chrono-nutrition. This evidence gap represents an opportunity to advocate for or co-sponsor large-scale randomized controlled trials examining TRE efficacy specifically on blood pressure endpoints, which would generate the high-quality causal evidence needed to either validate or refute TRE as a complementary hypertension management strategy.

Association between Life Satisfaction and Incident Stroke in Middle-aged and Older Chinese Adults: A Prospective Cohort Study from the CHARLS.

Revue : Cerebrovascular Diseases Extra | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-05-23

Resultats principaux : This large prospective cohort study included 15,225 Chinese adults aged 45 years and older from the China Health and Retirement Longitudinal Study (CHARLS, 2011-2020), examining the association between baseline life satisfaction and incident stroke over 9 years of follow-up using Cox proportional hazards models with extensive covariate adjustment including sociodemographic factors, lifestyle behaviors, comorbidities (including hypertension), and depressive symptoms. Over the 9-year follow-up period, 1,296 participants (8.5%) developed stroke, and higher baseline life satisfaction was associated with a significantly reduced risk of stroke (HR: 0.83; 95% CI: 0.72-0.95), representing a 17% relative risk reduction per unit increase in life satisfaction. Subgroup analyses demonstrated that the protective association was more pronounced among older adults, females, and participants with prevalent chronic conditions including hypertension, diabetes, and heart disease, as well as among overweight individuals and those with depressive symptoms, suggesting differential cardioprotective effects of psychosocial well-being across clinical risk profiles. The dose-response nature of the protective association was consistent across all sensitivity analyses and subgroup strata, reinforcing the robustness of the primary finding. Key limitations include reliance on self-reported stroke outcomes without clinical or imaging verification, single time-point measurement of life satisfaction without accounting for temporal changes, potential residual confounding despite extensive adjustment, and restriction to a Chinese population limiting broader generalizability.

Implications Medical Affairs : The finding that higher life satisfaction reduces stroke risk with particularly pronounced effects in hypertensive individuals provides empirical support for integrating psychosocial well-being assessments into routine hypertension management and cardiovascular risk stratification frameworks, with potential implications for updated guideline recommendations on holistic patient monitoring. For Medical Affairs, this evidence reinforces the scientific rationale for including patient-reported quality of life and well-being measures as secondary endpoints in hypertension clinical trials and real-world evidence studies, supporting richer outcome narratives that resonate with both payers and healthcare practitioners beyond traditional blood pressure metrics. The specific protective effect observed in hypertensive patients presents a compelling opportunity for KOL engagement on developing integrated care pathways that combine evidence-based antihypertensive therapy with structured psychosocial support programs to optimize cardiovascular outcomes in high-risk patient populations.

Higher light at night exposure is associated with increased risk of diabetes mellitus: A cross-sectional study of the NHANES database.

Revue : Medicine (Baltimore) | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-05-22

Resultats principaux : This cross-sectional study analyzed 4,498 US adults from the NHANES 2011-2014 cycles to investigate the association between objectively measured light at night (LAN) exposure via actigraphy and diabetes mellitus (DM) risk, with hypertension included as a key subgroup modifier and covariate in all analyses. The prevalence of diabetes was substantially higher in the high-LAN exposure group compared to the no-LAN group (21.01% vs. 14.48%), and multivariable-adjusted logistic regression showed high-LAN exposure was independently associated with a 39% increased risk of DM (OR=1.39, 95% CI: 1.00-1.92) after full covariate adjustment. Secondary analyses of metabolic biomarkers (HbA1c, fasting blood glucose) showed initial associations that attenuated to non-significance after full adjustment, and no significant associations were found for fasting serum insulin or HOMA-IR, suggesting LAN may act through pathways beyond classical insulin resistance. Subgroup analyses showed the LAN-DM association was more pronounced in middle-aged adults (40-64 years), individuals with obesity, and those with dyslipidemia or hypertension, though formal interaction tests were all non-significant (all P for interaction >0.05). Notable limitations include the cross-sectional design precluding causal inference, potential residual confounding from sleep disorders or shift work, a borderline primary confidence interval (lower bound at 1.00), and restriction to two NHANES cycles.

Implications Medical Affairs : The identification of light at night as a modifiable environmental risk factor for diabetes, with amplified risk in hypertensive subgroups, supports expanding the environmental cardiovascular risk framework to include circadian light disruption, with implications for hypertension prevention guidelines that may need to incorporate sleep environment recommendations alongside traditional lifestyle advice. Medical Affairs teams can leverage these findings in scientific exchanges with endocrinology and cardiology KOLs on the emerging role of light-mediated circadian disruption in cardiometabolic risk, supporting positioning of comprehensive antihypertensive management within a broader metabolic risk reduction and environmental health context. These findings create a timely opportunity to advocate for prospective studies examining whether effective antihypertensive treatment modifies the LAN-diabetes association, reinforcing the systemic metabolic benefits of blood pressure control in light-exposed urban populations.

The role of nitrogen dioxide in the prevalence of adverse cardiovascular and cerebrovascular diseases in China: a national multi-pollutant geospatial analysis.

Revue : BMC Public Health | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-05-22

Resultats principaux : This large-scale national study integrated CHARLS data (n=20,990 for AQI analysis 2015-2020; n=22,517 for machine learning analysis 2013-2020) with the China High-resolution Air Pollutants (CHAP) dataset to systematically investigate associations between seven air pollutants (PM1, PM2.5, PM10, NO2, SO2, CO, O3) and three cardiovascular and cerebrovascular outcomes (hypertension, heart disease, stroke) in middle-aged and elderly Chinese adults. City-level analyses confirmed significant positive correlations between annual average AQI and prevalence of hypertension (β=0.0006, P=0.0125), heart disease (β=0.0008, P=0.0147), and stroke (β=0.0005, P=0.0248); at the individual level, each 1-unit AQI increase was associated with 3% higher hypertension risk (OR=1.003, P<0.001), 6% higher heart disease risk (OR=1.006, P<0.001), and 4% higher stroke risk (OR=1.004, P=0.0433) after full adjustment. Restricted cubic spline analyses revealed non-linear dose-response relationships with accelerated cardiovascular risk increases at moderate-to-high AQI levels (≥80 for heart disease, ≥100 for stroke), indicating threshold effects of particular clinical relevance. Machine learning models (AUC range 0.720-0.771) combined with SHAP value analyses identified NO2 as the single most impactful pollutant across all three cardiovascular outcomes, with significantly higher impact weights than PM2.5 or other pollutants, acting through mechanisms including endothelial dysfunction, oxidative stress, and inflammatory pathway activation. Key limitations include the observational cross-sectional nature of primary analyses preventing causal attribution, ecological air pollution assignment at city level, and potential selection bias from CHARLS cohort composition.

Implications Medical Affairs : The consistent identification of NO2 as the dominant cardiovascular pollutant for hypertension, heart disease, and stroke in this large nationally representative Chinese cohort provides actionable evidence supporting integration of traffic-related air pollution exposure markers into cardiovascular risk stratification tools used by cardiologists and hypertension specialists in urban clinical practice. From a Medical Affairs perspective, this evidence base supports scientific advocacy for environmental health policies that specifically target nitrogen dioxide emissions, and positions pharmaceutical industry stakeholders as credible voices in cross-sectoral health policy conversations about pollution-driven cardiovascular disease burden and the limits of pharmacotherapy alone. The non-linear dose-response relationships with identified AQI threshold effects create a research opportunity to design trials examining whether intensified antihypertensive treatment in high-pollution-exposed populations achieves superior cardiovascular event reduction compared to standard care thresholds.

Finding Associative and Causal Effects of Temporal Changes in Health Features for Prevalent and Incident Cancer in Males: A Machine Learning Approach.

Revue : Studies in Health Technology and Informatics | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-05-21

Resultats principaux : This population-based cohort study used longitudinal data from 6,409 male participants in the Atlantic Partnership for Tomorrow's Health (PATH) cohort in Canada to investigate how temporal changes in health-related features associate with and causally relate to prevalent and incident cancer cases using advanced causal inference machine learning methods. For prevalent cancer cases, 12 binary, 2 continuous, and 2 categorical feature changes were identified as statistically significant through univariate and bivariate analyses; for incident cancer cases, significant predictors were subjected to causal analysis using the X-Learner algorithm implemented with Gradient Boosting, LightGBM, and Random Forest models to estimate Average Treatment Effects (ATEs) with 95% confidence intervals. Among incident cancer predictors, changes in age, arthritis, and hypertension demonstrated modest but consistent causal effects on incident cancer development, with hypertension identified as one of three variables with demonstrable causal rather than purely associative effects on incident cancer across all three machine learning models. Causal inference analyses using the potential-outcomes framework add methodological rigor beyond correlation-based approaches, though the magnitude of hypertension's specific ATE on incident cancer risk was not explicitly quantified in the published abstract. Key limitations include restriction to male participants limiting generalizability to female and mixed-gender populations, reliance on observational data with causal inference assumptions that cannot be fully empirically validated, potential measurement heterogeneity in hypertension ascertainment across follow-up waves, and the absence of external cohort validation.

Implications Medical Affairs : The causal inference framework linking hypertension to incident cancer risk beyond mere association adds methodological credibility to the emerging evidence base on the cardiometabolic-oncology interface, with potential implications for framing hypertension as a systemic disease condition with consequences extending beyond traditional cardiovascular endpoints in guideline narratives and risk communication. Medical Affairs teams can use these findings to enrich scientific narratives around the multisystem consequences of uncontrolled hypertension, supporting KOL education on multi-disease risk profiling that may strengthen prescriber awareness of the full burden of inadequate blood pressure management beyond cardiovascular events. Replication in mixed-gender, multi-ethnic prospective cohorts with clinically validated cancer ascertainment is a clear research priority, representing an opportunity for pharmaceutical companies to champion real-world evidence partnerships that explore hypertension control as a modifier of long-term cancer incidence.
