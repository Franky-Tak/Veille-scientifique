Prevalence of masked hypertension in patients with diabetes: a study in Mozambique.

Revue : Journal of Hypertension | Tier : Tier 2 | IF estime : 10-20

Date de publication : 2026-04-07

Resultats principaux : This cross-sectional study enrolled Black patients with type 2 diabetes mellitus (T2DM) having normal office blood pressure (SBP less than 140 mmHg, DBP less than 90 mmHg) from primary and outpatient clinics in Maputo and Matola, Mozambique, aiming to quantify the prevalence of masked hypertension using 24-h ambulatory blood pressure monitoring (ABPM). The primary endpoint was prevalence of 24-h masked hypertension, with secondary endpoints including daytime and nighttime masked hypertension prevalence and associated clinical factors. The overall 24-h masked hypertension prevalence was 53% (95% CI: 42.8-63.1%), with daytime prevalence at 50% and nighttime at 63%, representing a strikingly high burden in this sub-Saharan African population. In multivariable analysis, office SBP above 130 mmHg (age-adjusted OR 3.40, P=0.041) and pulse wave velocity above 12 m/s indicating arterial stiffness (age-adjusted OR 6.90, P=0.003) were independent predictors of masked hypertension. Limitations include consecutive rather than random sampling, single-center urban recruitment limiting rural generalizability, and lack of nocturnal dipping pattern analysis.

Implications Medical Affairs : This study reveals an alarming prevalence of masked hypertension exceeding 50% in Black African T2DM patients with normal office readings, significantly impacting positioning of ABPM-guided antihypertensive strategies in sub-Saharan Africa and supporting advocacy for expanded out-of-office BP monitoring. From a Medical Affairs perspective, this data underpins positioning of ABPM and home BP monitoring tools in emerging market strategies, particularly for diabetic patients in resource-limited settings where masked hypertension remains systematically underdiagnosed. This publication provides actionable insight for KOL engagement with African hypertension societies and for framing real-world evidence gaps in sub-Saharan Africa to support phase IV study design.

Real-world emergency analysis of Italian patients with acute type A aortic dissection.

Revue : Giornale italiano di cardiologia (2006) | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06

Resultats principaux : This retrospective observational study used Italian administrative healthcare data from over 12 million individuals (2010-2024) to evaluate pre-hospital management and pharmacological adherence in 1,625 patients urgently hospitalized for acute type A aortic dissection (AADA; mean age 67.3 years, 65.6% male). Despite 65.8% of patients having at least one antihypertensive prescription in the preceding year, only 35% showed evidence of continuous treatment, indicating widespread therapeutic non-adherence in this high-risk population. Diagnostic procedures were markedly underutilized: echocardiography was performed in only 12.3%, cardiac CT/MRA in 1.8%, and ambulatory blood pressure monitoring in only 2.4% prior to the event. Comorbidities included diabetes (8.5%), cardiovascular disease (21.2%), and ascending aortic aneurysm in 6%, highlighting the complex pre-dissection risk profile. Key limitations include the administrative database nature relying on prescription proxies rather than clinical diagnoses, potential coding inaccuracies, and the inability to assess actual blood pressure control levels.

Implications Medical Affairs : The finding that fewer than one-third of AADA patients showed evidence of continuous antihypertensive treatment underscores a major adherence failure in the highest-risk hypertensive population, supporting Medical Affairs messaging around fixed-dose combinations and once-daily formulations to improve adherence and reduce aortic dissection risk. This real-world Italian data reveals critical pre-hospital surveillance gaps including very low ABPM uptake (2.4%), supporting initiatives to position home BP monitoring and ABPM more prominently within cardiology and primary care pathways for high-risk patients. The publication provides grounds for KOL engagement with Italian cardiology societies to develop structured aortic dissection prevention programs integrating antihypertensive optimization and systematic cardiovascular imaging protocols.

Lived experiences of postpartum hypertensive women in Accra, Ghana.

Revue : PloS one | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-05-27

Resultats principaux : This qualitative descriptive-phenomenological study purposively recruited 16 postpartum women diagnosed with hypertension from the postnatal clinic of Greater Accra Regional Hospital, Ghana, collecting data via in-depth semi-structured interviews analyzed using NVivo 11.0. Three major themes emerged: expectations about living with hypertension (encompassing optimism/hope for recovery and fear/uncertainty about complications), self-efficacy in managing hypertension (confidence in treatment adherence vs. challenges with lifestyle modification), and environmental factors on management (spousal/family support, healthcare provider roles, social stigma). Participants expressed mixed emotions combining hope with significant fear of cardiovascular complications, while social stigma associated with hypertension was identified as a prominent barrier to treatment engagement in this West African context. Spousal and faith-based organizational support were identified as key protective factors, while healthcare provider communication was cited as critical to disease understanding and adherence. Limitations include the small purposive sample of 16 women limiting transferability, single-center recruitment, and potential social desirability bias in qualitative interviews.

Implications Medical Affairs : This study highlights stigma and inadequate social support as major barriers to hypertension management in postpartum African women, directly informing patient education materials and community engagement strategies for medical affairs teams targeting maternal health in sub-Saharan Africa. The emphasis on faith-based organizations and spousal involvement as protective factors provides a unique community-engagement framework that medical affairs teams can leverage when designing real-world evidence programs in West African markets. These findings support KOL engagement with midwifery and obstetric societies in Ghana and broader West Africa to advocate for integrated postpartum hypertension care pathways that address psychosocial determinants of treatment adherence.

Efficacy and Safety of Telmisartan 20 mg/S-Amlodipine 2.5 mg versus Telmisartan 40 mg in Patients with Hypertension, Inadequately Controlled on Telmisartan 20 mg Monotherapy: A Randomized, Double-Blind, Multicenter, Phase 3 Trial.

Revue : Drug design, development and therapy | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-05-20

Resultats principaux : This 8-week, randomized, double-blind, multicenter Phase 3 clinical trial enrolled 108 Korean patients with essential hypertension inadequately controlled on telmisartan 20 mg monotherapy (after a 4-week run-in), comparing low-dose combination telmisartan 20 mg/S-amlodipine 2.5 mg (T20/SA2.5) versus telmisartan 40 mg (T40) monotherapy. At week 8, T20/SA2.5 achieved significantly greater MSSBP reduction versus T40 (LS mean -16.97 vs -7.42 mmHg; P less than 0.001) and MSDBP reduction (-7.98 vs -4.01 mmHg; P=0.006), with a significantly higher BP control rate (53.70% vs 29.63%; P=0.010). The response rate was numerically higher with T20/SA2.5 (12.96% vs 5.56%; P=0.182) and the incidence of TEAEs was lower (9.26% vs 20.37%, P=0.104), though these differences did not reach statistical significance. Subgroup analyses were not reported for specific patient segments, and the trial was conducted exclusively in Korean centers. Limitations include the small sample size (n=108), single-country design, short 8-week duration, and the use of S-amlodipine (not racemic amlodipine) which may limit global generalizability.

Implications Medical Affairs : This Phase 3 trial provides head-to-head evidence supporting the superiority of upfront low-dose ARB+CCB combination therapy over ARB dose escalation, directly reinforcing the Medical Affairs narrative for early combination strategies aligned with current ESC/ESH guidelines recommending combination therapy as initial treatment for most hypertensive patients. The significantly superior BP control rate (53.7% vs 29.6%) with T20/SA2.5 provides a compelling efficacy differentiator for positioning fixed-dose ARB/CCB combinations in the Korean and broader Asian hypertension market, particularly in the large segment of patients inadequately controlled on ARB monotherapy. This multicenter Korean Phase 3 trial with cardiovascular society collaboration provides a strong scientific platform for symposia on early combination therapy at regional cardiology congresses in Asia.

Ultra-Processed Food Consumption Patterns and Their Association with Blood Pressure Among Young Adults: A Cross-Sectional Study.

Revue : Nutrients | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-05-20

Resultats principaux : This cross-sectional study enrolled 311 undergraduate students aged 18-24 years from three colleges in Hyderabad, India, assessing ultra-processed food (UPF) consumption via a validated 24-item food frequency questionnaire and blood pressure using a validated automated device. Overall hypertension prevalence was 12.5% (BP at or above 140/90 mmHg), with a higher rate among males and students aged above 20 years, revealing a substantial early-onset hypertension burden in this young urban Indian cohort. Consumption of high-fat and/or high-salt UPFs at or above the median was independently associated with elevated BP (AOR: 2.85; 95% CI: 1.16-6.99), with male sex (AOR 4.96; 95% CI 1.64-15.01) and higher household income (AOR 3.22; 95% CI 1.07-9.66) as additional independent risk factors. Taste, availability, and low cost were the most common self-reported drivers of UPF intake, suggesting structural barriers to dietary improvement in college environments. Limitations include the cross-sectional design, self-reported dietary data subject to recall bias, single blood pressure measurement, and restriction to urban college students in a single Indian city.

Implications Medical Affairs : The near 3-fold increase in hypertension odds with high-salt/fat UPF consumption in young Indian adults documents the emerging hypertension epidemic in rapidly urbanizing LMIC populations, supporting the framing of early dietary intervention as a primary prevention strategy and widening the relevant population for antihypertensive awareness programs. This evidence reinforces Medical Affairs messaging that hypertension onset is increasingly occurring in young adults, expanding the potential treated population and supporting earlier initiation of lifestyle interventions before pharmacological treatment becomes necessary. The study identifies young urban high-income males as a key high-risk segment for targeted population health screening initiatives and real-world evidence programs in India.

Hemodynamic and Thrombotic Vulnerability in Pulmonary Arterial Hypertension at High Altitude: Multivariable Predictors of Mortality.

Revue : Medicina (Kaunas, Lithuania) | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-05-20

Resultats principaux : This retrospective cohort study included 165 patients with confirmed Group 1 pulmonary arterial hypertension (PAH) diagnosed by right heart catheterization and treated between 2017 and 2022 in Bogota, Colombia (approximately 2,600 m altitude), examining predictors of 5-year mortality using penalized logistic regression with Elastic Net methodology. The population was predominantly female (84.2%, mean age 41 years), with congenital heart disease as the most frequent etiology (50.3%), followed by idiopathic PAH (33.3%) and connective tissue disease-associated PAH (12.7%). Five-year mortality was 13.3% (22/165), with independent mortality predictors including acute pulmonary embolism (coefficient 0.196; OR 1.216; 95% CI 1.16-1.27), ESC/ERS risk stratification (OR 1.171), pulmonary vascular resistance above 25 Wood units (OR 1.198), and age at or above 65 years (OR 1.187); female sex showed a protective effect (OR 0.353). Idiopathic PAH was significantly more frequent among deceased patients (59.1% vs 29.4%, P=0.025), highlighting the worse prognosis of this subtype at altitude. Limitations include the retrospective single-center design, relatively small sample size, and the specific challenges of extrapolating high-altitude findings to sea-level PAH populations.

Implications Medical Affairs : This study provides unique epidemiological data on PAH outcomes at high altitude—a significantly underrepresented population in PAH clinical trials—with implications for guideline adaptation and clinical decision-making in Latin American highland populations representing millions of potential patients. The identification of pulmonary vascular resistance above 25 Wood units and acute PE as leading mortality predictors reinforces the importance of aggressive vasodilatory therapy and anticoagulation in this vulnerable group, supporting Medical Affairs messaging for PAH-focused product positioning in high-altitude markets. Engaging Latin American pulmonary hypertension KOLs with these altitude-specific findings represents an opportunity to differentiate regional evidence generation strategies and support label expansion discussions for existing PAH therapies.

Dietary Approaches to Stop Hypertension (DASH) Diet, Incident Heart Failure and Its Associated Risk Factors in Australian Women.

Revue : Medicina (Kaunas, Lithuania) | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-05-18

Resultats principaux : This 21-year prospective cohort study analyzed data from 10,594 Australian women (mean age 52.5 years) enrolled in the Australian Longitudinal Study on Women's Health (ALSWH, 2001-2022), examining the association between DASH diet adherence (calculated from FFQ quintiles) and incident heart failure, hypertension, and diabetes. No statistically significant association was found between highest DASH quintile and incident heart failure (OR 0.73; 95% CI 0.37-1.43; P=0.20) during follow-up, likely due to the low HF incidence (n=136, 1.3%) limiting statistical power. However, DASH adherence was significantly associated with lower risk of hypertension (HR 0.73; 95% CI 0.63-0.84; P less than 0.001) and diabetes (HR 0.65; 95% CI 0.53-0.81; P less than 0.001) in Cox proportional hazards models with time-varying dietary exposure. These results, adjusted for age, socioeconomic factors, and multiple lifestyle confounders, represent the first prospective investigation into DASH diet-HF associations in Australian women. Limitations include the observational design, FFQ-based dietary assessment subject to measurement error, predominantly Australian-born White female population, and the relatively low HF incidence precluding definitive conclusions.

Implications Medical Affairs : This 21-year prospective data demonstrates that DASH diet adherence significantly reduces hypertension risk by 27% and diabetes risk by 35% in women, providing robust long-term evidence supporting dietary intervention as a cornerstone of cardiovascular risk reduction and reinforcing lifestyle modification messaging alongside pharmacotherapy. While the HF endpoint was underpowered, the strong hypertension prevention signal supports Medical Affairs messaging that non-pharmacological interventions can meaningfully reduce population-level hypertension burden, informing primary prevention campaigns and product complementarity narratives. This study's sex-specific, long-term focus provides valuable data to support KOL engagement with female cardiovascular disease prevention societies and to frame real-world evidence studies exploring gender-specific dietary and pharmacological intervention synergies.

Mortality Assessment in Patients with Cardiovascular Disease and COVID-19: A Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis.

Revue : International journal of molecular sciences | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-05-14

Resultats principaux : This systematic review and meta-analysis (random-effects model, STATA) analyzed studies from PubMed, EMBASE, and Google Scholar (August 2020-June 2025) examining mortality risk in COVID-19 patients with cardiovascular disease (CVD) and comorbidities including hypertension, diabetes mellitus, and chronic kidney disease. Patients with CVD and COVID-19 demonstrated a 3-fold higher mortality risk compared to COVID-19 patients without CVD (OR=3.00; 95% CI: 2.06-4.38), establishing CVD as the dominant comorbidity driver of COVID-19 death. Hypertension was associated with nearly doubled COVID-19 mortality risk (OR=1.94; 95% CI: 1.50-2.52), diabetes with over doubled risk (OR=2.17; 95% CI: 1.64-2.86), and CKD with the highest risk among comorbidities (OR=3.91; 95% CI: 2.50-6.10). A funnel plot was generated for publication bias assessment, though significant heterogeneity across included studies was noted. Limitations include significant heterogeneity, variation in COVID-19 treatment protocols across time and regions, potential publication bias, and the retrospective observational nature of most source studies.

Implications Medical Affairs : The quantification of hypertension as independently almost doubling COVID-19 mortality reinforces the importance of optimal BP control in improving resilience to infectious disease complications, extending the value proposition of antihypertensive therapy beyond traditional cardiovascular endpoints to include pandemic resilience. From a Medical Affairs perspective, this meta-analysis provides supporting data for KOL messaging at the intersection of infectious disease and cardiology, particularly relevant for post-pandemic chronic disease management strategy and health system preparedness programs. The finding that hypertensive patients face nearly 2-fold COVID-19 mortality underscores the need for risk stratification tools and proactive treatment optimization targeting hypertensive patients in primary care settings as a systemic vulnerability reduction strategy.

Hypertension and Social Capital in Indonesia.

Revue : International journal of environmental research and public health | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-05-08

Resultats principaux : This cross-sectional study used survey data from Indonesia—the fourth most populous country and a large developing economy where community programs play a significant cultural role—to investigate the association between social capital (measured by community program participation as proxy for structural social networks) and hypertension prevalence and blood pressure levels. Significant negative associations were found between community program participation and both blood pressure levels (p less than 0.05) and hypertension likelihood in multivariable regression analyses controlling for socioeconomic factors, suggesting that social network density independently reduces hypertension burden through resource sharing and health information access. The study uniquely frames hypertension prevention through social capital theory, expanding the risk factor model to include community social infrastructure as a non-clinical determinant of cardiovascular health. Subgroup analyses were not reported for specific sociodemographic segments, and the specific magnitude of effect size was not precisely quantified beyond statistical significance. Limitations include the cross-sectional design precluding causal inference, potential endogeneity in community participation, use of a single proxy measure for social capital, and limited ability to control for unmeasured confounders.

Implications Medical Affairs : Community social infrastructure in Indonesia is independently associated with lower hypertension rates, suggesting that social cohesion and community engagement programs could serve as complementary non-clinical levers in national hypertension prevention strategies in Southeast Asian markets, informing multi-stakeholder health partnership models. From a Medical Affairs perspective, these findings support the development of community-based patient adherence and disease awareness programs as part of comprehensive antihypertensive therapy strategies, particularly in emerging Southeast Asian markets where social determinants play an outsized role. Development and Temporal Validation of a Multinomial Prediction Model for Phenotypes of Undiagnosed Hypertension in Peru: A Population-Based Study.

Revue : Medical sciences (Basel, Switzerland) | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-04-29

Resultats principaux : This population-based study developed and temporally validated a multinomial logistic regression model to predict normotension and three undiagnosed hypertension phenotypes (isolated diastolic hypertension, isolated systolic hypertension, systolic-diastolic hypertension) using ENDES survey data: development cohort (2017-2019, n=62,091) and temporal validation cohort (2021-2024, n=77,372), representing over 139,000 adult Peruvians. The model used eight non-laboratory predictors (age, BMI, sex, residential altitude, smoking, alcohol consumption, vegetable intake, fruit intake), making it directly deployable in resource-limited settings without blood draws or laboratory tests. AUC was 0.789 in training (95% CI: 0.783-0.795) and 0.776 in temporal validation (95% CI: 0.770-0.781), with sensitivity 78.7%, specificity 60.9%, and NPV 95.2% at prespecified thresholds. Undiagnosed hypertension prevalence was 12.6% in the validation set, confirming a substantial undetected burden in Peru. Limitations include the observational cross-sectional survey design, imperfect temporal validation alignment due to COVID-19 disruptions in 2020, and potential bias from self-reported lifestyle predictors.

Implications Medical Affairs : This externally validated prediction model using non-laboratory data achieves near-80% AUC in detecting undiagnosed hypertension in a large Latin American population, providing a practical screening tool applicable to primary care settings in Peru and comparable low-resource settings, supporting earlier diagnosis and treatment initiation. From a Medical Affairs perspective, this predictive modeling approach supports the development of digital screening tools and population health programs that can identify the large undiagnosed hypertension population in Latin America, expanding the patient identification pipeline and commercial opportunity. This work provides an ideal platform for KOL engagement with Peruvian national health agencies and serves as a model for validation studies in other Latin American countries with high hypertension burden and undetected disease.

Revue : International journal of environmental research and public health | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-04-24

Resultats principaux : This cross-sectional analytical study with retrospective cohort component enrolled 1,673 patients diagnosed with hypertension, diabetes, and/or HIV in Limpopo Province, South Africa, evaluating acceptability and psychosocial predictors of retention in a linkage-to-care (LTC) intervention using theory-informed interviewer-administered questionnaires (January-June 2024) and clinic records (July-December 2023). Only 25.1% of participants maintained consistent care retention over 6 months, while 74.9% had inconsistent retention (at least one gap above 6 months), revealing a severe retention gap in this rural chronic disease population. Implications Medical Affairs : The finding that 75% of hypertensive patients in rural South Africa maintain inconsistent care retention represents a major gap in the antihypertensive treatment cascade, underscoring the critical importance of adherence-support interventions and patient-centered care models in sub-Saharan African markets where treatment gap closure is the primary commercial opportunity. Medical Affairs teams can leverage this data to position adherence support tools, community health worker programs, and simplified single-pill combination regimens as solutions to the documented retention crisis in African hypertension management, targeting 6-month consistent engagement as a key performance metric. Engaging South African hypertension and primary care society KOLs with this retention data provides evidence to advocate for policy-level changes in chronic disease management programs and supports real-world evidence initiatives addressing the adherence gap.

MicroRNAs in Pediatric Primary Hypertension: Promising Biomarkers or Context-Dependent Regulators? A Narrative Review.

Revue : Biomolecules | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-04-24

Resultats principaux : This narrative review examined current evidence on microRNAs (miRNAs) as biomarkers and therapeutic targets in pediatric primary hypertension, which affects approximately 4% of children globally with about half having primary hypertension, focusing on six miRNAs (miR-16, -21, -27a, -27b, -133a, and -145) with demonstrated roles in hypertension pathogenesis. Key mechanistic findings indicate that miRNAs modulate blood pressure by acting on vascular walls, smooth muscle cells, and renal tubules through multiple pathways, with experimental evidence from both spontaneously hypertensive rat models and human observational studies supporting their functional relevance. Critical challenges highlighted include context-dependent miRNA expression (tissue-specific effects, regulatory feedback loops), lack of standardized measurement platforms, and the complexity of miRNA-mRNA interaction networks producing contradictory phenotypic effects in different studies. The review identifies miR-21 and miR-27a/b as particularly promising biomarker candidates, though clinical validation in pediatric cohorts remains limited and no commercially viable diagnostic assay has yet been validated. Limitations include the narrative review design susceptible to selection bias, limited human pediatric data compared to animal models, and the absence of validated miRNA assays for routine clinical application.

Implications Medical Affairs : The identification of specific miRNAs (miR-21, miR-27a/b, miR-133a, miR-145) as promising biomarkers for pediatric primary hypertension opens a novel biomarker development opportunity that Medical Affairs teams should monitor as a future diagnostic positioning opportunity in early-onset hypertension and pediatric cardiovascular disease. This review positions miRNA research as a growing area at the intersection of epigenetics and precision medicine in hypertension, creating potential KOL engagement opportunities with pediatric cardiologists and hypertension researchers working on novel biomarker and theranostic platforms. The clinical translation challenges identified (standardization, tissue specificity, clinical validation) inform the R&D monitoring agenda and signal the need for investment in miRNA-based early hypertension detection tools for the pediatric population segment.

Hypertensive Acute Heart Failure: An Evidence Based Approach.

Revue : Critical care nursing quarterly | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-05-21

Resultats principaux : This evidence-based narrative review for nurse practitioners examined current management approaches for hypertensive acute heart failure (H-AHF), emphasizing the paradigm shift from volume overload to vascular redistribution as the primary congestion mechanism, which fundamentally changes the therapeutic approach. Key pharmacological strategies discussed include high-dose intravenous nitroglycerin and sequential nephron blockade (adding thiazide diuretics to loop diuretics) to overcome diuretic resistance, alongside ACE inhibitors and SGLT2 inhibitors for their cardioreductive properties in the H-AHF setting. Non-invasive ventilation was endorsed for respiratory support in acute pulmonary edema complicating H-AHF, with evidence from relevant clinical trials cited supporting this respiratory intervention. Vasodilators were positioned as primary agents over diuretics in patients without overt fluid overload, a clinically significant distinction with major management implications for the vasospastic, redistributive form of H-AHF. Limitations include the narrative review format without systematic evidence synthesis, the primary nursing practitioner audience introducing potential selection bias in evidence discussed, and the focus on acute rather than long-term management outcomes.

Implications Medical Affairs : This review's explicit positioning of ACE inhibitors and SGLT2 inhibitors alongside nitrates as evidence-based agents in H-AHF has direct Medical Affairs implications for expanding the ACE inhibitor narrative into the acute heart failure setting, supporting differentiated messaging that extends beyond chronic hypertension management to acute cardiovascular presentations. The endorsement of sequential nephron blockade and vasodilator-first strategies in diuretic-resistant H-AHF broadens the clinical narrative for antihypertensive and cardioprotective therapies in acute care settings, an underserved communication area at the cardiology-critical care interface. This publication provides a platform for KOL engagement with critical care nurse practitioners and hospitalist physicians who manage H-AHF, supporting educational programs on the evidence-based vasodilator-first approach and its mechanistic rationale.
