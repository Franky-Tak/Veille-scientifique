Long-term effects of weight-reducing drugs in people with hypertension.

Revue : The Cochrane Database of Systematic Reviews | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-19

Resultats principaux : This Cochrane systematic review (fourth update) synthesized evidence from 8 RCTs involving approximately 13,000 adults with hypertension, evaluating long-term weight-reducing drugs (orlistat, phentermine/topiramate, naltrexone/bupropion, semaglutide, tirzepatide) versus placebo over durations of 6 to 48 months. Orlistat showed no significant effect on all-cause mortality or cardiovascular morbidity (very low certainty), but probably increased serious adverse events (RR 1.45; 95% CI 1.10–1.91; moderate certainty). Phentermine/topiramate probably increased all adverse events compared to placebo (RR 1.13; 95% CI 1.08–1.20; moderate certainty) with uncertain effects on mortality and cardiovascular morbidity. Naltrexone/bupropion showed no significant SAE difference but probably increased overall adverse events (RR 1.69; 95% CI 1.58–1.80; moderate certainty). Crucially, semaglutide and tirzepatide trials provided no data on all-cause mortality, cardiovascular morbidity, or adverse events in hypertensive patients specifically; 7 of 8 trials were industry-funded, and the evidence base for critical outcomes remains very low certainty overall.

Implications Medical Affairs : This review exposes a critical evidence gap for modern GLP-1 receptor agonists (semaglutide, tirzepatide) in hypertensive populations, providing a compelling scientific rationale for Medical Affairs to advocate for dedicated subgroup trials in this population. The documented safety concerns with older agents (orlistat, naltrexone/bupropion) contrast with the emerging cardiovascular benefit signals of GLP-1 agents, creating a defensible differentiation narrative for product positioning. KOL engagement should prioritize advocating for pre-specified hypertensive subgroup cardiovascular outcome reporting in all future weight management trials.

Trends in the hypertension care cascade under dual guideline criteria in Peru, 2014-2024: a decade of nationally representative surveys with joinpoint analysis and projections toward the WHO 2030 targets.

Revue : Frontiers in Public Health | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-03

Resultats principaux : This repeated cross-sectional analysis examined 238,738 Peruvian adults (18+ years) from the nationally representative ENDES survey across 2014–2024 using two diagnostic thresholds (ESH 2023: ≥140/90 mmHg; ACC/AHA 2025: ≥130/80 mmHg). Joinpoint regression revealed no significant change in ESH-defined prevalence (AAPC 0.88%; p=0.261) but an increase under ACC/AHA criteria (AAPC 1.92%; p=0.022), with growing guideline discordance (AAPC 2.88%; p=0.002). In 2024, only 53.6% of hypertensive adults were aware of their diagnosis, 60.3% of those diagnosed received pharmacological treatment, and just 59.0% of treated patients achieved control — yielding a population-level control rate of 19.1%. Wealth-related inequalities as measured by the Erreygers concentration index were more pronounced for treatment than for awareness. Projections to 2030 leave gaps of 25.8, 30.5, and 30.2 percentage points relative to the WHO HEARTS 80-80-80 targets for awareness, treatment, and control respectively. Limitations include the self-reported survey design and potential measurement variability across survey waves.

Implications Medical Affairs : Peru's persistent population-level control rate below 20% over a decade reveals a massive treatment gap in Latin America with direct commercial implications for antihypertensive market growth in emerging economies. The growing discordance between ESH and ACC/AHA thresholds amplifies diagnostic ambiguity at the population level, underscoring the value of Medical Affairs engagement with regional guideline harmonization efforts. Equity-focused KOL strategy targeting primary care infrastructure and addressing socioeconomic treatment disparities should be a cornerstone of regional market access planning.

A Clinical Prediction Model for Selective NT-proBNP Testing in Hypertension.

Revue : Journal of Clinical Hypertension | Tier : 3 | IF estime : 3–5

Date de publication : 2026-06

Resultats principaux : This cross-sectional study analyzed 4,642 adults with hypertension from NHANES 1999–2004 to develop a clinical prediction model for heart stress, defined as a biomarker-confirmed pre-HF state using age-specific NT-proBNP cutoffs. Heart stress was prevalent in 31% of hypertensive participants. Multivariable logistic regression identified older age, current smoking, prior coronary heart disease or myocardial infarction, higher SBP, lower DBP, and reduced eGFR as independent predictors; a point-based score with an optimal cutoff of 6 points showed acceptable discrimination (AUC 0.71; 95% CI 0.70–0.73), internally validated across 1,000 bootstrapped samples. The high-probability group had markedly higher all-cause mortality (HR 3.90; 95% CI 3.55–4.29) and cardiovascular mortality (HR 5.18; 95% CI 4.44–6.04). Performance was consistent across race/ethnicity and BMI subgroups but was notably lower in females (AUC 0.66) versus males (AUC 0.78). Limitations include the cross-sectional NHANES design using historical data from 1999–2004 and internal validation only without external cohort confirmation.

Implications Medical Affairs : This practical risk stratification tool for pre-heart failure identification in hypertensive patients supports Medical Affairs messaging around integrated biomarker-guided cardiovascular risk assessment, potentially expanding NT-proBNP testing utilization in hypertension clinics. The significant sex-specific performance differential (AUC 0.66 in women vs. 0.78 in men) highlights an unmet need for sex-disaggregated risk prediction in hypertension research that Medical Affairs can champion through KOL advocacy and research partnerships. Aligning this model with heart failure prevention guidelines creates a strategic positioning bridge between established antihypertensive therapies and emerging HF prevention frameworks.

Effect of Body Mass Index on the Efficacy and Safety of Esaxerenone Versus Trichlormethiazide for the Treatment of Japanese Patients with Uncontrolled Essential Hypertension: A Subanalysis of the Excite-HT Study.

Revue : Journal of Clinical Hypertension | Tier : 3 | IF estime : 3–5

Date de publication : 2026-06

Resultats principaux : This prespecified BMI-stratified subanalysis of the randomized, open-label, multicenter EXCITE-HT study enrolled 585 Japanese patients with uncontrolled essential hypertension treated with esaxerenone or trichlormethiazide for 12 weeks, stratified by baseline BMI (low: less than 25 kg/m2 and high: 25 kg/m2 or above). In both BMI subgroups, esaxerenone produced significantly greater reductions in morning home SBP compared to trichlormethiazide (low BMI: -2.1 mmHg; 95% CI -4.1 to -0.1; high BMI: -2.0 mmHg; 95% CI -4.0 to -0.1). Serum potassium below 3.5 mEq/L was more frequent with trichlormethiazide, while potassium at or above 5.5 mEq/L was more frequent with esaxerenone, but critical hyperkalemia (6.0 mEq/L or above) was never observed in either subgroup. Both drugs caused transient eGFR reductions that stabilized by week 12. This exploratory subanalysis is not independently powered for formal hypothesis testing and was conducted in a Japanese population (Daiichi Sankyo funded), limiting generalizability to other ethnic groups and geographic markets. The open-label design may introduce home BP measurement bias.

Implications Medical Affairs : These subanalysis data support a BMI-independent blood pressure efficacy profile for esaxerenone compared to thiazide diuretics, providing valuable differentiating evidence for product positioning of this mineralocorticoid receptor antagonist in the increasingly obese Japanese hypertensive population. Medical Affairs should use these electrolyte safety data to refine potassium monitoring recommendations and physician education materials, particularly emphasizing the favorable hyperkalemia safety margin versus thiazide-induced hypokalemia risk. The consistently superior early-morning SBP reduction profile of esaxerenone, observed across BMI strata, represents an actionable scientific message for KOL strategy targeting the clinical problem of morning blood pressure surge.

Intra- and inter-rater reproducibility of the echocardiographic data of the Nigerian UPRIGHT-HTM trial participants.

Revue : Cardiovascular Journal of Africa | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-03-13

Resultats principaux : This reproducibility sub-study of the UPRIGHT-HTM randomized controlled trial assessed intra-rater (168 image pairs) and inter-rater (84 image pairs) repeatability of key echocardiographic parameters in 42 hypertensive patients across three Nigerian tertiary care centers. For left ventricular mass (LVM) index, coefficients of variation (CV) were approximately 30%, intra-class correlation coefficients (ICC) ranged from 0.74 to 0.84, and Cohen's kappa for LVH classification varied from 0.49 to 0.64, indicating moderate to good agreement. Ejection fraction (EF) showed intra- and inter-rater CVs of 13.0–14.5% with ICCs of 0.60–0.69 and kappa values of 0.71 and 0.53 respectively. Left ventricular diastolic dysfunction (LVDD) reproducibility was moderate with kappa values around 0.50. These findings were consistent with a systematic review of 27 prior publications from the global literature. Key limitations include the small sample size of 42 patients, restriction to three tertiary centers in Nigeria, and the potential impact of operator expertise variability across sites.

Implications Medical Affairs : The demonstration of moderate-to-acceptable echocardiographic reproducibility at Nigerian tertiary centers validates the scientific rigor of the UPRIGHT-HTM trial and establishes a foundation for including cardiac imaging endpoints in future antihypertensive cardiovascular outcomes studies in sub-Saharan Africa. These results support Medical Affairs advocacy for standardized echocardiography training and certification programs in Africa, which would directly strengthen the infrastructure for region-specific hypertension research and drug registration pathways. Engaging African cardiology KOLs through this scientific platform could accelerate partnerships for new hypertension trial networks in an underrepresented yet high-burden global region.

Effectiveness of a hybrid model education program for hypertension management: a secondary analysis of healthy community clinic records in Jordan.

Revue : Journal of Hypertension | Tier : 2 | IF estime : 10–20

Date de publication : 2026-06-22

Resultats principaux : This prospective longitudinal secondary analysis examined 9,193 hypertensive adults (mean age 55.6 years; 63.9% female; 25.5% smokers) drawn from 17,519 beneficiaries attending Healthy Community Clinics (HCCs) across Jordan between April and December 2024. A five-session Hybrid Model Education Program covering diet, physical activity, and self-management was evaluated using repeated measures analyses. Statistically significant improvements were observed across all sessions: mean weight decreased by 3.0 kg (95% CI: -3.9 to -2.1), systolic BP fell by 8.1 mmHg (95% CI: -9.5 to -6.7), diastolic BP fell by 3.0 mmHg (95% CI: -3.8 to -2.2), vegetable consumption increased by 0.39 meals/day (95% CI: +0.31 to +0.47), and the proportion of physically active participants increased by 9.8% (p less than 0.001). The Middle region accounted for 63.8% of participants. Key limitations include the secondary observational design with administrative data, absence of a control group, and potential selection bias toward motivated clinic attendees.

Implications Medical Affairs : This large real-world study from Jordan demonstrates that structured community lifestyle education can achieve clinically meaningful SBP reductions of 8.1 mmHg, providing strong evidence for non-pharmacological intervention as a first-line strategy in Middle Eastern LMIC settings — a context where Medical Affairs positioning of pharmacological adjuncts must account for lifestyle intervention uptake. The results can inform strategies to position antihypertensive therapy as complementary to behavioral programs, particularly in high-burden regions where lifestyle modification may delay but not replace pharmacological control. KOL partnerships with community health program leaders in Jordan and the broader MENA region could open pathways for integrated lifestyle-plus-pharmacotherapy protocols that demonstrate incremental BP control benefit.

Prevalence and risk factors associated with diabetic retinopathy: a cross-sectional study in northern Xinjiang.

Revue : Scientific Reports | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-19

Resultats principaux : This hospital-based cross-sectional study in six geographic areas of northern Xinjiang, China enrolled 3,253 diabetic patients above 20 years old managed in chronic diabetes programs, of whom 2,972 had graded fundus photographs assessed using DR grading software following UK guidelines. The age-standardized prevalence of diabetic retinopathy (DR) was 17.9% and of vision-threatening DR (VTDR) was 8.7%. Binary logistic regression identified hypertension as an independent risk factor for both DR and VTDR, alongside younger age, male sex, longer diabetes duration, higher fasting blood glucose, and insulin treatment (vs. diet only); male sex was not a significant predictor for VTDR specifically. Hypertension contributed to elevated DR risk independently of glycemic control parameters, underscoring its role as a distinct vascular risk factor. Limitations include the hospital-based sampling frame which may overestimate prevalence relative to the general diabetic population, limited data on antihypertensive treatment type and duration, and the cross-sectional design precluding causal inference.

Implications Medical Affairs : Hypertension as an independent risk factor for diabetic retinopathy in this large Chinese cohort of nearly 3,000 patients reinforces the Medical Affairs message that optimal BP control delivers microvascular protection beyond cardiovascular risk reduction, a key dual-benefit positioning argument for antihypertensive therapy in diabetic patients. The high DR prevalence (17.9%) in this population highlights a significant complication burden where inadequate hypertension management contributes directly to patient harm, strengthening the value narrative for proactive antihypertensive intensification in type 2 diabetes. KOL engagement bridging hypertension, diabetology, and ophthalmology societies should emphasize developing shared care pathways with explicit BP targets linked to retinal protection outcomes.

Associations of genetic risk factors and air pollution with incident pulmonary embolism: a prospective cohort study in the UK Biobank.

Revue : Heart & Lung | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-19

Resultats principaux : This prospective cohort study enrolled 362,672 UK Biobank participants without prior pulmonary embolism (PE) history and with complete genotyping data, followed for a median of 14.2 years, during which 5,586 incident PE events were documented. Cox proportional hazards models revealed statistically significant increased PE risk per interquartile range increment for PM2.5 (HR 1.08; 95% CI 1.02–1.15), PM2.5-10 (HR 1.10; 1.02–1.19), NO2 (HR 1.13; 1.04–1.23), and NOx (HR 1.13; 1.05–1.20), while PM10 was not significant. Participants in the highest air pollution score quartile had an HR of 1.33 (95% CI 1.10–1.62) for incident PE versus the lowest quartile. A critical gene-environment interaction was identified: individuals combining high polygenic risk score (PRS) and high air pollution exposure had nearly twice the PE risk (HR 2.15; 95% CI 1.56–2.96) compared to those with low genetic risk and low pollution exposure. Subgroup and sensitivity analyses were performed to validate robustness. Limitations include the observational design, potential residual confounding from unmeasured lifestyle factors, limited ethnic diversity of UK Biobank, and ecologic-level rather than individual-level pollutant exposure assessment.

Implications Medical Affairs : The demonstrated interaction between air pollution exposure and genetic predisposition in driving cardiovascular events including PE has indirect strategic relevance for hypertension Medical Affairs, as both conditions share upstream pathological mechanisms including endothelial dysfunction, oxidative stress, and pro-thrombotic states exacerbated by air pollutants. Medical Affairs teams should monitor the rapidly evolving exposome-cardiovascular evidence base, as future hypertension guidelines may incorporate environmental risk factors into patient risk stratification algorithms, creating new evidence requirements for positioning antihypertensive therapies in high-pollution, high-genetic-risk populations. Proactive engagement with environmental cardiologists and precision medicine KOLs positions a company favorably ahead of regulatory guidance evolution in this domain.

Risk of Metabolic Disease After Right- vs Left-Sided Colectomy for Colon Cancer: A Nationwide Cohort Study.

Revue : Diseases of the Colon and Rectum | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-19

Resultats principaux : This retrospective nationwide cohort study from South Korea analyzed 8,228 adults (mean age 58.8 years; 56.3% male) with histologically confirmed colon cancer who underwent colectomy between 2013 and 2019 using data from the Korean Clinical Data Utilization for Research Excellence project. Over a median follow-up of 4.0 years (IQR 2.5–5.8), 12.8% of patients developed new-onset hypertension and 14.2% developed dyslipidemia. Right-sided colectomy was associated with a 20% lower risk of incident dyslipidemia versus left-sided colectomy (adjusted HR 0.80; 95% CI 0.71–0.91; p=0.005), while no significant differences in incident hypertension (HR not significant) or diabetes were observed by laterality. Inverse probability of treatment weighting was applied to address residual confounding. Subgroup analyses by sex and stage were performed. Limitations include the retrospective administrative data design limiting causal inference, absence of direct metabolic biomarker measurements such as bile acids or microbiome composition, and limited generalizability beyond Korean patients.

Implications Medical Affairs : The 12.8% incidence of new-onset hypertension among colon cancer survivors post-colectomy reveals an underappreciated and clinically significant cardiovascular burden in cancer survivorship, representing an emerging patient population for hypertension management strategies. These findings support Medical Affairs initiatives targeting oncology care teams — including colorectal surgeons and oncology nurses — to implement structured post-colectomy cardiovascular screening protocols as part of survivorship care plans. Positioning antihypertensive therapies within the cardio-oncology continuum, supported by KOL partnerships with surgical oncologists, could expand the recognized therapeutic landscape for hypertension management into the growing cancer survivor population.

Environmental endocrine disruptors in cardio-oncology: emerging modifiers of cardiovascular vulnerability in patients with cancer.

Revue : Cardio-Oncology | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-19

Resultats principaux : This narrative review critically synthesized experimental, translational, and epidemiological evidence on endocrine-disrupting chemicals (EDCs) — including bisphenols, phthalates, PFAS, persistent organic pollutants (POPs), parabens, and pesticides — as emerging cardiovascular risk modifiers in cancer patients. These ubiquitous contaminants encountered through food systems, plastics, water, and healthcare materials were linked to multiple pathways including oxidative stress, mitochondrial dysfunction, endothelial injury, chronic inflammation, metabolic reprogramming, and epigenetic remodeling relevant to both oncogenesis and cardiovascular disease. Associations between EDC exposure and hypertension, accelerated atherosclerosis, heart failure, and metabolic syndrome were documented across reviewed studies. A novel hypothesis is proposed that lipophilic EDCs accumulate in epicardial adipose tissue, creating a local toxicological niche amplifying cardiovascular vulnerability in cancer survivors, though direct supporting evidence is currently unavailable. For breast cancer specifically, EDC modulation of ER activation, HER2 crosstalk, and AHR signaling pathways is discussed. The review acknowledges its narrative design, substantial study heterogeneity, and the hypothesis-generating rather than confirmatory nature of current EDC-cardiovascular evidence.

Implications Medical Affairs : Environmental endocrine disruptors represent a biologically plausible and scientifically monitored emerging risk domain for hypertension in cancer patients, and Medical Affairs teams should track this literature to anticipate potential future guideline inclusions of environmental risk factors in cardiovascular risk scoring. The intersection of EDC exposure, adipose tissue dysfunction, and hypertension provides a compelling translational narrative for positioning antihypertensive therapies in cardio-oncology guidelines, particularly as cancer survivorship care becomes increasingly formalized. Proactive KOL engagement with cardio-oncology, environmental medicine, and metabolic syndrome experts positions pharmaceutical companies to shape the scientific discourse before regulatory guidance on environmental cardiovascular risk factors emerges.

Development and validation of a healthy lifestyle and behavioral score for predicting metabolic syndrome: evidence from a 24-year longitudinal health check-up cohort in Taiwan.

Revue : International Journal of Behavioral Nutrition and Physical Activity | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-18

Resultats principaux : This longitudinal study analyzed 201,123 participants from the Mei Jau Health Management Institution in Taiwan (2000–2023) to develop and validate the Healthy Lifestyle and Behavioral Scale (HLBS) integrating diet, physical activity, sleep, and substance use domains through exploratory and confirmatory factor analyses and item response theory modeling. Using log-binomial GEE models, moderate (RR 1.12; 95% CI 1.09–1.16) and low (RR 1.46; 95% CI 1.41–1.50) adherence to healthy lifestyle behaviors were significantly associated with increased risk of metabolic syndrome (MetS), which includes hypertension as a defining component, compared to high adherence. Each HLBS domain independently contributed to elevated MetS risk in multivariable models. Stratified analyses showed consistent linear associations across all age and sex groups, with stronger effects observed among older adults and the elderly. The scale demonstrated acceptable model fit and strong internal consistency. Limitations include health check-up cohort selection bias, limited generalizability beyond the Taiwanese context, and absence of dietary biomarker validation.

Implications Medical Affairs : The HLBS provides a validated, multidimensional lifestyle assessment tool applicable to patient education programs, offering Medical Affairs a scientifically credible framework to identify hypertension-at-risk individuals in preventive care settings and create touchpoints for early pharmacological intervention. The independent contributions of sleep quality and substance use to metabolic syndrome risk — beyond diet and physical activity — expand the lifestyle risk conversation and reinforce the need for comprehensive antihypertensive management strategies addressing multifactorial patient profiles. Partnership with preventive medicine and occupational health KOLs using this validated instrument could generate real-world evidence supporting the value of antihypertensive therapy in metabolic syndrome prevention programs.

Perinatal and Socio-Demographic Factors Associated With Autism Spectrum Disorder in Children Aged Three to Six Years: A Hospital-Based Cross-Sectional Study.

Revue : Cureus | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-05-17

Resultats principaux : This hospital-based cross-sectional study at a tertiary center in Bhubaneswar, India enrolled 160 children aged 3–6 years with suspected autism spectrum disorder (ASD), using the TABC for screening and INDT-ASD for confirmatory diagnosis, yielding a confirmed ASD rate of 15.6% (n=25). Perinatal factors assessed included pregnancy-induced hypertension (PIH) among other gestational complications; however, PIH did not emerge as a statistically significant predictor of ASD in this study population. Statistically significant associations were identified for male sex (OR 3.61; 95% CI 1.17–11.10; p=0.026), maternal anemia (OR 3.35; 95% CI 1.39–8.05; p=0.007), and maternal smoking (OR 10.22; 95% CI 3.97–26.29; p less than 0.001), while nuclear family structure was protective (OR 0.09; 95% CI 0.04–0.24; p less than 0.001). The study found no significant association between PIH and ASD risk at this sample size. Limitations include the very small confirmed ASD sample (n=25), single-center convenience sampling, and inadequate statistical power to detect modest PIH-ASD associations.

Implications Medical Affairs : While pregnancy-induced hypertension did not reach statistical significance as an ASD risk factor in this small study, the inclusion of PIH as a study variable reflects growing scientific interest in maternal hypertension as a determinant of neurodevelopmental outcomes in offspring — a domain Medical Affairs teams monitoring antihypertensive safety in pregnancy should follow closely. The study indirectly highlights the importance of optimal maternal blood pressure management as part of holistic perinatal care, reinforcing the case for antihypertensive agents with established pregnancy safety profiles. Future adequately powered prospective studies on maternal hypertension and neurodevelopmental outcomes should be monitored for any signals that could affect antihypertensive product labeling for use in gestational hypertension and preeclampsia.
