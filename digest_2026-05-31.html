Prevention of heart failure.

Revue : European Heart Journal | Tier : Tier 2 | IF estime : ~35

Date de publication : 2026-05-30

Resultats principaux : This scientific statement, produced by the Heart Failure Association and the European Association of Preventive Cardiology of the European Society of Cardiology and led by Piepoli et al., provides a comprehensive and updated synthesis of evidence on primary and secondary prevention of heart failure (HF). The document highlights a pivotal shift in the prevailing HF phenotype: while coronary artery disease historically drove HF incidence, contemporary epidemiology reflects an increasing contribution from metabolic, renal, and cardiometabolic comorbidities, leading predominantly to HF with preserved ejection fraction (HFpEF). Arterial hypertension is explicitly identified as a central, high-priority modifiable risk factor, alongside chronic kidney disease, type 2 diabetes mellitus, obesity, dyslipidemia, sedentary lifestyle, and female-specific cardiovascular risk determinants (including hypertensive disorders of pregnancy). The statement also updates guidance on cancer therapy-related cardiotoxicity, the protective role of vaccination against infectious triggers, and the growing evidence for environmental and socioeconomic determinants of cardiovascular health. Updating the 2022 ESC position paper in light of significant post-2022 evidence — particularly regarding SGLT2 inhibitors, GLP-1 receptor agonists, and finerenone in metabolic and renal disease — the authors advocate a holistic, tailored, and multidisciplinary prevention strategy to reduce the global HF burden.

Implications Medical Affairs : This ESC scientific statement unequivocally positions arterial hypertension as the cornerstone modifiable contributor to HF prevention, reinforcing guideline alignment for antihypertensive agents with proven end-organ protection and providing a strong evidence framework for positioning products in hypertension-to-HF continuum conversations with cardiologists and nephrologists. The growing emphasis on HFpEF driven by cardiometabolic comorbidities opens strategic positioning opportunities for antihypertensive therapies with demonstrated benefits in CKD and diabetes — particularly in combination with or comparison to SGLT2 inhibitors and GLP-1 receptor agonists — and should inform lifecycle management and medical education priorities. For KOL engagement, this statement provides a high-visibility platform to initiate structured dialogues with European opinion leaders around early integrated risk factor management, supporting speaker bureau development and advisory board agenda-setting focused on the prevention-to-HFpEF evidence gap.
