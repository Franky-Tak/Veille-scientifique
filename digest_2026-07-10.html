What happens when public primary care is ill-prepared to respond to non-communicable diseases: a mixed-method study of diabetes and hypertension care in urban Nepal.

Revue : Journal of Global Health | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-10

Resultats principaux : This convergent mixed-methods study conducted in urban Nepal examined the readiness of public and private primary care facilities to manage hypertension and diabetes in the context of rapid urbanization. Among 660 facilities mapped, 134 provided diabetes and hypertension services, of which 63% were private sector. Median service readiness scores were similarly low in both public and private facilities, with trained human resources and guideline availability scoring the lowest across all components. Qualitative data from focus groups involving marginalized urban communities revealed a widespread reluctance to take long-term medication, heavy reliance on pharmacies for self-management, and a normalization of hypertension and diabetes as inevitable parts of aging. A major limitation of the study is the reliance on cross-sectional mapping and self-reported qualitative data, which precludes causal inference, and the absence of blood pressure control rate outcomes limits the ability to assess the direct clinical impact of these readiness gaps.

Implications Medical Affairs : These findings highlight critical structural deficiencies in primary care infrastructure for hypertension management in low- and middle-income countries, underscoring the need for guideline-supported, adequately trained healthcare workforces in resource-constrained urban settings. From a Medical Affairs positioning standpoint, this study supports partnerships with global health initiatives and health system strengthening programs targeting high-prevalence hypertension markets in Asia, where treatment gaps present both unmet need and market opportunity. Medical Affairs teams should engage with global health KOLs and public health authorities to promote evidence-based pharmacy-assisted hypertension programs and fund implementation science research addressing adherence barriers in LMIC populations.

Preliminary Application of Antihypertensive Gene Detection in the Treatment of Hypertension.

Revue : Medical Science Monitor | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-09

Resultats principaux : This prospective randomized controlled trial enrolled 900 hypertensive patients in China, randomly assigned in a 1:1 ratio to pharmacogenomic-guided therapy (n=450) or conventional care (n=450) over a 6-month follow-up period. Six gene loci were tested — CYP2D6, CYP2C9, AGTR1, ACE, NPPA, and CYP3A5 — covering five antihypertensive drug classes, and results were used to guide individualized treatment selection in the study group. The pharmacogenomic-guided group achieved significantly higher blood pressure control rates at both 3 months (80.7% vs 70.2%, P<0.001) and 6 months (78.4% vs 65.1%, P<0.001) compared with conventional care. Treatment compliance was markedly higher in the study group (98.0% vs 74.0%, P<0.001), and ACE inhibitor-induced dry cough incidence was substantially reduced (1.6% vs 8.3%, P<0.001), with fewer drug adjustments during hospitalization and a lower 6-month readmission rate (2.4% vs 8.9%, P<0.001). A key limitation is the single-center design at a hospital in Foshan, China, which may limit generalizability to populations with different allele frequency distributions, and the 6-month follow-up precludes assessment of long-term cardiovascular outcome benefits.

Implications Medical Affairs : This trial provides important clinical evidence that pharmacogenomic-guided antihypertensive therapy can significantly improve blood pressure control and medication adherence while reducing adverse effects such as ACE inhibitor-induced cough, with direct implications for the personalization of hypertension treatment algorithms in future guidelines. For Medical Affairs, this work strengthens the scientific narrative for precision medicine strategies in antihypertensive therapy, particularly for companies with products whose pharmacokinetics intersect with the CYP enzyme and renin-angiotensin system gene loci tested. Medical Affairs teams should partner with pharmacogenomics KOLs to explore companion diagnostic development and prioritize prospective trials in ethnically diverse populations to validate findings beyond the Chinese Han population.

The Association of Hypertension Based on Systolic and Diastolic Blood Pressure With Dental Visits in the Population Aged 45 and Older: Cross-Section Study Results From the China Health and Retirement Longitudinal Study (CHARLS).

Revue : Clinical Cardiology | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07

Resultats principaux : This cross-sectional study drew on nationally representative data from the 2013 and 2015 waves of the China Health and Retirement Longitudinal Study (CHARLS), including 4,770 participants aged 45 years and older, of whom 1,568 were identified as hypertensive. Logistic regression analyses, treating systolic and diastolic blood pressure first as continuous and then as categorical variables, revealed positive and significant associations between blood pressure levels and dental visit frequency. The hypertensive subpopulation was characterized as predominantly married elderly men with higher rates of smoking and alcohol consumption, comorbid chronic conditions, and lower self-reported health, reflecting a complex multimorbidity burden. Both curve fitting and restricted cubic spline analyses further validated a stable cross-sectional positive association between hypertension and oral health service utilization across multiple adjustment models. The study is limited by its cross-sectional design, which precludes causal inference, and by reliance on self-reported dental visit data that may be subject to recall bias and does not capture quality of care received.

Implications Medical Affairs : This study draws attention to a clinically relevant yet underexplored intersection between systemic hypertension and oral health service utilization in middle-aged and older Chinese adults, suggesting that dental encounters may provide an untapped touchpoint for hypertension identification and management referral. From a Medical Affairs perspective, these findings can inform cross-disciplinary disease awareness campaigns that bridge cardiology and dentistry, creating novel patient identification pathways in healthcare settings where cardiologists are not the first contact. KOL engagement strategies should leverage CHARLS cohort data to explore integrated chronic disease management models for hypertensive patients in Asia-Pacific markets, identifying actionable insights for real-world evidence programs.

24-h Movement Behaviors and Incident Hypertension Among Adolescents: A Prospective Study From the ABCD Study.

Revue : Scandinavian Journal of Medicine & Science in Sports | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07

Resultats principaux : This prospective cohort study analyzed data from 2,067 adolescents enrolled in the Adolescent Brain Cognitive Development (ABCD) Study, followed across 21 U.S. cities between 2018-2020 and 2020-2022 to examine the relationship between 24-hour movement behavior guideline adherence and hypertension prevalence and incidence. Hypertension was defined as systolic or diastolic blood pressure at or above the 95th percentile for age, sex, and height, and movement behaviors — physical activity, screen time, and sleep — were classified against international recommendations. Adolescents with daily screen time exceeding 2 hours showed higher hypertension prevalence (1.9%) and incidence (4.0%) compared to those meeting the guideline threshold. Adherence to two or more movement behavior recommendations was associated with a significantly reduced hypertension risk (RR=0.31; 95% CI: 0.09-0.98), though this estimate was based on a small number of events and should be interpreted with caution. Social determinants of health as measured by the Child Opportunity Index did not modify these associations; however, major limitations include self-reported behavioral data, a relatively short 2-year follow-up, and small absolute event counts that limit statistical precision for subgroup analyses.

Implications Medical Affairs : This study provides prospective evidence that combined adherence to multiple 24-hour movement behavior guidelines — particularly screen time restriction — is associated with substantially lower hypertension incidence in U.S. adolescents, reinforcing the primacy of behavioral prevention over early pharmacological intervention in youth populations. For Medical Affairs, these data support engagement with pediatric cardiology and public health KOLs to develop evidence-based messaging on lifestyle-based hypertension prevention programs targeting adolescents, a population that may benefit most from primary prevention before pharmacological thresholds are reached. Future research should investigate whether digital health or school-based behavioral interventions targeting screen time reduction translate into measurable, sustained blood pressure reductions in adolescent cohorts with long-term follow-up.

Changes in SSM Assessed via 100 Hz-VCTE Are Associated With Acute HVPG-Response to i.v. Propranolol: A European Multicentre Study.

Revue : Liver International | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-08

Resultats principaux : This European multicentre observational study enrolled 94 patients with advanced chronic liver disease and clinically significant portal hypertension across three expert centres between 2019 and 2024, evaluating whether changes in spleen stiffness measurement (SSM) at 100 Hz correlated with acute hepatic venous pressure gradient (HVPG) response to intravenous propranolol (0.15 mg/kg). The primary analysis assessed the correlation between relative changes in SSM and HVPG, with HVPG-response defined as a decrease of at least 10%. The median HVPG decreased significantly from 17 mmHg to 16 mmHg post-propranolol (p<0.001), with 45.7% of patients achieving an acute HVPG-response; SSM decreased by a median of -8.7 kPa overall (p<0.001). HVPG-responders showed a significantly more pronounced SSM decrease (-12.4 kPa, p<0.001), while non-responders showed no meaningful change; a moderate correlation was observed between relative changes in SSM and HVPG (Spearman's rho=0.387, p<0.001), yielding an AUROC of 0.717 for predicting HVPG-response. Key limitations include the non-randomized observational design, heterogeneity of liver disease etiology across centers (predominantly alcohol-related and metabolic-associated), and the AUROC deemed insufficient for direct clinical use as a decision-making tool.

Implications Medical Affairs : Although focused on portal rather than systemic arterial hypertension, this study is clinically significant for demonstrating that non-invasive spleen stiffness dynamics can track hemodynamic response to beta-blocker therapy, potentially informing surrogate endpoint development for portal hypertension clinical trials. From a Medical Affairs perspective, these findings are relevant to companies developing non-selective beta-blockers or adjunctive therapies for portal hypertension, as validated non-invasive response biomarkers could streamline trial design and reduce the burden of invasive HVPG measurement. KOL engagement in hepatology and interventional gastroenterology should focus on advocating for SSM integration as an exploratory endpoint in future phase II/III trials targeting portal hypertension, pending validation in larger prospective cohorts.

The Role of Group Supportive Psychotherapy on Quality of Life and Its Relationship with Heart Rate Variability and Blood Serotonin Levels in End-Stage Renal Disease (ESRD) Patients Undergoing Chronic Hemodialysis.

Revue : Acta Medica Indonesiana | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-04

Resultats principaux : This randomized, parallel-group, single-blind controlled trial enrolled 52 end-stage renal disease (ESRD) patients undergoing chronic hemodialysis at a tertiary referral hospital in Jakarta, Indonesia, randomized to group supportive psychotherapy plus standard care (n=26) or standard care alone (n=26). Primary outcomes included quality of life (KDQOL-36), heart rate variability (SDNN), and plasma serotonin levels measured by ELISA, assessed at baseline and post-intervention. The psychotherapy group demonstrated significantly greater improvement in KDQOL-36 scores compared to controls (mean change 8.46-16.92 vs. 0.27-11.36; p=0.046 before, p<0.001 after ANCOVA adjustment), with a large effect size (Cohen's d=1.16). Plasma serotonin levels were significantly higher in the intervention group post-intervention (p=0.028; r=0.30); however, no significant between-group or within-group differences in heart rate variability (SDNN) were observed (p=0.805). Limitations include a small sample size (n=52), single-center design, single-blind assessment by the investigator, and a limited intervention window that may not capture sustained long-term psychotherapy benefits in this population.

Implications Medical Affairs : This trial demonstrates that adjunctive group supportive psychotherapy significantly improves quality of life and modulates serotonin pathways in ESRD patients on hemodialysis, highlighting the biopsychosocial complexity of chronic kidney disease management and the potential of non-pharmacological co-interventions. From a Medical Affairs perspective, these findings are relevant to companies operating in the nephrology and hypertensive CKD space, as quality of life endpoints are increasingly valued in clinical trial design and reimbursement decisions for chronic disease therapies. Medical Affairs teams should engage nephrology and psychiatric KOLs to promote integrated care pathways incorporating psychosocial interventions alongside pharmacological management, particularly given the high prevalence of hypertension in the ESRD population.

Explainable machine learning integrating environmental chemical biomarkers and maternal clinical factors for prediction of hypertensive disorder of pregnancy.

Revue : Scientific Reports | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-09

Resultats principaux : This study developed and validated an explainable machine learning model for predicting hypertensive disorders of pregnancy (HDP) using data from 4,260 pregnant women enrolled in the Korean Children's Environmental Health Study (Ko-CHENS) prospective birth cohort. An XGBoost classifier was trained integrating 18 environmental biomarkers — including blood lead, cadmium, mercury, and endocrine-disrupting chemicals — alongside maternal clinical covariates using stratified 60/20/20 data splits with Bayesian hyperparameter optimization. The optimized model achieved a cross-validated ROC-AUC of 0.787 and an independent test ROC-AUC of 0.748 (95% CI: 0.587-0.887), with a negative predictive value of 0.994 under severe class imbalance (HDP prevalence: 1.7%). SHAP analysis identified late-pregnancy blood lead concentration and pre-pregnancy BMI as dominant predictors with non-linear risk gradients; formal SHAP interaction analysis indicated additive rather than synergistic BMI-lead pathways. Limitations include derivation from a single Korean cohort limiting generalizability, the observational design precluding causal inference, and the use of blood biomarker data not routinely available in standard prenatal care settings.

Implications Medical Affairs : This study provides a compelling proof-of-concept for integrating environmental chemical exposure data into obstetric risk stratification models for HDP, a hypertensive complication with significant maternal and neonatal morbidity that remains poorly predicted by conventional clinical factors alone. For Medical Affairs, these findings reveal an emerging scientific frontier linking environmental toxicology and hypertensive pregnancy disorders, suggesting that pre-conception environmental exposure screening could become a component of comprehensive hypertension prevention strategies in women of reproductive age. KOL strategy should prioritize collaboration with maternal-fetal medicine specialists, environmental health researchers, and regulatory bodies to advance scientific consensus on environmental biomarker-informed risk stratification tools in obstetric hypertension.

Workplace size and cardiovascular disease subtypes among 11 million Korean wage workers: a nationwide cross-sectional study.

Revue : BMC Public Health | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-08

Resultats principaux : This nationwide cross-sectional study analyzed data from 11,212,512 Korean wage workers from the National Health Insurance Service database (2021), examining associations between workplace size (eight regulatory-threshold-aligned categories) and the prevalence of 10 cardiovascular disease subtypes using multivariable logistic regression. Crude hypertensive disease prevalence was highest in one-person workplaces (11,273.5 per 100,000), but after age and sex adjustment the direction reversed (aOR 0.85, 95% CI 0.83-0.87), indicating the crude association was largely driven by demographic confounding. Mid-range workplaces (30-999 workers) showed the highest adjusted hypertensive disease odds ratios (up to 1.09), while ischemic heart disease showed modestly elevated odds in the smallest workplaces (one-person aOR: 1.12, 95% CI 1.05-1.19) and cerebrovascular disease showed slightly elevated odds in the largest workplaces (≥1000 workers, aOR: 1.06, 95% CI 1.03-1.08). Hospitalization-based sensitivity analyses confirmed the elevated IHD odds in small workplaces under stricter case definitions. Limitations include the cross-sectional design precluding causal inference, adjustment for age and sex only, administrative ICD-10 diagnosis codes which may miss undiagnosed cases, and restriction to formal wage workers excluding self-employed individuals.

Implications Medical Affairs : This large-scale epidemiological study reveals that hypertension's adjusted prevalence peaks in mid-size Korean workplaces (30-999 workers), a finding with important implications for targeting workplace-based cardiovascular prevention programs beyond the commonly studied large-employer context. From a Medical Affairs perspective, these nationally representative data support the development of occupational health partnerships and targeted educational initiatives directed at medium-sized employers who may represent the highest hypertension burden but lack the health infrastructure of large corporations. KOL collaboration with occupational medicine and public health experts in South Korea and Asia-Pacific could drive real-world evidence research evaluating worksite hypertension screening program effectiveness in the mid-size workplace stratum.

A two-phase machine learning framework for coronary heart disease risk prediction: Integrating population ecology with individual pathophysiology.

Revue : Digital Health | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-07

Resultats principaux : This methodological study developed and validated a novel two-phase interpretable machine learning framework integrating population-level ecological determinants with individual-level pathophysiology for coronary heart disease (CHD) risk prediction, using data from the Global Burden of Disease study, CDC WONDER, and the Health and Retirement Study (2018-2021). In Phase 1 (population-level discovery), an XGBoost model achieved a test R-squared of 0.874 and RMSE of 2.910 for predicting state-level CHD mortality, with SHAP analysis identifying ambient ozone pollution, population-level physical inactivity, and dietary risks as dominant predictors. In Phase 2 (individual-level validation), an ExtraTrees classifier achieved 0.897 accuracy, with SHAP analysis revealing hypertension, diabetes, and smoking as the three strongest individual-level CHD risk factors. Both models demonstrated excellent calibration and positive net benefit across the full range of risk thresholds in Decision Curve Analysis, with smoking and physical activity serving as bridging variables significant at both ecological and individual levels. Limitations include the ecological fallacy risk in Phase 1, reliance on historical cohort data from 2018-2021, cross-sectional individual-level design, and the absence of prospective outcome validation.

Implications Medical Affairs : This two-phase machine learning framework demonstrates that incorporating environmental and population-level determinants alongside individual clinical risk factors yields superior CHD risk models, with hypertension consistently emerging as the dominant modifiable individual-level predictor — reinforcing its centrality in cardiovascular prevention strategy. For Medical Affairs, these findings strengthen the evidence base supporting comprehensive antihypertensive treatment as a cornerstone of integrated cardiovascular risk management, providing computational tools and data-driven arguments that can inform value demonstration to payers and health systems. Akkermansia muciniphila in cardiovascular diseases: opportunities and challenges.

Revue : Frontiers in Microbiology | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-24

Resultats principaux : This narrative review synthesizes current evidence on the role of Akkermansia muciniphila, a key constituent of the human gut microbiota, in cardiovascular disease (CVD), including hypertension, atherosclerosis, heart failure, and abdominal aortic aneurysm. Under favorable conditions, A. muciniphila exerts cardioprotective effects by repairing the gut barrier, modulating glucose and lipid metabolism, regulating immune-inflammatory responses, and producing protective metabolites such as short-chain fatty acids. However, the review identifies a context-dependent dual role: in pathological states such as gut barrier damage or low-fiber diets, A. muciniphila may over-proliferate, accelerate mucus layer breakdown, and paradoxically exacerbate inflammation and CVD progression. The authors note that diet, medications, and baseline gut microbiota composition directly influence A. muciniphila abundance, underscoring the need for individualized, precision approaches to any microbiome-based therapeutic strategy. Limitations include the predominantly preclinical and observational evidence base, absence of adequately powered randomized controlled trials in CVD patients, significant strain-level heterogeneity, and unresolved questions about safe dosing and optimal delivery systems for clinical application.

Implications Medical Affairs : This review highlights the gut-heart axis as an emerging mechanistic frontier in hypertension and CVD pathophysiology, with A. muciniphila's dual protective-and-pathological role signaling that microbiome modulation must be approached with precision to avoid paradoxical cardiovascular harm. From a Medical Affairs perspective, these findings are relevant to pharmaceutical companies exploring combination strategies involving antihypertensive therapy with diet-based or probiotic microbiome-modulating interventions, supporting the scientific narrative for future mechanistic combination trials at the gut-cardiovascular interface. KOL engagement should target gastroenterology, cardiomicrobiology, and nutrition science experts to develop translational research programs investigating how established antihypertensive drug classes interact with gut microbiota composition and the gut-heart axis.

Metabolic biomarkers and cardiometabolic risk among night shift workers: evidence from night shift workers in Europe.

Revue : European Journal of Public Health | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-10

Resultats principaux : This observational study analyzed data from the EPHOR-NIGHT cohort including 860 workers (362 day shift, 498 night shift) from Sweden, Spain, and Denmark, examining associations between night shift work, plasma metabolite profiles, and cardiometabolic risk factors using multivariable linear regression. Night shift work was significantly associated with higher mean systolic blood pressure (beta=1.89 mmHg, 95% CI 0.00-3.79), higher BMI (beta=1.14 kg/m², 95% CI 0.44-1.84), and greater odds of hypertension (OR=1.38, 95% CI 1.00-1.89) and overweight/obesity (OR=1.37, 95% CI 1.02-1.82) compared to day shift work. Night shift workers exhibited specific metabolic alterations including lower polyunsaturated fatty acid fractions, higher mono- and saturated fatty acid fractions, and elevated branched-chain amino acids (isoleucine, valine, phenylalanine), findings that persisted after dietary adjustment. Sex-stratified analyses revealed stronger associations in women, and dose-dependent effects were observed with increasing consecutive night shifts and for permanent versus rotating schedules. Limitations include the cross-sectional design precluding causal inference, potential residual confounding from unmeasured lifestyle factors, the modest sample size, and restriction to European populations limiting global generalizability.

Implications Medical Affairs : This multicountry cohort study provides metabolomic mechanistic evidence linking circadian disruption from night shift work with elevated hypertension risk and specific metabolic pathway alterations, adding important biomarker granularity to the epidemiological literature on occupational determinants of cardiovascular risk. For Medical Affairs, these findings strengthen the scientific rationale for targeted hypertension screening programs in shift-worker populations and support the scientific narrative for chronopharmacology approaches — timing antihypertensive therapy to account for circadian blood pressure patterns in night-shift hypertensive patients. KOL collaboration with chronobiology, occupational medicine, and preventive cardiology experts should be pursued to develop evidence-based clinical recommendations for antihypertensive therapy optimization and lifestyle modification in shift-working hypertensive populations.

Resultats principaux : This narrative review synthesized literature from PubMed, Medline, and Web of Science (January 2010-November 2025) on nutritional challenges and precision dietary strategies for Korean middle-aged adults aged 40-64 years, with a focus on the triple burden of malnutrition — obesity, micronutrient insufficiency, and sarcopenia onset — compounded by excessive sodium and sugar intake and socioeconomic risk factors such as single-person households.
