Development of age-specific and sex-specific blood pressure norms and their associations with lipid profile in children aged 6-16 years from urban Bengaluru: a cross-sectional study

Revue : BMJ Paediatrics Open | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-03

Resultats principaux : This cross-sectional observational study enrolled 9,051 apparently healthy school-going children aged 6 to 16 years from urban Bengaluru, India, to develop region-specific blood pressure (BP) normative data and compare them with American Academy of Pediatrics (AAP) guidelines. The study-derived systolic and diastolic BP percentiles increased steadily with age in both sexes but differed markedly from AAP reference values, reflecting distinct growth and body composition patterns in Indian children. Using AAP guidelines, 14.1% of children had elevated BP and 23.9% had hypertension, compared with only 6.9% and 8.1% respectively when using the study-derived percentiles, indicating significant overestimation of hypertension prevalence when applying Western norms to Indian children. No significant difference was found in hypertension proportions derived from the study percentiles when using CDC versus Indian Academy of Pediatrics (IAP) height centiles (p=0.178), validating the robustness of the local normative approach. Elevated BP was significantly associated with a higher proportion of abnormal total cholesterol (8.6% vs 3.2%, p=0.027) and higher LDL-cholesterol levels, particularly among girls, suggesting early clustering of cardiometabolic risk factors in this urban Indian pediatric population.

Implications Medical Affairs : This study provides critical evidence that global BP reference standards may substantially overestimate hypertension prevalence in South Asian children, with direct implications for updating pediatric hypertension screening guidelines in India and similar low- and middle-income country settings. From a Medical Affairs perspective, the early co-occurrence of elevated BP and dyslipidemia in girls represents an important window for dual cardiovascular risk intervention, potentially informing product positioning for combination cardiometabolic therapies in a pediatric-to-adult continuum. This research should prompt KOL engagement with the Indian Academy of Pediatrics to advocate for region-specific normative guidelines and could anchor Medical Education initiatives around early cardiometabolic risk screening in pediatric populations.

Conjoint association of gestational diabetes mellitus and hypertensive disorders of pregnancy with long-term risk of mortality: a population-based cohort study

Revue : Journal of Global Health | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-03

Resultats principaux : This population-based cohort study used data from the UK Biobank, including 220,953 women who reported at least one live birth and were followed for a median of 12.9 years to evaluate independent and joint effects of gestational diabetes mellitus (GDM) and hypertensive disorders of pregnancy (HDPs) on long-term mortality. Women with GDM demonstrated a significantly increased risk of all-cause mortality (HR 1.57, 95% CI 1.26-1.96), premature mortality (HR 1.60, 95% CI 1.24-2.06), and cardiovascular disease (CVD) mortality (HR 2.60, 95% CI 1.70-3.96). Women with a history of HDPs showed a significantly elevated CVD mortality risk (HR 2.07, 95% CI 1.51-2.83), though their associations with all-cause and premature death were not statistically significant in isolation. Crucially, the co-occurrence of both GDM and HDPs was associated with a 3.9-fold increased risk of all-cause mortality (HR 3.93, 95% CI 1.88-8.24) and a 4.3-fold increased risk of premature mortality (HR 4.31, 95% CI 1.94-9.57) compared to women without either condition, indicating a strong synergistic effect. Key limitations include the self-reported nature of pregnancy complications, potential selection bias inherent to UK Biobank participants (a healthier-than-average population), and the lack of gestational age and neonatal outcome data that may confound risk estimates.

Implications Medical Affairs : This large-scale longitudinal study reinforces the concept of pregnancy complications as sentinel events for long-term cardiovascular mortality, particularly when GDM and hypertensive disorders coexist, which should accelerate the integration of obstetric history into cardiovascular risk stratification algorithms and clinical guidelines. For Medical Affairs teams, this evidence supports positioning antihypertensive and cardiometabolic therapies for women with a history of hypertensive pregnancy disorders as a high-risk segment warranting earlier and more intensive pharmacological intervention. KOL strategies should target collaboration with obstetrics and gynecology societies to develop postpartum cardiovascular surveillance programs and promote guideline updates incorporating composite pregnancy complication scoring into routine cardiovascular risk assessment.

[Association between the trajectory of body mass index and elevated blood pressure in children: a cohort study from 2013 to 2024]

Revue : Wei Sheng Yan Jiu (Journal of Hygiene Research) | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-05 (published online)

Resultats principaux : This prospective cohort study from the TAWS longitudinal study in Taicang, China, followed 1,274 children from birth to age 7-9 years (2013-2024), measuring BMI-for-age Z-score (BAZ) at 15 time points and blood pressure at the final visit. Group-based trajectory modeling identified three distinct BAZ trajectories: low-decreasing (34.5% of children), moderate-stable (48.3%), and high-increasing (17.2%), with the overall prevalence of elevated blood pressure (EBP) at 20.6% in this cohort. Children in the high-increasing BAZ trajectory had a significantly elevated risk of EBP compared to the moderate-stable group (OR 1.55, 95% CI 1.07-2.23, p=0.02), while the low-decreasing group showed no significant association (OR 0.77, 95% CI 0.55-1.06, p=0.11). From age 2.5 years onward, BAZ values were consistently and significantly higher in children who later developed EBP compared to those with normal blood pressure (p<0.05 at each subsequent time point), identifying this as a critical window for intervention. Study limitations include single-site recruitment in Taicang, the relatively modest sample size limiting subgroup analyses, and use of Chinese national BP screening references rather than international standards that may affect generalizability.

Implications Medical Affairs : This longitudinal study establishes that rapidly rising childhood BMI trajectories beginning as early as age 2.5 years are independently associated with elevated blood pressure at school age, providing actionable evidence for the design of early childhood obesity prevention programs with a blood pressure reduction endpoint. For Medical Affairs strategy, this data supports positioning preventive cardiometabolic interventions for pediatric target populations and reinforces the disease burden rationale for anti-obesity therapies with proven BP-lowering effects in high-risk childhood cohorts. KOL engagement with pediatric endocrinology and public health societies in China represents a key opportunity to co-develop childhood BP surveillance protocols that incorporate growth trajectory monitoring as a predictive biomarker, generating clinical evidence for early intervention strategies.

Blood Pressure Control With Clevidipine Is Associated With Hematoma Volume Reduction in Acute Hypertensive Intracerebral Hemorrhage: A Single-Center Prospective Cohort Study

Revue : European Journal of Neurology | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07 (online)

Resultats principaux : This single-center prospective cohort study with historical controls compared clevidipine (2023-2025, n=25) to standard antihypertensive therapy with labetalol, clonidine, and/or diuretics (2020-2022, n=39) in patients with acute hypertensive intracerebral hemorrhage (ICH), with groups well-matched on demographics and admission NIHSS scores. Clevidipine administration achieved effective blood pressure control below 140/90 mmHg within a mean of 3.5 +/- 3.2 hours from treatment onset, with no hematoma expansion observed in any clevidipine-treated patient versus 7.7% in controls (p=0.172). Clevidipine was associated with significant ICH volume reduction at 24-hour follow-up brain CT (14.7% vs. 4.5% in controls, p=0.023), and this effect remained independent in multivariable linear regression adjusting for confounders (beta = -0.16, 95% CI: -0.30 to -0.02, p=0.024). A trend toward improved NIHSS scores during hospitalization and numerically lower 3-month mortality was observed in the clevidipine group, though these secondary outcomes did not reach statistical significance due to limited sample size. No serious adverse events were reported in clevidipine-treated patients; key limitations include the single-center non-randomized design with historical controls, modest sample size, and potential confounding from practice changes between the two time periods.

Implications Medical Affairs : This prospective real-world dataset positions clevidipine as a clinically differentiated intravenous calcium channel blocker for acute hypertensive ICH management, a high-unmet-need setting where rapid and precise BP titration is critical to limiting hematoma expansion and improving neurological outcomes. From a Medical Affairs perspective, these findings support expanding clevidipine positioning into the hypertensive emergency and neurocritical care segments, moving beyond its traditional cardiac surgery indication to align with emerging acute stroke management guidelines that emphasize titratable IV antihypertensive therapy. A KOL engagement strategy targeting stroke neurologists and neurocritical care specialists, supported by investigator-initiated randomized trials with functional outcome endpoints including modified Rankin Scale at 90 days, would be essential to build the evidence base required for international guideline incorporation.

[Effect of community-based intensive smoking cessation intervention on smoking behavior and blood pressure control in hypertensive patients]

Revue : Wei Sheng Yan Jiu (Journal of Hygiene Research) | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-05 (published online)

Resultats principaux : This cluster randomized controlled trial enrolled 803 hypertensive smokers (398 intervention, 405 control) across primary healthcare institutions in Yinchuan and Chongqing, China, comparing a 1-month intensive smoking cessation intervention to routine care over 6 months, with 93.77% completion rate. In the per-protocol analysis, continuous abstinence rates in the intervention group were significantly higher than controls at 1 month (28.01% vs 12.40%), 3 months (19.37% vs 9.97%), and 6 months (16.75% vs 8.36%; all p<0.001), with adjusted relative risks for continuous abstinence of 2.43, 2.08, and 2.11 respectively. Blood pressure control rates improved in both groups throughout follow-up; the intervention group showed significantly higher control rates at 7 days (77.75% vs 65.50%), 1 month (81.41% vs 72.77%), and 3 months (86.65% vs 73.58%), with the between-group difference narrowing and becoming non-significant at 6 months. Smoking reduction rates were also consistently higher in the intervention group across all follow-up periods (p<0.05), with adjusted relative risks ranging from 1.50 to 2.07, demonstrating a dose-response relationship between smoking reduction and improved BP control. Principal limitations include the 6-month follow-up window insufficient for conclusions about long-term smoking cessation and BP maintenance, single-country generalizability constraints, and the predominantly older (mean age 61 years) lower-education Chinese urban sample.

Implications Medical Affairs : This cluster RCT provides robust evidence that a brief, intensive community-based smoking cessation program substantially improves both smoking abstinence rates and short-term blood pressure control in hypertensive patients, reinforcing smoking cessation as a critical non-pharmacological component of comprehensive hypertension management guidelines. From a Medical Affairs standpoint, this study supports co-positioning antihypertensive therapies alongside structured smoking cessation support programs, creating integrated patient pathways that address multiple cardiovascular risk factors concurrently and enhancing the overall value proposition of BP-lowering agents deployed in community settings. Collaboration with public health agencies and primary care KOLs to embed systematic smoking cessation interventions within national hypertension management protocols represents a high-impact Medical Affairs initiative that can amplify treatment outcomes while generating real-world evidence for combination lifestyle-pharmacological approaches.

Diet quality and progression from health to chronic disease, multimorbidity and mortality in the UK Biobank

Revue : BMC Medicine | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-03

Resultats principaux : This prospective multi-state modeling study from the UK Biobank included 84,293 healthy individuals aged 40-70 years at baseline, followed for a mean of 11.2 years to assess the association between adherence to four validated dietary patterns and transitions from health to first chronic disease, multimorbidity, and death. Higher adherence to the Alternate Mediterranean Diet (aMED), DASH, and AHEI-2010 was each associated with a 6-8% reduction in risk of developing one predefined chronic disease (HRs for highest vs. lowest tertile: 0.92, 0.94, and 0.92 respectively). aMED and DASH adherence were each associated with a significantly lower risk of progression to multimorbidity (HRs: 0.92 and 0.90 respectively), while aMED was also associated with reduced risk of death without any disease (HR 0.72, 95% CI 0.56-0.92). All dietary scores except the healthful Plant-based Diet Index (hPDI) were associated with reduced risk of death after multimorbidity (HRs: 0.76, 0.71, and 0.80 for aMED, DASH, and AHEI-2010 respectively), demonstrating benefits across the full disease trajectory. Limitations include use of a single dietary recall assessment potentially introducing measurement error, the healthy volunteer bias inherent to the UK Biobank design, and the observational nature precluding causal inference despite sophisticated multi-state modeling.

Implications Medical Affairs : The finding that DASH dietary adherence is independently associated with reduced progression from health to chronic disease and multimorbidity directly supports the clinical evidence base for dietary intervention as a foundational element of cardiovascular disease and hypertension prevention guidelines. For Medical Affairs, this large-scale UK Biobank evidence can be leveraged to position antihypertensive therapies within integrated cardiovascular risk management frameworks that include structured DASH-aligned dietary counseling, supporting product differentiation through lifestyle program partnerships and health coaching platforms. KOL engagement with preventive cardiology and clinical nutrition societies should focus on co-developing practical dietary guidance tools that complement pharmacological BP management, backed by this quality of multi-state epidemiological evidence to strengthen guideline recommendations for combined lifestyle-pharmacotherapy approaches.

Counteracting obesity discrimination and stigma: A scoping review

Revue : Advances in Clinical and Experimental Medicine | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07-03

Resultats principaux : This scoping review examined the evidence on obesity-related discrimination and stigmatization, characterizing mechanisms, contexts, and consequences of weight stigma affecting individuals with obesity, a chronic disease with serious comorbidities including type 2 diabetes, hypercholesterolemia, and hypertension. The review identified that people with obesity experience stigmatization across multiple settings including workplaces, educational institutions, online environments, and healthcare facilities, driven by misconceptions that obesity results from inappropriate lifestyle choices rather than a complex multifactorial disease. In Poland, high levels of obesity stigmatization coexist with low levels of public and professional knowledge about the disease, creating a dual barrier where both patient avoidance of care and provider bias reduce access to effective treatment for obesity and its cardiovascular comorbidities including hypertension. Despite low knowledge levels, healthcare professionals in Poland expressed willingness to help patients with obesity, suggesting that targeted education could substantially improve clinical engagement and outcomes for this population. Proposed countermeasures include well-designed educational interventions, social campaigns, medical society guidelines, communication manuals for healthcare professionals, and policy-level advocacy to prevent exclusion of patients with obesity from the healthcare system; however, the review's geographic focus on Poland and limited quantitative evidence on intervention effectiveness are notable limitations.

Implications Medical Affairs : While related to hypertension through obesity as a shared and mutually reinforcing comorbidity, this review highlights that obesity stigma in healthcare settings may represent a critical but underrecognized barrier to appropriate screening and treatment of obesity-related hypertension, a dimension relevant to any comprehensive cardiovascular disease management program. From a Medical Affairs perspective, anti-stigma educational programs targeting healthcare professionals could indirectly improve hypertension detection and treatment adherence in the obese patient segment, supporting integrated disease management positioning for cardiometabolic therapies and strengthening patient pathway design. KOL engagement should include collaborations with primary care organizations, obesity medicine societies, and patient advocacy groups to develop destigmatizing communication frameworks that facilitate earlier identification and treatment of comorbid obesity and hypertension, potentially reducing the substantial burden of undertreated resistant hypertension in this population.

Resultats principaux : This narrative review addressed the underrecognized role of hypercortisolism as a secondary and potentially treatable cause of resistant hypertension, synthesizing evidence from the CATALYST study and MOMENTUM registry against the backdrop of 1.28 billion adults globally affected by hypertension with fewer than one in four achieving optimal BP control. Resistant hypertension, defined as BP above goal despite three maximally tolerated antihypertensive agents including a diuretic or requiring four or more agents for control, represents a high-risk subset carrying disproportionately elevated risk of myocardial infarction, stroke, heart failure, and chronic kidney disease. Revue : Indian Journal of Medical Research | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07 (online)

Resultats principaux : This network meta-analysis included 22 randomized controlled trials with a total of 18,133 participants in intervention groups and 17,971 in placebo groups, evaluating oral nutritional supplements for prevention of preeclampsia, eclampsia, and gestational hypertension. Compared to placebo, L-arginine combined with vitamins B, C, and E (OR 0.33, 95% CI 0.20-0.54), linoleic acid-calcium combination (OR 0.16, 95% CI 0.041-0.50), vitamin D (OR 0.34, 95% CI 0.17-0.65), and calcium alone (OR 0.77, 95% CI 0.65-0.91) were each significantly more effective in preventing preeclampsia. Limitations include indirect comparisons inherent to network meta-analysis, heterogeneity in study populations, dosing regimens, and intervention formulations across included trials, and the possibility of publication bias affecting point estimates and supplement ranking.

Implications Medical Affairs : This network meta-analysis provides the strongest comparative evidence to date supporting specific nutritional supplement combinations, particularly L-arginine with multivitamins and vitamin D, as effective strategies for primary prevention of preeclampsia, a hypertensive disorder of pregnancy with major maternal and perinatal mortality burden globally. For Medical Affairs, these findings support a scientific narrative around nutritional supplementation as a cost-effective complement to pharmacological management of hypertensive disorders in pregnancy, potentially informing obstetric practice guideline updates and creating strategic synergy with antihypertensive product portfolios targeting the maternal health market. Inhibition of SGLT2 reduces blood pressure in the early phase of salt-sensitive hypertension in male Dahl-SS rats independently of changes in renal inflammation

Revue : Physiological Reports | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-07 (online)

Resultats principaux : This preclinical study employed male Dahl salt-sensitive (SS) rats fed a 4.0% NaCl high-salt diet for 7 days to investigate albuminuria and renal inflammation as initiating mechanisms of salt-sensitive hypertension and to assess the effects of the SGLT2 inhibitor dapagliflozin. Urinary albumin and CCL2 (a monocyte chemoattractant cytokine) were significantly elevated in high-salt SS rats compared to control-salt animals before any blood pressure differences emerged between groups, indicating that albuminuria and cytokine signaling are primary events preceding BP elevation in salt-sensitive hypertension. Key limitations include the use of a rodent animal model that may not fully replicate human salt-sensitive hypertension, the very short 7-day observation window limiting conclusions about chronic salt loading, and the exclusively male cohort precluding sex-specific mechanistic insights.

Implications Medical Affairs : This study advances the mechanistic understanding of SGLT2 inhibitors in salt-sensitive hypertension by demonstrating that dapagliflozin lowers BP through pathways dissociated from renal inflammation, strengthening the scientific rationale for SGLT2 inhibition as a distinct antihypertensive mechanism beyond glycemic control and volume reduction.
