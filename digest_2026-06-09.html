Impact of health-promoting text messages on cardiovascular risk: findings from a randomized controlled trial in primary care.

Revue : Frontiers in Medicine | Tier : NA | IF estimé : NA

Date de publication : 2026-05-21

Résultats principaux : This multi-centre randomised controlled trial enrolled 401 Swedish primary care patients with hypertension across several sites; the intervention arm (n=193) received four health-promoting text messages per week for six months targeting healthy lifestyle behaviours, while the control arm (n=208) received standard care only. The primary composite endpoint assessed change in the number of CVD risk factors and improvement in overall CVD risk factor control, analysed by intention-to-treat principles. No statistically significant between-group difference was observed in the primary outcome of overall CVD risk profile improvement; however, both groups reduced their number of CVD risk factors at the six-month follow-up point. Pre-specified subgroup analyses revealed significant improvements in CVD risk factor control in the intervention versus control group among sedentary participants (Z = -2.551, p = 0.01) and those with poor self-rated health (p = 0.041), suggesting the intervention has targeted utility. A modest but statistically significant reduction in HbA1c of -0.61 mmol/L (p = 0.04) was recorded in the intervention arm, though the authors themselves acknowledged limited clinical impact. Key limitations include the absence of blinding (inherent to behavioural trials), potential contamination between arms in shared primary care settings, the relatively short six-month follow-up period, and restricted generalisability to Swedish healthcare system patients with existing hypertension diagnoses.

Implications Medical Affairs : The selective benefit in sedentary patients and those with poor self-rated health supports a precision digital health strategy where text-message-based lifestyle interventions are positioned as adjuncts to antihypertensive pharmacotherapy in clearly defined high-risk subgroups, providing a differentiated value narrative for digital therapeutic partnerships. Medical Affairs teams should monitor this evidence to inform patient support programme design and real-world evidence generation strategies in digital health, especially in markets where payers increasingly require behavioural complement data alongside pharmacological efficacy. KOL engagement priority should focus on primary care and digital health thought leaders to co-develop patient stratification frameworks identifying candidates most likely to benefit from text-based lifestyle interventions, and to design adequately powered and longer-duration follow-up studies.

Association of the planetary health diet score with obesity, high blood pressure, dyslipidemia, and cardiometabolic risk markers: Using data from the 2016-2020 Korea National Health and Nutrition Examination Survey.

Revue : PLoS One | Tier : NA | IF estimé : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-08

Résultats principaux : This large cross-sectional study analysed nationally representative data from 25,336 Korean adults enrolled in the Korea National Health and Nutrition Examination Survey (KNHANES) 2016-2020. The Planetary Health Diet (PHD) score was computed in accordance with the EAT-Lancet Commission guidelines and participants were stratified into quartiles; multivariable logistic regression models examined associations with obesity, hypertension, dyslipidaemia, elevated triglyceride-to-HDL ratio, impaired fasting glucose, and elevated HbA1c. A higher PHD score was significantly associated with lower odds of hypercholesterolaemia in women and with reduced age-stratified prevalence of abdominal obesity, hypertension, low HDL-cholesterolaemia, and elevated HbA1c. No significant associations were identified in men across any of the cardiometabolic risk outcomes, indicating a robust sex-specific dietary effect that warrants further mechanistic investigation. The study is limited by its cross-sectional design — preventing causal inference — dietary data derived from a single 24-hour recall susceptible to recall bias, and uncertainty about whether the PHD scoring system translates validly to the Korean dietary context without cultural adaptation.

Implications Medical Affairs : The sex-specific protective association of a plant-forward sustainable dietary pattern with hypertension and cardiometabolic risk markers in a large Asian cohort provides evidence for dietary intervention messaging targeted at female hypertensive patients, particularly in markets where cardiovascular disease burden in women is growing. For Medical Affairs, this data reinforces lifestyle modification as an evidence-based complement to antihypertensive pharmacotherapy, supporting the development of patient education materials and adherence programmes that integrate dietary scoring tools within hypertension management pathways. Engagement with nutritional epidemiology and preventive cardiology KOLs in Asia-Pacific would strengthen the evidence base for guideline integration of dietary scoring approaches alongside traditional pharmacological treatment algorithms.

Menopausal timing and its determinants among Iranian women: findings from the 2021 national STEPS survey.

Revue : Journal of Diabetes and Metabolic Disorders | Tier : NA | IF estimé : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-04

Résultats principaux : This population-based cross-sectional study analysed data from 14,656 Iranian women aged ≥18 years collected during the 2021 Iran WHO STEPwise approach to NCD risk factor surveillance (STEPS), a nationally representative survey using complex survey sampling. Menopause was classified into four categories: premature ovarian insufficiency (POI, <40 years), early menopause (40-44 years), normal menopause (45-54 years), and late menopause (≥55 years); 32.72% (95% CI: 31.87-33.57) of women were postmenopausal. Among postmenopausal women, 74.37% had normal menopause, 11.15% late menopause, 9.83% early menopause, and 4.65% POI; in adjusted multinomial logistic regression, hypertension was significantly and positively associated with late menopause (≥55 years). Early menopause was associated with higher educational attainment and poor nutritional status, while moderate and good dietary patterns were independently protective against early menopause. Substantial provincial variation in menopause prevalence and timing was documented, highlighting major regional health disparities across Iran. Key limitations include self-reported menopause status and age without biochemical confirmation, cross-sectional design precluding causal inference, and the inability to account for hormonal therapy exposure history.

Implications Medical Affairs : The positive association between hypertension and late menopause in a large national Iranian cohort highlights a cardiovascular risk window during the menopausal transition that supports integration of blood pressure screening and management into women's health care pathways in the MENA region. For Medical Affairs, this evidence can inform guideline advocacy that incorporates menopausal status as a risk stratification variable in hypertension guidelines, creating cross-specialty educational opportunities at the intersection of cardiology and gynaecology. Strategic engagement with gynaecologists, endocrinologists, and women's health KOLs in Iran and neighbouring markets could support development of menopause-hypertension co-management programmes and identification of research collaboration opportunities.

Risk Factors and Awareness of Preeclampsia Among Pregnant Women Attending Antenatal Care in Nasarawa State.

Revue : Public Health Challenges | Tier : NA | IF estimé : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-01

Résultats principaux : This descriptive cross-sectional study recruited 423 pregnant women attending antenatal clinics at selected General Hospitals in Nasarawa State, North-Central Nigeria, using a structured interviewer-administered questionnaire. Participants had a mean age predominantly in the 30-49 year range (60.1%), were mostly multiparous (63.4%), urban-dwelling (88.7%), and had at least secondary education (71.9%); 52.2% initiated ANC before 12 weeks of gestation. Chronic hypertension was significantly associated with preeclampsia (χ² = 66.829, p < 0.001) and diabetes mellitus was equally strongly associated (χ² = 423.000, p < 0.001), establishing these as the two leading modifiable clinical risk factors in this population. Knowledge of preeclampsia was high (85.3%) but attitudes were predominantly neutral (52.0%), revealing an intention-action gap that likely contributes to late presentation and inadequate prevention. Major structural barriers to ANC utilisation included transportation costs (78%), distance to facilities (69%), and low health insurance coverage (22.9%), highlighting systemic access issues beyond individual knowledge gaps. Limitations include cross-sectional design, self-reported data subject to recall and social desirability bias, restricted geographic scope to a single Nigerian state, and potential selection bias from hospital-based convenience sampling.

Implications Medical Affairs : The strong, statistically significant association between chronic hypertension and preeclampsia in a Nigerian hospital setting underscores the critical importance of pre-pregnancy blood pressure control and expanded access to antihypertensive therapies in sub-Saharan African markets, where maternal mortality from hypertensive disorders remains disproportionately high. For Medical Affairs, this study provides real-world evidence supporting guideline advocacy for routine hypertension screening integrated into antenatal care protocols in low-income settings, and can inform global health equity positioning of antihypertensive products in maternal care frameworks. Partnership with maternal-fetal medicine specialists, obstetricians, and public health researchers in Nigeria and West Africa as KOLs would support advocacy efforts and enable contextually appropriate clinical research on hypertension management in pregnancy.

Association of Chronic Diseases With Herpes Zoster in China: A Nationwide Population-Based Survey.

Revue : Health Care Science | Tier : NA | IF estimé : NA

Date de publication : 2026-03-27

Résultats principaux : This population-based cross-sectional study used data from the 2024 China Family Panel Survey, with probability-proportional-to-size sampling covering 25 provincial-level administrative divisions representing 95% of the Chinese mainland population (n=27,868 total; 21,496 adults). A total of 809 adult herpes zoster (HZ) cases were identified; 64.40% (n=521) had at least one chronic condition, and incidence increased progressively with age from 8.07/1000 person-years (18-29 years) to 13.36/1000 person-years (≥70 years). Among 14 chronic diseases analysed by multivariable logistic regression, hypertension was independently associated with elevated HZ risk (aOR = 1.89; 95% CI: 1.47-2.42), ranking third behind heart disease (aOR = 2.91; 95% CI: 2.07-4.10) and diabetes (aOR = 2.04; 95% CI: 1.48-2.80). Female adults with chronic diseases had significantly higher HZ incidence (12.94/1000 PY; 95% CI: 11.45-14.56) than males (8.78/1000 PY; 95% CI: 7.55-10.12), suggesting sex-based immunological differences modify the relationship between chronic disease and HZ susceptibility. Limitations include reliance on self-reported disease diagnoses and HZ cases, potential underdiagnosis in rural areas with limited healthcare access, cross-sectional design preventing causal inference, and inability to account for prior varicella or vaccination history.

Implications Medical Affairs : The significant and quantified association between hypertension and herpes zoster risk in a nationally representative Chinese cohort establishes hypertensive patients as a high-priority target group for preventive vaccination strategies, providing a rationale to advocate for HZ vaccination recommendations integrated into hypertension management guidelines. From a Medical Affairs perspective, this evidence can support cross-specialty educational initiatives bridging cardiology and infectious disease, positioning comprehensive preventive care packages — combining antihypertensive therapy with recommended vaccinations — as a model for integrated chronic disease management in China. KOL engagement should target both cardiologists and immunologists to co-design prospective studies assessing whether optimised blood pressure control attenuates HZ risk, which would provide mechanistic evidence directly relevant to hypertension treatment value arguments.

A comparative study of the gut microbiome and fecal metabolome in hypertensive patients from middle-temperate and tropical cities of China: Daqing and Haikou.

Revue : Frontiers in Microbiology | Tier : NA | IF estimé : NA

Date de publication : 2026-05-22

Résultats principaux : This cross-sectional comparative study enrolled 60 hypertensive patients — 28 from Daqing (middle-temperate climate) and 32 from Haikou (tropical climate) — between May and December 2024 in China, analysing fecal samples via shotgun metagenomic sequencing and untargeted metabolomics. At the genus level, 34 microbial genera differed between the two geographic groups; after adjusting for age, BMI, smoking, and alcohol consumption, 6 genera remained significantly associated with geographic location, and a logistic regression model based on these genera achieved an AUC of 0.8069. Functional pathway analysis indicated that xylene degradation and biofilm formation pathways were relatively reduced in the temperate-climate Daqing group, suggesting climate-driven differences in microbial functional activity in hypertensive patients. Metabolomic profiling identified 38 differentially abundant metabolites including 15-hydroxyeicosatetraenoic acid (15-HETE), 7α,25-dihydroxycholesterol, ginsenoside Rg3, and (3-hydroxypentadecanoyl)lysine, with dysregulated pathways primarily involving glycerophospholipid metabolism, ABC transporters, and choline metabolism. Correlation analysis revealed potential associations between the differential microbial genera and specific metabolite profiles, suggesting a geographically influenced gut-microbiome-metabolome axis in hypertension. Major limitations include the small sample size (n=60 total), single-timepoint cross-sectional design, inability to separate climate from lifestyle-driven microbiome differences, and limited demographic diversity restricting broader generalisability.

Implications Medical Affairs : The identification of geographically and climatically distinct gut microbial and metabolomic signatures in hypertensive patients provides a scientific basis for microbiome-targeted therapeutic strategies in hypertension, including probiotic or dietary interventions personalised to regional environmental context, which represents a nascent but growing area for pharmaceutical innovation. For Medical Affairs, these findings justify investment in longitudinal mechanistic studies examining how microbiome-modulating interventions interact with antihypertensive pharmacotherapy, with potential for microbiome biomarkers to guide personalised treatment selection and positioning. Strategic collaboration with microbiome research KOLs and gastroenterology-cardiology cross-specialty networks in China would be valuable to advance the evidence base and establish the gut-hypertension axis as an actionable therapeutic target in future clinical research programmes.

Cardiovascular risk in seasonal migrant workers in Southern Italy: clinical-epidemiological evaluation.

Revue : Journal of Preventive Medicine and Hygiene | Tier : NA | IF estimé : NA

Date de publication : 2026-02-28

Résultats principaux : This cross-sectional epidemiological study evaluated 135 male seasonal agricultural migrant workers from 12 Central African countries (mean age 37.08 ± 9.8 years) residing in two reception centres in Basilicata, Southern Italy, who voluntarily participated in health screening activities as part of the GOV.E.R.NI and Più Su.Pr.Eme public health projects. Clinical assessments included blood pressure measurement, 12-lead electrocardiogram, spirometry, BMI calculation, and collection of physiological and pathological history. A striking 51.1% of participants had higher-than-optimal overall blood pressure values; specifically, 42.2% had above-normal systolic blood pressure and 11.1% had above-normal diastolic blood pressure, indicating very high hypertension prevalence in this relatively young male cohort. Electrocardiographic abnormalities consistent with pathological alterations were found in 20% of subjects, and 14% had abnormal spirometry traces attributable to a pathological respiratory state; the overweight rate was 30.8% while only 1.5% had grade I obesity. The authors identify that combined risk from elevated blood pressure plus occupational factors — maintaining difficult postures and working in high-heat field conditions during tomato harvesting — constitutes a compounded cardiovascular health risk in this population. Limitations include convenience-based voluntary sampling at two centres, small absolute sample size, restricted geographic scope (single Italian region), and inability to differentiate pre-existing hypertension from occupational or stress-related blood pressure elevation.

Implications Medical Affairs : The prevalence of elevated blood pressure exceeding 51% in a young (mean age 37 years) African migrant agricultural worker population in Italy reveals a highly underserved hypertensive population systematically excluded from routine cardiovascular care and clinical trials, with direct implications for health equity and EU public health mandates. For Medical Affairs, this evidence supports advocacy for targeted hypertension screening programmes in occupationally vulnerable and ethnically diverse populations, and can be used to promote inclusive real-world evidence generation and clinical trial diversity strategies aligned with EMA and EU4Health policy priorities. Engagement with occupational medicine, public health, and migrant health KOLs in Southern Europe would be strategically valuable for developing sustainable intervention models and for aligning product access strategies with EU health equity frameworks.
