Race and Ethnicity, Hypertension, and Neuroimaging Markers of Brain Aging: A Causal Mediation Analysis in the HABS-HD Study.

Revue : Neurology | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-12

Resultats principaux : This prospective cohort study leveraged the Health and Aging Brain Study-Health Disparities (HABS-HD) cohort, a racially diverse sample of 1,347 Black and 1,333 Hispanic/non-Hispanic White (NHW) participants engaged in longitudinal cognitive aging research, using marginal structural models with inverse probability weights to estimate causal mediation by hypertension. Among Black participants (mean age 61 years, 68% female), 75% had hypertension; among Hispanic participants (mean age 63 years, 71% female), 67% were hypertensive. Both groups showed significantly greater white matter hyperintensity volume (WMHV) versus NHW: Black beta 2.08 (95% CI 1.68-2.59) and Hispanic beta 0.99 (95% CI 0.91-1.08). After analytically setting hypertension to absent, the Black-NHW WMHV disparity was attenuated to beta 1.30 (95% CI 1.01-1.65), suggesting partial mediation by hypertension of subclinical cerebrovascular disease. Hypertension did not meaningfully mediate amyloid deposition differences or Alzheimer disease cortical thickness disparities; a key limitation is the observational basis of the counterfactual mediation estimates, which cannot fully exclude residual confounding by socioeconomic or behavioral factors.

Implications Medical Affairs : These data elevate hypertension as a modifiable mediator of racial disparities in subclinical cerebrovascular disease, supporting advocacy for intensive blood pressure control targets in Black and Hispanic populations as a guideline-aligned strategy to reduce dementia inequities. From a Medical Affairs standpoint, antihypertensive therapies with demonstrated cerebrovascular protection may be positioned around brain health endpoints beyond traditional cardiovascular outcomes, particularly to neurology and health-equity KOL audiences. Collaborative research with population-health and cognitive aging investigators could generate supportive evidence for labeling language around racial disparity reduction, a high-priority area in current regulatory science.

Ambulatory hypertension and left ventricular hypertrophy in pediatric chronic kidney disease: a systematic review and meta-analysis.

Revue : European Journal of Pediatrics | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-12

Resultats principaux : This systematic review and meta-analysis (PROSPERO CRD42023426891) included 51 full-text studies comprising 6,509 pediatric patients with chronic kidney disease (CKD) published between 1974 and 2025. The primary endpoints were left ventricular mass index (LVMI) and odds of left ventricular hypertrophy (LVH) compared between normotensive and ambulatorily hypertensive pediatric CKD groups. Ambulatory hypertension was significantly associated with increased LVMI (mean difference 5.15 g/m, 95% CI 3.16-7.15) and a 2.82-fold increased odds of LVH (95% CI 2.18-3.65) versus normotensive controls. Children with 24-hour mean arterial pressure below the 50th percentile had significantly lower LVMI (-8.2 g/m, 95% CI -16.0 to -0.4), as did those with 24-hour systolic BP between the 50th-90th percentiles (-10.8 g/m, 95% CI -20.8 to -0.8), suggesting a dose-response relationship. Significant heterogeneity was noted across studies in design, outcome definitions, and hypertension classification criteria, which limits the strength of pooled estimates and their generalizability across different pediatric age groups and CKD stages.

Implications Medical Affairs : This meta-analysis robustly reinforces ambulatory blood pressure monitoring (ABPM) as the preferred method for detecting hypertensive target organ damage in pediatric CKD, with implications for BP target recommendations in forthcoming pediatric nephrology guidelines and potential ABPM-based trial endpoints in this population. For Medical Affairs, this evidence base supports educational programs directed at pediatric nephrologists and cardiologists emphasizing aggressive BP control and ABPM monitoring in CKD, differentiating antihypertensives with evidence of cardiac regression in pediatric populations. Positioning antihypertensive therapy with demonstrated LVH regression data in pediatric CKD remains an underexplored but strategically valuable differentiation lever, given the high cardiovascular mortality burden in this vulnerable group.

How ominous are persistent pain and hypertension for uncomplicated Type B aortic dissections?

Revue : Seminars in Vascular Surgery | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-04-03

Resultats principaux : This narrative review examined available evidence — primarily observational studies and case series — regarding the prognostic significance of recurrent or persistent pain and uncontrolled hypertension in patients with uncomplicated Type B aortic dissection (TBAD), where medical management with blood pressure control is the established standard of care. The study population encompasses patients presenting with acute TBAD without ascending aortic involvement, malperfusion, or rupture. Available literature indicates that both persistent pain and refractory hypertension may signal progression from uncomplicated to complicated dissection, but evidence is inconsistent across observational cohorts with variable definitions and conflicting results on thresholds for surgical escalation. Thoracic endovascular aortic repair (TEVAR) has emerged as a potential disease-modifying intervention in high-risk uncomplicated TBAD, though the evidence base for this indication remains limited. The authors recommend that persistent pain or hypertension should trigger aggressive evaluation for conversion to complicated dissection rather than serve as automatic surgical triggers; a critical limitation is the complete absence of prospective randomized trial data on symptom-based surgical indications in this setting, with all evidence derived from observational studies and expert opinion.

Implications Medical Affairs : This review underscores that rigorous antihypertensive therapy remains the cornerstone of uncomplicated TBAD management, and that evidence-based BP control protocols are essential to preventing dissection progression, creating a Medical Affairs communications opportunity around guideline-concordant acute hypertension management in vascular emergencies. From a product positioning standpoint, IV and oral antihypertensives with rapid onset and heart-rate-limiting properties are relevant for acute TBAD indications, particularly in the absence of evidence supporting automatic surgical escalation based on BP alone. Future collaboration with vascular surgery and aortic disease KOLs around standardizing BP management algorithms in uncomplicated TBAD could strengthen product positioning in this critical care segment.

Cardiovascular Risk Factors in Greece: Looking Beyond the Classics. A narrative review.

Revue : Hellenic Journal of Cardiology | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-12

Resultats principaux : This narrative review surveys the evolving cardiovascular risk landscape in Greece, characterizing both traditional and non-traditional risk factors in a population where cardiovascular disease remains the leading cause of death despite declining age-standardized mortality in older cohorts. Traditional risk factors — dyslipidemia, hypertension, diabetes, obesity, unhealthy diet, smoking, and physical inactivity — remain highly prevalent and frequently suboptimally controlled, compounded by population aging, the COVID-19 pandemic, persistent socioeconomic pressures, and increasingly stringent ESC lipid and blood pressure targets. Non-traditional determinants reviewed include lipoprotein(a), systemic inflammation, metabolic dysfunction-associated steatotic liver disease (MASLD), chronic kidney disease, chronic obstructive pulmonary disease, sleep disturbances, infections, vaccination, environmental exposures, mental health, and social determinants of health. The authors propose a broader cardiovascular prevention framework that integrates selected non-traditional determinants alongside conventional risk-factor assessment to improve individual risk stratification and inform prevention policy. Limitations include the narrative (non-systematic) design which introduces selection bias in evidence reviewed, and the focus on a single national population limiting generalizability to broader European contexts.

Implications Medical Affairs : The integration of environmental and psychosocial risk factors into cardiovascular risk frameworks aligns with ESC 2024 prevention guidelines and creates opportunities to position antihypertensive therapies within holistic cardiometabolic risk management programs in Southern Europe. For Medical Affairs, this signals a strategic opportunity to support educational initiatives with Greek cardiologists and general practitioners around beyond-traditional risk assessment, particularly leveraging KOL engagement around ESC guideline implementation in real-world clinical practice. Comparative Efficacy of Nutraceuticals on Endothelial Function: A Systematic Review and Network Meta-Analysis.

Revue : Current Atherosclerosis Reports | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-12

Resultats principaux : This network meta-analysis (PROSPERO CRD420251041093) compared the efficacy of multiple dietary interventions in improving flow-mediated dilation (FMD), a validated endothelial function surrogate, in individuals with cardiovascular diseases and hypertension. The most substantial improvements in FMD were observed for magnesium (mean difference 8.17; certainty of evidence: High) and vitamin D (MD: 7.84; certainty: High), followed by flaxseed (MD: 7.39; certainty: Low), barberry (MD: 6.64; certainty: Moderate), folic acid (MD: 3.36; certainty: Low), and omega-3 fatty acids (MD: 1.83; certainty: Very Low). Sensitivity analyses confirmed the robustness of findings for the highest-certainty interventions. The clinical populations studied included individuals with existing cardiovascular disease and hypertension, positioning these nutraceuticals as potential adjunct therapies rather than primary prevention strategies. A key limitation is the highly variable methodological quality and evidence certainty across dietary interventions, with most nutraceuticals receiving low or very low certainty ratings, significantly limiting clinical translation for the majority of agents beyond magnesium and vitamin D.

Implications Medical Affairs : High-certainty evidence for magnesium and vitamin D supplementation on endothelial function in hypertensive populations may gradually influence adjunct non-pharmacological management guidelines, and Medical Affairs teams should monitor this evidence base to contextualize pharmacotherapy positioning relative to nutraceutical alternatives. From a product differentiation perspective, the modest and uncertain FMD improvements for most nutraceuticals versus the robust and reliable blood pressure reduction achieved with pharmacotherapy provides a compelling scientific counterpoint for branded antihypertensive medical communications to cardiologists and endocrinologists. KOL partnerships with vascular biology investigators studying endothelial endpoints could generate comparative real-world data supporting the superiority of pharmacological BP management over dietary supplement regimens in high-risk hypertensive populations.

Acute and chronic kidney injury following COVID-19 infection and vaccination: a narrative review.

Revue : European Journal of Translational Myology | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-11

Resultats principaux : This narrative review examines the spectrum of renal injury associated with COVID-19 infection and vaccination, focusing on mechanisms, risk factors, and clinical outcomes. SARS-CoV-2 entry through the ACE2 receptor — highly expressed in renal tissue — facilitates direct viral renal invasion, with viral RNA detected in urine of infected patients. Acute kidney injury (AKI) occurred in 29-46% of hospitalized COVID-19 patients during early pandemic waves, with key identified risk factors including older age, male sex, diabetes, hypertension, cardiovascular disease, and pre-existing CKD; hypertension was consistently among the most frequently cited comorbidities increasing AKI risk. Histopathological findings included acute tubular injury, collapsing glomerulopathy, and focal segmental glomerulosclerosis. Post-vaccination renal complications, though rare, encompassed minimal change disease (most frequent), IgA nephropathy, membranous nephropathy, anti-GBM nephritis, and ANCA-associated vasculitis. A dose-dependent relationship between CKD stage and adverse COVID-19 outcomes was noted. Limitations include the narrative design susceptible to selection bias, the rapidly evolving COVID-19 literature, and wide variability in AKI incidence across different viral variants and geographic populations.

Implications Medical Affairs : The identification of hypertension as a major risk factor for COVID-19-related AKI reinforces the critical importance of optimized pre-infection blood pressure management, supporting Medical Affairs messaging that controlled hypertension confers renal protective benefits during acute infectious illness and may reduce severe COVID-19 complications. The shared ACE2 receptor biology between COVID-19 pathophysiology and RAAS inhibitor mechanism of action provides ongoing scientific context for Medical Affairs communications reassuring prescribers about the safety and continued use of ACE inhibitors and ARBs during respiratory viral illnesses, a question still raised in clinical practice. Future real-world evidence studies examining long-COVID nephropathy trajectories in hypertensive patients receiving RAAS inhibitor therapy represent a high-value research opportunity for Medical Affairs-sponsored investigator-initiated research programs.

Impact of heatwave exposure on chronic kidney disease risk among middle-aged and older adults: evidence from the China health and retirement longitudinal study.

Revue : BMC Nephrology | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-11

Resultats principaux : This longitudinal study analyzed data from 6,718 participants aged 45 years and older in the China Health and Retirement Longitudinal Study (CHARLS) over a 4-year follow-up period (2011-2015), with kidney function assessed via estimated glomerular filtration rate (eGFR) at baseline and endpoint. Heatwaves were defined using temperature thresholds at the 90th, 95th, and 97.5th percentiles with durations of 2-4 consecutive days. Logistic regression demonstrated consistent positive associations between heatwave exposure and CKD risk across all heatwave definitions, with higher intensity and longer duration showing a dose-response relationship. Geographic clustering analysis identified 'dual high-risk zones' with both high CKD prevalence and prolonged heat exposure, and 'heatwave-dominant zones' with lower CKD prevalence but sustained heat exposure. Significant interactions were observed between high-intensity heatwaves (97.5th percentile, 3-4 days) and pre-existing hypertension, diabetes, and heart disease, identifying these comorbidities as critical effect modifiers. Limitations include the observational design precluding causal inference, administrative heatwave definitions that may not reflect individual heat exposure, and the 2011-2015 follow-up period that predates more recent climate extremes.

Implications Medical Affairs : The identification of hypertension as a significant effect modifier of climate-related renal injury positions optimal blood pressure control as an evidence-based preventive strategy against climate-amplified CKD progression in aging populations, a novel and compelling argument for antihypertensive therapy relevant to Medical Affairs communications in the context of climate health. This intersection of climate science and cardiorenal medicine opens high-profile research opportunities for Medical Affairs-sponsored real-world evidence generation on whether antihypertensive therapy attenuates climate-related AKI risk in high-risk elderly patients, a scientifically differentiated narrative for advocacy before nephrologists and public health authorities. Engaging KOLs at the climate medicine-nephrology intersection, particularly in Asian markets with aging populations and increasing heatwave burden, could position the Medical Affairs function as a forward-thinking contributor to emerging preventive medicine paradigms.

Adherence to the DASH Diet in the Spanish Population and Its Environmental Impact: An Ecological Study.

Revue : Nutrients | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-05

Resultats principaux : This ecological epidemiological study analyzed DASH (Dietary Approaches to Stop Hypertension) dietary adherence and its relationship with environmental sustainability in Spain from 2006 to 2023, using Spanish Household Budget Survey data from approximately 24,000 randomly selected households annually. DASH adherence was scored using a standard 80-point methodology derived from daily food servings; environmental impact was quantified through the Ecological Footprint (EF) methodology using the Agribalyse3.2 database. Over the 17-year study period, DASH adherence increased by 8.26%, peaking in 2020. The overall EF decreased significantly, largely driven by reduced consumption of meat, fish, and eggs. A strong inverse correlation was found between the annual DASH score and EF (r = -0.8237, 95% CI: -0.932 to -0.580; p < 0.001), indicating that increased DASH adherence was associated with lower environmental impact. Temporal trends were assessed using segmented regression models selected by AIC and the Davies test. Limitations include the ecological design precluding individual-level causal inference, reliance on household expenditure surveys rather than individual dietary recalls, and confounding by unmeasured socioeconomic factors affecting food purchasing behaviors.

Implications Medical Affairs : The demonstrated alignment between DASH adherence and environmental sustainability supports positioning non-pharmacological hypertension management within broader public health and planetary health frameworks, which may resonate with HTA bodies and European payers increasingly incorporating environmental considerations into value assessments for preventive health programs. Revue : Nutrients | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-04

Resultats principaux : This prospective analysis included 53,269 participants aged 30-79 years from the China Kadoorie Biobank (CKB) Suzhou Wuzhong subcohort, with baseline diet assessed via food frequency questionnaire and three dietary clusters identified by K-means analysis: a Traditional/Preserved-Heavy cluster, a Meat-Centric cluster (reference), and a Plant-and-Dairy-Abundant cluster. BMI modified the T2D all-cause mortality association (p interaction = 0.033). Limitations include the observational design limiting causal inference, reliance on self-reported baseline dietary data without repeat assessments, and potential unmeasured socioeconomic and lifestyle confounding.

Implications Medical Affairs : The strong association between plant-and-dairy-abundant dietary patterns and reduced CVD and all-cause mortality in hypertensive and diabetic patients reinforces the complementarity of dietary intervention and pharmacotherapy in comprehensive cardiometabolic risk reduction, providing a scientific basis for Medical Affairs communications emphasizing holistic hypertension management beyond blood pressure control alone. Efficacy and Safety of Sakurajima Radish in Patients with Metabolic Syndrome: A Phase IIb Randomized, Three-Period Crossover Trial.

Revue : Nutrients | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-03

Resultats principaux : This multicenter, open-label, randomized three-period crossover phase IIb trial enrolled 21 patients with metabolic syndrome to evaluate trigonelline-rich Sakurajima radish on vascular endothelial function over three 14-day sequences with 14-day washout periods. The primary outcome was flow-mediated dilation (FMD); secondary outcomes included blood pressure, urinary nitric oxide metabolites (NOx), and the oxidative stress marker 8-hydroxy-2'-deoxyguanosine (8-OHdG). Sakurajima radish did not improve FMD versus the usual diet (p = 0.58) or Aokubi radish (p = 0.59), with a significant negative carryover effect from the Aokubi period likely confounding the primary outcome. Despite successfully stimulating nitric oxide (NO) production (elevated urinary NOx, p = 0.03), the intervention paradoxically increased oxidative stress (elevated 8-OHdG/creatinine, p = 0.02) and significantly elevated systolic blood pressure vs. usual diet (+9.67 mmHg, p = 0.03) and Aokubi radish (+8.86 mmHg, p = 0.04). Limitations include the very small sample size (n=21) substantially limiting statistical power, the open-label design precluding blinding, the susceptibility of the crossover design to carryover effects, and the 14-day intervention duration that may be insufficient for detecting functional vascular improvements in metabolic syndrome.

Implications Medical Affairs : The paradoxical finding that a dietary NO-boosting functional food elevated systolic BP in metabolic syndrome — a pro-oxidant environment — highlights the complexity and potential safety risks of nutraceutical interventions for hypertension, and provides Medical Affairs with compelling scientific evidence reinforcing the superiority and predictability of pharmacological antihypertensive therapy in this population. These null or adverse results for a marketed nutraceutical BP claim support the regulatory and scientific position that pharmacotherapy remains the evidence-based gold standard for hypertension management in metabolic syndrome, directly relevant for label differentiation discussions with prescribers and HTA value dossiers. This study provides robust talking-point data for medical information responses to physicians and pharmacists receiving patient inquiries about functional food supplements as alternatives to antihypertensive medications in metabolic syndrome.

Real-World Associations Between Physical Activity, LDL Cholesterol, and Functional Performance in Primary Care: A Cross-Sectional Study.

Revue : Healthcare (Basel) | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-05-30

Resultats principaux : This cross-sectional observational study enrolled 863 adult primary care patients (mean age 52.4 +/- 14.8 years, 53.3% female) evaluated between February 2021 and March 2026, with physical activity (PA) self-categorized as low, moderate, or high during routine clinical evaluation. Higher PA categories were significantly associated with lower LDL-C concentrations across all groups (p < 0.001): mean LDL-C was 3.68 +/- 1.05 mmol/L in the low PA group, 3.39 +/- 0.97 mmol/L in the moderate group, and 3.12 +/- 0.89 mmol/L in the high PA group, representing a dose-response pattern. Hypertension was included as a covariate and controlled for in the functional performance analysis, confirming that PA associations remained independent of hypertensive status. Limitations include the cross-sectional design precluding causal inference, self-reported PA subject to recall bias, absence of data on lipid-lowering medication use and other cardiovascular confounders, and single-center design limiting generalizability.

Implications Medical Affairs : Although primarily focused on LDL-C and functional performance, this real-world primary care study reinforces physical activity as a modifiable cardiometabolic risk modifier in populations where hypertension is a highly prevalent comorbidity, supporting Medical Affairs messaging around integrated lifestyle counseling as an adjunct to pharmacotherapy for comprehensive cardiovascular risk management. The primary care setting and real-world methodology of this study make findings directly relevant for developing educational programs targeting general practitioners and internists who routinely manage hypertension alongside dyslipidemia and functional decline, particularly in aging European populations. KOL partnerships with primary care medicine and preventive cardiology specialists could leverage these real-world data to advocate for PA assessment as a routine component of hypertension management protocols in primary care guidelines.

Risk Factors for Postoperative Complications in Different Fusion Surgical Approaches for Lumbar Degenerative Diseases.

Revue : Journal of Clinical Medicine | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-05-29

Resultats principaux : This retrospective cohort study enrolled 203 patients who underwent lumbar fusion surgery (PLIF, PLF, or Hybrid) for lumbar degenerative diseases at a single institution, with 80 complications in the PLIF group, 64 in the Hybrid group, and 59 in the PLF group. Primary outcomes were cage migration (CM) and adjacent segment degeneration (ASD). Logistic regression identified elevated BMI (PLIF OR 1.18, 95% CI 1.05-4.38; Hybrid OR 1.14, 95% CI 1.07-2.54), osteoporosis (OR 5.62-7.62 across groups), advanced superior adjacent disc degeneration (OR 2.87-8.04 across groups), prolonged operative time, and increased intraoperative blood loss as independent risk factors for postoperative complications. Of direct relevance to hypertension medicine, hypertension was explicitly evaluated as a risk factor and was not found to be a statistically significant independent predictor of surgical complications in any of the three fusion groups (p > 0.05). Similarly, age, sex, smoking, diabetes mellitus, and several inflammatory biomarkers were not independent predictors of CM or ASD in this series. Limitations include the retrospective single-center design with selection bias risk, relatively small subgroup sample sizes, and absence of long-term follow-up beyond the index surgical admission.

Implications Medical Affairs : The finding that hypertension was not an independent predictor of lumbar surgical complications, when presumably controlled perioperatively, provides Medical Affairs with reassuring safety data supporting the perioperative continuation of antihypertensive therapy in orthopedic surgical patients and reinforcing that optimized blood pressure control mitigates hypertension as a surgical risk modifier. This evidence supports Medical Affairs messaging to anesthesiologists and orthopedic surgeons regarding the safety of continued antihypertensive regimens in the perioperative period, particularly for newer agent classes whose perioperative safety profile is often questioned by surgical colleagues.
