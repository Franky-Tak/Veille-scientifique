Guide from the Colegio Mexicano de Reumatologia for the Pharmacological Treatment of Systemic Sclerosis Part 1: Cutaneous Involvement, Interstitial Lung Disease, and Pulmonary Arterial Hypertension.

Revue : Reumatologia Clinica (Engl Ed) | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-10

Resultats principaux : This manuscript presents the first national pharmacological treatment guideline from the Colegio Mexicano de Reumatologia for systemic sclerosis (SSc), covering cutaneous involvement, interstitial lung disease, and pulmonary arterial hypertension (PAH) — a condition characterized by fibrosis, inflammation, and vasculopathy with high morbidity and mortality. The guideline was developed by a multidisciplinary panel using GRADE methodology, with a systematic search across PubMed, Cochrane Library, EMBASE, and LILACS (2006 to February 2024) that identified 594 records, of which 114 studies were ultimately included. From this evidence base, 26 recommendations were issued covering steroids, immunosuppressants, immunomodulators, hematopoietic stem cell transplantation, and vasodilators for the three disease domains. All 26 recommendations underwent Delphi consensus with agreement defined as at least 80% acceptance from the expert panel. Unlike international guidelines, this proposal prioritizes clinical applicability and context-specific decision-making through a flexible, stepwise therapeutic algorithm adapted to the heterogeneous resources and treatment access within the Mexican healthcare system — a key limitation that restricts direct generalizability to other settings.

Implications Medical Affairs : This guideline establishes a formalized evidence-based framework for PAH treatment within SSc in a major Latin American market, creating a strategic opportunity for pharmaceutical companies with vasodilator portfolios to engage with Mexican rheumatology and pulmonology KOLs around guideline adherence and product positioning within the stepwise algorithm. The emphasis on resource-adapted decision-making highlights an unmet need for health-economic data supporting targeted vasodilator therapy in settings with limited treatment access, making this a key document for Market Access and Medical Affairs teams active in Latin America. Companies should consider sponsoring multidisciplinary educational activities aligned with these 26 recommendations and initiating scientific exchanges with the guideline panelists to strengthen brand visibility in this emerging market segment.

Transforming hemodialysis care: a tripartite collaboration model among medical staff, AI agents, and robots.

Revue : Clinical and Experimental Nephrology | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-11

Resultats principaux : This narrative review from St. Marianna University (Japan) proposes a tripartite collaboration model for hemodialysis care redesign involving medical staff, AI agents, and robots, developed in response to rising dialysis demand driven by aging populations and increasing CKD burden alongside persistent workforce constraints. The model is not derived from a clinical trial but conceptually redesigns hemodialysis workflows through three AI modules: "Eye" for multimodal data integration from dialysis machines, EHRs, labs, and wearables; "Brain" for machine-learning-based complication prediction including intradialytic hypotension and dry-weight optimization; and "Language" using large language models for structured clinical documentation with mandatory clinician review. Robots are proposed for equipment preparation, transport, and potentially robotic ultrasound-guided vascular access surveillance, with clinicians retaining accountability for all value-laden decisions and authorization of automated outputs. No primary quantitative data or statistical endpoints are reported, as this is a conceptual framework paper. Key limitations include absence of prospective clinical validation, reliance on theoretical governance frameworks, and acknowledged risks of AI hallucinations, interoperability challenges, and cybersecurity vulnerabilities in the proposed architecture.

Implications Medical Affairs : Although conceptual, this paper signals a directional shift in nephrology care toward AI-assisted management of CKD complications including hypertension-related end-organ damage, creating an early engagement opportunity for Medical Affairs teams supporting renal-protective antihypertensive agents at the interface of digital health and nephrology. The intradialytic hypotension prediction module is directly relevant to antihypertensive medication management in dialysis patients, and companies with medications impacting hemodynamic stability should monitor operationalization of such models in Japan as early reference centers for real-world hypertension data in dialysis cohorts. Medical Affairs should prioritize scientific engagement with nephrology digital health KOLs to ensure antihypertensive treatment protocols are appropriately integrated into any AI-guided dialysis care frameworks as they are validated and rolled out.

The association between triglyceride-glucose index and major adverse cardiovascular and cerebrovascular events in patients with heart failure.

Revue : Journal of Health, Population, and Nutrition | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-10

Resultats principaux : This single-center retrospective cohort study enrolled 927 patients with heart failure (HF) from Bishan Hospital of Chongqing Medical University (China) and assessed the association between the triglyceride-glucose (TyG) index — a surrogate marker of insulin resistance — and major adverse cardiovascular and cerebrovascular events (MACCE), which occurred in 80 patients (8.6%). Multivariable Cox regression, adjusted for lifestyle factors, comorbidities, treatments, and echocardiographic parameters, demonstrated that each 1-unit increase in TyG index independently increased MACCE risk by 85.4% (HR 1.854, 95% CI 1.336–2.572, P less than 0.001), and patients in the highest TyG quartile (Q4) had a 3.679-fold higher MACCE risk compared to Q1 (HR 3.679, 95% CI 1.702–7.956, P=0.001). Subgroup analyses revealed that the association was significantly more pronounced in females, patients aged over 75 years, those with chronic HF, and those with comorbid hypertension (P for interaction 0.021 for sex and 0.035 for hypertension subgroup). ROC analysis showed a modest discriminative ability (AUC=0.587), Kaplan-Meier curves demonstrated significant event-free survival differences across quartiles (Log-rank P=0.025), and restricted cubic spline analysis confirmed a significant positive linear dose-response relationship. The retrospective single-center design and potential unmeasured confounders represent the primary limitations of this study.

Implications Medical Affairs : The finding that TyG index — a simple, universally available metabolic marker — independently predicts MACCE risk with particular amplification in hypertensive HF patients (P for interaction 0.035) reinforces the cardiometabolic continuum framework that supports integrated antihypertensive-metabolic therapeutic strategies. For Medical Affairs, the hypertension subgroup interaction finding creates a scientific narrative to inform product dossiers and KOL advisory board discussions on risk stratification tools for antihypertensive therapies in the HF population, particularly in Asian markets where insulin resistance clustering is prevalent. Companies with antihypertensive agents demonstrating metabolic benefit should actively incorporate TyG index as an exploratory biomarker endpoint in future cardiovascular outcomes trials to capture the full risk modification profile.

Associations of genetic variants in TCF7L2, MC4R, AGT and ACE genes with cardiometabolic diseases in Black South Africans.

Revue : BMC Medical Genomics | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-10

Resultats principaux : This cross-sectional study from the Cape Peninsula University of Technology investigated associations between eight SNPs in TCF7L2, MC4R, AGT, AGT1R, FTO, and ACE genes with type 2 diabetes (T2D), obesity, and hypertension in 560 Black South Africans aged 25–74 years, a population historically underrepresented in cardiometabolic genomics research. Multiple hypothesis testing was controlled using the Benjamini-Hochberg FDR method; systolic blood pressure was significantly higher in carriers of the MC4R-rs229616 G/G genotype compared to G/A carriers (p=0.006, p-FDR=0.030). The MC4R-rs17782313 C/C genotype was associated with significantly higher odds of hypertension in the unadjusted model (OR=1.37, p=0.023) but this association was attenuated after adjusting for age, sex, and BMI (p-FDR=0.140), suggesting partial mediation through adiposity. TCF7L2-rs7903146 C/C genotype was associated with significantly lower T2D risk in both crude (OR=0.66, p=0.043) and adjusted (OR=0.63, p=0.041, p-FDR=0.108) models. The cross-sectional design, modest sample size of 560 participants, and lack of prospective longitudinal follow-up are the primary limitations of this study.

Implications Medical Affairs : The identification of MC4R and TCF7L2 SNPs as potential pharmacogenomic markers for hypertension and T2D risk in Black African populations provides novel evidence supporting precision medicine approaches to antihypertensive therapy in sub-Saharan Africa, a region with rapidly rising hypertension burden. For Medical Affairs, these findings underscore the critical importance of ethnicity-stratified genomic data in future clinical trial designs, supporting advocacy for inclusion of African patient cohorts in phase 3 hypertension studies to strengthen global product labeling and enable regulatory submissions for African markets. Companies with RAAS-targeting antihypertensive agents should monitor downstream research on AGT and AGT1R variant pathways, as these may yield predictive biomarkers for treatment response optimization in diverse African patient populations.

Dietary intake, fatty acid in adipose tissue and cardio-metabolic health: cross-sectional study from the TwinsUK cohort.

Revue : Nutrition and Diabetes | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-10

Resultats principaux : This cross-sectional study from the TwinsUK cohort (King's College London) quantified 18 subcutaneous adipose tissue fatty acid proportions in 569 participants alongside eight dietary pattern scores and clinical cardiometabolic phenotypes including adiposity, lipids, glycemic markers, and cardiovascular risk factors. The DASH diet showed a specific negative correlation with adipose arachidonic acid (Beta=-0.09, 95% CI -0.17 to -0.006, P=0.04) and adrenic acid (Beta=-0.09, 95% CI -0.18 to -0.004, P=0.04), while most other dietary scores positively correlated with overall polyunsaturated fatty acid content. The Healthy Diet Indicator showed the broadest associations across adipose fatty acid profiles. Mediation analysis demonstrated that adipose arachidonic acid proportion mediated approximately 24.7% of the total effect of the DASH diet on visceral-to-gluteofemoral fat ratio, providing biological mechanistic support for the diet's cardiometabolic benefits. Key limitations include the cross-sectional design precluding causal inference, the predominantly female TwinsUK cohort limiting generalizability to male and non-European populations, and the absence of prospective follow-up data linking adipose fatty acid changes to incident hypertension events.

Implications Medical Affairs : The demonstration that the DASH diet — specifically recommended in major hypertension guidelines — measurably reduces pro-inflammatory arachidonic acid in adipose tissue reinforces the biological plausibility of dietary interventions as adjunctive antihypertensive strategies, strengthening the scientific narrative for combination pharmacological-lifestyle approaches. For Medical Affairs, the mediating role of arachidonic acid on visceral fat distribution provides a mechanistic framework to enrich antihypertensive clinical trial protocols with adipose tissue biomarker endpoints, enabling differentiated product positioning at the lifestyle-drug interaction interface. Advisory board agendas and KOL engagement programs should incorporate this mechanistic evidence to elevate discussions beyond blood pressure lowering to full cardiometabolic risk reduction, particularly for guideline-concordant combination therapy strategies.

Date de publication : 2026-06-10

Resultats principaux : This clinically-oriented narrative review from the Mayo Clinic (Jacksonville, USA) synthesizes published evidence on the use of cystatin C as a complementary kidney function marker in hospitalized adults, with particular emphasis on scenarios where serum creatinine is unreliable due to variations in muscle mass, diet, medications, or fluid status. The review covers a broad inpatient population without restriction to a specific cohort, summarizing that cystatin C is less influenced by non-renal factors and may detect changes in glomerular filtration more rapidly than creatinine, thereby improving recognition of acute kidney injury and refining CKD staging. Published evidence reviewed also supports cystatin C's prognostic utility in heart failure, where elevated levels have been independently associated with higher risks of mortality and hospitalization in multiple published cohorts. No novel quantitative data or primary statistical endpoints are reported, as the study design is a narrative review; secondary endpoints and subgroup analyses from cited studies are summarized qualitatively. Principal limitations are the non-systematic narrative review methodology, the potential for publication bias in cited studies, and the absence of head-to-head clinical trial data comparing cystatin C to creatinine specifically in hypertensive inpatient populations.

Implications Medical Affairs : This review reinforces the clinical utility of cystatin C for refining kidney function assessment in hypertensive patients with comorbid CKD or heart failure, directly relevant to Medical Affairs teams supporting renal-protective antihypertensive agents where accurate GFR estimation is essential for patient selection, dosing, and treatment monitoring. The well-summarized prognostic value of elevated cystatin C in heart failure provides a scientific rationale for its formal inclusion as a secondary or exploratory endpoint in future antihypertensive cardiovascular outcomes trials, particularly for products targeting the cardiorenal syndrome. Plasma vitamin C levels are associated with brain structural networks on MRI: A large cohort study.

Revue : PloS One | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-10

Resultats principaux : This large cross-sectional cohort study from Hirosaki University (Japan) enrolled 2,044 older adults (median age 69 years, 61.1% female) who underwent 3T MRI brain scanning with quantification of total intracranial volume, gray matter volume (GMV), and white matter volume (WMV), alongside default mode network (DMN) connectivity assessed by independent component analysis. Multiple regression analyses — adjusted for age, sex, MMSE score, diabetes, hypertension, hyperlipidemia, education, and lifestyle factors (smoking, alcohol, physical activity) — demonstrated that low plasma vitamin C levels were significantly and independently associated with a lower GMV/ICV ratio (p less than 0.001) and reduced DMN connectivity (p less than 0.001). The inclusion of hypertension as a covariate confirmed that the vitamin C-brain structure association persisted independently of blood pressure status, adding specificity to the nutritional brain health hypothesis. No formal subgroup analysis by hypertension status was performed to quantify effect modification, which represents a methodological limitation. The cross-sectional design precludes causal inference, plasma vitamin C was measured at a single timepoint without longitudinal dietary assessment, and the study population was exclusively Japanese adults limiting generalizability.

Implications Medical Affairs : The confirmation that low plasma vitamin C is independently associated with reduced gray matter volume and DMN connectivity in older adults — after full adjustment for hypertension — adds mechanistic evidence to the growing literature linking nutritional antioxidant status to hypertension-related cerebrovascular damage, relevant to the cognitive protection narrative for antihypertensive therapies. For Medical Affairs, this finding supports positioning antihypertensive agents within a broader neuroprotection framework, as hypertensive patients with low antioxidant status may face compounded risk of structural brain damage and cognitive decline that standard blood pressure endpoints do not capture. Companies conducting phase 3 or phase 4 trials in older hypertensive populations should consider integrating brain MRI-based structural endpoints and antioxidant biomarkers as exploratory measures to demonstrate the full cerebroprotective benefit profile of effective blood pressure control.

'Bringing services to communities: Identifying service users at risk of developing cardiovascular disease through mobile opportunistic screening in deprived or underserved communities'.

Revue : Public Health in Practice | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2025-12-13

Resultats principaux : This retrospective cross-sectional study analyzed data from 2,973 adults (aged 18 years and above) attending a mobile opportunistic CVD risk screening clinic across deprived communities in Cheshire and Merseyside, UK, from January to June 2023. Among the total study population, 79% had at least one modifiable cardiovascular risk factor identified at screening, illustrating the substantial unmet burden in this underserved segment. Multivariable logistic regression demonstrated that, among modifiable risk factors, only hypertension (as detected or self-reported) and mental health diagnosis were significant independent predictors of CVD, while non-modifiable factors age and sex also reached significance with males showing higher odds of CVD. The primary intervention focus was screening delivery — specific blood pressure thresholds used, antihypertensive medication uptake rates post-screening, and long-term follow-up outcomes were not reported in this dataset. Key limitations include the absence of post-screening treatment outcomes, BMI excluded from multivariable analysis due to missing data, reliance on self-reported medical history for several covariates, and the short six-month observational window that limits assessment of screening program sustainability.

Implications Medical Affairs : The identification of hypertension as the primary modifiable CVD predictor in socioeconomically deprived UK communities — with 79% of screened individuals carrying at least one modifiable risk factor — quantifies the gap between hypertension prevalence and effective blood pressure control in populations least likely to access standard primary care, directly relevant to real-world antihypertensive effectiveness challenges. For Medical Affairs, this paper provides a compelling real-world evidence framework for supporting community-based hypertension screening programs as part of brand campaigns or patient support programs targeting high-risk urban populations in the UK and Europe. Engagement with NHS commissioners, public health KOLs, and community pharmacy networks around mobile screening model replication represents a concrete Medical Affairs action to enhance population-level reach of antihypertensive therapy initiation in underserved communities.

Tanzania's NCD Transition: A Comparative Analysis of Healthy Lifestyle Behaviors for Hypertension Prevention Between Rural and Urban Adults.

Revue : Inquiry: A Journal of Medical Care Organization, Provision and Financing | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-11

Resultats principaux : This analytical cross-sectional study enrolled 838 adults from contrasting rural (Kilombero District, n not disaggregated) and urban (Morogoro Municipal Council) settings in Tanzania's Morogoro Region, using a composite healthy lifestyle index (HLI) incorporating BMI, physical activity, smoking, alcohol use, and fruit and vegetable intake to characterize hypertension prevention behaviors. High healthy lifestyle adoption was significantly more prevalent in rural Kilombero (55.5%) than urban Morogoro (47.0%, p=0.017), documenting a statistically significant rural health advantage that challenges conventional NCD risk assumptions. In rural areas, HLI adoption varied independently by income, employment, and age, while in urban settings income was the primary socio-demographic determinant of HLI adoption; stratified multivariable logistic regression models were used to estimate adjusted odds ratios with 95% confidence intervals for each setting. The findings suggest that urbanization-associated behavioral transitions — reduced physical activity, increased processed food consumption, alcohol use — may already be emerging in rural Kilombero, eroding the rural lifestyle advantage. Limitations include the cross-sectional design precluding causal inference, the composite HLI potentially masking individual behavior variation, absence of blood pressure measurements to directly link HLI to hypertension prevalence, and restricted regional generalizability across Tanzania's diverse geographic and ethnic landscape.

Implications Medical Affairs : The documentation of a measurable rural healthy lifestyle advantage in an East African country undergoing NCD transition challenges standard rural-urban hypertension risk assumptions and provides epidemiological data that can inform differentiated hypertension prevention and market access strategies in sub-Saharan Africa. For Medical Affairs, these findings argue for context-specific patient support programs that preserve protective behaviors in rural settings while prioritizing healthy food access and physical activity infrastructure in urban areas, directly supporting country-level NCD prevention plans where pharmaceutical companies seek to demonstrate societal value beyond pill dispensing. Companies with antihypertensive portfolios targeting African market expansion should leverage these urban-rural behavioral differences to inform KOL engagement, patient advocacy partnerships with Tanzanian and regional public health bodies, and economic modeling of prevention versus treatment cost offsets.
