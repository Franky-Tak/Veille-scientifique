[Lu]Lu-dota-tate versus sunitinib in patients with metastatic progressive neuroendocrine tumours of the pancreas (OCLURANDOM): a randomised, controlled, phase 2 trial

Revue : The Lancet. Oncology | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06

Resultats principaux : OCLURANDOM is a randomised, open-label, non-comparative phase 2 trial conducted across 10 French academic centres, enrolling 84 patients (41 in the DOTATATE arm and 43 in the sunitinib arm) with pretreated somatostatin receptor-positive metastatic pancreatic neuroendocrine tumours (NETs); the primary endpoint was progression-free survival (PFS) at 12 months, which was achieved at 80.5% for DOTATATE (90% CI 67.5-89.9) versus 41.9% for sunitinib (90% CI 29.1-55.5), representing a clinically meaningful separation between arms. The key safety finding directly relevant to hypertension management was grade 3-4 hypertension occurring in 10% of DOTATATE-treated patients compared with 19% of sunitinib-treated patients, reflecting the known VEGF-pathway-mediated hypertensive toxicity of sunitinib. Overall grade 3-4 adverse events were substantially more frequent in the sunitinib arm (72%) than the DOTATATE arm (44%), and patient-reported Global Health Status scores were 10.3 points better with DOTATATE (95% CI 2.4-18.2), highlighting the quality-of-life advantage of radionuclide therapy. Late adverse events of grade 2 or higher occurred in 60% of DOTATATE patients, reflecting the delayed haematologic and renal toxicity profile of lutetium-based therapy, and one treatment-related death from acute leukemia was observed in the DOTATATE arm. The study was non-comparative by design and limited by its small size (84 patients), open-label conduct, and single-country enrollment in French academic centres, which may limit generalisability to real-world practice settings where patient selection and supportive care infrastructure differ.

Implications Medical Affairs : Although OCLURANDOM does not evaluate antihypertensive agents directly, it reinforces the superior cardiovascular safety profile of [177Lu]Lu-DOTATATE over sunitinib, with nearly half the rate of grade 3-4 hypertension events, which is directly relevant when advising oncology teams on the management of hypertension as a limiting toxicity of VEGF-targeted therapies. From a Medical Affairs positioning standpoint, this trial strengthens the argument for radionuclide therapy as a first-line option in somatostatin receptor-positive pancreatic NETs, reducing the need for intensive antihypertensive co-management that sunitinib typically demands, and provides KOLs with robust clinical evidence to frame lutetium therapy not only as more efficacious but also more hypertension-friendly. KOL engagement strategy should include a focused scientific exchange with oncology-cardiology teams to build interdisciplinary consensus around the cardiovascular risk management implications of this trial, particularly the differential hypertension burden across treatment modalities.

Urine glyphosate levels and kidney function outcomes in a cohort study of workers in El Salvador and Nicaragua

Revue : Journal of Exposure Science & Environmental Epidemiology | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-02

Resultats principaux : This prospective cohort study from the Mesoamerican Nephropathy Occupational Study enrolled 285 male agricultural workers aged 18 to 45 in El Salvador and Nicaragua, with urine glyphosate and its metabolite AMPA quantified by HPLC-MS/MS at enrollment and one year later; a key methodological context is that Mesoamerican nephropathy is explicitly defined as not sharing the conventional CKD risk factors of diabetes and hypertension, making it a distinct agrochemical-driven entity. Higher glyphosate urinary levels were associated with a lower eGFR over one year in the lower-exposure group, with a mean difference of -4.0 mL/min/1.73m2 (95% CI -7.2 to -0.87), suggesting a dose-related nephrotoxic signal even at lower concentrations. Paradoxically, workers with detectable glyphosate at both timepoints showed a protective association for 10% eGFR decline (OR 0.33, 95% CI 0.16-0.65), an artefact attributed to healthy worker bias whereby those remaining in agrochemical roles are healthier survivors. Having an agrochemical job was the strongest predictor of 20% eGFR decline (OR 1.7, 95% CI 1.3-2.3), emphasising that occupational context, not simply a single biomarker, drives nephropathy risk. Key limitations include the exclusively male, young workforce (not representative of general CKD populations), the single-biomarker focus, and the absence of blood pressure or diabetes as confounders since they are explicitly excluded from the Mesoamerican nephropathy framework.

Implications Medical Affairs : While hypertension is explicitly excluded as a risk factor in this Mesoamerican nephropathy model, this study is important for Medical Affairs because it underscores the existence of a CKD subtype where conventional antihypertensive risk-stratification frameworks do not apply, which has implications for labelling, indication boundaries, and real-world evidence strategies in agricultural populations in Central America. The identification of glyphosate as a potential nephrotoxic agent in a hypertension-independent pathway calls for Medical Affairs to monitor emerging regulatory and epidemiological discussions around environmental determinants of kidney disease, which could indirectly affect prescribing patterns and nephrologist positioning of renoprotective antihypertensives. KOL engagement with occupational health and nephrology thought leaders in Latin America would be strategic to anticipate how this growing body of evidence might reframe hypertension management guidelines in the context of mixed CKD etiologies.

A 10-Year Review of Back Injury Amongst Republic of Singapore Air Force Fighter Aircrew

Revue : Aerospace Medicine and Human Performance | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06

Resultats principaux : This retrospective case-control study examined Singapore Air Force F-16 and F-15 fighter aircrew over a 10-year period (2014-2024), identifying 26 cases of back injury matched 1:3 to 78 controls, for a total study population of 104 individuals. The primary findings were that BMI of 25 or greater was a statistically significant risk factor for back injury, while exercise frequency of three or more times per week was a statistically significant protective factor. Critically for hypertension surveillance, hypertension along with hyperlipidemia, smoking, alcohol use, depression, and anxiety were all tested and found to be non-significant associations with back injury in this population. The study thus provides negative evidence for hypertension as a musculoskeletal injury risk factor in a highly selected, high-fitness occupational cohort, which is methodologically informative but not generalisable. The principal limitation is the very small sample size (N=104), which substantially reduces statistical power to detect modest effects, and the retrospective single-centre design limits external validity beyond the Singapore Air Force aircrew context.

Implications Medical Affairs : The non-significant association between hypertension and back injury in this elite occupational cohort adds a minor but useful data point to the broader Medical Affairs narrative that hypertension does not invariably cluster with all musculoskeletal risks, and reinforces the importance of fitness and BMI management as independent targets distinct from blood pressure control. For Medical Affairs strategy, this study is low direct impact but relevant when advising occupational medicine or aviation medicine clients on the specificity of cardiovascular risk factor profiling in high-performance athletes and military personnel. KOL engagement value is limited, but the findings may be cited in discussions with occupational health specialists to contextualise why antihypertensive therapy alone does not substitute for structured physical conditioning programmes in physically demanding professions.

An International, Multi-Specialty Consensus Document on the Optimal Management of Patients with Internal Carotid Artery Occlusion

Revue : Journal of Vascular Surgery | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-01

Resultats principaux : This international consensus document combined a systematic review of 10,588 PubMed, Scopus, and Embase records from 1980 to 2025 (yielding 28 included studies on internal carotid artery occlusion, ICAO) with a formal consensus process involving a 60-member voting panel of US and European specialists across multiple disciplines; consensus was defined as 75% agreement or more, achieved in 11 of 17 statements. The most directly relevant finding for antihypertensive therapy was that 98.3% of panel members agreed that optimal medical therapy, explicitly including antihypertensives, antiplatelets, statins, and glycemic control, constitutes the cornerstone of ICAO management. Additionally, 93.3% agreed that ICAO represents a marker of systemic advanced atherosclerosis, and 100% agreed that lifestyle and metabolic risk factor optimisation, specifically targeting smoking, weight, diet, and exercise, is strongly recommended. A 98.3% consensus was also reached on duplex ultrasound as the first-line diagnostic modality, and 93.3% agreed on the need for a global ICAO registry. The study's limitations include the inherent subjectivity and potential conflicts of interest within consensus methodology, the heterogeneous quality of included studies, and the fact that 11 of 17 statements did not reach the predefined consensus threshold, reflecting genuine clinical uncertainty in several management areas.

Implications Medical Affairs : This high-visibility international consensus document directly endorses antihypertensive therapy as a cornerstone of ICAO management, providing Medical Affairs with strong, multi-specialty, peer-validated support for positioning antihypertensive products within the secondary stroke prevention and cerebrovascular atherosclerosis treatment landscape. The near-universal consensus on optimal medical therapy as the primary treatment strategy, in the absence of strong evidence for surgical intervention, creates a favourable environment for Medical Affairs to reinforce educational messaging to neurologists, vascular surgeons, and cardiologists about aggressive blood pressure control goals in patients with established cerebrovascular occlusive disease. KOL strategy should prioritise vascular neurologists and cerebrovascular surgeons who participated in or reference this consensus, using it as a platform for advisory board discussions on the specific blood pressure targets and drug class preferences most supported by emerging evidence in ICAO patients.

Population-Level Health Gains from PM2.5 Reduction in an Intermediate-Pollution Region: A Multistate Analysis of Metabolic Multimorbidity in Korea

Revue : Environmental Pollution | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-01

Resultats principaux : This prospective cohort study enrolled 6,720 participants aged 40 years or older from the Korean Genome and Epidemiology Study, all with one or fewer metabolic diseases (hypertension, diabetes, or dyslipidemia) at baseline, with annual PM2.5 exposure modelled at 10 to 35 micrograms per cubic metre using CMAQ atmospheric modelling, and multistate Cox models applied to capture disease progression transitions. Each 10 microgram per cubic metre increase in PM2.5 was associated with a hazard ratio of 1.61 (95% CI 1.41-1.83) for progression from zero to one metabolic disease, and a hazard ratio of 1.26 (95% CI 1.13-1.40) for progression from one disease to multimorbidity, confirming that PM2.5 accelerates hypertension and cardiometabolic disease onset. Population-level modelling among 10.7 million Korean adults aged 45 to 64 estimated that reducing PM2.5 to 15, 10, and 5 micrograms per cubic metre would prevent approximately 350,000, 560,000, and 740,000 multimorbidity cases respectively over a 10-year horizon. Subgroup and sensitivity analyses using nationwide claims data confirmed the robustness of the findings. Limitations include the observational design precluding causal inference, reliance on modelled rather than individual-level PM2.5 measurements, and the Korean-specific intermediate pollution context, which may not apply to high-pollution settings in other Asian countries or to low-pollution Western countries.

Implications Medical Affairs : This study provides compelling quantitative evidence that air pollution is a major, potentially modifiable environmental driver of hypertension onset and metabolic multimorbidity, which Medical Affairs can leverage to advocate for hypertension as a disease requiring both pharmacological management and broader environmental policy engagement. The dose-response relationship between PM2.5 and hypertension progression strengthens the scientific basis for including air quality as a modifiable risk factor in patient education materials and clinical guidelines discussions, and positions antihypertensive therapy within a broader preventive medicine framework that extends beyond the clinic. KOL strategy should engage environmental health researchers, preventive cardiologists, and public health policymakers in Korea and comparable Asian markets to build a cross-disciplinary narrative where pharmacological prevention of hypertension multimorbidity is embedded within ambient air quality reduction programmes.

Development of a Confined-Space Suitability Index (CSSI) using LASSO regression: a pilot study for structured fitness-for-duty screening

Revue : PLoS One | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-02

Resultats principaux : This pilot study enrolled 111 workers and applied LASSO regression to develop a Confined-Space Suitability Index (CSSI) for structured fitness-for-duty screening across three risk domains: anthropometric and metabolic, functional and physiological, and lifestyle. The model demonstrated strong discriminative ability with an area under the receiver-operating-characteristic curve (AUROC) of 0.940, an area under the precision-recall curve (AUPRC) of 0.567, sensitivity of 0.833, specificity of 0.933, and a negative predictive value of 0.990, indicating excellent ability to correctly identify suitable workers. Hypertension, dyslipidemia, and low cardiorespiratory fitness were consistently selected as predictors across bootstrap resampling iterations, confirming their stability as occupational risk factors in confined-space fitness assessments. The CSSI classified 86 workers as suitable, 19 as requiring caution, and 6 as unsuitable, compared with 7 classified as unsuitable by a specialist physician, suggesting the index may be more sensitive in flagging at-risk workers. Limitations include the small pilot sample (N=111), single-site design, the unknown generalisability to different industries and confined-space types, and the absence of longitudinal outcome validation to confirm that CSSI classifications predict actual adverse events.

Implications Medical Affairs : The consistent selection of hypertension as a LASSO-stable predictor of occupational unfitness in confined-space environments reinforces the Medical Affairs message that uncontrolled hypertension carries tangible functional and occupational consequences beyond cardiovascular morbidity alone, strengthening the case for proactive screening and treatment in working-age populations. This evidence can be incorporated into occupational medicine-targeted communications, positioning effective antihypertensive therapy not only as a life-prolonging intervention but as a determinant of workplace safety clearance and continued employability. KOL engagement should extend to occupational physicians and industrial health policymakers who are developing standardised fitness-for-duty frameworks, where Medical Affairs can advocate for hypertension screening and control as a formal component of occupational health protocols.

Association of cumulative low-dose occupational ionizing radiation exposure with hypertension and blood pressure: a cross-sectional study of Chinese medical radiologists

Revue : Journal of Hypertension | Tier : 2 | IF estime : ~10

Date de publication : 2026-05-26

Resultats principaux : This cross-sectional study examined 5,420 medical radiologists from Guangdong Province, China, with cumulative effective dose (CED) of ionizing radiation retrospectively reconstructed from occupational records spanning 1980 to 2022; hypertension was defined using the 2017 ACC/AHA threshold of 130/80 mmHg or higher, yielding an overall hypertension prevalence of 53.4% in this occupational cohort. After multivariable adjustment, CED was positively associated with hypertension prevalence, with odds ratios of 1.35 (95% CI 1.06-1.70) for 5.00-9.99 mSv and 1.73 (95% CI 1.27-2.37) for 10.00 mSv or more, compared with less than 1.00 mSv, establishing a clear dose-response gradient. Linear regression analyses confirmed that CED was also positively associated with systolic and diastolic blood pressure as continuous outcomes, reinforcing the mechanistic plausibility of radiation-induced vascular injury leading to elevated blood pressure. Gender-specific subgroup analyses revealed that the CED-hypertension association reached statistical significance at lower thresholds in men (5.00 mSv) compared with women (10.00 mSv), suggesting possible sex-specific differences in radiation-induced cardiovascular susceptibility. Limitations include the cross-sectional design precluding temporal inference, potential residual confounding by lifestyle and other occupational factors, the use of retrospective CED reconstruction, and the fact that the 2017 ACC/AHA hypertension definition (130/80 mmHg) inflates prevalence estimates compared with older thresholds, which should be considered when comparing with other populations.

Implications Medical Affairs : This high-quality Tier 2 study published in the Journal of Hypertension establishes a novel and statistically robust dose-response relationship between occupational ionizing radiation and hypertension, opening a new occupational subgroup that may require tailored antihypertensive screening and management protocols, which Medical Affairs can highlight in targeted communications to occupational medicine specialists and radiation safety practitioners. The finding that over half of this Chinese radiologist cohort meets the 2017 ACC/AHA hypertension definition underscores the enormity of the unaddressed hypertension burden in healthcare workers, a group that ironically may be under-screened for the very conditions they diagnose in others, representing an under-exploited access channel for antihypertensive therapy. KOL strategy should include engagement with occupational cardiologists and radiation safety researchers in China and other high-volume radiology markets to position this evidence within broader conversations about expanding cardiovascular screening in healthcare professional occupational health programmes.

Association between dietary patterns and sleep duration among hypertensive patients

Revue : Journal of Hypertension | Tier : 2 | IF estime : ~10

Date de publication : 2026-05-18

Resultats principaux : This cross-sectional study enrolled 3,269 hypertensive patients and characterised dietary patterns through a semi-quantitative food frequency questionnaire, deriving four distinct dietary patterns via factor analysis; sleep duration was categorised as normal (55.86% of participants), short (38.15%), or long (6.00%), with abnormal sleep duration affecting 44.14% of the cohort, underscoring the high prevalence of sleep disturbance in this hypertensive population. A grain-vegetable dietary pattern was negatively and protectively associated with short sleep duration (OR 0.757, 95% CI 0.614-0.934), suggesting that traditional plant-based diets may mitigate sleep disturbance in hypertensive patients. Conversely, a bean-and-fried food processing pattern was positively associated with short sleep (OR 1.249, 95% CI 1.003-1.556), while a red meat-and-fruit pattern was positively associated with long sleep duration (OR 1.791, 95% CI 1.144-2.803). These bidirectional associations highlight the complexity of diet-sleep interactions and suggest that hypertension management should incorporate dietary counselling as part of a sleep optimisation strategy. Limitations include the cross-sectional design preventing causal inference between diet and sleep, potential misclassification from self-reported dietary data via FFQ, the absence of objective sleep measurement (polysomnography or actigraphy), and the lack of data on antihypertensive medication types, which could confound both dietary choices and sleep outcomes.

Implications Medical Affairs : Published in the Tier 2 Journal of Hypertension, this study provides patient-level evidence linking dietary patterns to sleep quality in hypertensive patients, reinforcing the Medical Affairs message that optimal hypertension management requires holistic lifestyle intervention addressing both diet and sleep, not pharmacological therapy alone. The finding that nearly 44% of hypertensive patients experience abnormal sleep duration identifies a large, underserved clinical subgroup where sleep disturbance may be perpetuating treatment resistance, offering Medical Affairs an opportunity to position sleep optimisation as an adjunctive strategy in treatment-refractory hypertension discussions with cardiologists and general practitioners. KOL strategy should focus on hypertension specialists and sleep medicine researchers, with the goal of co-developing patient education materials and clinical decision support tools that integrate dietary pattern screening and sleep assessment into routine hypertension follow-up visits.

Metabolic Vulnerability, Genetic Susceptibility and Risk of Hypertension: UK Biobank Cohort Analysis

Revue : The British Journal of Nutrition | Tier : NA | IF estime : NA

Date de publication : 2026-06-02

Resultats principaux : This prospective cohort study leveraged 150,591 UK Biobank participants, of whom 32,198 developed hypertension during follow-up, to investigate the joint contribution of two novel metabolic vulnerability indices, the Inflammatory Vulnerability Index (IVX, based on GlycA and small HDL particles by NMR spectroscopy) and the Metabolic Vulnerability Index (MVX, based on the branched-chain amino acids leucine, valine, isoleucine, and citrate), alongside a polygenic risk score for hypertension. Cox proportional hazards models showed that the highest versus lowest quartile of IVX carried a hazard ratio of 1.25 (95% CI 1.20-1.31) for incident hypertension, while the highest versus lowest quartile of MVX yielded HR 1.19 (95% CI 1.15-1.24); each standard deviation increase in IVX conferred 9% higher risk and each SD increase in MVX conferred 7% higher risk of developing hypertension. Interestingly, the MMX (a mitochondrial metabolite index) showed a slight protective U-shaped relationship with hypertension (HR 0.96, 95% CI 0.92-0.99). Critically, a subgroup analysis revealed that IVX and MVX associations with incident hypertension were significantly stronger in non-obese participants (BMI below 30) compared with obese individuals (p-interaction less than 0.001), suggesting that metabolic vulnerability indices may be particularly informative for identifying at-risk individuals who would be missed by conventional BMI-based screening. The polygenic risk score did not substantially modify these findings, indicating that metabolic vulnerability captures predominantly environmental and lifestyle-driven pathways, independent of genetic predisposition; limitations include the predominantly White, UK-based UK Biobank population, limiting generalisability to other ethnicities and healthcare systems.

Implications Medical Affairs : This large-scale UK Biobank analysis introduces the concept of metabolomic-based metabolic vulnerability indices as novel, actionable hypertension risk predictors, particularly powerful in non-obese individuals, and Medical Affairs should monitor this emerging biomarker field as it has the potential to define a new precision medicine subgroup for early antihypertensive intervention. The finding that genetic predisposition (polygenic risk score) does not substantially modify the metabolic vulnerability associations reinforces the centrality of modifiable lifestyle and metabolic factors in hypertension pathogenesis, providing a strong evidence base for communications emphasising the efficacy of pharmacological and lifestyle intervention even in genetically predisposed individuals. KOL strategy should engage metabolomics researchers, preventive cardiologists, and UK Biobank-affiliated investigators to explore collaborative publications or advisory board discussions that connect these novel biomarkers to clinical decision thresholds relevant to antihypertensive treatment initiation.
